# Inner Tepenia – Act 2 Progression (Flexible Structure)

**Goal**: Provide a flexible, player-driven Act 2 with multiple optional hooks for each major transition. Everything remains tied to the energy crisis, district conflicts, and player agency.

---

## Starting Point (End of Act 1)

After completing (or partially completing) the Junction 12 diagnostic in the **Capricorn District**, the player has the data-core and a clearer picture of the spreading crisis.

---

### 1. From Capricorn District → Taurus District (or Cancer District)

**Possible Hooks** (choose 1–3 that feel natural):
- A desperate resident or maintenance worker from the Taurus District approaches the player at Junction 12, begging for help rerouting emergency heat to their bonded community before people freeze.
- While reviewing the data-core with Calethina (or independently), the player sees clear evidence that residential districts are being deprioritized — motivating them to investigate the Taurus District directly.
- A Capricorn worker (possibly an early contact like Flora) mentions that the Taurus District has been loudly complaining about Capricorn “hoarding” power and suggests the player go see for themselves.
- An optional rumor or broadcast highlights suffering in the Taurus District, framing it as a human-interest story.
- The player simply explores outward from Capricorn and naturally stumbles into the Taurus District while looking for resources or side work.

### 2. From Taurus / Cancer District → Scorpio District (or Virgo District)

**Possible Hooks**:
- Residents in the Taurus or Cancer District mention rising despair, strange behavior, and people disappearing into the Undergrid or Scorpio District looking for “answers” or radical solutions.
- While helping with blackouts, the player finds data logs or witness accounts pointing to psychological strain and directs them toward the Scorpio District.
- A faction member asks for help investigating why people are turning to extreme coping methods in the Scorpio District.
- An environmental clue — strange energy fluctuations or “hauntings” in the tunnels — leads the player into the Virgo District as a natural next step.
- Optional rumor from the Black Market or a broadcast suggests something darker is happening beneath the city.

### 3. From Scorpio / Virgo District → Gemini District

**Possible Hooks**:
- While investigating in the Virgo District or Scorpio District, the player discovers corrupted Arcanet data packets that can only be properly analyzed at the Gemini District.
- An NPC suggests the Gemini District has the tools and expertise to trace the data corruption causing the grid failures.
- A data-core or terminal the player finds contains partial logs that point directly to anomalies in the Gemini District.
- The player overhears that the Gemini District is one of the few places still receiving somewhat stable power and might have answers (or spare parts).
- Optional: A faction contact asks the player to deliver or retrieve information from the Gemini District.

### 4. From Gemini District → The Black Market

**Possible Hooks**:
- The Gemini District analysis reveals that critical parts or uncensored data needed to stabilize the grid can only be found through unofficial channels.
- An NPC at the Gemini District recommends the Black Market as the only realistic source for rare components or forbidden pre-war schematics.
- The player’s growing reputation or data fragments lead them to realize that official channels are blocked or compromised, pushing them toward the Black Market.
- A broadcast or rumor in the Virgo or Scorpio District points to the Black Market having useful (if dangerous) information.
- Optional side content: Someone in the Gemini or Scorpio District needs something specific that can only be acquired through the Black Market.

### 5. From The Black Market → The Frostlands

**Possible Hooks**:
- While dealing with the Black Market, the player learns that rare materials or components needed for grid repairs can only be sourced from the frontier.
- A contact mentions that the Frostlands have been hoarding or discovering new resources in the ice.
- Environmental or data clues suggest that the grid failures are worse on the outer edges, prompting the player to investigate the Frostlands.
- A rumor or distress signal indicates that the Frostlands outposts are freezing and may have critical information or supplies.
- Optional: The player is asked to deliver something to (or retrieve something from) the Frostlands as part of a deal made in the Black Market.

### 6. From The Frostlands → Aquarius District (or directly to Aries District)

**Possible Hooks**:
- In the Frostlands, the player finds evidence or samples that point to experimental solutions only available in the Aquarius District.
- A frontier contact suggests that the researchers in Aquarius might have prototype tech that could help stabilize the grid (or at least buy time).
- The player’s data-cores or investigations reveal that experimental projects in the Aquarius District are both causing and potentially solving parts of the crisis.
- Optional: A request from the Governmental Apex or another faction sends the player to the Aquarius District for expert analysis.

### 7. From Aquarius District / Frostlands → Aries District (The Power Core)

**Possible Hooks**:
- Analysis or samples from the Aquarius District or Frostlands indicate that the root problems (and potential fixes) lie deep in the Aries District itself.
- Multiple districts or factions begin pressuring (or begging) the player to go to the Aries District as the crisis worsens.
- A major environmental event or data breakthrough makes it clear that the Aries District is the key to everything.
- Optional: The player’s growing reputation leads a faction (or Calethina) to directly ask them to investigate or stabilize the core.

---

**Design Notes for Maximum Freedom**
- No hard requirements: The player can jump between districts at any time based on exploration, side quests, or personal interest.
- Multiple on-ramps: Most districts can be reached through 3–5 different hooks, so skipping one step doesn’t break the experience.
- Natural convergence: By the time the player reaches the Aries District, they will likely have visited most major districts organically.
- Optional content: The Leo District and Calethina’s personal story can be slotted in at almost any point as flavorful side content.
