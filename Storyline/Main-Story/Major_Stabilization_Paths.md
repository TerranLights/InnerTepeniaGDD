# Major Stabilization Paths
### These are the primary philosophical approaches the player can champion while working on the power grid
The player doesn’t have to commit to only one — they can mix elements early on, but their final choices in the later chapters will push the game toward one dominant path (with hybrid possibilities).

#### 1. Technocratic Renewal (Centralized + MACHINE-First)
**Core Idea**: Fully restore and upgrade the original pre-war central grid under a new technocratic authority (possibly backed by the MACHINE or a new council). Prioritize efficiency, automation, and long-term technological progress.

**Key Features**:
- Heavy investment in the damaged geothermal core + Arcanet integration
- Robots and high-MACHINE players get priority
- Strong governance through a central authority

**Bittersweet Trade-offs**:
- Fastest stabilization, highest tech output
- Human districts feel sidelined → resentment and possible uprisings
- Risk of over-reliance on the MACHINE

#### 2. Lattice Federation (Distributed + Independent)
**Core Idea**: Abandon the failing central grid in favor of many smaller, district-level or neighborhood-level “Independent Lattices” (micro-grids). Emphasize local autonomy and resilience.

**Key Features**:
- Ties directly into existing “Independent Lattice” secret ending tech
- Districts gain real sovereignty over their own power
- Slower overall recovery but very robust against future disasters

**Bittersweet Trade-offs**:
- High local freedom and fairness
- Some districts will still struggle or fail
- Weaker coordinated defense or big projects

#### 3. Human-First Consolidation
**Core Idea**: Prioritize human survival and comfort above all else. Ration power heavily toward residential districts, medical, and food production. Robots and non-essential systems get reduced allocation.

**Key Features**:
- Strong alliances with human-centric districts
- Possible forced shutdowns or “recycling” of older robot populations
- Cultural emphasis on “humanity” over MACHINE stats

**Bittersweet Trade-offs**:
- Best short-term quality of life for humans
- Massive robot unrest and possible MACHINE backlash
- Slower technological recovery

#### 4. Hybrid Equilibrium (Balanced Compromise)
**Core Idea**: A negotiated middle path — partial central grid repair + widespread micro-lattices + fair (but not equal) distribution. Try to keep both humans and robots satisfied.

**Key Features**:
- Requires the most negotiation and diplomacy
- Calethina’s personal questline can give big bonuses here (if pursued)

**Bittersweet Trade-offs**:
- Most “fair” feeling outcome
- Fragile compromise — nothing is truly optimized
- Highest chance of long-term instability if tensions flare later

#### 5. Radical Reclamation (Aggressive + Expansionist)
**Core Idea**: Use the crisis as an opportunity to aggressively expand outward or tap dangerous new power sources (deeper geothermal, experimental Arcanet draws, or pre-war relics). Growth at any cost.

**Key Features**:
- High risk, high reward
- Strong military/industrial focus
- Possible contact with outside forces (including Calethina ties)

**Bittersweet Trade-offs**:
- Fastest power growth and potential territorial expansion
- Environmental damage, accidents, or attracting external threats
- Very high chance of new conflicts
