### High-Level Proposal – Main Quest Structure

**Core Objective**  
Stabilize Concordia’s failing power grid and make meaningful decisions about **how power should be generated, distributed, and used**. There is no single “perfect” fix — only different philosophies with real trade-offs and consequences.

The structure is **modular / chapter-based**, allowing players to tackle chapters in flexible order, skip some content, or sequence-break heavily for speedruns.

| Chapter                    | Core Problem                              | Key Decision                                      | Fallout / Consequences                          | Speedrun Potential |
|---------------------------|-------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|---------------------|
| **The Core Problem**      | Understand why the grid is failing + perform initial crisis triage | How much effort to put into immediate stabilization vs long-term restructuring | Early blackouts, rising faction tension, urgency | Very High (can rush key locations) |
| **Power Source**          | Identify and secure viable power sources (geothermal core, backups, Lattice, alternatives) | What primary source(s) to rely on long-term      | Environmental damage, political backlash, or technological dependency | High |
| **Distribution Philosophy** | Decide who gets priority access to power | Fairness vs Efficiency vs Survival vs Special Interests | District loyalty shifts, unrest, alliances or rebellions | Medium-High |
| **Governance & Control**  | Determine who will control and maintain the stabilized grid | Centralized authority, distributed control, hybrid, or something radical | New power struggles, fragile peace, or emerging factions | Medium |
| **Fallout & Legacy**      | Live with the consequences of your choices | Final stance on the new order                    | Bittersweet epilogues based on cumulative decisions | Varies |

---

**Design Notes**
- Chapters can be approached in almost any order (with light gating for story coherence).
- The game is built for **speedrunning**: a dedicated player who knows the optimal path could theoretically finish the main quest in under an hour (with a theoretical minimum of ~10–15 minutes if heavy sequence breaking is possible).
- All paths lead to **bittersweet endings** — there is always some cost and compromise, never a flawless victory.
- Calethina remains a **minor character** unless the player actively pursues her optional personal questline (which can unlock her as a companion/guide on the player’s wrist device).
