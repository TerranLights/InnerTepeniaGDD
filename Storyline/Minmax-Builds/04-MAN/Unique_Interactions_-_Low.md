# Minmax Build 04: Might + Agility + Nerve (Three Low Stats) - Special Interactions and Questlines

**Document Status**: Draft  
**Build Overview**: This "Min" build focuses on a character with **low Might (≤3)**, **low Agility (≤3)**, and **low Nerve (≤3)**. The player is physically weak, clumsy and uncoordinated, and mentally fragile — easily frightened, quick to panic, low stress tolerance, and prone to giving up under pressure.

This creates a highly vulnerable, "frail coward" archetype who is physically limited, moves awkwardly, and breaks down easily in dangerous or stressful situations. The character is often pitied, protected, dismissed as harmless, or used as bait. They must rely heavily on other stats (especially Calculation, Humanity, Investigation, or Engine) or turn their obvious weakness into unexpected advantages through pity, being overlooked, or accidental outcomes.

**Design Philosophy**: Interactions emphasize physical frailty, clumsiness, and emotional/mental fragility. They should feel limiting and stressful, but also open unique paths through pity, protective alliances, being ignored as non-threatening, or "coward's luck" moments. Many can lead to underdog questlines, survival-through-fear, or unconventional routes toward minmax endings.

## District-Specific Special/Unique Interactions (Minimum 5 per District)

- Gets heavily pitied by caregivers, who assign them "safe" tasks or offer extra protection.
- Clumsy panic movements accidentally knock over equipment, revealing hidden compartments or data.
- Freezes or runs from minor dangers, creating opportunities for others to step in and share secrets.

## Design Notes
- These interactions highlight the character's **physical weakness, clumsiness, and mental fragility** — they panic easily, freeze under pressure, and are often a liability, but this invites pity, protection, and being underestimated.
- Many interactions can combine with high stats in Calculation, Investigation, Humanity, or Engine for strong contrast (e.g., a brilliant mind trapped in a weak, fearful, clumsy body).
- Questlines often lean into pity-based alliances, "harmless coward," or accidental discovery archetypes.
