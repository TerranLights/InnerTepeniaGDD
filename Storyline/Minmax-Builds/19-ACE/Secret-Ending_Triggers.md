# Alternate Minmax Endings - "Not My Fucking Problem" Path
## Minmax Build 19: Agility + Calculation + Engine

**Document Status**: Draft  
**Build Focus**: High **Agility** (speed, reflexes, coordination, stealth, and graceful movement), **Calculation** (strategic planning, optimization, predictive modeling, and foresight), and **Engine** (mechanical mastery, crafting, repair, optimization, and fabrication).  
This creates a lightning-fast strategic artisan: a ghost-like operative who moves with perfect grace, calculates optimal outcomes, and fabricates or optimizes machines on the fly while rejecting all external dependencies.

**Target Endings**:  
- Phantom Courier  
- Iron Nomad (high-mobility variant)  
- Eternal Engine (speed variant)  
- Architect of Collapse (mobile variant)  
- Forge Lord (lightning artisan variant)  
- Any other "Not My Fucking Problem" endings that reward high-speed fabrication, calculated mobility, and graceful self-sufficiency.

**Design Philosophy**: Triggers reward fluid high-speed action, cold strategic foresight, and masterful on-the-move engineering. The player darts through danger like lightning, calculates perfect solutions, and turns scrap into superior personal technology in real time — then vanishes before the city can react.

## Design Notes
- All triggers heavily favor the Agility + Calculation + Engine triad — blending graceful high-speed movement, strategic foresight, and masterful on-the-move fabrication.
- Many triggers can chain: e.g., World Trigger 3 (route data) → NPC 6 (high-speed reconnaissance rig) → NPC 9 (high-mobility caravan leadership).
- Reputation impact: These paths grant unmatched mobility and fabrication depth while alienating districts reliant on slow planning or static infrastructure.
- Tone: Every trigger reinforces the "Not My Fucking Problem" mindset — you move like lightning, calculate the perfect solution, fabricate it on the fly, and vanish before the city can react.
