# Minmax Build 07: Might + Humanity + Engine (Three Low Stats) - Special Interactions and Questlines

**Document Status**: Draft  
**Build Overview**: This "Min" build focuses on a character with **low Might (≤3)**, **low Humanity (≤3)**, and **low Engine (≤3)**. The player is physically weak, emotionally/socially tone-deaf (blunt, awkward, low empathy), and has extremely poor endurance — tires out almost immediately from any effort.

This creates a **"frail, socially awkward, and constantly exhausted"** underdog archetype. The character is physically limited, frequently says the wrong thing, struggles to read emotions or build connections, and burns out so quickly that they become a major burden. They are often pitied, protected, dismissed as useless or rude, or treated as a harmless but annoying burden. Their triple limitations can open unique paths through sympathy, being overlooked, accidental blunt honesty, or reliance on other stats (especially Calculation, Investigation, Nerve, or Agility).

**Design Philosophy**: Interactions emphasize physical weakness, emotional/social clumsiness, and rapid burnout. They should feel limiting, awkward, and exhausting, but also create opportunities via pity, protective alliances, accidental raw honesty that cuts through social barriers, or "harmless fool" dynamics. Many can lead to underdog questlines, pity-based relationships, or unconventional routes toward minmax endings.

## District-Specific Special/Unique Interactions (Minimum 5 per District)

- Clumsy exhaustion causes minor accidents that accidentally reveal hidden areas or data.
- Socially awkward reactions to trauma create uncomfortable but sometimes cathartic moments.
- Rapid burnout during crises leads to being carried or shielded, exposing private conversations.

## Design Notes
- These interactions highlight the character's **physical weakness, emotional/social awkwardness, and extremely poor stamina** — they tire out quickly, say the wrong thing, and are often a burden, strongly inviting pity mixed with frustration.
- Many interactions can combine with high stats in Calculation, Investigation, Nerve, or Agility for strong contrast (e.g., a brilliant but frail, blunt, and quickly exhausted character).
- Questlines often lean into pity-based alliances, "awkward burden," or accidental honesty archetypes.
