# Minmax Build 01: Might + Agility + Computation (Three Low Stats) - Special Interactions and Questlines

**Document Status**: Draft  
**Build Overview**: This "Min" build focuses on a character with **low Might (≤3)**, **low Agility (≤3)**, and **low Calculation (≤3)**. The player is physically weak, clumsy/uncoordinated, and struggles with logic, optimization, and complex problem-solving.  

This creates an underdog playstyle where the character is often underestimated, physically limited, and intellectually challenged — but can have unique, flavorful interactions that high-stat characters miss. These can lead to pity, mockery, unexpected alliances, accidental discoveries, or workarounds that leverage other strengths (assuming average-to-high stats in Humanity, Investigation, Nerve, and/or Engine).

**Design Philosophy**: Interactions emphasize the limitations and unexpected opportunities of being weak, clumsy, and not very smart. They should feel authentic to the stats while offering special dialogue options, environmental workarounds, social dynamics, or quest branches that reward creative or empathetic play. Many can chain into larger "underdog" questlines or lead toward specific minmax endings through unconventional means.

---

## District-Specific Special/Unique Interactions (Minimum 5 per District)

### 1. Cancer (Nurturing/Protective Sanctuary)
- Too weak to carry patients or heavy medical supplies, forcing reliance on coordination, empathy, or clever workarounds (e.g., using carts or getting help in humiliating ways).
- Clumsily knocks over equipment or trips in crowded care areas, accidentally revealing hidden compartments, smuggling, or experimental notes.
- Misunderstands medical instructions or triage priorities, leading to unique dialogue where NPCs explain things simply or pity the player enough to share restricted information.
- Struggles with fine motor tasks like adjusting delicate medical devices, but can use raw honesty or emotional appeals to get others to do it for them.
- Gets treated as harmless/non-threatening by staff or refugees, allowing access to restricted grief sanctums or private conversations that stronger characters are denied.

### 2. Taurus (Residential/Stability)
- Physically unable to help with heavy lifting, repairs, or moving furniture, leading to being assigned "lighter" (often more social or observational) tasks that reveal neighborhood secrets.
- Clumsily damages personal property while trying to help, creating opportunities for apologetic interactions or debt-based questlines.
- Misunderstands complex household systems or resource allocation, leading to humorous or insightful "dumb" questions that expose hoarding or black-market activity.
- Gets underestimated as non-threatening, allowing stealthy (or accidental) eavesdropping in residential courtyards.
- Struggles with endurance during neighborhood patrols or manual labor, forcing creative delegation or alliance-building with residents.

### 3. Leo (Cultural/Performative)
- Too clumsy and weak for stage work or physical performance roles, but can accidentally disrupt shows in ways that lead to unique backstage access or comedic relief roles.
- Misunderstands artistic or technical instructions for lights/sound/stagecraft, leading to "helpful" mistakes that reveal hidden mechanisms or celebrity secrets.
- Gets treated as harmless comic relief by performers, allowing close access to VIP areas or private conversations.
- Struggles with crowd navigation or quick movements during events, creating opportunities to observe from odd angles or be "rescued" into interesting situations.
- Fails at complex optimization of performance systems but can offer emotionally intuitive (if logically flawed) suggestions that resonate with artists.

### 4. Scorpio (Psychological/Transformative)

- Clumsily interferes with delicate shadow-work setups, accidentally triggering revelations or forcing improvisations.
- Misunderstands complex psychological concepts or instructions, leading to blunt but sometimes insightful questions that bypass normal defenses.
- Gets underestimated as non-threatening or pitiable, allowing access to private therapy sessions or forbidden knowledge.
- Struggles with fine motor tasks in augmentation parlors but can use emotional honesty to build trust with practitioners.

### 5. Aries (Power Core / Raw Survival)

- Gets treated as non-threatening by security or stressed engineers, allowing closer observation of restricted areas.
- Struggles with sustained physical work in the core, creating opportunities for emotional/support roles or alliances with sympathetic staff.

## Design Notes
- These interactions emphasize the **limitations and unexpected advantages** of being physically weak, clumsy, and intellectually limited. The player is often underestimated, which can be a strength.
- Many interactions can chain or combine with high stats in Humanity, Investigation, Nerve, or Engine for powerful roleplaying (e.g., using empathy or intuition to compensate for low Calculation).
- Questlines can lead toward protector/underdog endings, information-gathering paths, or unconventional alliances.
- Consider combining with low-stat single interactions for even more depth.
