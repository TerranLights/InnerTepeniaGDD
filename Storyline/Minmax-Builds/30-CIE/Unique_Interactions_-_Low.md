# Special Interactions & Quest Triggers - Low Minmax Builds
## Minmax Build 30: Calculation + Investigation + Engine (Low)

**Document Status**: Draft  
**Build Focus**: Low **Calculation** (poor strategic planning, weak foresight, and difficulty with optimization), **Investigation** (poor perception, weak deduction, difficulty noticing clues, and missing obvious details), and **Engine** (poor mechanical aptitude, inability to repair or craft effectively, tendency to break things).  
This creates an **oblivious, destructive, terrible-planner**: a character who misses obvious clues, makes awful strategic decisions, and breaks or ruins technology they touch, often becoming a pitiable burden or walking disaster.

**Design Philosophy**: Interactions and triggers emphasize the player’s poor planning, investigative blindness, and mechanical incompetence. These limitations frequently cause mishaps, broken equipment, missed dangers, failed repairs, and situations where the player is seen as a liability or harmless fool. This can lead to pity-based alliances, being dismissed as irrelevant, or accidental usefulness when others fix what the player broke.

## District Interactions

1. Makes terrible long-term migration calculations and misses obvious trail markers or resource signs.
2. Tries (and fails) to repair damaged scouting gear or vehicles, often breaking them further.
3. Investigative weakness leads to overlooking clear threats or opportunities until it’s too late.
4. Gets pitied as “the one who can’t plan or fix anything,” earning reluctant protective escorts.
5. Mechanical failure and obliviousness make you seem completely non-threatening, allowing passive overhearing of private frontier conversations.
