# Alternate Minmax Endings - "Not My Fucking Problem" Path
## Minmax Build 30: Calculation + Investigation + Engine

**Document Status**: Draft  
**Build Focus**: High **Calculation** (strategic planning, optimization, predictive modeling, and foresight), **Investigation** (perception, deduction, clue-tracking, forensics, and secret uncovering), and **Engine** (mechanical mastery, crafting, repair, optimization, and fabrication).  
This creates a brilliant relic-forger genius: a calculating, hyper-perceptive artisan who uncovers lost secrets, predicts optimal outcomes, and fabricates superior technology on the fly while pursuing total independence.

**Target Endings**:  
- Relic Hunter  
- Architect of Collapse  
- Ghost of the Undergrid  
- Eternal Engine (analytical variant)  
- Forge Lord (mastermind variant)  
- Any other "Not My Fucking Problem" endings that reward information dominance, strategic fabrication, and calculated self-sufficiency.

**Design Philosophy**: Triggers reward sharp deduction, cold strategic foresight, and masterful hands-on reconstruction. The player uncovers what others cannot, calculates perfect solutions, and turns lost or broken tech into superior personal systems — then vanishes with their hoard, leaving Concordia to its ignorance and collapse.

## Design Notes
- All triggers heavily favor the Calculation + Investigation + Engine triad — blending strategic foresight, relentless truth-seeking, and masterful on-the-move fabrication.
- Many triggers can chain: e.g., World Trigger 3 (route data) → NPC 6 (mobile fabrication lab) → NPC 9 (scavenging expedition leadership).
- Reputation impact: These paths grant massive information dominance, personal crafting depth, and tactical advantages while alienating districts that prefer secrets stay buried or regulated.
- Tone: Every trigger reinforces the "Not My Fucking Problem" mindset — you uncover the truth, calculate the perfect solution, rip the past out with your hands, rebuild it better for yourself, and vanish before the city can react.
