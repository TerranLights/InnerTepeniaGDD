# Minmax Build 03: Might + Agility + Investigation (Three Low Stats) - Special Interactions and Questlines

**Document Status**: Draft  
**Build Overview**: This "Min" build focuses on a character with **low Might (≤3)**, **low Agility (≤3)**, and **low Investigation (≤3)**. The player is physically weak, clumsy and uncoordinated, and perceptually oblivious — frequently missing obvious details, clues, and environmental cues while struggling with basic movement and strength.

This creates a highly vulnerable, "blind and bumbling" underdog who is often overlooked, pitied, or dismissed as harmless. The character frequently causes accidental chaos through clumsiness and misses critical information that others spot immediately. They must rely heavily on other stats (especially Calculation, Humanity, Nerve, or Engine) or turn their limitations into unexpected advantages (being ignored, pitied, or underestimated).

**Design Philosophy**: Interactions emphasize physical frailty, lack of grace, and perceptual blindness. They should feel frustrating and limiting, but also open unique paths through being dismissed as non-threatening, accidental discoveries caused by clumsiness, or reliance on pure luck/other strengths. Many can lead to "underdog" questlines, pity-based alliances, or unconventional routes toward minmax endings.

## District-Specific Special/Unique Interactions (Minimum 5 per District)

### 1. Cancer (Nurturing/Protective Sanctuary)
- Too weak and clumsy to carry patients or navigate crowded care halls without tripping and causing minor accidents.
- Completely misses subtle signs of distress, smuggling, or hidden injuries that others notice immediately.
- Gets pitied by caregivers as "the poor clumsy one," receiving extra help or access to restricted areas out of sympathy.
- Clumsily knocks over equipment or bumps into things, accidentally revealing hidden compartments or data shards.
- Struggles with basic environmental awareness (e.g., missing wet floors or low obstacles), leading to comedic or dangerous slips.

### 2. Taurus (Residential/Stability)
- Physically unable to help with lifting or repairs, often getting in the way and causing more problems.
- Misses obvious signs of resource hoarding, neighbor disputes, or hidden entrances due to poor perception.
- Clumsy movement in tight residential spaces leads to breaking items or alerting people unintentionally.
- Gets underestimated and dismissed as harmless, allowing passive eavesdropping or access to private areas.
- Fails to notice social tensions or "old-timer vs newcomer" cues, leading to awkward but revealing interactions.

### 3. Leo (Cultural/Performative)

- Fails to notice crowd patterns or security movements, creating opportunities to stumble into restricted zones.

## Design Notes
- These interactions highlight the character's **physical weakness, clumsiness, and perceptual blindness** — they frequently miss the obvious and cause minor chaos, but this can make them underestimated or pitied in useful ways.
- Many interactions can combine with high stats in Calculation, Humanity, Nerve, or Engine for strong contrast (e.g., a brilliant strategist trapped in a weak, clumsy, oblivious body).
- Questlines often lean into "harmless fool," pity-based alliances, or accidental discovery archetypes.
