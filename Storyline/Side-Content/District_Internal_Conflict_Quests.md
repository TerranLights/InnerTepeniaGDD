# District Internal Conflict Quests
## One capstone quest per district, designed from the internal conflict parties in District_Unity_of_Opposites.md

Each quest puts the player between the specific factions within a district — not between districts. The resolution paths include a "Unity Path" where the player can find and act on the underlying interdependence between conflicting parties. These quests are designed to be available during Act 2 and to feed into the District Idolized ending conditions.

For inter-district quest triggers, see `Quest-Triggers.md`.

---

## District 1 — Cancer: **"The Night of Locked Doors"**

**Internal Conflict:** The Unlimited vs. Triage Realists vs. Trauma-Bearers vs. Surveillance Faction

**Inciting Situation:** A refugee wave from Sagittarius arrives larger than anticipated. Cancer is already at capacity. The Mother's Circuit — the district's distributed caregiving data network — has begun generating intake priority rankings. The Surveillance Faction's algorithm is being used to determine who gets through the doors. The Trauma-Bearers recognize the logic: it's the Overcrowding Decision of 2721 again, just dressed in data. Meanwhile, specific people are waiting outside in the cold.

**Parties:**
- **The Unlimited** (elder caregiver, has run Cancer's primary shelter for forty years): "The doors stay open. We have always found a way."
- **The Triage Realists** (current district intake administrator): "The circuit tells us who has the best chance of integrating. We use the information or we abandon all of them."
- **The Trauma-Bearers** (a robot who was on the wrong side of 2721 and lived): "I've seen this algorithm before. It had a different name then. We destroyed the records. Are we going to let it happen again with better documentation?"
- **The Surveillance Faction** (Mother's Circuit architect): "The circuit doesn't make the choice. It gives us better information. The choice is still ours."

**The Dilemma:** The player traces the algorithm's logic and finds a specific family being denied entry: Upper Earth Defectors, flagged as "low integration probability." The circuit isn't neutral — "integration probability" was trained on historical intake data that encodes the district's existing biases about who is welcome. The algorithm is reproducing a prejudice without anyone having decided to.

**Resolution Paths:**

1. *Back the algorithm.* The district survives the wave with minimal resource strain. The family is turned away. Three of them die in cold-exposure before reaching another shelter. The Mother's Circuit has now made a life-and-death decision that will appear in no record Cancer controls.

2. *Override the algorithm.* Let everyone in. Care quality drops across the entire district as staff are overwhelmed. An existing long-term patient dies of a preventable complication. The family survives. The cost is real and distributed rather than concentrated.

3. *Unity Path (requires H 6+ or I 6+):* Expose the bias without destroying the circuit. The Surveillance Faction's architect, confronted with the specific data chain that produced the "low integration probability" for Upper Earth Defectors, cannot defend it as methodology. Work with the Trauma-Bearers to rewrite the intake criteria — explicitly, on the record, in a way that makes the next 2721 harder to paper over. The family gets in. The Mother's Circuit is improved. But the Trauma-Bearers are now on the record about 2721 by name, and some of them didn't want that. The past is now less buried. That's the cost of this path.

**Ripples:** Unity Path is the only resolution that improves Cancer's intake system long-term. Paths 1 and 2 leave Cancer in the same structural position for the next wave. All paths affect Cancer's reputation with the Upper Earth Defector community at Echo Camp (Sagittarius).

## District 2 — Taurus: **"What the Cold Side Knows"**

**Internal Conflict:** The Established vs. Cold-Siders vs. Unverifiables vs. Newcomers

**Inciting Situation:** A Cold Side apartment block's insulation has failed — built to lesser spec decades ago, finally killed by a recent Aries power fluctuation. The district council is slow to respond. A Cold-Side robot (sixty years in Taurus, warm-side political capital: none) wants to use the emergency to force a renegotiation of insulation resource allocation. An Unverifiable family — bonds never re-verified after the Registry Collapse of 2698 — is among those in the failing building. Without verified bond status, they have no housing rights in Taurus. The district is technically not obligated to help them.

**Parties:**
- **The Established** (district council representative): "We'll fix the insulation. We always do. But this isn't the moment for political restructuring — people are cold."
- **Cold-Side rep** (robot, sixty-year resident, never held a council seat): "This is exactly the moment. This exact thing happened before. It will happen again. A patch is just the next time, scheduled."
- **The Unverifiables** (family spokesperson, exhausted): "We just want to not freeze. Can we argue about the structure later?"
- **Newcomer** (arrived from Cancer six months ago): "In Cancer we'd have everyone sheltered already. I don't understand why this is complicated."

**The Dilemma:** Fixing the immediate problem (patching the insulation) is fast and technically easy. The Cold-Side rep is structurally correct — it will happen again. But pushing for the systemic fix takes weeks, and people are cold now.

**Resolution Paths:**

1. *Patch it fast.* Everyone survives. The Cold-Side rep is grateful but bitter. The Established have demonstrated that problems get fixed — eventually, on their schedule. In ten years, same building, same failure.

2. *Force the political fight.* The insulation gets fixed, but weeks later, after a successful council petition. Someone on the Cold Side gets sick from prolonged exposure during the delay. The systemic reform happens. The cost has a name and a face.

3. *Unity Path (requires H 5+ or N 6+):* Make the case in the Established's own language. Frame insulation equity as a bond preservation issue — cold homes break families, and broken families are the one thing Taurus cannot tolerate. Bring the Unverifiable family's situation into the council chamber as the proof case. The Established can't argue against family stability without contradicting everything they stand for. The emergency fix and the structural reform happen together. The Unverifiables receive provisional bond recognition as part of the agreement. The Cold-Side rep is genuinely surprised. The Newcomer has just watched Taurus work the way it should.

**Ripples:** Unity Path is the only resolution that addresses the Unverifiables' legal status. All paths affect Cold-Side/Warm-Side relationship tracking within Taurus for the rest of the game.

## District 3 — Leo: **"Two Houses, One Stage"**

**Internal Conflict:** Grand Performance Faction vs. Intimate Underground vs. Cymaticists

**Inciting Situation:** "Radiant Nights" — Leo's annual festival — is approaching. The Grand Faction controls the main amphitheater and is planning its most ambitious spectacle in years. The Intimate Underground has booked a small venue nearby for "The Candlelight" — explicitly invoking The Dimming as sacred memory, with a robot performer who was a child during The Dimming and has never performed publicly. The Cymaticists want to use the festival as a city-wide acoustic experiment, tuning the district's resonance architecture to a specific frequency achievable only during a rare planetary alignment: once every eleven years.

All three events are scheduled simultaneously. The Cymaticist experiment requires resonance conditions that the Grand spectacle's bass arrays will destroy.

**Parties:**
- **Grand Faction leader**: "There is one Radiant Nights. These parallel events are parasites on our hype. The Cymaticists will ruin our acoustics."
- **Intimate Underground lead**: "The Grand Faction has claimed Radiant Nights for fifteen years. The Dimming wasn't grand — it was people performing by candlelight because it was all they had. That's what we're honoring."
- **Cymaticist elder**: "Both of them are thinking about what they produce. We're thinking about what all of it does together. The resonance of three concurrent events at different scales has never happened. We don't know what it makes."

**The Dilemma:** Coordination requires all three leaders in the same room. Each believes their event is the real one. The Cymaticist experiment cannot be rescheduled. The Underground performer's window — their health, their nerve, their first and possibly last attempt — won't wait either.

**Resolution Paths:**

1. *Back the Grand Faction.* Radiant Nights runs as planned. The Underground cancels. The Cymaticist experiment fails. Leo's social hierarchy is confirmed. The Underground performer doesn't try again for years.

2. *Mediate a staggered schedule.* Each event gets a window. It works logistically but dilutes every experience. Nothing exceptional happens. The Cymaticist data is partial. The Underground performer performs to a tired audience after the main event.

3. *Unity Path (requires H 6+ or Sonic Resonance trait):* The Cymaticists' resonance model, when explained properly to someone willing to understand it, reveals that the three-event cascade — if coordinated rather than competing — produces exactly the conditions the Cymaticists need. Grand scale plus intimate precision plus frequency targeting simultaneously has never been attempted. Bring all three leaders to the same table with this information. The Grand Faction participates because it's the biggest thing Leo has ever done. The Underground performs at The Candlelight to twelve people — and every journalist in the city cites it as the performance of the decade. The Cymaticist elder doesn't speak for twenty minutes after the experiment concludes. Nobody fully understands what happened. Leo talks about it for a generation.

**Ripples:** Unity Path is the prerequisite for the Cymaticist faction becoming accessible in later acts. The Underground performer — if given their moment — becomes an important NPC contact. The Grand/Intimate divide in Leo's social hierarchy is unresolved by any path; this quest shifts the balance of power between them but doesn't end the conflict.

## District 4 — Scorpio: **"The Shape of What Remains"**

**Internal Conflict:** Clinical School vs. Sacred/Goth School vs. The Altered

**Inciting Situation:** A high-profile case comes to Scorpio: a former city council aide who survived the Long Night War by making choices they have never fully disclosed. They've undergone multiple rounds of rebirth therapy with diminishing returns. Now they're requesting memory reconstruction — not erasure, but construction: filling the gaps in their memory with plausible material synthesized from their personality profiles. The Clinical School has ethics board approval. The Sacred School believes the procedure is a desecration. The Altered — one specific survivor of a similar procedure that went slightly wrong — has been asked to provide testimony.

**Parties:**
- **Clinical School lead**: "The patient is in ongoing suffering. The procedure is approved. We proceed."
- **Sacred School practitioner**: "You cannot construct a self from outside. What you build is a monument, not a person. The patient doesn't need reconstruction — they need confrontation."
- **The Altered** (stable, functional, and recognizably different from who they were): "I'm the outcome of what happens when the reconstruction works correctly by clinical standards and produces something the person didn't expect. You should tell the patient that. Before."

**The Dilemma:** The patient has a right to the procedure. The Clinical approval is legitimate. The Sacred School's objection is philosophical, not legal. But The Altered's testimony reframes the question: this isn't about whether the procedure is ethical in principle. It's about whether the patient knows what "success" looks like from the inside.

**Resolution Paths:**

1. *Allow the Clinical procedure.* It goes well technically. The patient is stable and functional. Six months later they live in a gentle persistent dissociation from their pre-procedure self that no clinical category covers. The Clinical School calls this acceptable. The Sacred School says this is exactly what they described.

2. *Support the Sacred School.* The procedure is refused. The patient is guided into confrontation therapy. It's brutal. Two years of it. They come out the other side having faced what they did and who they are. They're changed. They're whole. They're not happy — but they're present.

3. *Unity Path (requires H 6+ or C 7+):* Bring The Altered directly into the patient's consultation — not as a warning, as a witness. Not "this could happen to you" but "this is what it actually felt like from the inside." Give the patient access to a genuinely informed decision: not between Clinical and Sacred, but between two kinds of change they now understand experientially. Whatever path the patient chooses after that conversation is theirs. The quest resolves not with an outcome but with consent that is real. In Scorpio's terms, that's the only clean ending.

**Ripples:** Unity Path is the only resolution that earns the trust of all three Scorpio factions simultaneously. The patient's eventual choice (if alive at the climax) affects one of Scorpio's minor ending branches. The Clinical/Sacred tension is unresolved by any path — it's structural to the district.

## District 5 — Aries: **"Thirty-Seven Minutes"**

**Internal Conflict:** Overclockers vs. Safety Engineers vs. Political Faction

**Inciting Situation:** A section of the power core begins failing in a pattern that matches documented conditions from the Black Silence. The crew on shift has been trained on the official record (4 minutes to resolution) and the oral tradition (12 minutes). They're now 37 minutes into an active crisis with no precedent they know of that lasted this long, making decisions based on wrong information. Libra's classified file on the Black Silence says 47 minutes — and the player's investigation has turned up the file number, though not the contents.

**Parties:**
- **Overclocker shift leader** (on-site, core failing): "We push through. The core has survived this before. We go down with it or we stop it."
- **Safety Engineer on shift**: "We've been told three different timelines. I want the real number before I commit this crew to another hour of this."
- **Political Faction operative** (monitoring remotely): "Whatever happens here — success or failure — becomes the argument. If we survive without Libra's file, we prove we don't need them. If we fail, we prove they've been lying to us about our own history."

**The Dilemma:** Getting the real timeline from Libra's classified file requires either a political contact (time-consuming under active crisis conditions) or breaking into the archive (a crime, and Libra will know). The Overclocker wants to act without the information. The Political Faction wants to use the crisis regardless of outcome.

**Resolution Paths:**

1. *Push through with the Overclockers.* It works, barely. The crew is heroes. The actual timeline is never established. Aries' oral tradition gains "37 minutes." Libra's files stay sealed. Nothing changes about the information asymmetry.

2. *Get the Safety Engineer the real timeline.* This means either breaking into Libra's archive (high risk, possible in several ways) or calling in a political favor. The real timeline is 47 minutes — they're almost out of time. The knowledge changes the crew's decision-making in the final stretch. They survive. Aries now has Libra's classified file. The Political Faction gets what they wanted.

3. *Unity Path (requires I 6+ or C 7+):* The Safety Engineer's request for the timeline reaches a True Believer inside Libra — a junior archivist who has been looking for exactly this kind of moment. The archivist breaks protocol and transmits the classified timeline. The crew survives. The archivist's breach is the first crack in Libra's dossier-wall. It becomes a political event that neither district fully controls, that runs in parallel with the energy crisis, and that feeds into the Libra internal conflict quest ("The Suspended Compact") if that quest is also active.

**Ripples:** Path 3 links Aries and Libra's internal conflicts into a single chain event. The Political Faction's goals are partially achieved by any path in which the classified file is obtained. The Overclocker/Safety Engineer tension is never fully resolved — it's structural to Aries.

## District 6 — Capricorn: **"The Audit"**

**Internal Conflict:** Merit Establishment vs. Robot Rights Underground vs. Guild Loyalists

**Inciting Situation:** Someone has leaked the full original Merit Board Audit of 2761 onto Janbogo's public channels — not the sanitized summary Capricorn published, but the complete dataset: sixty-three years of equivalent output, different merit ratings, sorted by species. The Underground has been waiting for this. The Establishment is in damage control. The Guild Loyalists — human and robot workers who have moved up through the system and believed in it — are being forced to pick a side for the first time.

**Parties:**
- **Establishment foreman** (upper-spire, earned his position over thirty years): "The methodology was flawed. We've implemented the relevant improvements. This leak is a destabilization campaign."
- **Underground organizer** (robot, fifteen years in Capricorn, never above mid-spire): "We have sixty-three years of records in this document. Sixty-three years of 'equivalent output, different rating.' This is not a methodology dispute."
- **Guild Loyalist** (human, thirty years in the district, recently promoted to upper-mid): "I believe in the system. I've moved up in it. But I've also watched people not move up when they should have. If the audit is correct, I have to think about where I am."

**The Dilemma:** The audit is already public — that ship has sailed. The question is what happens next. The Underground wants immediate reclassification of all robot merit ratings, which would disrupt spire allocations and slow production during the energy crisis. The Establishment wants to offer minor concessions that leave the structure intact. The Guild Loyalists have the social capital the Underground lacks and the credibility the Establishment needs — they're the swing.

**Resolution Paths:**

1. *Help the Establishment control the narrative.* Minor public commitments, minor adjustments. The Underground goes underground again. In five years, nothing has changed except the audit exists publicly.

2. *Back the Underground's full reclassification push.* Production slows for six weeks during spire recalculation. The merit system is genuinely restructured. The disruption costs the city siligel output at a time when the energy crisis makes that costly. Cancer and Taurus feel it. The reform is real.

3. *Unity Path (requires C 7+ or I 5+):* The Establishment's entire identity rests on Capricorn producing the best outcomes through the best methods. Make that argument back at them. A merit system with a sixty-three-year structural bias is not producing best outcomes — it's producing outcomes from a compromised dataset. Bring the Guild Loyalists in first: they're persuadable, and they have credibility the Underground lacks. A coalition of Guild Loyalists and Underground members, making the case in the Establishment's own language, is harder to dismiss than a protest. The reform happens. Slower and less complete than the Underground wanted. More complete than the Establishment intended. The Robot Rights Underground surfaces — publicly, legally — for the first time. The Merit Board Audit of 2761 becomes curriculum in Capricorn's guild apprenticeship programs.

**Ripples:** Unity Path is the prerequisite for the Robot Rights faction becoming a named, accessible faction in Act 3. Path 2 produces the reform but at a cost that affects relations with Cancer and Taurus. Path 1 sets up a more explosive conflict event later.

## District 7 — Aquarius: **"The Living Network"**

**Internal Conflict:** Pure Researchers vs. Applied Innovators vs. Utopians

**Inciting Situation:** The Living Network Experiment — Aquarius' longest-running communal society project, now approximately thirty years old — is approaching collapse for an unexpected reason: it worked. Residents have developed a genuine collective identity so robust that individual decision-making is compromised. They are not prevented from leaving. The idea of individuation has simply become uncomfortable in a way that functions like addiction. The Applied Innovators want to document the results and dissolve the experiment. The Pure Researchers want five more years of data. The Utopians who designed it are defensive and horrified in equal measure.

**Parties:**
- **Applied Innovators lead**: "This is the most significant social data we've ever produced. We document everything thoroughly, then we safely dissolve the experiment. The residents need individual autonomy restored."
- **Pure Researchers lead**: "Dissolving it now destroys the longitudinal dataset. We are on the verge of understanding what actually happens to identity in long-term collective conditions. Five more years."
- **Utopian designer**: "The experiment didn't fail. The residents are not suffering. Calling this a collapse because it doesn't look like what you expected is the bias, not the experiment."

**The Dilemma:** To assess what should happen, the player must go into the experiment and ask residents what they want. This is harder than it sounds: "what do you want?" addressed to people whose individual preference-formation has been shaped by thirty years of collective living is not a straightforward question. The player's own assessment of whether the residents are in distress is a genuine interpretive challenge.

**Resolution Paths:**

1. *Support Applied Innovators.* Dissolve and document. Residents are gradually re-individuated through Scorpio therapy. Most recover. Some don't — their individual voice has atrophied past recovery. The data is extraordinary. Something was lost.

2. *Support Pure Researchers.* Five more years. The data gathered is extraordinary. The residents who would have wanted to leave five years ago are no longer capable of wanting to. The experiment ends eventually anyway, worse.

3. *Unity Path (requires H 6+ or C 7+):* Do what none of the researchers thought to do — ask residents individually, one at a time, in private, with enough separation from the collective that an individual voice can emerge. Some want to leave. Some want to stay. Some genuinely don't know and need time to find out. Build a voluntary dissolution protocol that honors all three answers — a phased, resident-driven end to the experiment, supported by Scorpio therapy for those who need it. The experiment ends the way the Utopian designer meant it to: a genuine community making its own choice. The data is incomplete by Pure Research standards. The methodology is, by any standard, the most important contribution Aquarius has made to understanding collective identity. The Utopian designer, watching the last residents choose their own paths, understands that this is what success actually looks like.

**Ripples:** Unity Path is the only resolution that earns Scorpio's trust alongside Aquarius' — it requires establishing a working relationship between the two districts. The Living Network data, however the experiment ends, becomes a resource in the main story's Aquarius questline if that district is prioritized.

## District 8 — Libra: **"The Suspended Compact"**

**Internal Conflict:** True Believers vs. Pragmatists vs. Operators

**Inciting Situation:** A junior archivist — a True Believer — has found the original Suspended Compact: the founding document of the Tepenian Federation, containing democratic provisions that were suspended during the Long Night War and never restored. The document proves the suspension was legal under emergency powers that should have expired decades ago. The Pragmatist leadership knows the archivist has found it. The Operators want it handled. The archivist has already made copies.

**Parties:**
- **Pragmatist council chair**: "The timing is catastrophic. We are managing an active energy crisis. I am asking for a controlled delay. Not suppression — a delay. Thirty days."
- **True Believer archivist**: "You have been asking for a delay for two hundred and thirty years. The emergency never ends because the emergency is useful. I am not waiting."
- **Operator** (senior, unnamed, meeting the player in a side chamber): "The archivist has copies. The copies need to not exist. I am asking you to help with that or to look away while it is handled."

**The Dilemma:** The Compact being public is already effectively inevitable — the archivist has prepared distribution across Janbogo's channels. The player's choice isn't whether the information gets out but how, and at what cost.

**Resolution Paths:**

1. *Help the Operators suppress it.* The archivist is discredited or the copies disappear. Libra's status quo holds for another generation. The True Believers inside the institution know what happened. It will happen again.

2. *Side with the True Believer.* The Compact goes public through Janbogo. The resulting constitutional crisis runs in parallel with the energy crisis for the rest of Act 2. It's ugly and costly. It produces the beginning of real political reform but destabilizes the city at the worst possible moment. Some districts use the chaos to advance secession rhetoric.

3. *Unity Path (requires C 7+ or H 5+ plus positive Libra reputation):* The Compact going public is inevitable. The Pragmatist's only real choice is between controlled release and chaotic release. Help the Pragmatist see this — that the archivist is going to release it regardless, and that the only remaining question is whether Libra shapes the story or gets shaped by it. Engineer a controlled acknowledgment: Libra publicly recognizes the Compact's existence, announces a structured timeline for democratic restoration, and frames it as Libra's own decision rather than an exposed secret. The True Believer accepts this (the Compact is acknowledged; the restoration process begins). The Pragmatist saves institutional face. The Operators lose. The city learns of the Compact during an energy crisis, which means the two stories become entangled — Libra's political legitimacy and the city's physical survival are now inseparable questions.

**Ripples:** Path 3 directly feeds into the Act 3 Libra summit; the player who took this path arrives at the final summit with a Libra that is voluntarily reforming rather than defending its position. Path 1 locks off the True Believer as a contact for the rest of the game. Path 2 is the highest-chaos outcome and produces the most interesting Act 3 political landscape.

## District 9 — Gemini: **"The Speed of Truth"**

**Internal Conflict:** Speed Faction vs. Verification Faction vs. Dual-Network Operators

**Inciting Situation:** A data archaeologist in the Verification Faction has found evidence that the Great Corruption — the mass deletion of pre-Falkland Treaty archives — was targeted, not accidental. Specific records were deleted in a specific order in the hours before the Treaty was signed. What survives suggests the Treaty was negotiated under false pretenses. The Speed Faction wants to broadcast immediately. The Verification Faction needs 6-8 weeks to authenticate. The Dual-Network Operators, when the player confronts them, reveal they've known about the archaeology dig for three months — and they have a third piece of information: some of what's in the archive is not just history.

**Parties:**
- **Speed Faction leader**: "This is the most important story in two hundred years. Every hour we wait, someone is making decisions based on false history."
- **Verification Faction lead** (the archaeologist): "If we broadcast before verification and one element is wrong, the entire story gets discredited. The people who buried it win. We have one chance."
- **Dual-Network Operator**: "Before you decide what to do with this, you should know what else is in the archive. Some of it is active — not historical. There is a specific person currently in power who is directly implicated."

**The Dilemma:** "Active" means a named individual, currently holding a position of authority in Concordia, is implicated in what happened before the Treaty. Broadcasting immediately (Speed Faction) loses that person in the noise — the story is too large and fast for accountability. Waiting to authenticate (Verification Faction) gives the person time to prepare. The Operators' shadow channel could be used to alert specific parties — but trusting the Operators with that kind of targeted action is its own risk.

**Resolution Paths:**

1. *Broadcast immediately.* The pre-Treaty history explodes across the city. The specific person survives politically because they're a footnote in an enormous story. Something true was told badly. The Verification Faction's credibility takes a hit it will spend years recovering from.

2. *Authenticate first.* Six to eight weeks. The specific person is quietly removed from power through Libra before the story breaks — or escapes consequence entirely, depending on whether Libra is trustworthy at this moment. The Verification Faction publishes something airtight. The history is rewritten cleanly. But the player may never know whether Libra helped the person escape or face accountability.

3. *Unity Path (requires I 7+ or C 6+):* Authentication and targeted accountability in parallel. Use the Operators' shadow channel to alert specific people — including the Verification Faction's allies inside Libra — while the Speed Faction prepares the public broadcast on a 72-hour delay rather than immediate release. The shadow channel creates the conditions for accountability in the 72 hours before the story is public. The public broadcast then creates the conditions for city-wide knowledge. Neither serves its purpose without the other. This requires trusting the Operators with targeted communication, which is never fully safe. What they do with the shadow channel access is partially outside the player's control — and the player will find out later what they chose to do with it.

**Ripples:** Path 3 has consequences in Act 3 depending on what the Operators did with the shadow access. The pre-Treaty revelation affects the Libra political landscape regardless of path. The specific implicated person — if not removed — appears at the Act 3 Libra summit as an obstacle.

## District 10 — Pisces: **"The Flood Protocols"**

**Internal Conflict:** Operators vs. Believers vs. Flood-Traumatized

**Inciting Situation:** A derivative of the neural interface mod that caused the Flood has been developed — more controlled, voluntary, with a documented safety profile. The Believers see it as the sacred practice made reliable. The Operators see it as a product with an obvious market: people across the city who would pay enormously to share memories with dying loved ones, lost bonded partners, or anyone whose interior life they can no longer access any other way. The Flood-Traumatized see it as the thing that destroyed them, now made safe enough to sell.

A specific case arrives: a dying robot wants to share their lifetime of memories with their bonded human partner before they die. Voluntarily. The window is real and short.

**Parties:**
- **Operator**: "It's voluntary. Everyone signs consent documentation. The previous Flood wasn't voluntary — that's what made it trauma. Consent changes everything."
- **Believer**: "This is not a product. This is a sacrament. The moment you monetize it, you've made it something else — and you've made that something else available to everyone with credits regardless of readiness."
- **Flood-Traumatized representative**: "They told us the first one was controlled. Consent requires the ability to imagine consequences you cannot have imagined yet. There is no such consent for this."

**The Dilemma:** The Flood-Traumatized's objection isn't that consent is impossible in principle. It's that the kind of consent being offered — "everyone signs" — is legal fiction rather than genuine informed agreement. The case of the dying robot and their partner is the purest possible version of the procedure. The question is whether a beautiful use case justifies the infrastructure being built around it.

**Resolution Paths:**

1. *Facilitate as the Operators want.* The dying robot and partner share memories. It's profound. Within six months the Operators have a waiting list. The Believers go underground. The Flood-Traumatized are publicly characterized as overcautious. Something irreversible has been commercialized.

2. *Back the Believers.* The sharing happens, as a ritual rather than a transaction. Access is restricted to a small practitioner community. It is not monetized. The dying robot gets what they asked for. The Operators are shut out. The Flood-Traumatized are partially reassured but not fully.

3. *Unity Path (requires H 7+ or N 6+):* The Flood-Traumatized's concern is not about this case specifically. It's about consequence-blindness in the consent architecture. Work with the Verification Faction in Janbogo to build a real informed consent protocol: not "everyone signs" but a protocol where each person is shown, experientially, a scaled version of what they're consenting to before they consent to it. The dying robot barely has time. They use what time they have. What they consent to after that experience is real — they've seen a small version of it and chosen to proceed. The Operators hate the protocol because it reduces uptake significantly. The Believers accept it as closer to the sacramental preparation they wanted. The Flood-Traumatized don't endorse it but stop opposing it. The dying robot and their partner have their moment. It is the best possible version of this outcome and it came at significant cost in time.

**Ripples:** Unity Path creates a named protocol in the game's world — it appears in later Janbogo broadcasts and Libra regulatory discussions. Path 1 is the prerequisite for a significant gray-market neural-interface black market emerging in Act 3. The dying robot NPC is a named character whose specific memory content becomes relevant if the player later pursues the Calethina devotion questline.

## District 11 — Sagittarius: **"The Name on the Rescue Roster"**

**Internal Conflict:** Expansionists vs. Consolidationists vs. Memory Keepers

**Inciting Situation:** Pre-war ruins have been located in the region where the Third Expansion Collapse occurred. The structural survey conditions are almost identical to the pre-Collapse reports. An Expansionist crew is already on-site. A Consolidationist engineer has reviewed the site reports and says the conditions match the Collapse precursors within normal deviation. A Memory Keeper — an elder storyteller who was on the original Collapse rescue team — has said, quietly, that she knows things about the last three days before the Collapse that aren't in any record.

**Parties:**
- **Expansionist expedition lead** (on-site): "The conditions aren't the same. The technology is different. The crew is better. We've learned."
- **Consolidationist engineer**: "I have the structural comparison. The precursor conditions are within normal deviation of the Collapse onset. I'm not saying don't go. I'm saying slow down. Three weeks of route reinforcement."
- **Memory Keeper elder**: "I was on the rescue team. I can tell you what the last seventy-two hours looked like before it happened. You tell me if what you're hearing from the site sounds similar."

**The Dilemma:** The crew is already out there. Three weeks of waiting means three weeks of exposure in deep Frostlands conditions. Going in fast risks the pattern repeating.

**Resolution Paths:**

1. *Support the Expansionists.* They move fast. If it works: extraordinary discovery, political vindication, and the lesson of the Third Expansion Collapse is partially un-learned. If it doesn't: the Collapse pattern repeats with names the player may know. Either outcome becomes Sagittarius oral tradition.

2. *Support the Consolidationists.* The crew is pulled back. Three weeks of route reinforcement. The ruins are reached safely. The resource value is confirmed. The Memory Keeper notes that the site was worth it — "this is what a Collapse avoided looks like."

3. *Unity Path (requires I 6+ or Frontier Hardened trait):* Don't use the Consolidationist's structural argument. Use the Memory Keeper's argument — specifically, get the Memory Keeper's direct testimony to the expedition lead, not through official channels but through the one communication line the player has established. The Memory Keeper describes the last seventy-two hours of the Collapse in the specific, experiential detail that lives in Sagittarius oral tradition and exists nowhere in Libra's administrative records. The expedition lead — if they respect this district's epistemology — makes their own judgment after hearing it. If they choose to wait, it's because someone who was there told them what it felt like, not because a structural model said so. The Memory Keeper's knowledge saves lives. The story she told becomes the new Collapse story — one with a different ending — and joins the oral tradition that will save lives again when the next generation faces the same choice.

**Ripples:** Unity Path is the only resolution that adds to the Memory Keepers' body of transmitted knowledge rather than depleting it. Path 1 (success or failure) affects Sagittarius' Act 3 position and supply chain stability. The Memory Keeper, if she witnesses the Unity Path outcome, becomes a key contact for the Sagittarius Idolized ending questline.

## District 12 — Virgo: **"The Year of Silence, Again"**

**Internal Conflict:** Documentarians vs. Leverage Holders vs. Deep Level Keepers

**Inciting Situation:** The active energy crisis has created the conditions the Leverage Holders have been waiting for. Every surface district is dependent on Virgo's work. If Virgo works to rule now — doing exactly and only what contracts specify, no improvisation, no coverage for other districts' failures — the city will begin failing within days, and the districts will capitulate quickly. But the Documentarians have run the numbers for an active energy crisis scenario and concluded that "days" is optimistic. They calculate four days before someone in Cancer dies from a heating junction failure that would normally have been caught on routine inspection. The Deep Level Keepers have been listening to this argument and believe there's a third option neither faction knows they're sitting on.

**Parties:**
- **Leverage Holders leader**: "The moment is now. There is no better moment. The city has never needed us more."
- **Documentarians lead**: "I have the casualty estimate. Four days. A name we don't know yet, in a facility we do know, from a failure we could prevent. We are not doing that."
- **Deep Level Keeper** (the senior tunneler, approaching this conversation very carefully): "You're both arguing about leverage we've already demonstrated. There's something in the Deep Level that Libra would do anything to access. We don't need to threaten to stop working. We can offer something they want."

**The Dilemma:** The Deep Level offer is a third way — but using it means telling Libra the Deep Level exists, which changes the relationship permanently and irrevocably. Once Libra knows, they will want access. Some of the Deep Level Keepers aren't sure what's down there is something Libra should ever have. The senior tunneler's offer is genuine but carries a cost only she fully understands.

**Resolution Paths:**

1. *Work to rule.* People die. Libra capitulates. Virgo receives full funding and formal acknowledgment in the city record. The Documentarians carry the cost. The city now understands that Virgo means it when they say the city dies without them.

2. *Stand down.* No Year of Silence. The Documentarians are right about the casualties. The Leverage Holders are right that nothing changes without pressure. Nothing changes. Virgo continues to be the most essential and least acknowledged district in Concordia.

3. *Unity Path (requires I 6+ or C 6+):* The Deep Level card, played through Libra's True Believers rather than through official channels. The True Believers are more likely to handle secret infrastructure with integrity than the Operators. Make a controlled disclosure: Virgo acknowledges the Deep Level exists and offers supervised access. In return, Libra formally acknowledges Virgo's historical underfunding, corrects the Long Night War memorials (Virgo's work is recognized publicly alongside Aries'), and commits to the specific funding adjustments the Documentarians have documented. The Deep Level Keepers get a seat at the table they have never had. When the Deep Level is properly explored, it contains something neither side expected: evidence of what Concordia was built on and built over — infrastructure predating the Federation, whose origin is unclear — that rewrites the city's history in ways that every district will spend the next fifty years processing. This is the one Virgo outcome where the city is permanently different because Virgo acted.

**Ripples:** Path 3 is the prerequisite for the Deep Level becoming an accessible location in Act 3. Path 1 produces the most immediate Virgo reputation gain but at a permanent moral cost tracked by the game. Path 2 is available as a conscious choice and is acknowledged by the Documentarians as the honest outcome.

## District 13 — Hub: **"Without Inscription"**

**Internal Conflict:** Professional Neutralizers vs. Hidden Opinion Holders vs. Hub Tribalists

**Inciting Situation:** A long-standing proposal to inscribe the Bridge Memorial has reached the Hub council with new urgency — five districts have submitted proposed inscriptions simultaneously, and the political pressure to resolve it is at a peak. The player is assigned to mediate the selection process. As they work through it, they discover that none of the Hub administrators assigned to each district's proposal are as neutral as they're supposed to be. The Tribalists have submitted a proposal of their own: not an inscription, but a placard explaining why the memorial has no inscription, making the absence itself the statement.

**Parties:**
- **Professional Neutralizer** (council chair, genuinely trying): "We find language all thirteen districts can accept. That is our function."
- **Hidden Opinion Holder** (career diplomat, twenty-two years in the Hub, private views well-developed): "Every submission is politically motivated — including the ones that appear neutral. I have an opinion about which one is actually honest. I'm offering it as information. You can use it or not."
- **Hub Tribalist** (Hub-born administrator, mid-career): "The only honest thing is a placard that explains why there's no inscription. We put the absence on the record and let it stand for what it is. That's what the Hub actually is."

**The Dilemma:** The Bridge Memorial's blankness has been its power — the capacity to hold all meanings simultaneously. An inscription doesn't resolve the meaning. It closes it. The Tribalist's meta-solution (inscribing the absence) is its own assertion. The Professional Neutralizer's anodyne solution is its own surrender. The Hidden Opinion Holder is the only party who's being honest about the politics, but their opinion is still a position.

**Resolution Paths:**

1. *Support the Professional Neutralizer.* An inscription is selected — something vague enough to offend no one and mean nothing. The memorial now has words on it. They are less than the silence was.

2. *Back the Tribalist.* The "no inscription" placard is installed, explaining why no inscription was chosen. It is simultaneously the most honest and most Hub-specific outcome. Every district hates it for different reasons. The Hub's identity — principled rootlessness as a virtue — is crystallized in stone.

3. *Unity Path (requires H 6+ and positive reputation with Scorpio):* There is a proposed inscription that predates all of these submissions. It was written in the original council session after the Long Night War, before the deadlock collapsed the process. It was never installed. It exists in Scorpio's Archive of Final Confessions, preserved as part of a dying testimony — someone who watched people leave through the Tower and later confessed what watching had felt like. Obtaining it requires a genuine working relationship with Scorpio (developed through their internal conflict quest or through the main story). The inscription, when found, is not neutral. It is specific, honest, and painful — written by a named person who is no longer alive. Installing it means the memorial has words chosen by someone who was actually there, who has since died, which makes the inscription itself a kind of transmission rather than a political decision. Every district finds in it what they were looking for — which is, functionally, the same as having no words, except now there is a person's name attached to the silence. The Hub, for the first time, has done something that belongs to all of them.

**Ripples:** Unity Path requires the Scorpio internal conflict quest to be resolved in any path other than Path 1 (Clinical suppression). Path 2 is the baseline "Hub" outcome and is what happens if the player ignores this quest. The inscription chosen (or not chosen) is physically present in the game world at the Bridge Memorial for the rest of Act 2 and Act 3, and specific NPCs comment on it differently depending on their district of origin.
