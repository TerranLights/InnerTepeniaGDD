# District Quest Consequence Web
## How each district's internal conflict resolution ripples into other districts and into Act 3

This document maps the interlinking consequences of the thirteen district internal conflict quests defined in `District_Internal_Conflict_Quests.md`. Every resolution path — including partial and failed ones — produces specific downstream effects in other districts and in the Act 3 summit.

**Design principle:** No quest resolves cleanly in isolation. The player who solves Cancer brilliantly but ignores Virgo arrives at a different Act 3 than the player who did both. The player who takes every Unity Path gets a harder political problem at the summit (more factions with legitimate claims, more voices that need to be heard) not an easier one.

---

## Part I — The Major Chains

These are multi-district sequences where the outcome of one quest directly shapes the conditions of the next. They're the game's spine of consequence.

---

### Chain 1: The Libra Chain
**Aries → Libra → Act 3 Summit**

The True Believer archivist inside Libra is the same person in both quests — the one who transmits the classified Black Silence timeline to the Aries crew (Aries Unity Path) and the one who finds the Suspended Compact (Libra quest).

- **Aries Unity Path + Libra Unity Path:** The archivist arrives at the Suspended Compact already having taken one act of institutional defiance — and survived it, because Aries' success made the breach impossible to punish publicly. They have precedent and nerve. Libra's controlled acknowledgment of the Compact is possible partly because the archivist's position is stronger. → *Act 3: Libra enters the summit as a voluntarily reforming institution. Every district's negotiating position is different. The player who built this chain has more options at the summit than any other path.*
- **Aries Unity Path + Libra Path 2 (chaos):** The archivist releases the Compact publicly, riding the momentum of the Black Silence revelation. The constitutional and energy crises merge into one story. → *Act 3: Destabilized, but Libra's official history is fully exposed. Sagittarius moves for autonomy. The player has a harder summit with higher stakes.*
- **Aries Path 2 (break into archive) + Libra quest:** Libra knows someone accessed the classified file. The archivist is under suspicion. If the player's identity as the person who broke in is known, the Libra quest is compromised before it starts — the archivist's credibility is tainted by association. → *Libra Unity Path becomes harder or unavailable.*
- **Aries Path 1 + any Libra path:** No connection to the archivist. The Libra quest proceeds independently.

---

### Chain 2: The Truth Chain
**Capricorn → Gemini → Libra → Act 3**

The Merit Board Audit leak (Capricorn) is the kind of verified, documented story that Gemini's Verification Faction needs. Libra is eventually forced to rule on its implications.

- **Capricorn Unity Path + Gemini Unity Path:** The Audit is Janbogo's first major authenticated story produced through the new parallel broadcast protocol. The Verification Faction's credibility is established on the Audit. Libra must rule on sixty-three years of discriminatory merit assessment; if Libra is in Unity Path mode (reforming), the ruling is genuine. If not, it's bureaucratic suppression. → *Act 3: The Robot Rights Underground is a named political faction at the summit with a documented legal case.*
- **Capricorn Unity Path + Gemini Path 1 (speed):** The Audit is broadcast before verification; one contested data point is wrong. The Establishment seizes on it. The Underground's case is partially discredited. Libra uses the contested data point to invalidate the whole claim. The reform is delayed. → *Act 3: The Underground surfaces anyway, but outside legal channels — with unpredictable methods.*
- **Capricorn Path 1 (suppression):** Nothing for Gemini to amplify. Libra is never forced to rule. → *Act 3: The Underground surfaces violently rather than politically. Libra has to manage a faction conflict on top of the energy crisis and whatever else the player has triggered.*
- **Capricorn Path 2 (full reclassification):** The disruption hits Cancer and Taurus hard during a critical window. Both districts register negative reputation effects with Capricorn that persist into the summit. The reform is real but costs relationships.

### Chain 3: The Transformation Chain
**Scorpio → Aquarius → Hub**

Scorpio's Unity Path (trust established with all three factions) is the prerequisite for Aquarius' Unity Path (which requires a genuine Scorpio partnership for the voluntary dissolution therapy) and for the Hub's Unity Path (which requires a working relationship with Scorpio to access the Archive of Final Confessions).

- **Scorpio Unity Path → Aquarius Unity Path:** The voluntary dissolution protocol for the Living Network requires Scorpio therapists embedded in the process. This only works if Scorpio trusts Aquarius — which only happens after the Clinical/Sacred/Altered coalition is intact. Without it, Aquarius cannot access Scorpio capacity, and Path 1 or 2 follows. → *When both Unity Paths are taken: a formal inter-district research protocol is established — the first Aquarius-Scorpio collaboration that Libra has ever officially acknowledged.*
- **Scorpio Unity Path → Hub Unity Path:** The Archive of Final Confessions contains the inscription. The player can access it only with Scorpio's trust. → *Hub Unity Path is locked off if Scorpio quest is Path 1 or unresolved.*
- **Scorpio Path 1 (Clinical only) → Aquarius:** Clinical School publishes results; Aquarius uses them to justify neural augmentation research without the psychological framework. → *Produces an Aquarius Path 1 or 2 outcome regardless of what the player chooses, because the ethical review process lacks the Scorpio grounding it needs.*
- **Scorpio Path 2 (Sacred confrontation) → Hub:** The patient's testimony, surfaced through confrontation therapy, is relevant to the Forbidden Archive War. Scorpio may release specific records under the right conditions — but the player has to ask. This is a minor questline branch rather than a full chain.

### Chain 4: The Information Underground Chain
**Gemini → Pisces → Act 3**

Gemini's shadow channel and Pisces' gray-market data networks are already overlapping infrastructure. The players who resolve both quests through Unity Paths produce a renegotiated Tolerance Pact.

- **Gemini Unity Path + Pisces Unity Path:** The informed consent protocol (Pisces) is built with Janbogo's Verification Faction (Gemini). The Operators — who brokered the pre-Treaty revelation's shadow channel use — are now operating within a visible governance framework for the first time. The Tolerance Pact is implicitly renegotiated: Pisces is no longer purely underground. → *Act 3: Pisces has a legitimate political seat at the summit for the first time — unofficial, deniable, but real.*
- **Gemini Unity Path (Operators used the shadow channel):** What the Operators did: they used the access to alert the implicated person — not to protect them, but to offer them a deal. The implicated person resigned voluntarily before the story broke. The Operators profited from brokering this. The player finds out at Act 3 when the resigned figure reappears as a free agent with their own agenda. The Operators' shadow channel action was the worst outcome the player couldn't control. It was also, arguably, better than public chaos.
- **Gemini Path 1 (speed broadcast) + Pisces:** The speed broadcast overwhelms the Pisces gray-market data channels with noise. The informed consent protocol development is disrupted. Pisces Path 1 or 2 follows.
- **Pisces Path 1 (commercialize) + Act 3:** A gray-market neural-interface black market emerges. Janbogo Speed Faction covers it as a tech story; Verification Faction cannot authenticate fast enough to prevent harm. Scorpio is overwhelmed with dissolution cases that weren't expected. → *Act 3: Scorpio arrives at the summit depleted rather than resourced.*

### Chain 5: The Resource Chain
**Sagittarius → Capricorn → Aries → Act 3 Grid**

The pre-war ruins discovered in Sagittarius contain raw materials and technology. Their fate directly affects Capricorn's production capacity and Aries' energy load.

- **Sagittarius Unity Path (ruins secured safely):** The Memory Keeper's testimony means the crew retreated and approached correctly. The ruins are accessed intact — including pre-war power technology that Aquarius can analyze. → *If Aquarius Unity Path is also active: the pre-war technology provides a genuine breakthrough toward the energy crisis resolution. The player who did both has a real Act 3 option that others don't.*
- **Sagittarius Path 1 (success, fast):** The ruins are found but partially damaged by the rushed approach. Less recoverable material. Capricorn gets raw inputs but not the full value. Aquarius gets damaged data, not intact technology.
- **Sagittarius Path 1 (failure):** Sixty-three crew lost. Cancer receives the casualties. Cancer's resources are strained in the lead-up to the Great Blackout. The Memory Keeper gains one more failure story. The oral tradition's warning library grows.
- **Sagittarius + Capricorn Unity Path:** The raw materials from the ruins are processed through a reformed production system — the merit reclassification has made Capricorn's output more accurate and efficient. → *Act 3: The combined Sagittarius-Capricorn supply chain is the strongest resource base the player can bring to the summit.*

---

### Chain 6: The Care Chain
**Cancer → Taurus → Virgo → Act 3 Social Stability**

The three districts that form Concordia's social substrate. Their aggregate state determines how many people the Great Blackout kills and whether the city holds together during it.

- **Cancer Unity Path:** Intake system improved. The next Sagittarius refugee wave is handled without a mortality event. Upper Earth Defectors are provisionally integrated. The Mother's Circuit is a more honest tool.
- **Cancer Unity Path + Taurus Unity Path:** The insulation equity reform uses the Cancer intake model as its template — both districts are now running on improved allocation logic. The Unverifiables' provisional bond recognition in Taurus creates a precedent that Cancer's Trauma-Bearers want applied to their own unrecognized residents. → *A minor third questline branch emerges: the precedent becomes a city-wide debate about what recognition means.*
- **Taurus Unity Path + Virgo Unity Path:** Virgo's formal acknowledgment and funding increase means the insulation repairs Taurus needs are actually done rather than deferred. The Taurus Cold Side no longer experiences the chronic underprioritization. → *Act 3: Taurus arrives at the summit without an active internal crisis for the first time in decades.*
- **Cancer Path 1 or 2 + Virgo Path 1 (work to rule):** The named person who dies in Virgo's casualty estimate is in Cancer. The player knows who they are by the time it happens. This is the game's hardest moral weight — a person with a face, a name, an NPC the player may have spoken to, dying because of a choice the player made in a different district.
- **All three Unity Paths:** The Great Blackout, when it hits in Act 3, kills fewer people in absolute terms than any other combination. The city does not hold together without other favorable outcomes, but it at least holds together better than any alternative.

---

### Chain 7: The Recognition Chain
**Virgo → Aries → Libra → Act 3**

Virgo's Unity Path discloses the Deep Level to Libra's True Believers, triggering a rewrite of the city's foundational history that ripples into Aries' memorial culture and Libra's political balance.

- **Virgo Unity Path:** The Deep Level's contents — pre-Federation infrastructure whose origin is unclear — rewrite the city's foundation story. The Long Night War memorials are corrected to include Virgo's contributions alongside Aries'. → *Aries reacts: the Overclockers are initially defensive; the Safety Engineers see it as validation; the Political Faction uses it as leverage. Aries' internal political balance shifts.*
- **Virgo Unity Path + Libra Unity Path:** Libra's True Believers, strengthened by the Deep Level achievement, are the faction most capable of handling the Suspended Compact's restoration process honestly. The two Unity Paths together produce a Libra that is simultaneously more legitimate and more transparent than either path achieves alone.
- **Virgo Path 1 (work to rule) + Libra:** Libra capitulates to Virgo. This is actually one of the conditions under which Libra's Pragmatists are most willing to make concessions — when the alternative is obvious and immediate. → *Libra Unity Path is still available, but the player arrives with more political capital from Virgo and a harder moral position to defend.*
- **Virgo Path 2 (stand down) + Act 3:** Nothing changes. Virgo enters the summit with the same unacknowledged status it has always had. The Deep Level is never disclosed. Whatever is down there stays down there.

## Part III — Per-Quest Detailed Ripples

More granular breakdown of each quest's consequences by path.

---

### Cancer: "The Night of Locked Doors"

**Unity Path →**
- Taurus: The Defectors, now provisionally integrated, begin moving into Taurus. The Newcomer/Established tension spikes briefly before settling.
- Libra: The Defectors' provisional status forces Libra to formally rule on recognition — a small case with large precedent implications.
- Sagittarius: Provisional Defector acceptance reduces the political pressure on Sagittarius' Echo Camp. The camp's Remorseful faction becomes more accessible.
- Act 3: Cancer's care infrastructure is more efficient going into the Great Blackout. Fewer casualty events. Cancer's leader arrives at the summit with moral authority rather than exhaustion.

**Path 1 →**
- Gemini: If the deaths are discovered (Investigation check in Janbogo), the Verification Faction investigates the Mother's Circuit as a bias story. This complicates the Cancer/Janbogo relationship.
- Scorpio: The unprocessed deaths — the family that died outside the doors — add to the community trauma load that Scorpio eventually absorbs. A named NPC in Scorpio references them.

**Path 2 →**
- Aries: The overcrowding increases Cancer's heating demand. This worsens the Aries internal conflict (more residential pressure on the Overclockers vs. Safety Engineers debate).
- Virgo: The overcrowded facilities produce more maintenance load. Virgo's strained-resources situation worsens slightly.

---

### Taurus: "What the Cold Side Knows"

**Unity Path →**
- Cancer: The insulation equity model becomes a template that Cancer's advocates want applied to their own district's resource allocation.
- Libra: The Unverifiables' provisional bond recognition is a small but real precedent. Libra either ratifies it city-wide (if in Unity Path mode) or quietly suppresses it (if Operators are dominant).
- Capricorn: The insulation materials for the reform come from Capricorn's supply chain. If Capricorn's Robot Rights Underground is active, the supply negotiations involve the Underground organizer — a small dialogue branch that can improve or complicate relations.
- Act 3: Taurus arrives at the summit without an active internal crisis. The Cold-Side rep is a named delegate. The Unverifiables have provisional representation.

**Path 1 →**
- Virgo: The same repair gets called in again in Act 3 during a resource-scarce period. The Virgo crew that handles it is one of the named crews from the Virgo quest.
- Aries: The power fluctuation that triggered the insulation failure was Aries-sourced. Taurus's resentment of Aries increases. This affects the Aries-Taurus relationship at the summit.

---

### Leo: "Two Houses, One Stage"

**Unity Path →**
- Gemini: The Cymaticist experiment is the biggest verified story Janbogo has ever received from Leo. The Verification Faction documents it in full — this is one of the events that establishes the Verification Faction's credibility ahead of the Gemini internal conflict quest. If the Gemini quest is already resolved, it adds to the Verification Faction's public standing retroactively.
- Cancer: The coordinated event's emotional resonance is noticed by Cancer practitioners. Scorpio therapists begin attending Leo events specifically as supplementary care options — the first formal acknowledgment that cultural experience is therapeutic.
- Scorpio: The Intimate Underground performer's debut — described in Janbogo's coverage as the decade's best performance — begins attracting Scorpio practitioners as cultural collaborators. A minor questline branch becomes available: Scorpio and Leo co-hosting grief ritual performances.
- Act 3: The Cymaticist faction is accessible. The Intimate Underground performer is a named character with contacts across three districts. Leo has cultural authority it lacked before.

**Path 1 →**
- The Underground performer disappears from public life. In Act 3 they appear once, briefly, in Pisces. They have nothing good to say.
- The Cymaticist experiment fails. The planetary alignment won't recur for eleven years. The Cymaticist elder is a sadder NPC for the rest of the game.

**Path 2 →**
- The staggered schedule dilutes all three experiences. None of the ripples above occur. The Cymaticists have partial data. The Underground performer's performance is attended by a tired post-main-event crowd. Moderate outcome.

---

### Scorpio: "The Shape of What Remains"

**Unity Path →**
- Aquarius: Enables the Aquarius Unity Path by establishing the clinical trust that makes the Scorpio-embedded voluntary dissolution protocol possible.
- Hub: Enables the Hub Unity Path by establishing the working relationship that grants access to the Archive of Final Confessions.
- Libra: The patient's consultation — which was partially about what they did or witnessed during the Long Night War — produces testimony that the Forbidden Archive War now hinges on. Scorpio holds it. This gives Scorpio leverage with Libra that it can use in either the main quest or Act 3.
- Act 3: All three Scorpio factions are intact and willing to work together. Scorpio enters the summit as a politically coherent district rather than a fractured one.

**Path 1 →**
- The patient's dissociation becomes a minor Act 3 event — they appear at the summit in a strange state, unable to clearly advocate for anything.
- The Clinical School publishes results. Aquarius uses them without the psychological framework. → Acts as a soft block on Aquarius Unity Path (the ethical review board lacks the grounding it needs).
- Pisces: The dissociated patient ends up in Pisces seeking dissolution as the next step. They're one of the Flood-Traumatized by Act 3.

**Path 2 →**
- The patient's confrontation produces testimony relevant to the Forbidden Archive War. If the player pursues it, this is a minor branch that partially compensates for the Hub Unity Path being unavailable.

### Aries: "Thirty-Seven Minutes"

**Unity Path →**
- Libra: The True Believer archivist has taken one successful act of defiance before finding the Suspended Compact. Their position is stronger. The Libra Unity Path becomes more achievable.
- Gemini: Janbogo's Verification Faction wants the classified Black Silence file. If the player releases it rather than keeping it within Aries, the Gemini quest gains a piece of authenticated historical documentation. Small ripple but real.
- Act 3: Aries enters the summit having cracked Libra's information monopoly — specifically, having shown that Libra's classified files disagree with official history. This is political leverage in grid negotiations.

**Path 2 (break-in) →**
- Libra: Libra knows the file was accessed. If the player's identity is traceable, the Libra quest begins with Libra treating the player as a hostile actor. The Libra Unity Path requires an additional step to rebuild trust.
- The Political Faction gets what they wanted. They become more aggressive in Act 3 — willing to use the blackout threat explicitly for the first time.

**Path 1 →**
- No connection to Libra. No classified file. Aries' oral tradition adds "37 minutes" as mythology. The Political Faction remains frustrated but dormant. Nothing structural changes in the Aries-Libra relationship.

### Capricorn: "The Audit"

**Unity Path →**
- Sagittarius: Sagittarius' raw material contracts were assessed under the same biased merit system. The Underground organizer, once surfaced, brings Sagittarius into the reclassification scope. A minor branch emerges: Sagittarius and Capricorn renegotiating resource contracts under the new system.
- Gemini: The Audit story, fully authenticated, is what the Verification Faction needed. This is the event that establishes the Verification Faction's credibility ahead of the pre-Treaty revelation (if the Gemini quest hasn't happened yet). If it has, it retroactively strengthens their standing.
- Libra: Libra is forced to rule on the legality of sixty-three years of discriminatory merit assessment. If Libra is in Unity Path mode, the ruling is genuine and creates legal precedent city-wide. If Libra is in Operator or Pragmatist mode, the ruling is bureaucratically suppressed — but the Underground now has a ruling on the record to appeal.
- Act 3: The Robot Rights Underground is a named political faction at the summit with documented legal standing.

**Path 1 →**
- Act 3: The Underground surfaces outside legal channels. Their methods are unpredictable. Libra has to manage a faction conflict during the summit that the player could have defused.

**Path 2 →**
- Cancer: Six weeks of production slowdown during a critical pre-Blackout window. Cancer's care facilities experience supply shortages that strain the district's already-stretched capacity. Negative reputation effect with Cancer persists.
- Taurus: Same production slowdown affects insulation material supply. The Cold-Side repair (if in progress) is delayed.

### Aquarius: "The Living Network"

**Unity Path →**
- Scorpio: The voluntary dissolution generates an expected Scorpio caseload that Scorpio planned for. The two districts have a formal protocol for the first time. This is referenced in Act 3 as a model for other inter-district resource sharing.
- Gemini: The methodology of the voluntary dissolution is the most important Aquarius contribution in decades. The Verification Faction documents it rigorously. → Strengthens the Verification Faction's standing ahead of or after the Gemini quest.
- Sagittarius Resource Chain: The pre-war technology from Sagittarius' ruins, if available, is integrated into Aquarius' energy research. The combination of intact pre-war tech plus a functioning Aquarius-Scorpio research protocol produces the breakthrough option that Aries needs going into Act 3.
- Act 3: Aquarius arrives at the summit with the trust of Scorpio and Gemini. Their energy research is the most credible path forward on the grid crisis.

**Path 1 →**
- Scorpio: Sudden large caseload without coordination. Scorpio's capacity is strained going into Act 3. → *If Pisces Path 1 (commercialize) also occurred: Scorpio is overwhelmed by two unexpected client floods simultaneously. The district arrives at the summit with nothing to offer anyone.*

**Path 2 →**
- The Living Network is an ongoing Act 3 crisis. It requires the player to make a forced resolution choice during the summit or immediately before it — under time pressure and without the full information they would have had in Act 2.

### Libra: "The Suspended Compact"

**Unity Path →**
- All districts: The Compact being acknowledged changes the political landscape of the Act 3 summit. Every district has a legitimate democratic claim — they just didn't have institutional recognition of it before. The summit becomes a genuine political negotiation rather than a managed presentation.
- Cancer: The Rationing of Grief directive is a campaign issue under the new democratic process. Cancer's elder is at the summit demanding formal acknowledgment. This is a named scene.
- Aries: The classified files that Libra used to suppress the Black Silence timeline are politically compromised under the new transparency commitment. The Political Faction in Aries uses this immediately.
- Scorpio: The Forbidden Archive War's suppressed testimony is now technically covered by the new disclosure commitment. Scorpio and Libra have a formal standoff that the player may have to resolve in Act 3.
- Act 3: This is the most consequential single quest for the shape of the summit. Unity Path Libra means the player is managing a genuine multi-party negotiation with legitimate democratic process. Operator-dominant Libra means the player is fighting an institution protecting itself.

**Path 1 →**
- True Believer lost as a contact. Operators' power consolidates. The summit is controlled by the faction most hostile to genuine resolution.

**Path 2 →**
- Sagittarius immediately moves for autonomy — the constitutional crisis gives them the legal pretext. Their delegate arrives at the summit with a secession motion. Leo's Leo uses the political chaos to push for cultural district independence. The summit is a crisis management session rather than a policy session.

### Gemini: "The Speed of Truth"

**Unity Path →**
- The Operators used the shadow channel to offer the implicated person a deal. The person resigned voluntarily before the story broke. The player finds this out in Act 3 when the resigned figure reappears as a free agent — no institutional protection, no clear agenda, unpredictable. The Operators profited from brokering the arrangement. The player couldn't fully control this.
- Libra: The implicated person's resignation reshapes Libra's internal balance before the summit. Depending on who the person was (determined by playthrough specifics), this may strengthen or weaken the True Believers.
- Pisces: The Operators' shadow work was a Pisces-adjacent operation. The Tolerance Pact is implicitly strengthened by the shadow channel use — Libra used Pisces-connected infrastructure for a politically sensitive operation. Libra now owes Pisces a debt it will not acknowledge.
- Act 3: The pre-Treaty revelation is public and authenticated. The city's foundational history is different. Every district's self-understanding is different. The summit happens against this backdrop.

**Path 1 →**
- The implicated person survives politically and arrives at the Act 3 summit with a defensive agenda. They are an obstacle in whatever negotiation they're adjacent to.

**Path 2 →**
- Depends on Libra's state. If Libra is in Unity Path mode: the person is removed through formal channels and faces genuine accountability. If Libra is in Operator mode: the person is "handled" — which means protected. The Verification Faction's story is airtight but Libra controls what happens with it.

### Pisces: "The Flood Protocols"

**Unity Path →**
- Gemini: The informed consent protocol is built with the Verification Faction. This is the most significant governance tool Janbogo has produced — a real standard rather than a legal fiction. The Verification Faction's credibility increases.
- Aquarius: The informed consent protocol is exactly what Aquarius' ethics review boards have needed for augmentation research. The Aquarius-Scorpio-Pisces research triangle becomes a named city institution. Libra, if in Unity Path mode, officially recognizes it.
- Act 3: Pisces has a legitimate political presence at the summit for the first time. Unofficial. Deniable. But the Operators are sitting at the table. Libra and Pisces are in a renegotiated relationship that everyone pretends not to notice.
- The dying robot's memories: if the voluntary sharing protocol was used and the player later pursues the Calethina devotion questline, the dying robot's specific memory content is relevant. Their lifetime of memories includes encounters with Calethina's early instances — pre-Long Night War memories of who she was before she became singular.

**Path 1 →**
- Gray-market neural-interface black market emerges in Act 3. Janbogo Speed Faction covers it as a technology story. Verification Faction cannot authenticate fast enough.
- Scorpio: Dissolution cases arrive faster than expected. Scorpio enters Act 3 depleted.
- Aquarius: Uses Path 1 commercialization as evidence for augmentation research validity. Produces one breakthrough and two disasters.

### Sagittarius: "The Name on the Rescue Roster"

**Unity Path →**
- Taurus: The ruins contain pre-war construction materials that affect insulation supply. If the Cold-Side reform is in progress and needs materials, this supply chain becomes relevant — for better or worse depending on Capricorn's state.
- Capricorn: The ruins' raw materials affect production capacity. If Capricorn's merit reform is active, the new supply is processed through a more efficient and equitable system.
- Aquarius: If the ruins are accessed intact (Unity Path), the pre-war power technology is available for Aquarius research. Combined with Aquarius Unity Path, this is the Act 3 breakthrough option.
- Memory Keepers: The elder storyteller's testimony is formally added to the oral tradition as a successful warning — the first time a Memory Keeper's knowledge demonstrably prevented casualties. Memory Keepers become a named faction with formal contact available in Act 3.

**Path 1 (success) →**
- The ruins are found but damaged. Capricorn gets raw inputs, not full value. Aquarius gets damaged data. The resource chain produces diminished results.
- The Expansionists are politically vindicated even though the approach was riskier than necessary. Their faction's influence in Sagittarius increases going into Act 3.

**Path 1 (failure) →**
- Cancer: Sixty-three casualties arrive. Cancer's resources are strained in the pre-Blackout period.
- The oral tradition gains another failure story. The Memory Keeper gains another chapter. The Consolidationists are vindicated but the victory is hollow.

### Virgo: "The Year of Silence, Again"

**Unity Path →**
- All districts: The Deep Level's contents — pre-Federation infrastructure of unclear origin — rewrite the city's foundation story. This information is released into the political discourse and every district processes it differently for the rest of the game.
- Aries: The Long Night War memorial correction is an Aries political event. The Overclockers are defensive; the Safety Engineers see it as institutional honesty; the Political Faction uses it as evidence that Libra has been managing history.
- Libra: The True Believers gain a major political achievement — they handled the Deep Level disclosure responsibly. This strengthens their position in the Libra internal conflict and makes the Libra Unity Path slightly more achievable if it hasn't happened yet.
- Act 3: The Deep Level is an accessible location. It contains something relevant to the main grid crisis — specifically, infrastructure that predates Concordia's construction and that the Chorus of the Deep (faction) has been monitoring for decades. The player who disclosed it has access to this. The player who didn't has to find it a different way, or not at all.

**Path 1 →**
- Cancer: The named person in Cancer dies. The player knows who they are. This is the game's hardest moral weight — a name, a face, an NPC the player may have spoken to, dying because the player let Virgo execute its leverage. The death is on the record. Virgo's documentation system records it. So does the player's reputation tracker.
- Libra capitulates. Virgo receives full acknowledgment and funding. The Political and leverage dimensions are resolved but at permanent moral cost. The player who chose this is never entirely wrong, and never entirely right.

**Path 2 →**
- Nothing changes. Virgo remains the most essential and least acknowledged district. The Deep Level stays undisclosed. Whatever is down there stays down there. In Act 3, Virgo's delegate says very little at the summit and is listened to even less.

### Hub: "Without Inscription"

**Unity Path →**
- All districts: The Bridge Memorial now carries an inscription written by a named person who was there. Every district's NPCs have something specific to say about it. The memorial is a location of actual political gravity in Act 3 — scenes happen there, not just passing references.
- Scorpio: The person whose dying testimony contained the inscription is now publicly named at the Bridge Memorial. Their testimony is no longer anonymous. This has implications for the Forbidden Archive War — a minor thread that Scorpio's Sacred School uses as evidence that secrets eventually surface regardless.
- Act 3: The Bridge Memorial serves as the location of the most politically significant conversation before the summit — the moment when the player can speak to representatives of every district simultaneously in the same physical space. This only happens if the memorial has weight.

**Path 1 →**
- An inscription exists. No one cares. The memorial is mentioned once and never returns.

**Path 2 →**
- The Hub's meta-statement is visible in the world. Every district finds it irritating for different reasons. Hub-born NPCs are very proud of it. Non-Hub NPCs express contempt that reads as exactly the tribalism the Hub was trying to transcend.
- The Hub is more isolated politically going into Act 3. The player who chose this has made a principled choice at a political cost.

## Part IV — Act 3 State Profiles

What the summit looks like given the major chain outcomes.

**Maximum Unity (all or most Unity Paths):**
The summit is a genuine multi-party negotiation with legitimate democratic process, a reformed Libra, a surfaced Robot Rights Underground, a Pisces delegate at the table, a Virgo whose work is acknowledged, a Deep Level that has rewritten the city's history, and a pre-war technological breakthrough available as the grid solution. It is the hardest summit to navigate because more factions have legitimate claims and more voices need to be heard. There is no clean answer but there are good ones.

**Maximum Suppression (all or most Path 1 choices):**
The summit is controlled by the most powerful factions in their most defensive configurations. The Operators dominate Libra. The Audit Underground surfaces violently. The Living Network is an ongoing crisis. Scorpio is overwhelmed. The energy crisis is worse because no breakthroughs were enabled. There are fewer voices at the table but the voices present are more dangerous. The summit is shorter and harder.

**Mixed (the expected state):**
Most playthroughs will produce a mixed state — some Unity Paths, some suppressions, some Path 2s. The summit reflects this: some factions are reformed, others aren't. Some chains fired, others didn't. The player's specific pattern of choices produces a unique political configuration that no summary can fully anticipate. This is the intended experience.
