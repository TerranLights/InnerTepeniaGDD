# Alternate Minmax Endings
**"Fuck You, Not My Problem" Endings for Extreme Specialization**

These endings are available only to characters with **three MACHINE stats at 8 or higher** from character creation. They represent the ultimate expression of player agency — paths that let extreme specialists bypass, escape, undermine, or transcend Concordia's central conflicts through sheer excellence in their specific domains.

There are **35 possible endings**, one per unique three-stat combination drawn from M, A, C, H, I, N, E. C(7,3) = 35.

## Design Philosophy

Every trigger is a **physical thing that exists in the world** — an object, a location, a document, an environmental state — that the player can find through exploration. No trigger requires an NPC to initiate contact first. No stat check is needed to recognize the trigger (only to act on it). This ensures that knowing the stat prerequisite is enough to know where to look.

Every ending maintains the core Inner Tepenia design principle: even the most optimized escape is bittersweet. Concordia has a problem. These characters did not solve it. The city pays a price for what they did or did not do. The price is specific, structural, and unavoidable.

## 01. M+A+C — The Iron Nomad
*Might + Agility + Calculation*

You find the vehicle frame. You build the vehicle. You leave. The city had its chance.

**World Trigger**
A derelict vehicle frame in Capricorn's outer scrap fields — large enough to matter, too damaged to move without serious work. It is physically distinct from ordinary salvage: a different scale, a different structural logic. A player who examines it and understands what it could become has found the trigger.

**The End**
The vehicle crosses the city perimeter under its own power with the player at the controls. The moment of crossing is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The engineering knowledge used to build the vehicle — improvised, optimized, entirely outside official processes — left with it. Capricorn has no record of the methods used and cannot replicate the result.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Key structural components taken from Capricorn's scrap economy create specific gaps in the parts supply for ongoing city maintenance
- Aries engineers, who later learn what was built from available materials, spend significant resources trying to reproduce it without the player's Calculation to guide them
- The route the vehicle took out of the city is tracked in its wake — others attempt to follow in less capable vehicles
- Virgo loses specific tunnel access infrastructure the player mapped during construction and then sealed behind them

## 02. M+A+H — The Wandering Guardian
*Might + Agility + Humanity*

You reject the city and become a nomadic protector. You defend the people the city couldn't protect, without ever fixing what broke.

**World Trigger**
A specific refugee family group is in an unclaimed space — a transit junction, an outer Hub corridor, an unregistered room at a district boundary. No district claims them. No district is coming. They are simply there, visible to anyone who passes through that corridor. That group, in that specific unclaimed space, is the trigger.

**The End**
The caravan of people the player has gathered crosses the city's last checkpoint and does not turn back. The moment the player keeps walking is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
Cancer and Taurus lose the specific people the player took with them — the most vulnerable members of their communities who also happened to be the most mobile and willing to trust a stranger. The districts' remaining populations skew toward those who couldn't or wouldn't leave.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- The informal route the player established for the caravan becomes known and used by others who do not have a guardian traveling with them
- Districts that were nominally responsible for the individuals the player gathered face administrative inquiries with no satisfying answers
- Cancer's "No One Left Behind" motto is tested by the departure — some people left, with help, and the district chose not to stop it
- The refugee problem that generated the trigger group remains exactly as unresolved as before

## 03. M+A+I — The Ghost of the Undergrid
*Might + Agility + Investigation*

You vanish into the tunnels and build a hidden empire from salvaged resources and secret routes.

**World Trigger**
A specific resource node in the Undergrid that is powered but unrecorded — not on Virgo's official maintenance map. Its anomalous power signature is detectable by anyone paying attention to which lights are on where they shouldn't be. A player who notices the light and investigates has found the trigger.

**The End**
The player's underground domain achieves supply-chain independence from the surface — a specific resource threshold crossed, after which the domain no longer requires anything from above.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
Virgo's maintenance authority over the Undergrid is permanently compromised. There is a claim on underground space they cannot document, cannot access, and cannot govern — and they cannot explain why their maps are wrong.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Knowledge that unclaimed territory exists in the Undergrid spreads and other parties compete for it without the player's capability to hold it
- Aries' power monitoring detects irregular draws it cannot locate, and the diagnostic effort is expensive
- Resources siphoned from surface systems create specific supply deficits in specific districts that manifest as unexplained shortages
- Virgo's social structure — built on exclusive knowledge of the tunnels — is undermined when those tunnels are shown to have occupants Virgo didn't know about

## 04. M+A+N — The Unbreakable Ronin
*Might + Agility + Nerve*

You become a legendary lone operative who answers to no faction. The crisis is background noise.

**World Trigger**
A contract board in Pisces — the black-market one, not the official bounty board — has a standing offer that has been there for years: something that pays too little for the risk, except for someone who has nothing to lose. The player's unique position makes the math work. That specific card on that board, unchanged for years, is the trigger.

**The End**
The moment the player walks away from the last faction that attempted to recruit them and is not followed. The epilogue confirms no faction ever successfully claims them.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The demonstration that a completely unaligned operator can function indefinitely inside Concordia creates a template others attempt to emulate without the player's capability. The template is accessible. The capability is not.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Pisces' black market commission structure is permanently disrupted by a fully independent operator who won't work within it
- Libra cannot close its file — the dossier grows indefinitely and consumes analyst resources
- Aries wastes patrol bandwidth tracking a target that never goes where expected
- Every faction that tried to recruit the Ronin and failed adjusts its recruitment approach in ways that make other targets less safe

## 05. M+A+E — The Eternal Engine

You augment yourself into a being that barely needs external power and wander the frozen wastes indefinitely.

**World Trigger**
A specific augmentation station in the Undergrid — still powered, clearly older than Virgo's maintenance record — is running on something nobody currently monitors. A player who finds it and interacts with it initiates the first augmentation that begins the self-sufficiency process.

**Minor Negative Effects**

- Capricorn's component supply registers specific depletions that maintenance teams attribute to misallocation rather than removal
- Virgo's maintenance map has an active, powered station that nobody is managing, drawing power nobody is accounting for
- The self-sufficient augmentation the player achieved becomes the subject of Aquarius research that produces theories but no reproducible results

## 06. M+C+H — The Iron Covenant
*Might + Calculation + Humanity*

You don't leave Concordia. You conquer a section of it through physical force, reorganize it through computational optimization, and govern it with genuine care for the people inside it.

**World Trigger**
A specific failing maintenance station in Aries has a worker roster posted outside — the standard assignment board. Every name on the roster is either absent or listed as "unassigned." The station is technically still under Aries authority and is technically still supposed to be staffed. It is not. That empty roster on that physically accessible board is the trigger: a space abandoned by its nominal authority, waiting for someone to fill it.

**The End**
The Covenant — the player's claimed station and its personnel — makes its first external transaction: another district comes to the station for services or goods it cannot get elsewhere. That first transaction is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
A functioning enclave inside the city that answers only to the player demonstrates that Aries' authority over its own infrastructure is porous. Other opportunists attempt to replicate the model in spaces they don't have the capability to hold, creating instability in multiple other stations simultaneously.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Aries devotes security resources to monitoring the Covenant rather than addressing energy infrastructure — the enclave becomes an administrative obsession
- The personnel concentrated in the Covenant were previously distributed across Aries as individual contributors; consolidating them creates specific skill gaps in their former positions
- Whatever the Covenant produces competes with official Aries output, creating political friction without any formal mechanism for dispute resolution
- Libra cannot classify the Covenant under any existing political category — its administrative status is permanently undefined

## 07. M+C+I — The Gray Eminence
*Might + Calculation + Investigation*

You find leverage on everyone, optimize its use through calculation, and hold force in reserve as a fallback. You become the hidden power behind whatever government emerges.

**World Trigger**
A specific Libra administrative corridor has a scheduling board with one official's slot marked "open/unavailable" simultaneously — a contradiction that indicates either deliberate blocking or an administrative error that revealed something. A player who notices that specific contradiction and investigates what it means finds the first thread of what someone is hiding.

**The End**
The player makes a single statement in a private meeting with Concordia's effective power-holder that cannot be denied, is not recorded, and changes the outcome of a governance decision. The moment of invisible influence is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
Effective governance is partially running on private leverage rather than institutional process. When the player dies or leaves, the leverage evaporates and the power structure built on it has nothing underneath.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Every arrangement brokered in private cannot be publicly audited, verified, or trusted by parties who weren't present
- Libra's formal governance apparatus is slowly displaced by the informal influence network the player operates — institutions exist but no longer decide things
- The investigation methods used — unauthorized access to scheduling, records, private meetings — leave a visible trail that later investigators find and follow
- When the leverage collapses, the power structure that depended on it collapses simultaneously, and the timing is beyond anyone's control

## 08. M+C+N — The Iron Philosopher
*Might + Calculation + Nerve*

You take territory through force, plan its governance through calculation, and sustain it through indomitable will. You don't just rule — you write the laws.

**World Trigger**
A specific large transit intersection in the Hub has a raised section of platform — clearly designed for public address — that nobody uses anymore. A player who stands on that platform during a period of civil disorder finds that people stop and look. The platform is the trigger: a physical affordance for authority that the city has left unclaimed.

**The End**
The player speaks from that platform during the Great Blackout crisis, and a specific number of people stay after the speech rather than dispersing. The first person who stays rather than leaving is the ending moment.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The laws the player writes are specific to their particular combination of force, calculation, and will. They are not transferable. When the player is gone, no institution understands the laws' rationale — they are either ignored outright or misapplied in ways that produce the opposite of the intended outcome.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- The precedent of speech from the platform is now established: every subsequent faction that wants authority will also use it, and the competition for the platform creates recurring conflicts at that specific location
- Libra's monopoly on legal authority is broken — there is now a competing source of law in the Hub that Libra did not sanction and cannot undo
- The force component of the player's authority requires perpetual maintenance — the moment it visibly weakens, the calculation becomes exploitable and the will is tested simultaneously
- The philosophical framework the player articulated is adopted by followers who do not have the Calculation to implement it correctly

## 09. M+H+I — The Just Sentinel
*Might + Humanity + Investigation*

You become a self-appointed guardian of justice: you investigate to find threats, use empathy to understand victims and perpetrators, and deliver physical consequences. Outside the law, but filling its function.

**World Trigger**
A victim's testimony — scratched into a surface in a Scorpio waiting room or a Cancer corridor, the kind of mark someone makes when they don't believe anyone official is listening — describes a specific act by a specific unnamed party. Those scratched words, in that specific location, are the trigger. A player who reads them closely enough can follow the description to find the party.

**The End**
The player concludes the investigation and delivers the consequence — whatever form that takes. The ending registers at delivery, not at decision.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The Sentinel's justice operates without process, without appeal, and without institutional verification. The cases closed correctly create a template for cases others close incorrectly using the same method.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Scorpio's therapeutic practice is complicated by clients who divert from therapy toward vigilante reporting to the Sentinel
- Libra's court processes suffer attrition as cases that would have gone to formal arbitration are resolved (or ended) outside its jurisdiction
- The investigation methods — effective, unauthorized — are visible in the records disturbed, creating a discovery trail that eventually leads back
- Those who disagree with the Sentinel's conclusions have no recourse, which is structurally the same problem as the institutions the Sentinel replaced

## 10. M+H+N — The Last Champion
*Might + Humanity + Nerve*

You refuse to leave. You refuse to bend. You walk through the crisis as a physical shield for everyone who cannot protect themselves.

**World Trigger**
A specific street in Cancer or Taurus is the site of a recurring low-level conflict — gang pressure, enforcement sweeps, whatever — that nobody has successfully resolved. A chalk mark on the wall of that street, new each morning, marks how many consecutive days the conflict has continued. The tally is renewed by a child who lives there. That chalk tally is the trigger — a visible count of how long the problem has been ignored.

**The End**
The tally is not renewed — when the child who kept the count decides they don't need to keep it anymore. The ending registers the morning the chalk mark doesn't appear.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The Champion's personal presence is the solution, which means it ends when the Champion ends. The specific conflict may be resolved, but the structural conditions that produced it are unchanged. The next version begins after they're gone.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Cancer and Taurus develop a reliance on the Champion that atrophies their own protective capacity — when the Champion leaves, the districts are less capable of self-protection than before
- The enforcement apparatus the Champion displaced relocates and concentrates elsewhere, making specific other areas worse
- The Champion's high-profile presence draws additional actors into the territory they protect
- The people the Champion protected become specific targets for displaced parties exactly when the Champion is gone

## 11. M+I+N — The Obsessive
*Might + Investigation + Nerve*

You pick one thing to understand or destroy and pursue it with absolute will and physical dominance until it is done. Nothing else gets your attention.

**World Trigger**
A physical object with an anomaly that cannot be immediately explained — a marking, a mechanism, a piece of construction that should not exist in its location — is found in a place where nothing significant was expected. The object does not explain itself. But it is physically wrong in a specific, verifiable way. A player who looks at it long enough knows that something is behind it. That object, in that location, is the trigger.

**The End**
The anomaly is fully resolved — the thing behind it is understood or destroyed. The ending registers at resolution, not at decision.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The investigation consumed the player entirely. The energy crisis, the political collapse, the human cost — all received exactly as much attention as was necessary to pursue the anomaly, which was none.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- What the anomaly pointed at — a conspiracy, an infrastructure failure, a historical cover-up — is now exposed in a partial, uncontrolled way because the investigation had no plan for what to do with the finding
- The physical path the investigation took through restricted spaces is a documented trail that other investigators and security personnel follow
- The resolution, if violent, creates a specific incident that requires accounting by parties who weren't involved in creating it
- The obsessive methods the player demonstrated are studied by Aquarius and deployed inappropriately in unrelated contexts

## 12. M+C+E — The Forge Lord
*Might + Calculation + Engine*

You establish a secret, hyper-efficient personal industrial enclave in the Undergrid or frontier. Your forge never fails. The main grid can.

**World Trigger**
A sealed hatch in the Undergrid's lower levels — marked on no map, locked with a mechanism that requires physical force to open, labeled as a utility access — opens onto a space large enough to build in. The enclave achieves first-cycle production output — when it produces something rather than only consuming. The first output is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The enclave draws power and material from Concordia's infrastructure in ways that look like malfunction rather than deliberate siphoning. Diagnostic teams chase ghost failures for years.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Whatever the enclave produces competes with Capricorn's official production, creating market distortions that manifest as unexplained pricing anomalies
- Virgo's maintenance teams eventually find the opened hatch and cannot get back through
- Aries detects power draws at unusual hours in a section of the grid that should be inactive
- The Forge Lord's enclave demonstrates self-sufficiency is possible at small scale, which inspires attempts at larger scales that fail because the Calculation enabling it was individual, not institutional

You gather a loyal group and lead them out of the dying city to found a new settlement.

The new settlement's first permanent structure is completed — when it becomes a place rather than a camp.

Cancer loses its most community-minded individuals — specifically the ones who were willing to sign up, make the journey, and start again. The district's remaining population skews toward those who couldn't or wouldn't leave.

- Sagittarius' frontier culture is complicated by a new community that did not come from frontier stock and does not share its values
- The route to the settlement becomes documented and used by others less prepared for what the journey requires
- The notice on the Cancer board is eventually taken down; the two names already on it become Concordia's shorthand for "people who gave up"

## 16. A+C+H — The Shadow Diplomat
*Agility + Calculation + Humanity*

You move too fast to catch, plan perfectly, and use genuine emotional intelligence to broker deals no one else can reach.

**World Trigger**
A junction between two districts — one of the border crossings that is nominally neutral and practically contested — has a specific empty table in a specific meeting space. The table was set up for negotiations that broke down before they started. The chairs are still arranged; the table has not been cleared because neither district claims the space. That specific table and those chairs, arranged for a meeting that never happened, are the trigger.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The agreement was brokered through personal emotional intelligence and physical presence. The institutions that need to implement it don't understand how it was reached or what made it acceptable to both sides. Implementation fails at the first obstacle not covered by the player's direct intercession.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Libra's formal mediation practice is embarrassed by the success of an unauthorized broker, and the political fallout complicates the agreement's ratification
- Both parties agreed before they fully understood what they agreed to — the speed of the negotiation left specific terms ambiguous
- Other border disputes are now compared to this agreement and found wanting, creating a standard that cannot be consistently met
- The agreement cannot be sustained without the player's continued involvement, and the player is gone

## 19. A+C+E — The Long Game
*Agility + Calculation + Engine*

You move fast, plan cold, and keep going indefinitely. You outlast everyone by being strategically efficient forever.

**World Trigger**
A specific supply cache near Sagittarius' inner boundary was stocked by someone before the current crisis. The contents are specifically chosen for long-term sustainability rather than immediate utility — not what you grab in an emergency, what you prepare before one. Someone planned for a very long stay. That cache and its specific, deliberate contents are the trigger.

**The End**
The player's own sustained resource network outlasts the last faction that tried to end it. The epilogue is told by an archivist years later about an optimization pattern in the city's resource data with no identifiable source.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The sustained optimization siphons resources from the city's common pool in ways that are undetectable because they are too rational — each individual extraction is justifiable, but the aggregate is a slow drain that compounds over years before anyone identifies it.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Capricorn's production accounting is permanently off by a small, unexplained margin that analysts spend years trying to locate
- The supply methodology — calibrated for individual long-term survival — is wrong at group or district scale, but is replicated at scale anyway with predictable failures
- Aries' power grid has a specific low-level draw that nobody can attribute and nobody can eliminate
- The Long Game's continued existence is, decades later, an urban legend that attracts imitators into the same resource spaces

## 21. A+H+N — The Unseen Guardian
*Agility + Humanity + Nerve*

Always where they're needed, emotionally present, psychologically unbreakable. Protection through presence rather than dominance.

**World Trigger**

## Design Notes

- These endings are available to **any character** with the qualifying stat combination. No additional quest completion, faction allegiance, or skill check required to access the trigger. The trigger is in the world.
- Multiple endings can be theoretically accessible to the same character. The player's choices determine which one resolves.
- Speedrun considerations: builds with Agility + Engine as two of the three qualifying stats have the fastest access to their triggers. The five best speedrun candidates remain M+A+E, M+A+C, A+N+E, M+A+I, and M+A+N, in roughly that order.
- Every ending produces a complete epilogue. These are not "you left" slides — they are stories. The city's suffering is not the epilogue's focus, but it is present.
- The `Fast_Minmax_Endings.md` file covers detailed optimized routing for the five fastest-access endings specifically.
