# Remaining Failsafe / Hidden Ending Categories to Design

These are the ending scenarios not yet covered by the existing four secret-ending categories (Pariah Failsafes, Minmax Endings, District Idolized Endings, Hidden Paths). Each represents a player state that could cause a softlock without a dedicated path.

All new endings to be designed with the standard treatment:
- In-world trigger (physical object, location, or environmental state — no NPC-initiated triggers)
- Defined end moment (the specific action/event that completes the questline)
- One main negative effect on Concordia
- A handful of minor negative effects on Concordia

---

## Categories Pending Design

### 1. Near-Pariah
Hated or Vilified with 10–12 districts, but not all 13. The player has burned most bridges but doesn't qualify for any Pariah failsafe (which requires all 13). They also can't access normal quest content in most districts. This is the highest softlock risk of all the remaining categories.

### 2. Wild Child
The player holds Idolized + Vilified status simultaneously with multiple districts — the FNV "Wild Child" mixed reputation state. This disqualifies from Pariah endings (not purely negative) and Idolized endings (not purely positive). A genuinely distinct in-between state that needs its own path.

### 3. Faction Devotion
Deep investment in a cross-district faction rather than a single district. The relevant factions (each could have its own ending, or they could be grouped):
- The Eyes of Gold
- The Red Spiral
- The Veilkeepers
- The Lattice / Bonded Lattice
- The Eternal Winter Collective / Frost Ascetics
- The Reclaimers
- The Echoes of Amundsen
- The Siligel Purists
- The Neon Nomads
- The Chorus of the Deep
- The Memory Weavers
- The Iron Gardeners

### 4. Calethina Devotion
The player completed the full Calethina personal questline ("Echoes of the Bridge") without the Pariah condition. This is distinct from the Pariah-edition Calethina ending (#9 in Failsafes.md). A player who pursued Calethina deeply on a normal playthrough needs their own resolution.

### 5. Identity Fragmentation
The player hit 75+ or 100 on the Identity Fragmentation meter (the world-state float that increases with re-specs). At 75+ specific events are supposed to trigger; at 100 a unique ending branch was flagged in the design. These need to be fully specified.

### 6. Robot-Aligned
The player consistently sided with the robot population throughout the game — in conflicts, resource allocations, political decisions, and moral choices. No single district Idolized ending covers this (districts are mixed human-robot), and no existing secret ending specifically rewards sustained robot-aligned play.

- **Two-stat 8+** — too cheesy, not adding
