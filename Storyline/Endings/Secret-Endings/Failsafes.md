# Failsafe / Pariah Endings (Universal Negative Reputation)

These endings are **only accessible** when the player has **negative reputation** (Shunned / Sneering Punk / Hated / Vilified tier or equivalent) with **every single district and faction** in Inner Tepenia.

They are designed as **world-driven failsafes** — no stat, perk, or skill requirements. Once the global negative reputation threshold is met, new quest hooks, NPCs, environmental clues, and events appear naturally across the city. Achieving them is difficult and dangerous, but always possible through exploration, risk-taking, and interacting with the living world.

---

## 1. The Vilified Sovereign
### Power Fantasy / Dark Ruler
Every district wants you dead, so they begin fighting each other over who gets the honor of killing you first. You exploit the resulting chaos and rise as the de facto tyrant of the fractured city — a ruler everyone fears but no one can unite against.

##### Trigger  
Once you have negative reputation with every district, shady informants and opportunistic NPCs begin approaching you in back alleys, neutral zones, the Black Market, and Undergrid safehouses.

##### How the ending becomes possible  
These contacts feed you leads on district leaders’ vulnerabilities, secret meeting points, and existing rivalries. You can steal documents, eavesdrop during blackouts, plant false evidence, or sabotage infrastructure in ways that make districts blame each other. Over time you broker tense “alliances of convenience” with desperate factions who need your unique position as the universal enemy. In the end, you become the only stabilizing (yet terrifying) force in a city tearing itself apart.

---

## 2. The Pariah’s Covenant
### Unite the Outcasts
All the exiles, criminals, dissidents, and outcasts who were already hated by their own districts gravitate toward you. You become the leader of a new “shadow faction” made entirely of the city’s rejects. Together you carve out your own territory or burn the old order to the ground.

##### Trigger  
Outcasts, exiles, and castaways start actively seeking you out with notes, messages, or direct encounters in abandoned or dangerous areas.

##### How the ending becomes possible  
You receive invitations and desperate requests from various outcast groups. Quest hooks include locating hidden camps, smuggling supplies, protecting them from district patrols, or helping them strike back at their former homes. By uniting these rejects under your banner, you create a new power in Concordia — one built from everyone the city discarded.

---

## 3. MACHINE’s Black Protocol
### AI Catastrophe / Min-Max Synergy
The central MACHINE detects your universal infamy as a “critical threat vector” and triggers a hidden failsafe protocol. You may become the MACHINE’s new avatar, initiate a controlled collapse, or force a full-system recalibration with you as the primary variable.

##### Trigger  
Strange anomalies appear: flickering terminals, distorted radio messages, unusual robot behavior, or corrupted systems that react only to your presence.

##### How the ending becomes possible  
You discover hidden MACHINE access points and old wartime terminals (especially during blackouts). Quest hooks involve following corrupted data trails, overriding failing subsystems, or deliberately provoking the grid. The central MACHINE eventually pulls you into its deeper protocols, giving you direct influence over the city’s failing systems.

---

## 4. The Last Exile
### Tragic / Bittersweet
You are driven out of every district. The ending follows your journey into the outer wastelands (or whatever lies beyond the city). You may discover something important out there, or eventually return years later as a changed legend.

##### Trigger  
Universal hostility makes every settlement dangerous. Messages and survivors begin hinting at routes used only by the truly desperate.

##### How the ending becomes possible  
You locate abandoned outposts in the Frostlands, find risk-taking smugglers, or repair old vehicles and gear left in dangerous zones. Quest hooks revolve around gathering supplies and intelligence for the journey outward. The ending branches based on what you do once you leave Concordia behind.

---

## 5. Total Betrayal Cascade
### Chaotic / War Ending
Because no one trusts you, every district assumes you are secretly working for their rivals. Your mere presence accelerates existing tensions into all-out war. You can “win” by making sure everyone else loses.

##### Trigger  
You begin overhearing or finding evidence of district leaders accusing each other of working with “that traitor” (you). Paranoia levels spike city-wide.

##### How the ending becomes possible  
New opportunities appear through intercepted messages, double agents, and chances to leak false intelligence. You can sabotage supply lines to look like rival attacks or arrange “accidental” meetings between hostile groups during blackouts. The existing inter-district conflicts explode, and you navigate (or escalate) the resulting chaos.

---

## 6. The Self-Fulfilling Prophecy
### Philosophical / Dark Humor
Everyone’s fear and hatred of you gradually turns you into the very monster they were afraid of. This ending leans heavily into roleplaying, dark irony, and nihilistic narration.

##### Trigger  
NPCs across the city begin treating you with extreme fear or open hostility, monologuing about how you embody their worst fears.

##### How the ending becomes possible  
You encounter songs, stories, warnings, and legends already being told about you. Quest hooks involve discovering these myths, confronting the people spreading them, or deliberately leaning into the role the city has assigned you. As fear grows, your legend becomes self-reinforcing — shaping reality through psychological warfare and the city’s reactions.

---

## 7. Silent Apex Predator
### Stealth / Assassin Build Synergy
With all normal paths closed, new stealth and assassination opportunities open up. You methodically eliminate key figures until the power vacuum lets you (or a chosen proxy) rule from the shadows.

##### Trigger  
Anonymous job boards, dead drops, and desperate people offering dangerous work appear in the world once normal quest givers vanish.

##### How the ending becomes possible  
You follow environmental clues — overheard conversations, patrol route maps, abandoned gear — to target important figures. Timing actions around blackouts and district conflicts lets you operate unseen. As leaders disappear one by one, the city’s power structures collapse, creating opportunities for shadow rule or quiet dominance.

---

## 8. The Reckoning
### High-Stakes Climax
All districts simultaneously dispatch hit squads or elite forces against you. The finale becomes a massive, high-stakes confrontation where you either survive against impossible odds or go out in a blaze of infamy.

##### Trigger  
Wanted posters, bounty hunters, and coordinated patrols begin hunting you across multiple districts.

##### How the ending becomes possible  
Quest hooks involve surviving or evading these hunters while uncovering why they’re so desperate to eliminate you. You can turn hunters against each other, use the energy crisis to your advantage, or deliberately draw multiple factions into the same location. The ending builds to one or more large-scale confrontations fueled by the full weight of the city’s hatred.

---

**Implementation Notes**  
- These endings appear **in addition to** (not instead of) other secret endings.  
- The game should clearly signal when universal negative reputation is reached (e.g., a special notification, changed world state, or new ambient dialogue).  
- Multiple Pariah endings can be reachable in one playthrough — the player’s final choices determine which one triggers.  
- They reinforce the core philosophy: **maximum player agency** — even going full chaos/pariah gives meaningful, complete endings.
