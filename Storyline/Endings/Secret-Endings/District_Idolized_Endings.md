# District Idolized Endings
**"Not My Fucking Problem" Endings for Full District Commitment**

These endings unlock when the player achieves maximum commitment to a single district. Each requires:
- **Idolized** reputation with that district
- Completion of **all major and minor quests** in that district
- **Maximum reputation** with that district's signature companion NPC
- **80+** in the district's key associated skill

There are **13 endings** — one per district, including the Hub.

---

## Design Philosophy

These are the opposite of the Pariah failsafes: instead of universal hatred producing escape routes, universal love for one district produces a path that is entirely about that district's particular flavor of "not my problem." Each trigger appears or becomes accessible *after* the final district questline completes — a physical signal that the secret path has opened. No trigger requires additional skill checks or stat gates beyond what completing the questline already demands.

As with all secret endings: the district wins, and something real is lost. The player's absolute commitment to one place means the rest of Concordia goes without them.

## 01. Cancer — The Eternal Caretaker

**Requirements**
- Idolized reputation with Cancer
- Complete all Cancer quests, including "The Last Harvest," "Shelter for the Unwanted," and the multi-stage "Cycle of Care" questline
- Reach maximum reputation with **TBN-NPC_D-01_Caregiver** (empathetic human-robot hybrid caregiver)
- Empathy Protocols 80+

**World Trigger**
After "Cycle of Care" concludes, a specific room in Cancer's central care facility — boarded up or under renovation throughout the questline — has its boarding removed and its door standing open for the first time. The room is clean, made up, waiting. A small handwritten card on the door frame, in the caregiver NPC's handwriting, reads "Ready." That open door and that card are the trigger: the sanctuary is prepared, and the decision to commit is now in the player's hands.

**The End**
The sanctuary faces its first genuine emergency — a real crisis that it resolves entirely without outside district support. The moment the crisis is resolved internally, without calling on anyone outside the sanctuary's walls, is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
Cancer's medical and caregiving capacity — which the whole city drew on during emergencies — is now reserved exclusively for sanctuary residents. A sanctuary that no longer needs the city is also one that the city can no longer call on. The cost of this is invisible until the next city-wide health crisis, when the gap left by Cancer's withdrawal becomes specific and measurable.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Other districts that had informal access to Cancer's care networks find those networks closed — a withdrawal that is not announced, simply enacted
- The sanctuary's success draws people from Taurus and other districts who want in, creating a selection process that Cancer was never designed to run and that produces specific injustices at its edges
- Libra's legal attempt to categorize the sanctuary consumes political resources and produces no resolution — the sanctuary is categorically novel
- The caregiving knowledge and methods developed during the questline are not shared with the city's struggling formal care infrastructure, which continues to deteriorate without them

## 02. Taurus — The Quiet Patriarch/Matriarch

**Requirements**
- Idolized reputation with Taurus
- Complete all Taurus quests, including "Warmth in the Dark," "Family Bonds," and the long-term "Legacy Homes" renovation chain
- Reach maximum reputation with **TBN-NPC_D-02_Cmty_Leader** (steadfast family patriarch/matriarch)
- Diplomatic Negotiation 80+ and Faction Reputation Management 70+

**World Trigger**
After "Legacy Homes" completes, the community noticeboard in the residential bloc — one the player has seen throughout the questline — displays a new document. Nobody requested it. A community member drew it: a floor plan or structural proposal for expanding the bloc's independent infrastructure, signed by a dozen residents. The document appearing without anyone asking for it is the trigger. The community now believes expansion is possible, and they are right.

**The End**
The residential bloc makes its first formal governance decision that requires no approval from any district authority — a self-authorized community choice. That first independent act is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
Taurus' community-building capacity was the model other districts pointed to when they needed an example of how coexistence worked. That model is now internal and exclusive. The stabilizing influence Taurus had on inter-district relations — its informal mediation, its demonstration that diverse groups could live together — is gone from the city's common life.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Neighboring districts that relied on Taurus' informal dispute mediation find the community has no interest in external conflicts anymore
- The renovation techniques developed in "Legacy Homes" are not shared with the city's broader housing infrastructure, which continues deteriorating without them
- Residents who don't qualify for the now-selective community face specific exclusion pressures at the bloc's edges
- Libra's property records are in conflict with the bloc's internal jurisdiction claims — a dispute that drags on indefinitely with no clean resolution

## 03. Leo — The Eternal Stage

**Requirements**
- Idolized reputation with Leo
- Complete all Leo quests, including "The Grand Performance," "Echoes of the Past," and the multi-act "Crown Eternal" storyline
- Reach maximum reputation with **TBN-NPC_D-03_Performer** (charismatic performer and cultural icon)
- Cultural Performance & Resonance 80+ and Sonic Attunement 75+

**World Trigger**
After "Crown Eternal" concludes, a specific performance space in Leo that was dark throughout the game — a historic venue that had been closed, condemned, or held in reserve — has its lights on for the first time. The door is unlocked. No performance is scheduled. The space is simply open and lit, waiting for whatever it will become. That lit, open, unscheduled space is the trigger.

**The End**
The enclave's first entirely self-produced performance — planned, resourced, and executed without any external district support or permission — is completed before an audience. The final bow is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
Leo's creative energy had always spilled outward: performances, festivals, public art that crossed district lines and softened political tensions. That outward flow is now contained within the sovereign enclave. The city loses the cultural cohesion Leo provided, which is invisible as it disappears and only obvious once it is completely gone.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Artists from other districts who depended on Leo's venues and networks lose access to the city's primary creative infrastructure without warning or replacement
- Leo's cultural outputs — which shaped Concordia's shared mythology and historical self-image — stop reaching the districts that needed them most
- The enclave's self-selection of what it produces reflects its specific internal culture, which diverges from the city's broader cultural needs as the enclave becomes more insular
- Libra's cultural governance frameworks become irrelevant — there is now a sovereign creative entity the city cannot regulate, fund, or direct, and has no precedent for ignoring

## 04. Scorpio — The Veilwalker

**Requirements**
- Idolized reputation with Scorpio
- Complete all Scorpio quests, including "Facing the Abyss," "Shatter and Rebuild," and the deeply personal "Veil Between Selves" questline
- Reach maximum reputation with **TBN-NPC_D-04_Psych-Hl** (radical mind-healer)
- Memory & Consciousness Manipulation 80+ and Moral Philosophy & Ethical Reasoning 75+

**World Trigger**
After "Veil Between Selves" concludes, a specific chamber in Scorpio's deepest therapeutic archive — one that has had a bureaucratic seal on its door throughout the game — has the seal removed. No ceremony. No announcement. The door is simply no longer sealed, and a player who tries it finds it opens. Behind it is a transformation record that, once read, makes the next step obvious. That unsealed door, in that specific corridor, is the trigger.

**The End**
The sanctuary undergoes its first transformation session that the player is not directing — the method has been passed on. When it happens without the player present, the ending registers.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
Scorpio's therapeutic methods — the most effective tools the city has for processing collective trauma — are now sequestered in a hidden sanctuary. The city's surface therapy infrastructure is left with the less effective methods that the sanctuary has moved beyond. The gap between what trauma care could be and what it now is widens permanently.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- People in Concordia who needed radical therapeutic intervention but could not access the sanctuary face specific deterioration — they know the option exists and cannot reach it, which is a particular kind of harm
- The transformation records in the unsealed archive contain information about specific individuals whose privacy is now in fewer and less accountable hands
- Scorpio's public-facing therapeutic practice is politically undermined by the knowledge that the genuine methods are elsewhere — confidence in the surface practice collapses
- The sanctuary's methods, practiced in isolation by a small chosen group, develop in ways that increasingly diverge from anything applicable to the city's actual population

## 05. Aries — The Overclocked Exile

**Requirements**
- Idolized reputation with Aries
- Complete all Aries quests, including "Heart of the Furnace," "Overclock Protocol," and the dangerous "Final Shift" questline
- Reach maximum reputation with **TBN-NPC_D-05_Combat-Vet** (veteran Spire engineer)
- Thermal Engineering 80+ and Engine 7+

**World Trigger**
After "Final Shift" completes, a specific access panel in a Spire maintenance corridor — one the player has passed throughout the Aries questline — has its lock disengaged. Not prominently. Not announced. The panel's indicator light, which has always been red, is now off. A player who checks it and finds it unlocked has found the trigger. The veteran NPC left it accessible specifically for the player, without saying so directly.

**The End**
The player draws the Spire's core technology for personal use and exits the Aries district without triggering a security response. The clean, unchallenged exit is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
Aries loses the specific technical knowledge that made the Overclock Protocol work as a managed, controlled process. The player extracted what they needed and left. What remains in the Spire is less than what was there, and the people who stayed don't know exactly what is missing — only that something is, and only when they next need it.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- The Spire's capability gap becomes apparent the next time Aries attempts a high-output operation — the discovery happens at the worst possible moment, during a crisis rather than a routine test
- The veteran NPC who left the panel unlocked faces internal accountability from Aries — their standing and career are damaged in ways that affect Aries' senior engineering culture
- The unlocked panel, once discovered by Aries security, triggers an internal review that disrupts ongoing operations for months
- Other engineers who wanted legitimate access to the Overclock Protocol for district purposes find the opportunity gone and no explanation for why

## 06. Capricorn — The Shadow Industrialist

**Requirements**
- Idolized reputation with Capricorn
- Complete all Capricorn quests, including "Iron & Ambition," "Production Eternal," and the massive "Forge Dominion" chain
- Reach maximum reputation with **TBN-NPC_D-06_Industrial-Craftsman** (ruthless industrial visionary)
- Siligel Chemistry 80+ and Precision Maintenance & Repair 75+

**World Trigger**
After "Forge Dominion" completes, a specific production terminal in Capricorn's deepest manufacturing facility — one the player has used throughout the questline — displays a manufacturing authorization code that was never officially issued. It appeared overnight. The industrial visionary NPC generated it outside the official allocation system. That unauthorized code, on that terminal, is the trigger. Using it initiates the hidden production line.

**The End**
The Shadow Industrialist's enclave achieves first-cycle production output — when it produces something rather than simply storing or processing. The first production run is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
A portion of Capricorn's industrial capacity has been quietly rerouted into a private enclave that reports to no one. The city's production metrics are inaccurate in specific ways that supply planners will not discover until a shortage cascades from the discrepancy — and by then the enclave is too embedded to dismantle cleanly.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- The siligel synthesis infrastructure feeding the enclave is diverted from Capricorn's official supply chain, creating deficits that manifest as unexplained shortages in the robot populations that depend on it
- The industrial visionary NPC's unauthorized code generation is eventually detected — the consequences damage Capricorn's internal engineering leadership at a time when it can least afford instability
- The techniques developed in the enclave are effectively proprietary to the Shadow Industrialist — the city's official industrial base does not benefit from them
- Capricorn's robot workforce, increasingly aware of the questline's activities, faces a governance question about whether the enclave's operations include them — a question nobody in authority wants to answer

## 07. Aquarius (The Labs) — The Rogue Researcher

**Requirements**
- Idolized reputation with The Labs
- Complete all Aquarius quests, including "Beyond the Horizon," "Forbidden Theorems," and the high-risk "Singularity Test" storyline
- Reach maximum reputation with **TBN-NPC_D-07_Eccentric-Dctr** (brilliant but unstable visionary)
- Decentralized Systems Design 80+ and Data Archaeology 75+

**World Trigger**
After "Singularity Test" concludes, a specific lab storage unit in Aquarius — one bearing a "RESTRICTED - PENDING REVIEW" label throughout the entire game — has had that label replaced. In its place is a handwritten note from the eccentric doctor NPC. It says only "Yours." That storage unit, with that changed label, in that specific lab corridor, is the trigger.

**The End**
The player arrives at the hidden laboratory site with the research intact and the lab activates for the first time. That first activation — the moment the lab becomes a functioning research station rather than a collection of stolen equipment — is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The research the player took was under review for a reason — the "Singularity Test" data was the most consequential output the Labs had produced. Removing it leaves Aquarius with the less significant projects and no ability to build on its most important work. The city loses its best scientific lead at the exact moment it most needs original solutions.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- The eccentric doctor NPC faces formal censure for authorizing the transfer — Aquarius' research ethics review process is triggered, disrupting multiple ongoing projects during the investigation
- The "Forbidden Theorems" research remaining in Aquarius' records can no longer be fully interpreted without the Singularity Test data — partially complete knowledge is in specific ways more dangerous than none
- The hidden laboratory operates with no peer review, no ethical oversight, and no external check — the research continues in whatever direction the player points it
- Aquarius' reputation in Concordia's governance structure is damaged by the apparent failure to secure its most sensitive materials

## 08. Libra — The Silent Arbiter

**Requirements**
- Idolized reputation with Libra
- Complete all Libra quests, including "Balance of Power," "The Grand Accord," and the politically intricate "Neutral Ground" questline
- Reach maximum reputation with **TBN-NPC_D-08_Diplomat** (master diplomat)
- Diplomatic Negotiation 80+ and Faction Reputation Management 80+

**World Trigger**
After "Neutral Ground" concludes, a specific meeting room in Libra's administrative complex — one the player has used repeatedly throughout the questline — has a document on its central table that was not there before. It is a formal sovereignty declaration for a specific neutral territory, with all relevant district acknowledgments already signed. One signature line is blank: the player's. That document, on that table, in that room, is the trigger.

**The End**
The player signs the document and the sovereignty of the neutral territory is formally established. The signature is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
Libra constructed the Neutral Ground as controlled neutrality — the appearance of balance with effective administrative oversight maintained underneath. The Silent Arbiter's sovereignty makes it genuinely neutral, which means outside Libra's control. The distinction destroys the careful balance Libra was sustaining between governance and the pretense of non-governance, and the collapse of that pretense is politically destabilizing.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Every district that signed the acknowledgments did so expecting Libra to retain effective control — when that proves false, the acknowledgments are contested and the political fallout is significant
- The master diplomat NPC, who facilitated the signatures, is seen as having acted in bad faith — their career and Libra's diplomatic relationships both suffer specific damage
- The neutral territory immediately becomes a destination for parties wanting to conduct business outside any district's jurisdiction, which is precisely the function Libra designed the Neutral Ground to prevent
- Libra's internal power dynamics shift when it becomes clear that the most respected diplomatic achievement of the current era produced a result Libra cannot control

## 09. Gemini (Janbogo Subnet Nexus) — The Untraceable Ghost

**Requirements**
- Idolized reputation with Janbogo Subnet Nexus
- Complete all Gemini quests, including "Whispers in the Wires," "Truth Archive," and the sprawling "Infinite Network" questline
- Reach maximum reputation with **TBN-NPC_D-09_Master-Hacker** (enigmatic information broker)
- Arcanet Navigation & Hacking 80+ and Deception & Narrative Crafting 75+

**World Trigger**
After "Infinite Network" concludes, a specific Arcanet node in Janbogo's deepest infrastructure — one the player visited during the questline — begins displaying a persistent signal pattern they will recognize from earlier in the questline. It is not a message. It is a confirmation: the final piece of the player's untraceable identity infrastructure has been installed. That pattern, on that specific node, is the trigger.

**The End**
The player disappears from every traceable record — the moment their last verifiable signal goes dark. The ghost is complete when there is no longer any evidence in the network that they exist.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The methods used to make an identity untraceable are now demonstrated to work. The master hacker NPC who built the infrastructure knows exactly how it functions. The techniques exist, and they will not remain exclusive to the player indefinitely — the knowledge of their possibility is already a permanent change.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Janbogo's Arcanet security infrastructure is permanently compromised — the specific vulnerabilities the untraceable network exploited remain open
- Truth Market operations in Gemini are disrupted by an identity that cannot be priced or tracked, creating specific failures in their predictive models
- Every faction that was monitoring the player's digital presence loses their primary intelligence asset simultaneously — multiple security operations develop specific blind spots at the same moment
- The master hacker NPC is now the only person with full knowledge of how the disappearance was accomplished, which makes them specifically valuable and specifically at risk

## 10. Pisces (The Markets) — The King/Queen of Shadows

**Requirements**
- Idolized reputation with The Markets
- Complete all Pisces quests, including "Beneath the Veil," "The Grand Heist," and the criminal empire-building "Shadow Throne" questline
- Reach maximum reputation with **TBN-NPC_D-10_Charmer** (charming underworld kingpin)
- Stealth & Infiltration 80+ and Jury-Rigging & Repurposing 75+

**World Trigger**
After "Shadow Throne" concludes, a specific back room in Pisces' deepest black market — a room the player has visited throughout the questline — has a chair placed at the head of a table, facing the door. The chair was not there before. No nameplate. No announcement. The chair in that position, placed by the charming kingpin NPC without a word, is the trigger.

**The End**
The shadow empire's first city-wide operation — one that crosses multiple districts simultaneously — is successfully executed without any enforcement response. The clean, undetected cross-district operation is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The most efficiently organized logistics network in Concordia now answers exclusively to the Shadow Throne. When the power grid fails and the city needs emergency resource mobilization, the only network capable of rapid cross-district supply movement is occupied with empire business. The city cannot commandeer it, negotiate with it, or replicate it in time.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- The black market's consolidation into a true shadow empire destabilizes Pisces' existing underworld equilibrium — smaller operators are absorbed, displaced, or eliminated, and the informal variety that made the Markets adaptive disappears
- Libra's commerce regulation is permanently undermined — there is now a logistics network larger than some districts' official economies operating entirely outside their framework
- The Pisces syncretic religion, which had a complex relationship with the underworld, faces a theological crisis when the empire absorbs spaces the religion considered sacred
- Districts that previously had informal black market access as a safety valve find that consolidation has replaced flexible informal access with structured dependency — they can no longer simply find what they need, only negotiate for it

## 11. Sagittarius (The Frostlands) — The Icewalker

**Requirements**
- Idolized reputation with The Frostlands
- Complete all Sagittarius quests, including "Into the White," "Frontier Justice," and the epic expedition "Beyond the Horizon"
- Reach maximum reputation with **TBN-NPC_D-11_Frontiersman** (legendary frontier scout)
- Frontier Survival & Cold Adaptation 80+ and Environmental Exploitation & Ripple Reading 75+

**World Trigger**
After "Beyond the Horizon" concludes, the frontier scout NPC has left a specific marker at the city's outermost manned checkpoint — a carved stone, a specific arrangement of materials, a painted heading. It was not there before. It is oriented outward. That marker, placed by the scout after the questline concludes, is the trigger. It points in a direction. The player decides whether to follow.

**The End**
The player and the expedition cross the furthest confirmed point in the documented frontier and continue beyond it into undocumented territory. The crossing of the undocumented threshold is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The most capable frontier operators in Concordia — the player and the scout NPC — have committed permanently to the unknown outside. Sagittarius loses its best people to the direction it always faced, and the city loses the operators who could have managed the relationship between the frontier and the interior.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- The expedition's permanent absence creates a power vacuum in Sagittarius' frontier culture that is filled by operators with less experience and less judgment
- The route the expedition took is known in outline, but subsequent expeditions attempting it without the Icewalker's capability face greater risk and worse odds
- The The Vigil [NAME TBD] faction, which hoped the expedition would find evidence of off-world return, receives no word from the disappeared expedition and must decide what to do with the silence
- Sagittarius' informal supply network to inner districts — which the scout NPC helped maintain — dissolves without its anchor, creating specific shortage points in Cancer and Taurus

## 12. Virgo (The Undergrid) — The Hidden Sovereign

**Requirements**
- Idolized reputation with The Undergrid
- Complete all Virgo quests, including "The Deep Maintenance," "Veins of the City," and the dangerous "Core Shadow" questline
- Reach maximum reputation with **TBN-NPC_D-12_Mtnc-Engineer** (quiet master of the tunnels)
- Precision Maintenance & Repair 80+ and Undergrid Navigation & Salvaging 80+

**World Trigger**
After "Core Shadow" concludes, a specific junction in the Undergrid's deepest layer — one the player has passed throughout the questline — has a new physical addition: a master control lever, junction key, or primary switch that was previously missing, removed, or sealed. The master engineer NPC installed it quietly, without filing any maintenance record. That specific new component, in that specific junction, is the trigger. It was not there before. Now it is, and it is functional.

**The End**
The Hidden Sovereign activates the master control for the first time. What it does is the player's choice. Having the choice — having the lever in their hand — is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The Hidden Sovereign has not destroyed anything. They have simply positioned themselves to do so, or not, at will. The city now operates with the permanent awareness that one person holds a lever that could shut it down, and has no institutional mechanism for addressing that. The infrastructure every district depends on now runs on the implied threat of someone choosing not to use a switch.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Virgo's community, which built its identity on collective stewardship of the tunnels, now lives in a district whose most critical infrastructure is effectively in one person's hands — the community that prided itself on shared ownership has lost that ownership without a vote
- The master engineer NPC's decision to install the control lever without authorization fractures Virgo's internal governance — some members consider it a gift to the district's greatest champion, some consider it a betrayal of collective principles
- Aries and Libra, upon learning the master junction control exists and is held by an individual, jointly attempt to negotiate access — the attempt itself reveals how exposed infrastructure governance actually is
- The knowledge spreads through Concordia's political community, and the resulting paranoia affects every infrastructure-adjacent decision made for the foreseeable future

## 13. Hub (Concordia Central Hub / Axis Mundi) — The True Nexus

**Requirements**
- Idolized reputation with the Concordia Central Hub
- Complete all Hub-based quests, including the transit worker questline, the crossroads neutrality questline, and the multi-stage "Axis Mundi" questline
- Reach maximum reputation with **TBN-NPC_Hub_Transit-Rep** (transit workers' collective representative)
- Diplomatic Negotiation 80+ and Humanity 7+

**World Trigger**
After the "Axis Mundi" questline concludes, a specific location in the Grand Transit Exchange — the Bridge Memorial, or the base of the central transit board — has a new physical addition. Not officially sanctioned. The Hub's transit workers, independently and without any authority's knowledge, have installed a small brass plate. It lists the player's name, and below it a phrase used internally among the workers: *"Goes Nowhere. Holds Everything."* No district knows it's there yet. That plate, in that location, is the trigger.

**The End**
During a moment of genuine inter-district crisis, the player is the only party that all sides are willing to speak through simultaneously. The first time a message from one district reaches another specifically because the player carried it — and is acknowledged by both sides as legitimate — is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The Hub's neutrality now depends on a single individual remaining alive, present, and trusted rather than on a system, a protocol, or an institution. A person cannot be infrastructure. When the True Nexus is gone — by death, departure, or loss of trust — the Hub's neutrality is gone with them, and there is no institutional successor. The city traded a fragile system for something more immediately effective and structurally worse.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Every district that accepted the player as a neutral conduit now routes communication through that channel at the expense of building direct relationships with rivals — when the player is absent, the districts' diplomatic capacity is reduced across the board
- Libra, which considered itself the city's primary arbitration center, is politically diminished by the emergence of a neutral authority that is not Libra and that Libra cannot absorb or co-opt
- The transit workers who installed the plate face administrative questioning from every district simultaneously — their act of unauthorized recognition creates the exact political problem it was celebrating the resolution of
- Parties who want to compromise the city's neutrality now have a specific human target to pressure, threaten, or remove, where previously they would have had to dismantle an institution

## Design Notes

- District Idolized endings are intentionally the **hardest secret endings to reach** — harder than Pariah failsafes (which require only sustained bad behavior) and harder than most Minmax endings (which require only stat investment). These require quest completion, companion relationship, skill investment, and full district commitment simultaneously.
- They can coexist with and sometimes branch from the Minmax endings. A player who qualifies for both a district ending and a Minmax ending will face a genuine choice at the trigger point.
- The Hub ending (#13) is the only Idolized ending that does not involve leaving Concordia or withdrawing from its common life — it is about becoming more embedded in the city's function, not less. This makes its specific consequence (the city depending on a person rather than a system) more insidious than the withdrawal consequences of the other twelve.
- Companion NPC names marked **TBN** are placeholders pending full character development. The design intent for each is specified in the Requirements section.
