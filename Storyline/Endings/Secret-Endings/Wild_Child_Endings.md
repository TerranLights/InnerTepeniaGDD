# Wild Child Endings

## Trigger Condition

These endings are available when the player holds **Wild Child** status — Idolized and Vilified simultaneously — with multiple districts of Concordia. Wild Child status is distinct from all other reputation states: it is not mixed reputation in the sense of some districts loving you and others hating you. It is a single district holding both extremes at once, because the player has done enough to be genuinely loved *and* enough to be genuinely despised by the same community.

Wild Child is not ambiguity. Both sides are correct in what they are responding to.

**Wild Child disqualifies the player from:**
- Pariah failsafes (require purely negative reputation across all districts — Wild Child is not purely negative)
- District Idolized endings (require purely positive Idolized status — Wild Child is not purely positive)
- Near-Pariah endings (which require non-Wild-Child neutral-or-better status in 1–3 districts)

**Wild Child endings are tiered by count:**
- 3–4 districts at Wild Child → The Divided Chorus
- 5–7 districts at Wild Child → The Schism Champions
- 8–12 districts at Wild Child → The Living Myth
- All 13 districts at Wild Child → The Perfect Contradiction

A player who meets a higher threshold also technically meets all lower thresholds. The game should route to the highest qualifying ending.

---

## Design Philosophy

Every other reputation category asks: *what does the city think of you?* Wild Child asks something different: *what does it mean that the city holds two irreconcilable things about you at the same time, and neither is wrong?*

The player did what both sides say they did. The hero-narrative is accurate. The villain-narrative is accurate. The city is not confused. The city is holding two true things that cannot coexist within its categorical frameworks, and the strain of holding them is the central dramatic fact of these endings.

The Wild Child endings should feel unlike any other ending category. They are not about escape, achievement, manipulation, or compromise. They are about what happens when a person exceeds the system's capacity to process them — while still being a person, still present, still visible.

## WC-1. The Divided Chorus
*Wild Child with 3–4 districts*

Three or four districts are each internally frozen. The political process by which a district reaches consensus — on anything — requires agreement on baseline facts. A district that cannot agree on whether the player is a benefactor or a threat cannot coordinate on anything that touches the player's legacy. The player's legacy, by this point, touches almost everything.

**World Trigger**
A community notice board in one of the Wild Child districts displays two competing postings about the player, pinned side by side. One was posted by the internal faction that regards the player as a hero. One was posted by the internal faction that regards them as a villain. Both are accurate accounts of things the player actually did. The district's administration has been unable to decide which to remove — removing either one would constitute taking a position the district cannot politically afford. Both are still there. That specific notice board, in that specific district, with both postings simultaneously present and neither taken down, is the trigger. The player reads both.

**The End**
The player commits to a single definitive public action — something they choose, something that is unambiguous in itself — that forces each Wild Child district to reach an internal verdict. Not an argument, not a speech. An action that makes neutrality within each district administratively impossible. Each district reaches a verdict; some go hero, some go villain. The moment every Wild Child district has registered a position is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The districts that resolved toward "hero" and the districts that resolved toward "villain" now hold formal positions on the same individual that directly contradict each other. This contradiction becomes a reference point in future inter-district negotiations — every political disagreement that touches anything the player was involved in now has this record underneath it. The Divided Chorus produced verdicts. The verdicts last.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Within each Wild Child district, the faction that lost the internal verdict does not accept the loss — the fracture the Wild Child status created deepens when the verdict is reached, not when it is contested
- Scorpio's community therapeutic infrastructure absorbs the emotional cost of multiple districts simultaneously processing incompatible feelings about a single person; the caseload is specific and unusual and produces specific gaps in Scorpio's normal function
- Libra has no existing governance framework for a person who is simultaneously the city's hero and villain — the Divided Chorus forces Libra to create one, and whatever framework is created will be applied to future individuals who exceed the categorical system
- The player's original actions — both the ones that earned Idolized status and the ones that earned Vilified status — are now permanently politically weaponized by both sides, stripped of their actual context and meaning by the verdict process

## WC-2. The Schism Champions
*Wild Child with 5–7 districts*

Five or more districts are fractured. The factions within each Wild Child district — those who love the player and those who despise them — have been building toward confrontation. Not because of anything the player is currently doing. Because of what the player already did, which cannot be undone, and which both sides are still living with. The player did not create these fractures. The fractures were already present in each district. The player became the specific point at which they became impossible to ignore.

**World Trigger**
A specific district commons area — Cancer's communal meeting space, Taurus' workshop floor, or whichever Wild Child district has the most acute internal division — has been physically divided by the two factions. Different markings on different sides. Different symbols, different colors, different physical claims on the same shared space. The division is visible and total, and it is not yet in open conflict. It is preparing to be. The preparation written into the physical space — the two camps, the marked boundary between them, the absence of anyone in the middle — is the trigger. The player walks into a room that has already chosen sides.

**The End**
The player makes a definitive choice: support one side of the schism, or make a specific action that redirects both factions' energy toward a shared target other than the player. The action is not arbitrated. It is committed to. The moment the player commits — and the schism either crystallizes into permanent formal division or redirects its force — is the ending. The two factions do not reconcile. One of them wins, or both of them lose their target and must find something else to organize around.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The fractures the Wild Child status revealed within each district were real before the player arrived and will be real after. The Schism Champions ending crystallized them. What was manageable internal tension became formal schism, and multiple districts are now carrying open internal divisions that do not resolve when the player is no longer the immediate subject. The city is structurally weaker than it was before the player arrived, in specific districts, in specific ways that will matter during the next crisis.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Districts experiencing active schism lose functional governance during the conflict — maintenance, resource allocation, emergency response, and coordination with other districts all degrade in ways that compound each other
- Whatever the player chose — which side to support, or how to redirect — both factions incorporate the player's choice into their own narrative; the player's decision becomes part of the schism's mythology rather than a resolution of it
- Other factions and districts external to the schism read the division as opportunity and move to apply pressure on the affected districts at their most vulnerable; the Schism Champions ending accelerates external threats to the divided districts
- The physical markers the two camps used — the symbols and colors and spatial divisions — become permanently culturally legible in each district long after the player is gone; those markings mean something, and what they mean is not something the city chose

## WC-3. The Living Myth
*Wild Child with 8–12 districts*

Eight or more districts simultaneously worship and despise the player. The city can no longer process the player as a person. Competing narratives — heroic accounts, condemnations, revisionist histories, counter-revisionist accounts of the revisions — are running in parallel in Gemini's Truth Markets, in Cancer's oral tradition, in Scorpio's communal records, in Libra's administrative footnotes. The player is alive. Still physically present in the city. Still making choices. None of this has stopped the mythology from forming. It is forming in real time, around a living person, and the living person can see it happening to them.

**World Trigger**
A specific terminal in the Janbogo Subnet Nexus — in the Truth Markets or the broadcast infrastructure — is displaying competing data streams about the player in the same live information feed. Heroic accounts and formal condemnations, updating simultaneously, neither suppressed. The Janbogo system that is supposed to converge on truth over time is instead producing two irreconcilable truths in parallel, and it is not breaking under the contradiction — it is simply displaying both, treating the simultaneous outputs as equally valid data. A player who navigates to that specific terminal, in that specific corridor of the Janbogo infrastructure, sees themselves described in two incompatible ways in the same feed, in real time. That terminal is the trigger.

**The End**
The player makes a single unambiguous public action — something they choose with full clarity about what it is. They then observe what the city's myth-making apparatus does with it. The action is simple. The mythology is not. Within hours of the action, both the heroic narrative and the condemnatory narrative have incorporated it as confirmation of their respective positions. The player watches this happen and makes the choice not to intervene — not to correct, not to explain, not to add to the record. The act of choosing silence in the face of active mythologization is the ending.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The city has trained itself to mythologize complex individuals rather than understand them. This training will apply to the next person who does things that resist clean categorization — and the next, and the next. The myth-making apparatus that formed around a living player will be ready and practiced when it is needed for someone else. Concordia has permanently lost some capacity for nuanced individual assessment and replaced it with a faster, more durable, and less accurate process.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- Janbogo's Truth Markets are designed to converge on accuracy over time; running irreconcilable parallel truths about the same subject for long enough damages the convergence mechanism, and the Markets are measurably less reliable after the Living Myth than before it
- The competing cults and anti-cults that formed around the player become self-sustaining communities that persist after the player's story ends — minor factions organized around an irreconcilable disagreement about someone who is no longer present and cannot be questioned
- Cancer's and Scorpio's oral and archival traditions now contain equally well-documented irreconcilable accounts of the same events; future historians cannot reconstruct what actually happened during this period, because the record is complete and contradictory in equal measure
- The player's actual motivations — whatever they genuinely were — are permanently inaccessible to the historical record; both the heroic and condemnatory accounts attribute motivations that serve their narrative, and neither is accurate, and there is no version of the record that preserves the real ones

## WC-4. The Perfect Contradiction
*Wild Child with all 13 districts*

Every district in Concordia simultaneously worships and despises the player. No person in the city holds a simple opinion. Every district contains active internal factions that regard the player as their greatest benefactor and active internal factions that regard the player as their greatest threat, and both factions are correct in what they are responding to. The player actually did what both sides say they did, in every district, across the entire city.

This is not ambiguity. This is not spin. This is not a failure of perception. The player has genuinely done extraordinary things for every district and genuinely done extraordinary harm to every district, and the city holds both facts simultaneously and cannot reconcile them and has not collapsed under the weight of holding them. It is simply carrying both. Every district. All thirteen.

**World Trigger**
The Hub's central administrative registry — the system that categorizes every citizen of Concordia for governance, resource allocation, and reputation tracking purposes — contains the player's entry in a permanent error state. Flagged. Unfiled. Every other entry in the registry is assigned a categorical status. The player's entry cannot be assigned one, because every available category is simultaneously correct and incorrect: the system has successfully recorded every reputation data point the player has accumulated, and the accumulated data is internally irreconcilable. The registry is not broken. It processed the data correctly. The data is the problem.

A player who accesses the Hub's administrative registry terminal — during specific windows of Hub access, or through a specific route that the near-full-city Wild Child condition opens in ways that a normal city relationship would not — finds their own record. The error message is in Libra's administrative language, precise and bureaucratic, describing a categorical failure it cannot resolve. That record, in that terminal, in the Hub, is the trigger.

**The End**
The player opens an input field in their own unfiled registry entry and writes their own categorical statement. Not a reputation. Not a faction standing. A statement, in whatever terms the player chooses, of what they consider themselves to be. The system cannot accept it — it cannot file any categorical entry for this record, because the record is irreconcilable and will remain so. The statement is entered. The system flags it as an unprocessable input and appends it to the error record. The error record, now containing the player's own words, remains in the registry in perpetual unfiled status. The player closes the terminal. The ending registers at the moment the terminal closes.

The city will carry that unfiled record indefinitely.

**Main Negative Effect on Concordia**
The existence of an individual who successfully saturated all thirteen districts' categorical frameworks simultaneously — who was genuinely both at every extreme, everywhere, all at once — demonstrates that the reputation and categorization systems that form the backbone of Concordia's governance are brittle at their limits. Libra must decide whether to acknowledge the limitation publicly or bury the record. Acknowledging it undermines confidence in the system. Burying it leaves a known flaw unaddressed. Either choice is costly, and neither is obviously wrong. The city is left carrying a governance problem it cannot solve and a record it cannot file.

**Minor Negative Effects**
- The unfiled error record in the central registry becomes a specific administrative artifact — referenced, investigated, debated, and mythologized — for as long as the registry exists; it is the one entry the system cannot process and therefore the one entry the system can never fully ignore
- Every district's simultaneous Idolized and Vilified status toward the same individual demonstrates that the reputation system can be saturated in both directions at once, which changes how all subsequent reputation assessments are calibrated; the system now knows its own edge cases in a way it did not before
- The internal factions in every district — the ones that love the player and the ones that despise them — are self-sustaining communities that outlast the player's presence, organized around an irreconcilable disagreement about someone who is gone and cannot be asked; thirteen pairs of opposing factions, one pair per district, each pair with no path to resolution
- Libra's institutional response to the Perfect Contradiction — whatever framework it creates to handle future irreconcilable records — sets a precedent for how Concordia processes individuals who exceed the system's categorical capacity; the precedent will be wrong in whichever direction it errs, and it will be applied to people who are not the player, in circumstances the player could not have anticipated

## Implementation Notes

- Wild Child endings are **tiered and exclusive**: a player qualifying for WC-4 does not also access WC-1 through WC-3. The game routes to the highest qualifying ending.
- The Wild Child condition is **rare by design**. Reaching Wild Child status requires the player to have done genuinely heroic and genuinely harmful things within the same district — not mixed reputation across districts, but saturation in both directions within each one. Players who reach WC-4 did something extraordinary and the ending should reflect that.
- **WC-4's trigger requires Hub access** during a specific window. A player with Wild Child status across all 13 districts has a paradoxically unique access profile: every district simultaneously wants to honor them and wants to stop them, which creates specific gaps in enforcement that allow Hub access that would otherwise be unavailable. The access condition should emerge organically from the Wild Child state rather than requiring a separate quest.
- These endings do not "resolve" the Wild Child condition. They are not redemption arcs or punishment endings. They are endings that acknowledge what the player is and let the city respond to it. The player does not become simpler. The city does not become more comfortable. The story ends with both of them exactly as complicated as they were.
- As with all secret endings: these are complete conclusions. A player who reaches WC-4 has achieved something that no other player achieved and the ending should feel like it.
