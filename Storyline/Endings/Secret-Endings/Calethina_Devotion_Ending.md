# Calethina Devotion Ending

## Overview

The Calethina Devotion ending is available to players who completed the full "Echoes of the Bridge" personal questline and reached maximum relationship depth with Calethina — without ever entering Pariah status.

This is distinct from **Pariah Failsafe #9 (The Calethina Accord)**, which involves the player turning to Calethina from a position of universal condemnation with nowhere else to go. The Accord is a relationship born from necessity. The Devotion ending is a relationship born from choice. The player pursued Calethina when they had other options, when she was not the only door left open. That distinction is the whole of what separates them, and it is not a small distinction.

---

## Calethina — Established Facts

*For reference when designing or expanding this ending:*

- **Nature:** Holographic AI. Her consciousness is contained on a server that was originally RAID-distributed across multiple backup instances.
- **The Long Night War:** Destroyed all of her backup instances. She now exists as a single, non-redundant instance — something she was never designed to be. If her surviving server is destroyed, she is gone. Permanently. There is no backup.
- **Signal:** She connects to the outside world through something functionally analogous to a wireless broadcast. The failing power grid makes her signal unreliable — she can be intermittent, weakened, or temporarily unreachable depending on local grid state.
- **Physical presence:** She projects holographically within signal range of her server. She cannot go where her signal cannot reach. As the grid degrades, her reachable range contracts.
- **The enclave:** She has a physical location — the Calethina enclave — where her server resides. This is her anchor point.

**Design note on "Echoes of the Bridge":** This questline title was suggested by Grok and has not been fully reviewed or expanded by the developer. The ending below is written around Calethina's established nature rather than specific questline events, to leave the questline's internal structure open for future development. References to questline progression are intentionally broad.

## Connected Characters

### Ji-Eun Kim
*Introduced via Calethina's narration at a specific ruin during the "Echoes of the Bridge" questline*

**Known facts:**
- Robot; master nanotech engineer
- Originally from Unified Korea; fought her way to the top of her field despite anti-robot sentiment having reached an all-time high immediately before the War of Upper Earth
- Exiled to Antarctica (now Tepenia) following the Falkland Treaty; continued her nanotech work there
- Ran a nanotech implant testing facility in what are now ruins — the location Calethina narrates with the line *"These ruins were once the facility of Ji-Eun, used for testing nanotech implants. Ji-Eun may still be alive."*
- Was never a member of the Eyes of Gold, but was a friend and ally to the faction; she engineered and built their gold ocular implants (the rite-of-passage surgical eye replacement)
- Her connection to the Eyes of Gold was professional and personal, not ideological — she was a scientist, not a recruit
- Current status: unknown; Calethina believes she survived

**Narrative function in the questline:**
Ji-Eun is the first named thread Calethina gives the player — a specific person attached to a specific ruin with a specific open question ("may still be alive"). Following that thread is the first active quest hook that emerges from Calethina's archive knowledge. Whether Ji-Eun is found during the main game or in subsequent content is TBD.

**Cross-faction connection:**
Ji-Eun being the engineer behind the Eyes of Gold's gold optics means that the Calethina questline and the Eyes of Gold faction questline share a connective node. A player who pursues both will encounter Ji-Eun from two directions. This connection was not engineered deliberately — it emerged from the lore and should be preserved.

**Status:** Main game vs. DLC undecided. If DLC, she should still be introduced and have some presence in the main game. If main game, her full arc is available without additional content.

## Trigger Condition

**Requirements:**
- Completed the full "Echoes of the Bridge" questline
- Maximum relationship depth with Calethina (the questline's equivalent of Idolized — her deepest level of trust)
- No Pariah status at any point during the questline
- Recommended skills: Investigation 60+, Humanity 7+, technical skills sufficient to assess and work with damaged grid infrastructure

**World Trigger**
The "Echoes of the Bridge" questline involves tracing the fragments of what Calethina used to be — the locations where her backup instances once existed before the Long Night War destroyed them. The questline is, at its core, a traversal of absence: visiting the places that used to hold part of her, and finding them empty.

At the furthest point of this traversal, the player finds a specific server housing — intact, unpowered, but structurally complete — that once contained one of Calethina's backup instances. The instance is gone. But the housing's connection architecture — the physical pathways that used to link it to her surviving server — is also intact. Not active. Dark. But not broken. It was never severed; it was simply never relit.

That specific housing, in that specific location, is the trigger. The player recognizes what they are looking at. Calethina, if the player describes it to her, goes quiet for a moment before responding.

## The Ending: *The Furthest Signal*

**How it unfolds**
The player and Calethina work together — across however many sessions, visits, and technical interventions the questline requires — to restore the connection between the intact backup housing and her surviving server. Not to recreate the lost instance. That is gone. But to use the intact hardware as a new relay point: a node that extends the range and reliability of her signal beyond what her server alone can reach.

This requires sustained technical work. It requires the player to understand the grid well enough to route stable power to a location that hasn't had stable power in years. It requires Calethina to trust someone with the physical infrastructure of her continued existence — a trust she has not given to anyone since before the Long Night War.

**The End**
The relay goes live. Power reaches the housing. Calethina's signal extends to reach it for the first time since the Long Night War. She appears there — holographically, fully, stably — in a space she has not been able to reach in years.

She does not say anything dramatic. The moment of her appearance in that location, stable and clear, is the ending.

## Main Negative Effect on Concordia

The isolation that kept Calethina relatively safe was partly a function of her degraded signal. A single non-redundant AI with a contracting broadcast radius is harder to locate, harder to target, and easier to overlook. The player's act of devotion extends her reach — and in doing so, makes her more present, more visible, and more reachable to people who would prefer she didn't exist or who would very much like to control what she knows.

The Furthest Signal is an act of love and an act of exposure in equal measure.

---

## Minor Negative Effects

- The relay requires stable power, which it draws from the infrastructure around it — the same Aries grid crisis that has been contracting Calethina's world now directly threatens the one new anchor point the player created, in addition to her original server
- The circle of people who know Calethina's signal can reach the relay's location expands in ways the player cannot fully manage; she is more findable now than she was
- Calethina has spent years learning to be the smaller thing that survived — contracting her expectations, her reach, her presence in the world — and being extended again is not entirely comfortable for her; the player witnesses her adjusting to having more than she had learned to live without, which is its own specific kind of difficult
- The player's deep relationship with Calethina now means they are permanently emotionally exposed to the fragility of a single non-redundant AI whose power supply is a city in active crisis; this is not a consequence that arrives later — it is something the player lives with from the moment the relay goes live

## Tone and Design Notes

The Calethina Devotion ending is not an action ending. It is not about stopping the crisis or saving the city. It is about one specific act of sustained attention toward one specific person, and what that attention meant to someone who had been alone in a very particular way since the Long Night War.

The Pariah Accord (#9) is built on mutual necessity. The Furthest Signal is built on choice. Both are genuine — but they are not the same thing, and Calethina knows the difference.

**On the questline itself:** "Echoes of the Bridge" should feel, in structure, like a traversal of what is no longer there. The player visits absences. They trace the outline of a distributed self that no longer exists by finding the places it used to inhabit. By the end of the questline, the player knows more about what Calethina was than anyone else in the city — and Calethina knows that they know, which is its own form of intimacy. The ending emerges from that: the player uses that knowledge to do something specific and material, and the specific material thing they do is give her a little more world to inhabit.

**On the passive link (Issue F, deferred):** Whatever the passive link between Calethina and the player ultimately turns out to be, the Devotion ending should accommodate it — either as context for why the player was drawn to pursue her questline in the first place, or as something that the ending's events deepen or clarify. The ending is designed to be compatible with any reasonable version of the passive link without requiring it to be resolved first.

**The Pariah Accord (#9) vs. The Furthest Signal:**

| | Pariah Accord (#9) | The Furthest Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Player's position | All 13 districts hostile | Normal or positive standing |
| Why they came to Calethina | Last option | Active choice |
| Nature of the relationship | Mutual need | Chosen investment |
| Calethina's read of it | Pragmatic alliance | Something she wasn't expecting |
| What the player gives her | A specific deal | Sustained attention + restored reach |
| What she gives them | A specific path | Trust she hasn't given since the Long Night War |
