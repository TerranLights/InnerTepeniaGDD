# Player Homes

---

## Overview

The player-character can acquire and use player homes across Concordia's districts. There are two categories:

**Standard player homes** — acquired through reputation, resource contribution, or quest completion. Documented in this file, organized by district.

**Companion homes** — each romanced companion unlocks their personal home as an additional player home. These are documented in `Game-Mechanics/Core-Mechanics/Companion_System.md` (Romance Reward section). They exist separately from and in addition to standard homes. See the cross-reference section at the bottom of this file.

---

## Standard Homes — Structure

Each district offers **four tiers** of player home:

| Tier | Acquisition Difficulty | Notes |
|------|----------------------|-------|
| 1 — Starter | Low to moderate | Reputation + modest resources; no quest lock |
| 2 — Mid-tier | Moderate | Regular contribution + faction standing |
| 3 — Established | Moderate to high | Strong loyalty; often quest-locked or quest-upgraded |
| 4 — Prestige | High; heavily quest-gated | Multi-stage questline required; unique rewards |

All homes are available by **purchase, quest completion, or a combination of both.**

## District 1 — Cancer

Cancer homes emphasize safety, emotional warmth, communal bonds, and quiet resilience amid resource strain and emotional labor. They feel like personal sanctuaries within the larger protective network of warm, humid domes, hydroponic gardens, and care facilities. Rarely luxurious — more like hard-won safe havens patched together from district resources. Upgrades involve helping with care work, gathering supplies, or strengthening bonds rather than pure currency.

### 1. Integrated Creche-Adjacent Shelter Pod *(Starter)*

**Feel:** Cozy, protective, and slightly communal. A compact, softly lit pod or small suite built into or right next to a creche/nursery cluster. Thick insulation, gentle blue-green lighting, the constant sound of water features or soft airflow, and the distant sounds of children and young robots. Feels like a warm burrow or safe nest — nurturing but with an underlying sense of shared vulnerability and emotional labor.

**Proximity:** Directly adjacent to creches, nurseries, and basic medical support areas. Short walk to hydroponic healing gardens and grief support circles. Easy access to caregivers, refugee integration staff, and vulnerable NPCs.

**Difficulty:** Low to moderate. Requires basic reputation with Cancer caregivers (triage help, supply runs, or simple integration tasks) plus modest resource contribution (scavenged materials, siligel donations, or volunteer shifts).

**Quest lock:** None for the base version. Minor upgrade path (extra storage or bonded companion slot) ties into side quests helping specific refugees or caregivers.

---

### 2. Hydroponic Garden Loft / Greenhouse Apartment *(Mid-tier)*

**Feel:** Serene, life-affirming, and hands-on. A modest loft or converted greenhouse space above or woven into the hydroponic healing gardens. Lush (but resource-strained) plants, soft natural-ish lighting filtered through insulation, the smell of soil and growing things, and gentle water sounds. Hopeful and nurturing, mixed with the practical strain of maintaining the gardens during shortages.

**Proximity:** Embedded in or overlooking the hydroponic healing gardens and therapy parks. Close to medical/psychological care centers and bonding ceremony halls. Good access to gardeners, therapists, and healing residents.

**Difficulty:** Moderate. Requires noticeable contributions to food/medicine production (regular gardening shifts, supply runs from other districts, or fixing garden systems during blackouts). Reputation with garden crews and care staff is key.

**Quest lock:** A premium version or major upgrade (better climate control, private meditation nook, or expanded growing plots) unlocked by completing a mid-tier questline involving protecting or expanding a specific healing garden plot.

---

### 3. Private Bonded Family / Grief Support Suite *(Established)*

**Feel:** Intimate, emotionally heavy but deeply comforting. A self-contained suite designed around the district's bonding ceremony halls and grief support circles. Soft, curving architecture, memorial elements (small personal shrines or memory walls), warm lighting, and space for quiet reflection or small gatherings. Feels like a personal sanctuary for processing trauma — warm and supportive but carrying the weight of loss.

**Proximity:** Very close to bonding ceremony halls, grief support circles, and advanced psychological care centers. Short distance to orphanages/integration centers and family habitation clusters.

**Difficulty:** Moderate to high. Requires building significant trust with the district's emotional care network (consistent help with grief support, refugee integration, or emotional labor tasks). Often involves resource donation during shortages or aiding in triage decisions.

**Quest lock:** Strongly recommended as a quest-reward home. Unlocked after completing a notable questline demonstrating loyalty — e.g., helping a specific group of refugees or orphans integrate, protecting a bonded human-robot pair through a crisis, or aiding in a major grief support initiative. This version can include unique upgrades like a private memorial alcove or bonded NPC companion options.

---

### 4. Emergency Shelter / Cold-Shock Recovery Annex *(Prestige)*

**Feel:** Rugged yet deeply protective and resilient. A reinforced annex or converted emergency shelter optimized for cold-shock recovery, with thick insulation, backup systems, and a no-nonsense but caring atmosphere. Functional bunks or pods alongside spaces for quiet support and healing — a "we survive together" practicality mixed with Cancer's emotional warmth.

**Proximity:** Within or right beside major emergency shelters and care facilities. Excellent access to medical staff, refugee intake areas, and the broader shelter network. Close to integration centers for new arrivals.

**Difficulty:** High. Base access requires substantial demonstrated value to the district's protective infrastructure. Pure reputation grinding is involved.

**Quest lock:** Yes. Unlocked only after completing a substantial, multi-stage quest arc showing deep loyalty to Cancer's ideals (e.g., organizing or protecting a large refugee/integration effort, uncovering and resolving internal resource-hoarding conflicts, or aiding the district during a major external threat like power diversion from Aries). Rewards can include advanced defensive upgrades, private medical storage, or story-significant NPC ties.

## District 2 — Taurus

Taurus homes emphasize stability, material comfort, self-sufficiency, and long-term security. They feel like hard-earned, well-insulated personal fortresses within family rings and residential clusters. Upgrades focus on better heating, storage, and defensive insulation rather than flashy luxuries. Acquiring them rewards consistent reliability, resource contribution, and respect for established families.

### 1. Modest Family-Ring Habitation Pod *(Starter)*

**Feel:** Solid, grounded, and quietly comfortable. A compact, heavily insulated pod or small apartment within one of the established family habitation rings. Thick walls, warm ambient heating, practical storage, and a small private courtyard or window overlooking communal green spaces. A "this is mine and it will last" reliability — cozy but never wasteful.

**Proximity:** Inside a stable residential ring, near communal heating hubs, small hydroponic micro-farms, and family gathering areas. Short walk to local markets and maintenance workshops.

**Difficulty:** Low to moderate. Requires basic reputation with Taurus family councils (supply runs, insulation help, or assisting during minor blackouts) plus a modest contribution of scavenged materials or siligel.

**Quest lock:** None for the base version. Minor upgrades (extra storage or better climate control) tie into small side tasks helping local families.

---

### 2. Reinforced Insulation Loft / Workshop Apartment *(Mid-tier)*

**Feel:** Practical, self-reliant, and enduring. A converted loft or reinforced workshop space with heavy insulation layers, tool benches, and visible repair projects. Warm, lived-in atmosphere with stockpiled goods, personal reinforcement plating on walls, and a "I built this to last" pride. Feels like a personal bunker that also functions as a comfortable home.

**Proximity:** Overlooks or sits near private maintenance yards and communal reinforcement projects. Close to material depots, family workshops, and residential security patrols.

**Difficulty:** Moderate. Requires regular contributions to district infrastructure (insulation repairs, material scavenging, or helping families fortify their homes). Reputation with workshop crews and family elders is essential.

**Quest lock:** A premium upgraded version (advanced private workshop, superior heating loops, or hidden safe room) can be earned through a mid-tier questline involving protecting a residential ring from a major threat or resolving a resource-hoarding dispute.

---

### 3. Multi-Generational Bonded Family Suite *(Established)*

**Feel:** Warm, rooted, and legacy-oriented. A larger, self-contained suite designed for bonded pairs or small chosen families. Multiple connected rooms, reinforced family memorial wall, private hydroponic nook, and spaces for quiet domestic routines. A deep sense of continuity and stubborn protection of "what matters most."

**Proximity:** Within established family clusters, near bonding ceremony areas (shared with Cancer) and long-term residential archives. Close to schools/creches for younger residents and elder gathering spots.

**Difficulty:** Moderate to high. Demands consistent trust-building with multi-generational families (long-term volunteer work, protecting family assets during shortages, or mediating internal disputes). Significant material investment required.

**Quest lock:** Yes. Unlocked after completing a notable questline demonstrating commitment to Taurus values — e.g., helping a bonded human-robot family survive a crisis, recovering lost family heirlooms from outside the district, or defending the ring against external pressure. Upgrades can include private family NPC ties or enhanced security features.

---

### 4. Old-Timer's Reinforced Corner Annex *(Prestige)*

**Feel:** Ruggedly luxurious in a practical way. A heavily fortified corner annex or converted old-timer's residence with the best insulation in the district, redundant heating systems, expansive storage, and a commanding view of the residential rings. The ultimate "I earned this and no one is taking it" safe haven — solid, warm, and quietly proud.

**Proximity:** In a prestigious, well-defended section of the district. Excellent access to family council meetings, elite workshops, and central heating distribution nodes.

**Difficulty:** High. Requires major reputation grinding, substantial resource donations during crises, and proven long-term reliability.

**Quest lock:** Yes. Only accessible after completing a complex, multi-stage questline proving deep loyalty to Taurus stability — such as exposing and stopping a large-scale resource sabotage plot, helping establish a major new family ring, or resolving a generational conflict that threatened the entire district's self-sufficiency. Rewards can include unique defensive upgrades, private siligel cache, or influential NPC allies.

## District 3 — Leo

Leo homes emphasize radiance, spectacle, self-expression, and public prestige. They feel like stages, dressing rooms, or personal spotlights — dramatic, warm, and designed to be seen. Acquiring them rewards charisma, public performance, festival contributions, and building a personal legend. Upgrades often involve showy customizations, lighting rigs, or reputation-boosting décor.

### 1. Backstage Bolthole / Green Room Pod *(Starter)*

**Feel:** Vibrant, energetic, and slightly chaotic. A compact, colorful pod tucked behind one of the smaller performance venues or rehearsal halls. Bright accent lighting, costume racks, half-finished props, and a large vanity mirror with glowing bulbs. A "just stepped off stage" buzz — warm, creative, and always ready for the next show.

**Proximity:** Directly behind or beneath mid-tier theaters, rehearsal domes, and minor festival stages. Short walk to performer hangouts, talent agencies, and street-level busking spots.

**Difficulty:** Low to moderate. Requires basic reputation with Leo's performance circles (running errands for troupes, performing small gigs, helping with stage setup, or donating glow-tech/scavenged lighting parts).

**Quest lock:** None for the base version. Cosmetic/performance upgrades (better lighting rig, small practice space) available through minor side gigs and talent contests.

---

### 2. Spotlight Balcony Loft *(Mid-tier)*

**Feel:** Flashy, elevated, and performative. A stylish loft with a large balcony overlooking a popular promenade or secondary performance square. Dramatic red-gold lighting, holographic poster walls that can display player achievements or custom art, and a small private stage area. "Rising star" energy — warm, proud, and intentionally visible.

**Proximity:** Overlooking major promenades, open-air performance plazas, and mid-level festival grounds. Close to talent scouts, street performers, and popular nightlife venues.

**Difficulty:** Moderate. Requires consistent public performances, helping organize or promote festivals, and building favor with venue owners or influential artists.

**Quest lock:** A premium version (larger balcony, interactive holo-wall, or private green room extension) unlocked via a mid-tier questline centered on helping a rising troupe succeed or stealing the spotlight at a major event.

---

### 3. Private Performer's Lounge Suite *(Established)*

**Feel:** Luxurious yet theatrical. A spacious, multi-room suite with high ceilings, custom lighting rigs, a small private performance space, and trophy/memorial walls for accolades. Rich fabrics, warm radiant heating tuned for long rehearsals, and subtle sensory elements (scent emitters, sound dampening). The residence of a respected artist or local celebrity — generous, proud, and a little larger than life.

**Proximity:** In the more prestigious performer residential rings, near grand theaters, arena back entrances, and high-end after-party venues. Close to established stars, patrons, and cultural influencers.

**Difficulty:** Moderate to high. Demands significant reputation with Leo's elite circles (headlining multiple shows, sponsoring events, or resolving rivalries). Notable contributions of rare performance tech or siligel for lighting are usually required.

**Quest lock:** Yes. Unlocked or significantly upgraded after a notable questline demonstrating commitment to Leo's spirit — organizing a major district festival, helping an aging star reclaim their legacy, or winning a high-stakes performance rivalry that boosts the whole district's prestige.

---

### 4. Radiant Apex Penthouse / Idol's Atrium *(Prestige)*

**Feel:** Spectacular, commanding, and legendary. A top-tier penthouse or illuminated atrium with panoramic views of the district's glowing domes, a full private stage with professional rigging, customizable public holo-displays, and luxurious radiant heating that makes the entire space feel like a permanent spotlight. "I am the main character" — dramatic, warm, and intentionally iconic.

**Proximity:** At the highest or most central prestige locations, overlooking the Central Amphitheater or major festival grounds. Excellent access to district leadership, top-tier talent, and major cultural events.

**Difficulty:** High. Requires exceptional reputation, major financial/resource donations to the arts, and visible long-term contributions to Leo's identity as the cultural heart of Concordia.

**Quest lock:** Yes. Only available after completing a complex, multi-stage questline that cements the player as a Leo legend — saving the district's biggest festival from sabotage, orchestrating a city-wide cultural renaissance event, or resolving a major inter-district scandal through a spectacular public performance. Rewards include unique showpiece upgrades, personal fan NPC followers, or story-significant celebrity status.

A compact, reinforced alcove or small chamber with heavy sound-dampening, low crimson/amber lighting, and symbolic rebirth motifs (shedding chambers or mirrored confession walls). Feels like a cocoon or confession booth — safe for vulnerability but always carrying an undercurrent of psychological intensity.

**Proximity:** Tucked within or near lower-level rebirth clinics and shadow-work centers. Short walk to the Archive of Final Confessions, endurance ritual spaces, and discreet counseling chambers.

**Difficulty:** Low to moderate. Requires basic trust with Scorpio practitioners (participating in introductory shadow sessions, helping with confidential errands, or donating psychological data/logs).

---

A converted loft in the mid-levels with exposed structural elements, adjustable lighting for different ritual moods, and spaces for personal endurance or identity work. Sparse but powerful décor — a central "shedding circle," private confession recorder, or symbolic art from past rebirths. A heavy, focused atmosphere of ongoing change.

**Proximity:** Near mid-tier rebirth facilities, underground endurance arenas, and taboo research archives. Close to shadow augmentation specialists and residents undergoing or recovering from major transformations.

**Difficulty:** Moderate. Demands active participation in district practices (assisting with rituals, protecting vulnerable patients during crises, or sourcing rare components for augmentation procedures). Reputation with both official clinics and underground practitioners is needed.

**Quest lock:** A stronger version (enhanced privacy shielding, custom augmentation station, or hidden sub-chamber) can be earned through a mid-tier questline involving helping a patient through a dangerous rebirth or resolving an internal clinic scandal.

### 3. Private Shadow Sanctum Suite *(Established)*

**Feel:** Deeply personal, luxurious in darkness, and psychologically potent. A multi-room suite with thick privacy barriers, ritual chambers, a secure memory archive, and symbolic rebirth elements (a custom "death/rebirth" alcove or dual-identity wardrobe). A true sanctuary for confronting the self — intimate, powerful, and slightly dangerous.

**Proximity:** In more exclusive transformation residential rings, near advanced psychological research labs and high-level confession archives. Good access to elite shadow workers and those who have completed profound rebirths.

**Difficulty:** Moderate to high. Requires significant demonstrated commitment to Scorpio's ethos (handling sensitive truths, protecting the district's secrets, or aiding in ethically complex transformations).

**Quest lock:** Yes. Unlocked or greatly enhanced after a notable questline showing dedication to transformation and truth — helping expose or bury a major hidden trauma affecting the district, guiding a powerful NPC through a full rebirth cycle, or resolving a crisis involving fractured identities.

---

**Feel:** Commanding, profound, and transformative. A high or deeply recessed sanctum with panoramic or deeply introspective views, multiple ritual spaces, advanced memory/past-life simulation tech, and symbolic architecture representing death and renewal. The residence of someone who has stared into the void and returned changed — intense, respected, and quietly feared.

**Proximity:** In the most prestigious or symbolically charged locations, overlooking or beneath major rebirth temples and the Archive of Final Confessions. Excellent access to district leadership, influential survivors, and hidden power brokers.

**Quest lock:** Yes. Only accessible after completing a complex, multi-stage questline proving profound loyalty to Scorpio's principles — descending into a dangerous collective shadow crisis affecting multiple patients, orchestrating a district-wide "great rebirth" event, or making a personally costly choice that reveals or buries a transformative truth with city-wide implications. Rewards include unique ritual upgrades, personal shadow NPC companion options, or story-significant identity-altering boons.
