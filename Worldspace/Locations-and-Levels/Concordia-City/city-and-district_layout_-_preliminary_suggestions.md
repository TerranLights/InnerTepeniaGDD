# Conflicts in Relation to the Main Story (preliminary suggestions)

## 1. Janbogo Central Hub (The Heart / Starting District)
### Atmosphere: Bustling, neon-lit administrative and transit core. Towering domes with glowing Arcanet billboards, constant hum of transit tubes, and holographic announcements. Feels like the “downtown” of Concordia — optimistic but strained.
### Key Locations:
* Calethina’s Engineering & Activation Lab (your starting build chamber)
* Grand Transit Exchange (major highway junction point)
* Arcanet Nexus Tower (main data hub for the Janbogo Subnet)
* Central Council Plaza (where faction representatives meet)
### Primary Factions: International Data Syndicate, Manufacturing Concord, Echoes of Amundsen
### Trade-off Tension: Knowledge & growth vs immediate stability (power constantly diverted between servers and factories).
### Quest Hooks:
* First main quest (Heating Grid Failure or Siligel contamination)
* Mediating early faction disputes at the Council Plaza
* Retrieving a lost data shard from a failing server
### Gameplay Flavor: Safe starter zone with lots of dialogue and character creation follow-up. High foot traffic = many NPCs to talk to.

## 2. Coastal Cut District (Hwy 110)
### Atmosphere: Humid, misty, greenhouse-like domes filled with hydroponic towers and residential hab-blocks. Salt corrosion on metal, glowing algae lights, sounds of water pumps and human voices.
### Key Locations:
* Vast Hydroponic Arcologies
* Coastal Heating Plants
* Bonded Residential Spires (where many human-robot couples live)
* Port of Concordia (small trucking docks)
### Primary Factions: Hydroponic Enclaves, Red Spiral, Lattice (bond-focused)
### Trade-off Tension: Feeding humans vs keeping the city warm vs supporting bonded pairs.
### Quest Hooks:
* Crop failure investigation
* Helping (or denying) a struggling bonded couple
* Defending hydroponics from an ice storm that threatens the district
### Gameplay Flavor: Strong human presence. Many emotional, personal stories. Excellent for empathy-based skill checks.

## 3. Mountain Cut Industrial Zone (Hwy 37)
### Atmosphere: Noisy, hot (relatively), industrial. Sparks, heavy machinery, robotic assembly lines, dust from siligel precursor material quarries, dramatic mountain walls visible through dome windows.
### Key Locations:
* Siligel Synthesis Facilities & Precursor Material Quarries
* Major Robot Manufacturing Foundries
* Highway Maintenance Depots
* Eyes of Gold training grounds / dueling arenas
### Primary Factions: Manufacturing Concord, Eyes of Gold, Siligel Purists, Reclaimers
### Trade-off Tension: Mass production & quality vs resource consumption & worker safety.
### Quest Hooks:
* Quality vs quantity dilemma in siligel production
* Retrieving rare components from dangerous quarry depths
* Helping (or stopping) an overly ambitious manufacturing project
### Gameplay Flavor: Industrial action sequences, technical puzzles, combat training opportunities.

## 4. TBN-district & Power Core
### Atmosphere: Stark, cathedral-like industrial temple. Massive geothermal pipes, glowing reactor cores, constant low-frequency rumble. Feels both sacred and precarious.
### Key Locations:
* The Great TBN-district (central heating distribution)
* Deep Geothermal Access Tunnels
* Emergency Cryo-Shelters
### Primary Factions: Thermal Collective, Chorus of the Deep, International Data Syndicate (secondary)
### Trade-off Tension: Keeping the entire city alive right now vs long-term stability of the heat source.
### Quest Hooks:
* Major Act 2–3 crisis (cascading failure)
* Ritual/technical maintenance with the Chorus
* Deciding whether to overdraw the core to save one district
### Gameplay Flavor: High-stakes environmental puzzles and moral decisions with city-wide consequences.

## 5. Ossuary Quarter
### Atmosphere: Somber, reverent, hauntingly beautiful. Dim blue lighting, walls and pillars made of polished robot chassis fragments (“metal bones”), soft choral music, snow gently falling inside broken domes.
### Key Locations:
* The Grand Ossuary Cathedral
* Memorial Gardens of the Fallen
* Quiet contemplation halls
### Primary Factions: Goth Ossuary Community, Memory Weavers, Echoes of Amundsen
### Trade-off Tension: Honoring the past vs using resources (and old parts) for the living.
### Quest Hooks:
* Consecrating a new wing of the Ossuary
* Helping a grieving human/robot pair
* Deciding whether to recycle ancient war relics
### Gameplay Flavor: Atmospheric exploration, deep philosophical dialogue, possible horror-tinged moments.

## 6. Cymatic Resonance District
### Atmosphere: Vibrant, artistic, slightly chaotic. Pulsing lights synced to music, public performances, resonance sculptures that vibrate the air, hidden speakeasies.
### Key Locations:
* The Grand Resonance Amphitheater
* Sonic Research Laboratories
* Underground music clubs
### Primary Factions: Cymaticists, Neon Nomads (fringe), Memory Weavers
### Trade-off Tension: Investing in culture and beauty vs strict survival needs.
### Quest Hooks:
* Protecting or shutting down a major festival during a resource crisis
* Investigating a dangerous new sonic weapon/tech
* Helping artists create something meaningful with limited power
### Gameplay Flavor: Mini-games involving sound/rhythm, social hubs, black-market connections.

## 7. Janbogo Undergrid (The Depths)
### Atmosphere: Dark, labyrinthine, dangerous but full of secrets. Old pre-war tunnels, glowing Arcanet cables, makeshift camps, dripping condensation, distant machinery echoes.
### Key Locations:
* Forgotten Arcanet Backup Nodes
* Smuggler enclaves
* Abandoned research labs
* Hidden highway access tunnels
### Primary Factions: Veilkeepers, Neon Nomads, Reclaimers, various independents
### Trade-off Tension: Using dangerous shortcuts vs maintaining safety and order.
### Quest Hooks:
* Recovering lost data from collapsed sections
* Investigating mysterious signals
* Confronting (or joining) underground factions
### Gameplay Flavor: Dungeon-crawling, stealth, high-risk/high-reward exploration.

## 8. TBN-district (The Spire of Governance)
### Atmosphere: Cold, formal, austere. Towering polished metal and glass spire rising above the domes. Holographic seals of the old Falkland Treaty, echoing halls, and constant low-level security drones.
### Key Locations:
* Concordia Governing Council Chambers
* Treaty Archive Vaults
* Bureau of Resource Allocation
* Diplomatic Reception Halls
### Primary Factions: International Data Syndicate (political wing), Echoes of Amundsen, Veilkeepers
### Trade-off Tension: Centralized control and long-term planning vs giving districts autonomy and immediate survival needs.
### Quest Hooks:
* Political intrigue and vote manipulation during resource crises
* Investigating corruption in the Bureau of Resource Allocation
* Mediating between rival council factions
### Gameplay Flavor: Heavy dialogue, persuasion, and skill-check focused. Reputation with multiple factions matters enormously here.

## 9. The TBN Black Market (Black Market & Smuggling Hub)A
tmosphere: Dimly lit, chaotic, neon-underlit tunnels and converted warehouses. Constant haggling, hidden entrances, suspicious deals, and the smell of recycled air and illicit siligel distilleries.
### Key Locations:
* The Shadow Exchange (main trading floor)
* Smuggler’s Highway Entrance (secret access to Hwy 183 & 37)

* Contraband Arcanet Nodes (uncensored data)
### Primary Factions: Neon Nomads, Reclaimers, Veilkeepers (as buyers), independent operators
### Trade-off Tension: Access to rare/illegal goods vs undermining official resource distribution and city stability.
### Quest Hooks:
* Smuggling critical parts during a shortage (risking Infamy with official factions)
* Hunting down stolen goods that belong to the player’s allies

### Gameplay Flavor: Stealth, bartering, moral gray-area choices, and high-risk combat if deals go bad.

## 10. The Bonded Habitation Rings (Primary Residential Sector)
### Atmosphere: Warm (by Antarctic standards), intimate, community-focused. Multi-level residential domes filled with personal touches — robot charging alcoves next to human living spaces, shared gardens, and family murals.
### Key Locations:
* The Ring of Pairs (popular bonded housing)
* Community Heating & Siligel Distribution Centers
* Schools and creches for human children
* Memorial Walls for lost loved ones
### Primary Factions: The Lattice, Red Spiral, Hydroponic Enclaves
### Trade-off Tension: Providing comfortable living space vs diverting resources to industrial or data needs.
### Quest Hooks:
* Helping (or failing to help) struggling bonded couples during shortages
* Investigating mysterious “bond failures” or suicides
* Evacuating or reinforcing a collapsing habitation ring
### Gameplay Flavor: Deep personal stories, romance/companion development, and emotional consequences.

## 11. The TBN-district Quarter
### Atmosphere: Sterile, futuristic, buzzing with experimental energy. Clean white and blue lighting, prototype robots walking around, constant humming of test chambers.### Key Locations:
* Advanced Robotics Institute
* Experimental Siligel & Hybrid Tech Labs
* Cold-Resistance Testing Chambers
* University of Concordia (Arcanet studies)
### Primary Factions: Manufacturing Concord, Siligel Purists, Memory Weavers, Chorus of the Deep
### Trade-off Tension: Investing in breakthrough technology vs immediate survival of the current population.
### Quest Hooks:
* Testing dangerous new robot chassis (you as the player character are a prototype)
* Ethical dilemmas involving experimental subjects
* Recovering failed experiments that have gone rogue
### Gameplay Flavor: Puzzle-solving, science-oriented skill checks, and opportunities for permanent character upgrades.

## 12. The The Frostlands (Outer / Frontier District)
### Atmosphere: Harsh, windswept, frontier feeling. Partially completed or damaged domes on the very edge of Concordia, overlooking the endless Antarctic ice. Makeshift repairs, hardy residents, and a sense of being “at the limit.”
### Key Locations:
* Outer Highway Terminus & Watchtowers
* Frontier Hydroponic Outposts
* Abandoned Pre-War Research Outposts
* Frost Ascetic encampments
### Primary Factions: Eternal Winter Collective (Frost Ascetics), Neon Nomads, Thermal Collective (border patrols)
### Trade-off Tension: Expanding the city outward vs concentrating resources in the safer inner districts.
### Quest Hooks:
* Defending the outer perimeter from ice storms or structural collapse
* Helping frontier settlers who feel abandoned by Central Hub
* Deciding whether to abandon or reinforce the Reaches during a major crisis
### Gameplay Flavor:
* Exploration
* Survival elements
* Large-scale tactical defense missions.



## Overall City Structure Notes
* Vertical & Radial Layout: Janbogo Central Hub sits at the center. The other districts radiate outward along the three major highways, with Undergrid connecting everything below.
* District Size: Not all districts need to be equally large at launch — you can start with 3–4 and expand.
* Inter-district Travel: Transit tubes, Kharkovchanka shuttles, or on-foot journeys with random events based on current city stability and your reputation.
* Dynamic State: Districts can visibly change based on your choices (e.g., Coastal Cut becoming frozen and miserable if you neglect heating).

## Additional Notes on the Overall City
* Verticality: Many districts have upper, mid, and lower levels (domes, ground level, undergrid).
* Highway Connections: Major transit hubs let you travel between districts with travel time, random events, or escort missions.
* Dynamic Changes: Depending on your choices, districts can visibly degrade (frozen sections, darkened lights, overcrowded habs) or improve.
* Fast Travel: Unlocked gradually via reputation with the Janbogo Transit Guild.

## Quick Summary of Naming Adjustments
* All “Janbogo” references inside the city have been changed to “Concordia” or “Janbogo Subnet” (to show it’s just the local data region, not the other city).
* The actual city of Janbogo remains off-limits and is only mentioned in lore (e.g., “supplies from Janbogo are delayed again” or “refugees who made it from Janbogo before the last highway collapse”).
