# City Refugee District Affinities — Concordia Integration Reference

**Purpose:** For each of the 32 non-South-Pole Tepenian cities, a ranked top-3 list of Concordia districts where refugees from that city would most likely settle, feel at home, or gravitate toward — based on cultural-national origin, economic character, city personality, and (for destroyed/ruined cities) the specific psychology of displacement and loss. Use for NPC writing, district flavor, refugee community design, and emergent political tensions.

**Destroyed/ruined cities** (Zukelli, Soyuz, Belgrano, Palmer City, Casey) receive additional notes on the specific grief-psychology driving their placement, since displacement trauma shapes these refugee communities differently from voluntary or managed migration.

**District shorthand used throughout:**
- **Cancer** — Sanctuary/caregiving/grief holding
- **Taurus** — Residential stability/community bonds/multigenerational families
- **Leo** — Performance/arts/pride/entertainment
- **Scorpio** — Psychological transformation/memory/Archive of Final Confessions
- **Aries** — Power generation/warrior-engineer culture/crew loyalty
- **Capricorn** — Industrial meritocracy/guild hierarchy/ambitious production
- **Aquarius** — Research/science/experimental idealism/The Labs
- **Libra** — Governance/law/diplomacy/Treaty Archive Vaults
- **Gemini** — Information/communication/Janbogo diaspora enclave
- **Pisces** — Black market/gray economy/dissolution/overflow/The Markets
- **Sagittarius** — Frontier/exploration/self-sufficiency/The Frostlands
- **Virgo** — Infrastructure maintenance/underground systems/The Undergrid
- **Hub** — Transit crossroads/rootless neutrality/crossroads identity

## Palmer Subnet — Antarctic Peninsula

### Esperanza
*The dominant mainland Peninsula city. Only true coastline settlement on the continental Peninsula itself. USA T1, European T2. South American secondary character from Argentine founding heritage. The gateway city — Esperanza means "hope" in Spanish. The administrative and commercial hub of the entire Peninsula subnet. Large, diverse, politically sophisticated. Every exile who landed on the Peninsula passed through here at some point.*

**1. Pisces / The Markets**
Esperanza was a gateway city in the most literal sense — the point of first contact between the outside world and the Peninsula subnet. Gateway cities generate gray economies, information brokers, transit smugglers, and gray-market supply chains as naturally as they generate official trade. Esperanza's commercial infrastructure was enormous and its shadow economy grew to match it. Refugees from Esperanza who worked in trade, shipping, or the vast unofficial economy that surrounded it will find Pisces not just comfortable but familiar — it operates on the same principles Esperanza did. *Effect: Esperanza refugees bring serious commercial networking experience into Pisces; they know how to move things without paper trails, which makes them valuable contacts for Pisces operators and Libra's deniable procurement simultaneously.*

**2. Libra**
The city that administered the Peninsula was a training ground for Tepenian political instinct. Esperanza's refugees include a disproportionate share of administrators, treaty interpreters, allocation officers, and diplomats — the people who managed the complex political relationships between seven distinct Peninsula communities. They understand how institutions work because they ran them. In Libra, their skills are immediately legible. *Effect: Esperanza refugees who enter Libra tend to advance quickly, which generates resentment from Hub-born Libra staff who see them as outsiders accelerating into roles that should require longer institutional seasoning. Creates an internal Libra faction tension.*

**3. Scorpio**
"Hope" was the city's name, and now its refugees live in a city called Concordia, displaced from even their place of exile. The irony is not lost on them. The specific grief of Esperanza refugees is not the grief of a destroyed city but something more complicated: the loss of the gateway, the loss of the city that was supposed to be the landing point for everyone who came after. Scorpio's rebirth clinics handle this kind of liminal grief — the loss not of a home but of a function, an identity, a purpose. *Effect: Esperanza refugees are overrepresented in Scorpio's rebirth intake. Their presence contributes to Scorpio developing specific protocols for what practitioners call "vocation loss" — the particular grief of losing not just a place but the role you played in it.*

### Sejong
*Korean T1, China/USA T2. King George Island, the most internationally diverse piece of Antarctic geography — virtually every nation had a station there simultaneously. Named after Sejong the Great, the Korean king who created Hangul to democratize literacy. Cosmopolitan by geography, Korean-primary by cultural character. Educational and research focused. The island's international heritage made Sejong residents comfortable navigating multiple cultural streams simultaneously.*

**1. Aquarius / The Labs**
Sejong's naming is the key to its district affinity. The man the city was named after was Tepenian civilization's closest analog to an Aquarius idealist — someone who identified a structural barrier to human flourishing (literacy gated by script complexity) and engineered a solution (a democratic alphabet). Sejong refugees carry this ethos: a belief that the right innovation can genuinely change who gets access to knowledge. The University of Concordia's student population has a visible Sejong cohort, and they're often among the most practically useful idealists — the ones who understand that vision requires engineering. *Effect: Sejong refugees bring a specific kind of interdisciplinary drive to Aquarius that bridges the gap between pure research and public application. They push back (gently, persistently) against the district's tendency to optimize for intellectual novelty over social impact.*

**2. Gemini / Janbogo**
King George Island was the most internationally diverse Antarctic location — multiple nations' stations operating simultaneously in close proximity, requiring residents to navigate genuinely different cultural assumptions every day. This produces a specific cognitive flexibility: the ability to switch between competing framings of the same events, to hold multiple versions of the truth simultaneously. This is Gemini's fundamental skill set. Sejong refugees aren't drawn to Gemini because they were Janbogo subnet (they weren't) but because they learned to live in a Gemini-like information environment before they ever arrived in Concordia. *Effect: Sejong refugees in Gemini become natural translators between the district's competing information channels — they're comfortable with the dual-network reality because they grew up in a multi-station reality.*

**3. Taurus**
Despite Sejong's cosmopolitan character, the Korean cultural primary created a deeply community-bonded residential layer underneath the international surface. Korean community bonds — the obligation to neighbors, the multigenerational household culture, the relationship between food and social cohesion — translate directly into Taurus. Sejong refugees who aren't drawn to the intellectual or information districts find Taurus's neighborhood warmth the closest available approximation to the community character of their home island. *Effect: Sejong refugees contribute a specifically Korean culinary tradition to Taurus's already rich preserved-food culture. The combination of Korean fermentation techniques with Taurus's own fermentation tradition produces something new that becomes a distinct neighborhood flavoring in whatever bloc Sejong refugees settle.*

### Marambio
*Argentine-founded military airstrip on Seymour Island. USA T1, South American T2. The primary air logistics hub for the Peninsula in the early Tepenian period. Military heritage creates an operational discipline culture alongside the South American warmth of the Argentine founding character. Residents are operationally focused, hierarchically comfortable, and accustomed to high-stakes decisions with minimal margin for error.*

**1. Capricorn**
Military airstrip culture is meritocratic in exactly the way Capricorn is: rank is earned through demonstrated performance in high-stakes conditions, hierarchy is respected because getting the hierarchy wrong kills people, and the guild-like structure of military specialization maps directly to Capricorn's apprenticeship and foundry culture. Marambio refugees who worked in aviation, logistics operations, or supply chain management find Capricorn's merit-board culture immediately legible. *Effect: Marambio refugees introduce a specifically military-operational quality to Capricorn's meritocracy — a focus on decision speed and hierarchical clarity that existing Capricorn culture, which tends toward methodical precision, sometimes lacks. Creates productive internal tension between Capricorn's planning instinct and Marambio's execution instinct.*

**2. Aries**
The airstrip is the geothermal plant's cultural cousin: both are places where keeping critical infrastructure running is existential, where the workers understand that failure means deaths, and where the culture has calcified around the specific honor of being the people who make the impossible work under pressure. Marambio's engineers and operational crews feel the Aries ethos in their bones — the "running hot" culture is just what they called "a normal shift." *Effect: Marambio refugees in Aries tend to rise into leadership faster than Aries-born workers because they bring a broader operational perspective. This is resented by some career Aries workers who feel their specific geothermal expertise is being supplanted by generalist military-operational thinking.*

**3. Taurus**
South American warmth — the Argentine asado culture, the late-night community meal, the fierce loyalty to family and neighborhood — is underneath the operational discipline like a substrate. When Marambio refugees are not at work, they're in Taurus, trying to recreate the neighborhood intimacy of a small island military community in the residential district of an Antarctic city. The warmth is not lost; it's just in a different room. *Effect: Marambio refugees create specifically Argentine social pockets in Taurus — informal neighborhood gathering traditions that other Taurus residents often find unexpectedly welcoming and then quietly adopt.*

### Juan Carlos I
*Spanish-founded, Livingston Island. USA T1, European T2. Spanish cultural character: vivacity, arts, food culture, social life as a form of philosophy, the tertulia tradition (the informal late-night intellectual gathering). Small island community but culturally expressive beyond its size. The Spanish temperament in Antarctic context produces warmth, intensity, and a specific kind of proud stubbornness about maintaining civilized life in barbaric conditions.*

**1. Leo**
Spanish cultural character maps to Leo more directly than any other Peninsula city. The tertulia — the Spanish tradition of gathering in cafés and public spaces for hours of informal argument, storytelling, and political commentary — is essentially Leo's Golden Ring promenade culture translated into a different idiom. The Spanish relationship with performance, honor, and expressive public life is Leo's native language. Juan Carlos I refugees find Leo's social hierarchy (known face → crowd-stopper → district legend) not alien but immediately legible — it's structurally identical to the tertulia's informal status hierarchy. *Effect: Juan Carlos I refugees bring a specifically Spanish performative tradition into Leo that complements rather than competes with existing cultural lineages. The tertulia format spreads beyond the refugee community and becomes adopted by some Leo residents as a recognized social institution — the late-night argument circle that anyone can attend and that generates both genuine intellectual exchange and reliable social drama.*

**2. Taurus**
Small island communities develop an intensity of neighborhood intimacy that larger cities cannot replicate, and the Spanish cultural emphasis on family and community bonds amplifies this. Juan Carlos I refugees in Taurus recreate the island community structure — the awareness of every neighbor's business, the door that is always open, the meal that is always being made — inside Concordia's residential district. *Effect: Juan Carlos I refugees are among the easiest Taurus arrivals to integrate. They already know how to be neighbors in the way Taurus requires. The integration tends to be warm and mutual, with existing Taurus families adopting incoming Juan Carlos I refugees into their social networks relatively quickly.*

**3. Pisces**
The Spanish relationship with boundaries — social, philosophical, economic — has always been fluid at the edges. The tertulia bleeds into the bordello; the café bleeds into the political meeting; the artistic and the criminal sit at the same table. This quality of boundary-dissolution in Spanish social life is not identical to Pisces' full dissolution philosophy, but it is adjacent enough that Juan Carlos I refugees who find Leo's hierarchies too rigid, or Taurus's expectations too settled, drift naturally toward Pisces' more permissive social structures. *Effect: Juan Carlos I refugees in Pisces introduce a specifically Spanish aesthetic sensibility — the relationship between beauty and melancholy, the tradition of the cantaor — that influences Pisces' existing syncretic cultural tradition.*

### Palmer City *(ruined)*
*USA T1. The first Tepenian city — where robot exiles first set foot in 2564. Research and tourism origin. Now ruins. Palmer City refugees carry the most acute founding-era identity of any displaced community in Tepenia: they are from the place where the story began. This creates a specific kind of grief that is not just personal loss but civilizational loss — the ruin of the city named after the founding is the ruin of something that was supposed to be permanent. The "first" city should outlast everything. It didn't.*

**1. Scorpio**
The founding place is ruins. The first Tepenian city — the one that was supposed to mean something permanent — is a shell. Palmer City refugees don't just grieve a home; they grieve a symbol, and the grief is complicated by the fact that nobody outside the Palmer subnet quite understands the full weight of what was lost. Scorpio is built for exactly this: transformation through descending into something too heavy to carry any other way. The Archive of Final Confessions holds Palmer City testimonies that are among the district's most politically significant records — what people said when they understood what the ruin of the first city meant. *Effect: Palmer City refugees have an outsized presence in Scorpio's Archive of Final Confessions community and exert significant influence on what counts as a testimony worth preserving. Some Scorpio practitioners believe Palmer City refugees unconsciously treat the entire Archive as an extension of their city's memorial function — a tension between personal grief processing and civilizational archiving that has produced interesting theoretical debates about the Archive's purpose.*

**2. Libra**
The loss of the founding city is a political fact, not just a personal one. Palmer City refugees who have the temperament for institutional engagement go to Libra to make that fact mean something: to argue for recognition, for legal status as displaced founding-city citizens, for whatever symbolic or material acknowledgment the bureaucracy is capable of producing. They are rarely satisfied with what Libra offers. The gap between what they believe the founding city's loss demands and what Libra's procedural apparatus can deliver is a permanent source of tension. *Effect: Palmer City refugees in Libra have become the city's most persistent petitioners on questions of historical recognition and founding-era rights. Libra's staff have developed a specific exhaustion around the Palmer City file — not because the arguments are unreasonable, but because the institutional tools for addressing civilizational symbolic grief simply don't exist.*

**3. Aquarius / The Labs**
Palmer Station was a significant research hub before it became a city. The scientific community from Palmer City — the researchers who built lives around the station's legacy — find Aquarius the most natural intellectual home. They bring with them the specific research traditions of the Peninsula's founding science, which predate the exile period and are among the oldest Antarctic scientific lineages in existence. *Effect: Palmer City researchers in Aquarius introduce what they call "deep time" methodology — the practice of grounding current research in the full historical record going back to the pre-exile era. This is received variably by Aquarius's project-based, future-oriented culture: some find it invaluable; others find it frustratingly conservative.*

### Rothera
*British-founded, USA T1, UK T2. Adelaide Island. One of the major Peninsula research stations. Methodical, precise, scientifically rigorous — a British empirical tradition in Antarctic clothes. The culture is understated to the point of being mistaken for coldness, but the community bonds, once formed, are very deep.*

**1. Aquarius / The Labs**
Rothera's primary identity is scientific research, and British empiricism is one of Aquarius's strongest cultural threads. Rothera refugees who worked in science, data analysis, atmospheric research, or the multidisciplinary teams that characterized Antarctic station life find Aquarius immediately legible. The methodological differences between Rothera's careful empiricism and Aquarius's more speculative research culture create productive friction: Rothera refugees become a useful conservative corrective to Aquarius's tendency to move fast and validate later. *Effect: Rothera refugees in Aquarius become the district's most reliable ethics review board members — not because they are more morally scrupulous but because they have the methodological patience to actually read the documentation that other researchers submit in summary form. This makes them valuable and, to a specific subset of Aquarius researchers, mildly infuriating.*

**2. Taurus**
British understatement and Taurus's domestic warmth are not opposites — they are different expressions of the same value, which is that home is the most important thing and the best way to defend it is not to make a fuss about it. Rothera refugees and established Taurus residents find each other mutually comprehensible in the way that two very quiet people sometimes do: by agreeing that the things worth saying have already been said by the choices you make about how to live. *Effect: Rothera refugees integrate into Taurus with unusual smoothness. Established Taurus families who adopt them occasionally remark that Rothera people seem to "already know how to be here" — which is not quite right, but what they're registering is cultural compatibility at the level of domestic values.*

**3. Virgo**
The people who maintained Rothera's complex Antarctic infrastructure — the heating systems, the research equipment, the supply chain across an island — are Virgo's natural constituents. They brought that same patient, meticulous relationship with systems to Concordia, and Virgo's culture of thorough documentation and careful maintenance feels to them not like a new discipline but like a proper acknowledgment of something they were already doing. *Effect: Rothera refugees in Virgo contribute a specific kind of institutional memory about Antarctic station infrastructure that predates most of Concordia's own construction records. Virgo's Deep Level surveyors occasionally consult Rothera elders when they encounter pre-Concordia infrastructure that matches patterns nobody current can explain.*

### Signy
*British-founded, South Orkney Islands. Very small island, very remote. USA T1 by census but dominated in daily reality by the small, intensely bonded community that extreme isolation produces. Refugees from Signy have the psychological profile of people who were accustomed to knowing every single person in their world by name, face, and history. Coming to Concordia is not just displacement — it is a fundamentally alien social experience.*

**1. Taurus**
The most intense small-community intimacy in the Palmer subnet was Signy's — because extreme isolation leaves no alternatives to knowing your neighbors completely. Taurus's culture of open doors, genealogical memory, and fierce neighborhood loyalty is the closest Concordia has to what Signy was. The scale difference is enormous, but the relational principle is the same: everyone's business is everyone's business, and that is not an intrusion but a form of care. *Effect: Signy refugees in Taurus tend to settle into very small sub-neighborhoods within the district and reconstruct Signy's social scale within Concordia's larger structure — creating micro-communities of 15–30 people who function, within the larger district, with the intimacy of an Antarctic outpost. These micro-communities become known for unusual mutual reliability and occasionally for unusual insularity.*

**2. Sagittarius / The Frostlands**
Signy's remoteness is not quite the same as Sagittarius's frontier character, but the psychological profile overlaps: both produce people who are fundamentally comfortable with the fact that help is not coming quickly, who have internalized self-sufficiency as a value rather than a necessity. Signy refugees who find Taurus's density overwhelming — who need space around them that feels like the kind of space they grew up in — go to the Frostlands, where the outer edges of Concordia still feel something like being at the edge of the world. *Effect: Signy refugees in Sagittarius often become skilled frontier guides because they bring a specific kind of environmental attentiveness — trained by years of small-island weather reading and coastal hazard awareness — that the primarily ice-shelf trained Sagittarius scouts find complementary.*

**3. Cancer**
The adjustment from Signy to any large settlement is significant, and the psychological toll of that adjustment — from a world where you knew everyone to a world where you know no one — is exactly the kind of quiet crisis Cancer is built to address. Many Signy refugees pass through Cancer not as permanent residents but as a necessary intermediate step: the district that holds them while they adjust, that doesn't require them to be okay yet, that gives them the warmth to find their footing before they move on to Taurus or Sagittarius. *Effect: Cancer's intake workers have developed specific protocols for what they call "scale trauma" — the disorientation of arriving in a large city from a very small community. Signy refugees are cited as the case population that first made this a recognized intake category.*

### Port Lockroy
*British-founded, Goudier Island — the smallest city in all of Tepenia. Real-world Port Lockroy was a heritage museum site before its development into a Tepenian city. The city retained this identity: it was effectively a living archive, a city built around the preservation of what came before. Its tiny population (~65,000 humans in the Before census, ~54,000 After) was intensely community-bonded and defined by the shared project of keeping something old alive in a new world.*

**1. Scorpio**
Port Lockroy was a city whose purpose was memory. Not the dramatic, transformative memory of Scorpio's rebirth protocols, but something quieter and in its way more stubborn: the keeping of a specific record, the maintenance of a specific object, the preservation of a specific way of being. The Archive of Final Confessions is the largest-scale version of what Port Lockroy residents did professionally and culturally their entire lives. In Scorpio, they find the philosophical framework for something they were already practicing. *Effect: Port Lockroy refugees become some of Scorpio's most skilled archivists — but they push back against the district's more dramatic therapeutic traditions. They argue, sometimes publicly, that preservation and transformation are not the same value, and that Scorpio's rebirth culture occasionally destroys things in the name of freeing people from them that would have been better preserved. This is a genuine and ongoing philosophical tension within Scorpio's practitioners.*

**2. Taurus**
A city of 65,000 people operates at neighborhood scale by definition — everyone in Port Lockroy knew everyone, and the community bonds were as intense as Signy's without quite the geographic extremity. Taurus's multigenerational genealogy culture — the specific chairs at dinner tables, the preserved routines, the heirloom objects — resonates deeply with Port Lockroy refugees who built an entire city around the philosophical position that old things deserve to be maintained. *Effect: Port Lockroy refugees in Taurus become the district's most respected keepers of the bonded pair genealogies — partly because they bring professional archival skills, partly because they understand instinctively why these records matter.*

**3. Aquarius / The Labs**
The heritage science that Port Lockroy preserved — early Tepenian-era research methodologies, pre-exile era documentation, the physical and digital records of Antarctic history before the exile period began — is exactly the "deep time" data that Aquarius occasionally needs and rarely has reliable access to. Port Lockroy researchers arrive in Aquarius as keepers of primary sources that the district's project-oriented culture had largely stopped bothering to consult. *Effect: Port Lockroy refugees in Aquarius become the curators of what eventually becomes a formal "Heritage Science" subdiscipline within The Labs — a methodological tradition that grounds current research in the full pre-exile record. This is either Aquarius's most important archive or its most elaborate excuse for conservatism, depending on who you ask.*

## Halley Subnet — Queen Maud Land

### Halley
*British-founded, USA T1, UK T2. The largest Halley subnet city. Historically famous for the ozone layer discovery. Situated on a floating ice shelf — a city that residents always knew could move, calve, or become structurally untenable. This produces a specific philosophical relationship with impermanence: Halley residents do not take permanence for granted because the ground they live on actively demonstrates that nothing is.*

**1. Aquarius / The Labs**
Halley's scientific legacy is genuine and specific: the ozone layer hole was discovered at Halley Research Station, which is one of the foundational data points in Earth's environmental history. Refugees who worked in Halley's research tradition arrive in Aquarius as bearers of one of Tepenia's most prestigious scientific lineages. The Aquarius culture of visionary research resonates with people who came from a city whose research changed how the world understood itself. *Effect: Halley researchers in Aquarius are among the most credentialed scientific immigrants in Concordia's history. They tend to occupy the most prestigious research positions relatively quickly, which generates some resentment from Aquarius-born scientists who feel their district's native talent is being overvalued by association with a famous historical station.*

**2. Sagittarius / The Frostlands**
The floating ice shelf creates the Sagittarius affinity: residents who knew their city could literally move — that the shelf could calve, that the city had been relocated before and could be again — developed an instinctive relationship with impermanence that matches Sagittarius's frontier philosophy. The Frostlands is a district of people who understand that the ground is not guaranteed. Halley refugees recognize this immediately. *Effect: Halley refugees in Sagittarius bring a specifically ice-shelf-competency skill set — understanding the behavior of floating ice structures, reading ice stress patterns, knowing when a structure is approaching instability — that is directly applicable to Concordia's own ice shelf proximate infrastructure. They become the district's experts on shelf-adjacent expansion planning.*

**3. Scorpio**
Living on impermanent ground, knowing that the city could be relocated at any moment, creates a specific relationship with the present tense. Halley's philosophical culture — understated British intellectualism combined with the existential backdrop of a floating foundation — produces exactly the kind of people who are drawn to Scorpio's confrontation-with-impermanence traditions. The British literary tendency toward melancholy and the Antarctic shelf's concrete demonstration of entropy combine into something Scorpio can work with. *Effect: Halley refugees in Scorpio contribute an unusual philosophical tradition: they approach transformation not as dramatic rupture but as incremental displacement — the ice shelf model, where everything moves slowly and the danger is in not noticing until it's too late. This inflects Scorpio's rebirth practice with a more gradual, patient methodology that some practitioners find more effective for certain patient profiles.*

### Neumayer
*German-founded, Germany/USA co-T1. One of the largest Halley subnet cities. German engineering character: systematic, precise, thorough, methodical. The Neumayer tradition values excellence demonstrated through process rather than inspiration. The apprenticeship culture (directly parallel to Germany's real Ausbildung system) created generations of specialists with deep mastery in narrow domains.*

**1. Capricorn**
The German Ausbildung tradition — structured apprenticeship culminating in recognized mastery, with career identity formed around a specific craft or discipline — is Capricorn's guild culture under a different name. Neumayer refugees find Capricorn's merit-board hierarchy not oppressive but legible and reasonable: it's a system where the rules are clear, the standards are defined, and advancement is genuinely connected to demonstrated competence. They are among Capricorn's most productive guild recruits and often among its most politically conservative, because they believe the system works when it's run correctly. *Effect: Neumayer refugees in Capricorn become the district's most vocal critics of the merit board's undervaluation of robot contributions — not because they are robot rights advocates in the conventional sense, but because their engineering precision makes them intolerant of any measurement system that produces systematically incorrect outputs. They are the unexpected allies of Capricorn's underground robot rights movement precisely because they come at the issue through methodology rather than politics.*

**2. Aquarius / The Labs**
German scientific tradition is one of the world's most distinguished — structured, rigorous, peer-reviewed in the most traditional sense. Neumayer's research community arrives in Aquarius with an instinctive push back against the district's tendency to route around ethical review boards: they want the protocols followed because they believe the protocols exist for good reasons that were discovered through historical failures. They are not conservatives; they are empiricists who have learned from the record of what happens when rigor is abandoned. *Effect: Neumayer researchers in Aquarius push for what they call "Protokoll culture" — a systematic approach to research documentation and review that is significantly more rigorous than what Aquarius currently practices. The district resists this as bureaucratic constraint; the Neumayer community argues it is basic scientific hygiene. The tension is ongoing and productive.*

**3. Virgo**
The people who maintained Neumayer's complex systems — the station that had to keep working in one of the most extreme Antarctic environments — are infrastructure specialists of the highest order. Virgo recognizes them immediately as its own kind: people who understand that meticulous documentation and systematic maintenance are not overhead but the entire point. Neumayer's engineers bring a specifically German precision to Virgo's already thorough documentation culture — their maintenance logs are models of clarity that become training materials for new Virgo recruits. *Effect: Neumayer refugees in Virgo contribute what becomes known as the "Neumayer Standard" for infrastructure documentation — a format so clear and thorough that Virgo eventually adopts modified versions of it as their district-wide reporting standard. The Neumayer community takes quiet, understated satisfaction in this outcome.*

### Belgrano *(ruined)*
*Argentine-founded, USA T1, South American T2. One of the major QML cities. Now ruins. Salagéa Aparast — the boat-dwelling datashard archivist — is a Belgrano native whose entire life project after the war was the preservation of Belgrano's civilizational knowledge. Naizelle d'Edjordoś also came from Belgrano. The city's destruction left refugees carrying both the grief of a South American community warmth now gone, and a specific mission: the knowledge of what Belgrano was must not die with it.*

**1. Scorpio**
Belgrano's destruction created exactly the kind of testimony that the Archive of Final Confessions was built for. The refugee community's defining orientation is preservation — of records, of testimony, of the memory of what the city was — and Scorpio's archive culture provides the infrastructure for this mission. Salagéa Aparast's datashard courier work and the Archive's reception functions are in direct conversation: she collects testimony from the Atlantic coast; Scorpio houses what she brings back. *Effect: Belgrano refugees are the single largest external contributor to the Archive of Final Confessions. Their testimonies constitute an entire wing of the Archive's collection. Scorpio's relationship with the Belgrano community is simultaneously the district's most politically significant (because Belgrano's records include testimony about events that Libra's official history disputes) and its most intimate (because the archive workers and the refugees have spent years in each other's company).*

**2. Pisces / The Markets**
South American commercial warmth and the gray economy instincts that come from rebuilding a community after catastrophic loss go directly to Pisces. The portion of the Belgrano community that is not engaged in the archival mission is rebuilding an economic life, and Pisces is where economic life rebuilds without requiring official registration, formal credentials, or a history that institutions can verify. Belgrano refugees who arrived with nothing except what they carried found Pisces the only district that didn't require them to prove who they had been before they could begin being someone now. *Effect: Belgrano refugees bring a specific South American street-market tradition into Pisces that complements the district's existing economy. The informal food culture, the social market tradition, the particular Argentine relationship between commerce and community event — these become a visible cultural presence in Pisces that other districts occasionally experience when they cross the border into the market district.*

**3. Taurus**
What Belgrano refugees want more than anything — after the archival mission is handled, after the economic survival is secured — is to be neighbors again. The Argentine community warmth, the asado, the shared meal as the basic unit of social maintenance, the expectation that your household is never really separate from the households around it: these are Taurus values expressed in South American idiom, and Taurus is where they find their most complete expression in Concordia. *Effect: Belgrano refugees in Taurus create a specifically Argentine flavor in the neighborhoods they settle, with cultural practices around communal cooking and shared outdoor space (adapted to Antarctic interior conditions, which produces some creative architectural solutions) that gradually influence the surrounding Taurus community beyond the original refugee bloc.*

### Troll
*Norwegian-founded, USA T1. Interior QML city, sitting at approximately 0° longitude — the Greenwich meridian. Norwegian character: understated, egalitarian, outdoor/nature-oriented, quiet competence, the cultural tradition of not making a fuss while doing things that are actually very difficult. The inland Antarctic position created residents who had an even more extreme relationship with isolation than coastal cities.*

**1. Sagittarius / The Frostlands**
Norwegian culture in Antarctic context produces the Sagittarius archetype in its purest form: people who are genuinely comfortable with the cold as a concrete physical fact rather than an abstraction, who have internalized the frontier ethic of the Amundsen heritage without having to perform it, who regard self-sufficiency not as a virtue to be demonstrated but as a basic operating assumption. Troll's inland position made its residents the most interior-adapted population in the QML subnet. In the Frostlands, they are among the most respected immigrants for the simple reason that they don't have to adjust — they already are what Sagittarius values. *Effect: Troll refugees become natural expedition leaders in Sagittarius at a rate disproportionate to their numbers. The explorer guild absorbs them readily and often promotes them quickly, which occasionally creates friction with Sagittarius-born scouts who find the ease of Troll integration slightly suspicious — they're too good, and competence that seems easy is sometimes treated as evidence of luck rather than skill.*

**2. Virgo / The Undergrid**
The Norwegian tradition of practical engineering without drama — the jantelov principle applied to infrastructure, where excellence is expected and drawing attention to it is considered poor form — maps precisely to Virgo's culture of quiet, meticulous maintenance. Troll's inland position meant that nothing the city needed could be taken for granted; everything had to be maintained, and maintaining it was just what you did, not something you celebrated. Virgo understands this immediately. *Effect: Troll refugees in Virgo are notably uncomfortable with the "We Keep You Alive" motivational poster culture — not because they disagree with the sentiment but because calling attention to the fact that you're keeping people alive feels, to them, slightly inappropriate. They are nevertheless some of Virgo's most reliable long-term employees, because they will simply keep working at the same quality level indefinitely without requiring recognition.*

**3. Taurus**

### Zukelli *(destroyed)*
*China/USA co-T1, Janbogo subnet, Terra Nova Bay. Pisces operators who learn to work within this network gain access to commercial channels that run through Concordia's Zhongshan community and extend, via former Mawson subnet connections, into a much wider Tepenian network.*

**3. Taurus**
The Chinese multigenerational household culture — the specifically Chinese version of the "family as the fundamental social unit" principle — maps to Taurus's genealogical community bonds. The hutong tradition (intimate neighborhood community built around shared courtyard space) is a variant of Taurus's open-door culture. Zhongshan refugees in Taurus create specifically Chinese neighborhood communities that maintain their own communal space traditions within the larger district structure. *Effect: Zhongshan refugees in Taurus become known for the communal courtyard culture they bring — the shared outdoor (dome) space organized around communal use rather than individual allocation. Taurus's existing culture adapts to accommodate this and eventually some of its practices spread beyond the Zhongshan community.*

### Sayowa
*Mawson subnet, East Ongul Island. Small population (~130,000 humans Before), outsized identity. The "gateway between the European Atlantic corridor and the East Asian Pacific corridor" — the only dual-approach Mawson subnet city. Sayowa residents lived at the intersection of two different cultural worlds simultaneously. Small population, dual approach, crossroads identity: this city was defined by being between things rather than being a thing.*

**1. Hub**
The Hub is the only district in Concordia whose identity is explicitly built around being between things. Sayowa's entire civilizational character was the crossroads — the place where Atlantic and Pacific cultural flows met without either dominating. The Hub's "principled rootlessness" and the Sayowa tradition of navigating dual cultural currents are the same disposition expressed in different contexts. Sayowa refugees in the Hub don't feel homeless — they feel, for the first time, in a place that was designed for people like them. *Effect: Sayowa refugees become among the Hub's most articulate advocates for what Hub-born residents call "the dignity of the crossroads" — the position that belonging to a transit point is not a lesser form of belonging but its own thing. They bring a theoretical framework for crossroads identity that the Hub had intuited but never fully articulated.*

**2. Pisces / The Markets**
Every gateway city generates a gray economy. Sayowa was small, which means its gray economy was proportionally enormous — in a small city serving as the meeting point between two major cultural corridors, the informal trade in information, goods, and access is economically significant. Pisces absorbs Sayowa's market tradition along with its refugee community, gaining a specifically dual-corridor commercial perspective. *Effect: Sayowa refugees in Pisces become particularly valuable as inter-network brokers — people who understand how the Atlantic-facing QML commercial networks and the Pacific-facing Ross Sea/Mawson commercial networks interface. This specific knowledge is valuable enough that Pisces treats Sayowa refugees as a strategic community.*

**3. Gemini / Janbogo**
The dual-network character of Gemini resonates with Sayowa's dual-approach identity. Sayowa residents who navigated two cultural corridors simultaneously understand Gemini's "everything has a public version and a real version" operating principle from a different angle: not as deception but as the natural consequence of being the meeting point between two systems that don't fully translate into each other. *Effect: Sayowa refugees in Gemini become inter-channel translators of a specifically cultural rather than political kind — people who understand not just that the two networks exist but how the assumptions of each network differently inflect the same information.*

### Soyuz *(destroyed)*
*Russian-founded, China T1, Russian T2. Mawson subnet. Russian cultural character: the literary tradition, the philosophical depth, the specific Russian relationship with collective memory and national identity, the dusha (soul) tradition — the belief that suffering is not incidental to meaning but its source. Now destroyed. The loss of Soyuz removed the most distinctly Russian-character voice from the Indian Ocean subnet.*

**1. Scorpio**
Russian literary culture has always lived in proximity to the Archive of Final Confessions — the Russian tradition of the final testament, the death-bed confession, the last letter, is one of the deepest literary traditions in Earth's history. Soyuz refugees arrive in Scorpio already culturally fluent in the mode of discourse the Archive requires. The dusha tradition — suffering as the medium through which meaning becomes accessible — is not identical to Scorpio's rebirth methodology, but it speaks to the same territory. *Effect: Soyuz refugees are among Scorpio's most philosophically sophisticated community members. They push back, consistently and with considerable eloquence, against what they see as the district's clinical tendency to treat grief as a problem to be solved rather than a condition to be inhabited. This tension is productive for Scorpio's self-understanding — the Soyuz community forces the district to articulate why transformation should be the goal, rather than taking it for granted.*

**2. Taurus**
Russian communal living tradition — the collective household, the apartment building as social unit, the shared kitchen as community center — maps to Taurus's neighborhood culture. The specific Russian relationship between food and communal identity (the communal meal as political act, as cultural preservation, as the thing you do when words fail) resonates with Taurus's food culture in ways that produce real warmth between communities. *Effect: Soyuz refugees in Taurus introduce a specifically Russian culinary tradition — fermentation, preservation, the specific soups and breads of cold-weather communal cooking adapted to Tepenian ingredients — that becomes one of Taurus's recognized cultural flavors. The tradition is maintained by the community as an explicit preservation act: cooking what Soyuz cooked is how they keep the city alive.*

**3. Leo**
Russian arts tradition — the literary and theatrical history that produced Chekhov and Dostoevsky and Tchaikovsky — is one of the great global arts traditions, and Leo is where arts traditions find their context in Concordia. Soyuz refugees who were in the arts arrive in Leo as bearers of one of the most serious artistic traditions in human history. The Russian relationship between tragedy and performance — the idea that the most important art deals with suffering without resolving it — creates friction with Leo's dominant "morale-as-infrastructure" orientation but also enriches it. *Effect: Soyuz refugees in Leo contribute a specifically tragic aesthetic dimension that Leo's culture, oriented around warmth and celebration, had largely been missing. The Soyuz community becomes associated with the intimate performance tradition — small venues, serious themes, audiences who arrive expecting to be changed rather than entertained — that fills a need Leo didn't know it had.*

## Mirny Subnet — Wilkes Land

### Mirny
*Russian-founded, China T1, Russian T2. One of the oldest continuously-occupied Soviet/Russian Antarctic stations — never abandoned for a single year in its first century of operation. The defining character of Mirny is continuity: the city that never left. In a continent where abandonment and relocation were common, Mirny's unbroken occupation is its entire identity.*

**1. Virgo / The Undergrid**
Mirny's century of continuous occupation required an infrastructure maintenance tradition of the highest order. The people who kept Mirny running through Soviet collapse, post-Soviet chaos, and everything that came after developed a relationship with infrastructure maintenance that is practically indistinguishable from religion. Virgo recognizes Mirny refugees as its deepest kin: people who understood, at the level of lived experience, that "We Keep You Alive" is not a motivational slogan but a statement of ontological fact. *Effect: Mirny refugees in Virgo become the district's most profound institutional memory on the subject of continuous-operation infrastructure. They know how to keep things running not for a crisis period but for a century — a fundamentally different knowledge from Virgo's existing culture, which tends to think in shorter timeframes. Their presence pushes Virgo's planning horizon significantly outward.*

**2. Taurus**
The Russian multigenerational household culture and the Mirny tradition of multigenerational occupation create a perfect match with Taurus's genealogical community bonds. Mirny refugees in Taurus maintain family histories that include the station's full operational record — the births, deaths, maintenance crises, political transitions, and social transformations of a century of unbroken occupation — as living community memory. *Effect: Mirny refugees in Taurus become the district's most elaborate genealogists — their family records extend not just through human-robot bond histories but through the full operational history of the city. Taurus's bonded-pair registry project finds Mirny's records invaluable and begins a collaborative archival effort with the Mirny community.*

**3. Scorpio**
The philosophical weight of being the city that never left — of having chosen continuity as an absolute value — creates a specific kind of community consciousness that Scorpio's depth orientation addresses. Mirny refugees carry the accumulated memory of a century of unbroken occupation: every crisis survived, every near-abandonment narrowly averted, every transition that could have ended the occupation but didn't. This is not simple grief but something more complex: the existential weight of being the inheritors of a century of collective decision to stay. *Effect: Mirny refugees in Scorpio contribute what becomes recognized as a unique therapeutic category: the burden of continuity. Unlike grief over loss, the Mirny tradition involves grief over the weight of what was preserved — all the cost, all the sacrifice, all the choosing-to-stay that went into a century of occupation, now displaced into a city that is not the place where all that staying happened. Scorpio develops new protocols to address this.*

### Casey *(destroyed)*
*Australian-founded, China T1, Australia T2. Mirny subnet. Destroyed in the Long Night War — one of the events that blocks Pink Lucy's Route B and that will be developed in DLC 7. Australian character, but the destruction creates the additional displacement layer.*

**1. Scorpio**
Destroyed city, Australian character. The grief is acute and the Archive of Final Confessions receives the testimony. Casey's destruction during the Long Night War, occurring in a subnet that was less immediately targeted than the Ross Sea or Peninsula, adds a specific quality of unexpected loss — Casey refugees did not necessarily believe their city was a primary target, which makes the destruction feel additionally senseless. *Effect: Casey's representation in the Archive of Final Confessions includes a significant body of testimonies organized around the theme of surprise — "we didn't think it would be us" — that Scorpio has found useful in developing protocols for what practitioners call "unchosen target grief," the specific trauma of being destroyed without the dignity of having been considered significant enough to be targeted deliberately.*

**2. Taurus**
Australian community character, as with Mawson and Davis, expressed now through the lens of displacement. Taurus offers the neighborhood bonds that Casey refugees lost, and Australian community culture — the willingness to show up without being asked, the meal as social infrastructure — integrates smoothly into Taurus's existing warmth. *Effect: Casey refugees in Taurus bring the Australian mateship tradition with an additional layer of intensity that comes from having lost everything. Their community bonds with both the Mawson and Davis refugee communities (all Australian-founded Mawson subnet cities) create a specifically Australian-Antarctic enclave within Taurus that is one of the more cohesive immigrant communities in the district.*

**3. Sagittarius / The Frostlands**
Australian frontier mentality in an aftermath context — the "she'll be right" philosophy tested against the fact that things were, specifically, not right — produces a portion of every Australian-character refugee community that heads to the edge. The Frostlands offers space and self-reliance and the opportunity to prove something again. Casey refugees in Sagittarius are testing whether the frontier still holds: whether the character that Casey built still works when the city doesn't exist anymore. *Effect: Casey refugees in Sagittarius become associated with a specific philosophical position within the frontier district: that the frontier is not a place to escape loss but a place to test whether who you are survived what happened to you. This framing shapes a generation of Sagittarius scouts who come from the Casey community.*

## Byrd Subnet — Ross Ice Shelf

### Framheim
*Norwegian/Amundsen heritage. Named after Amundsen's Antarctic camp — "Home of the Fram." Byrd subnet, Ross Ice Shelf. Rebuilt by expedition from Byrd using historical records. This city was not found; it was reconstructed. Its residents are the inheritors of a deliberate act of civilizational memory: the decision to rebuild something that had been lost, from archives, in the place where it was, because it deserved to exist again.*

**1. Sagittarius / The Frostlands**
People who chose to live in a rebuilt city on the interior Ross Ice Shelf chose the most interior, most frontier, most deliberately removed from everything option available in Tepenia. Framheim's residents were not people who happened to be there — they chose it, which means they chose the ice shelf, the interior, the furthest-from-the-coast option specifically. In Concordia, they go to the Frostlands for the same reason: because they know who they are, and who they are is frontier. *Effect: Framheim refugees in Sagittarius bring the Norwegian frontier tradition in its most distilled form — not the Norwegian coastal tradition but the Amundsen inland-ice tradition, the tradition of the person who went to the most difficult place not because they had to but because going there was the point. This inflects Sagittarius's culture with a specifically aspirational quality: not "we go to the frontier because we must" but "we go to the frontier because it is what we're for."*

**2. Scorpio**
A city rebuilt from archives has a profound relationship with memory as active rather than passive. Framheim residents didn't inherit their city's history — they reconstructed it. They built the relationship between archive and place from scratch. This is exactly Scorpio's territory: the work of making memory do something, of taking what was lost and producing something real from it. Framheim refugees arrive in Scorpio already practicing the district's deepest values. *Effect: Framheim refugees in Scorpio contribute a specifically constructive approach to the Archive of Final Confessions. Where most contributors focus on preservation, Framheim refugees push for active use: the archive should not just hold testimonies but use them to reconstruct what they describe. This is a significant philosophical intervention in how the Archive understands its own mission.*

**3. Taurus**
A community built from historical records had to create its own genealogy from scratch — had to build the bonded-pair lineages, the family histories, the community traditions that other cities inherited. The result is a Taurus-compatible culture of intense genealogical investment, but with an additional layer: they know their genealogy is partly constructed, partly real, and partly the boundary between those two things which they've never fully resolved. *Effect: Framheim refugees in Taurus become the district's most philosophically sophisticated genealogists — not because they have the deepest records but because they have thought most carefully about what records mean and what they cannot do. They bring a useful epistemic humility to Taurus's sometimes uncritical reverence for documented lineage.*

### Little America
*American heritage. Byrd subnet, Ross Ice Shelf. Rebuilt by expedition from Byrd using historical records, parallel to Framheim. USA T1 by character. American founding tradition in its most interior, most self-reliant form — the people who went to the deepest interior and built something.*

**1. Sagittarius / The Frostlands**
American frontier tradition in Antarctic interior form. The people who went to the Ross Ice Shelf's deepest reaches and chose to build a city there from historical records are the frontier tradition at its most extreme. In Concordia, they go to the Frostlands because the frontier is where they've always been. *Effect: Little America refugees in Sagittarius bring an American optimism about the frontier that is distinct from the Norwegian-introspective Framheim tradition — where Framheim refugees ask what the frontier means, Little America refugees push into it because it's there. The two traditions exist in productive tension within Sagittarius's explorer culture.*

**2. Aries**
American "can-do" culture under extreme conditions — the engineering confidence that says the problem is solvable if you throw enough ingenuity at it — resonates with Aries' warrior-engineer ethos. Little America's ice-shelf location meant its residents had to make things work far from any support; Aries' culture was built around making things work when the systems are failing. The shared operating assumption that failure is not an option creates mutual recognition. *Effect: Little America refugees in Aries contribute an American improvisational engineering tradition that complements Aries' existing culture of precise technical heroism. The combination produces a more adaptable Aries than existed before — one that can both execute with precision and improvise under constraint.*

**3. Taurus**
American community culture in its frontier expression — the neighbor you trust your life to, the community that shows up, the culture of "we're all in this together" born from circumstances where that was literally true — maps to Taurus's neighborhood solidarity. Little America refugees in Taurus bring a frontier-community warmth that is specifically American in its direct expressiveness: they tell you they're glad to see you, they mean it, and they don't treat that kind of directness as social performance. *Effect: Little America refugees in Taurus push back, gently but persistently, against the district's culture of implicit communication — the sense that showing warmth explicitly is somehow less authentic than showing it through action. This small cultural friction produces a Taurus neighborhood culture that is slightly more verbally expressive in the blocks where Little America refugees have settled.*

## Cross-Cutting Notes

**Districts that absorb the most refugees across all cities:**
- **Taurus** receives the most consistent cross-city settlement, appearing in the top 3 for nearly every city with a community-bonded character. It is Concordia's universal residential anchor.
- **Scorpio** receives the most concentrated representation from destroyed and ruined cities. The five destroyed/ruined cities (Zukelli, Soyuz, Belgrano, Palmer City, Casey) all list Scorpio as a top-2 district. The Archive of Final Confessions is bearing the weight of multiple civilizations' loss simultaneously.
- **Aquarius** draws scientific community from almost every city, regardless of cultural primary. The scientific character of Antarctic settlement — every city was built on a research station — creates a pan-Tepenian scientific community that converges on The Labs.

**The Scorpio burden:**
Five city communities — each carrying the acute grief of a city destroyed or ruined during the Long Night War — have identified Scorpio as their primary landing district. The Archive of Final Confessions is receiving testimony from populations that collectively represent millions of people's worth of catastrophic loss. This is a significant burden that the district may not be adequately resourced to carry, and the political tension between Scorpio (which wants more resources to handle this mission) and Libra (which sets resource allocations and prefers "productive grief timelines") is likely to be one of Concordia's most persistent inter-district conflicts.

**Gemini's complexity:**
The Janbogo enclave district hosts both Janbogo city survivors and Zukelli survivors simultaneously — the city that survived and the city that burned, watching each other from 10km across the water now watching each other across a district. This is the most loaded single-district refugee dynamic in Concordia.

**The Hub's underrepresentation:**
Only Sayowa, among the 32 cities, lists the Hub as a primary district. This is accurate to the Hub's self-understanding — it is a place of crossroads identity, and most cities produced people with strong place-identities rather than crossroads identities. Sayowa is the exception precisely because its entire civilizational character was the intersection. Other cities produce Hub residents as a byproduct (people who find they don't fit their home community in Concordia and drift to the neutral crossroads), but only Sayowa produces them systematically.
