## CANON NOTE

No Indians or people from the South Asian subcontinent (including Pakistan, Bangladesh, or related nations) ever came to Antarctica or settled in Tepenia. This is established world canon. Therefore:

- The Bharati Station infrastructure was present but **completely unoccupied** when the exile era began in 2564
- Whatever city exists at this location was founded entirely by a **non-Indian, non-South-Asian** exile population
- The city's name, culture, and identity have **nothing to do with India or South Asia**
- The founding population is **TBD** — needs to be decided
- The real-world station name "Bharati" (Sanskrit: goddess of learning, also a name for India) **does not carry forward** into Tepenia in any form

See also: Maitri_TBD.md (Halley subnet) for the same situation with the other Indian Antarctic station.

## Geographic Basis

The Bharati Station site sits in the Larsemann Hills, approximately 1-2km from the Progress Station site (Soyuz). The Hills are a small ice-free oasis: exposed bedrock, meltwater lakes, Prydz Bay maritime access. The physical infrastructure the Bharati Station left behind — built to Indian specifications in the 2010s, one of the newer pre-exile station designs — was ready to be occupied when whoever eventually settled here arrived.
