# Rothera

**Based on:** Rothera Research Station (UK / British Antarctic Survey), Rothera Point, Adelaide Island, Marguerite Bay, Antarctic Peninsula (~67°34'S, 68°07'W)
**Status:** Destroyed
**Arcanet Subnet:** Palmer ("American")
**Highway access:** TBD — Peninsula road network connecting north toward Palmer City and south toward Marguerite Bay coastal area; specifics not yet established
**Significance:** British Tepenian city on Adelaide Island; one of the largest BAS installations in Antarctic history; one of the few Tepenian cities with confirmed aviation infrastructure (a paved runway, in operation from the real-world BAS era); island-positioned city on the west coast of the Peninsula; part of the Palmer subnet, which was the Long Night War's first major target
**DLC:** Palmer subnet — DLC 3 (Antarctic Peninsula); Rothera is one of the destroyed cities that forms the ruin landscape of the Peninsula DLC

## Geographic Basis

Rothera sits on Rothera Point, a headland on the eastern shore of Adelaide Island — a large island (approximately 120km long) on the west coast of the Antarctic Peninsula, separated from the mainland by the Gullet. Marguerite Bay lies to the south; the Lemaire Channel, one of the most dramatic maritime passages in Antarctica, lies to the north. The position is coastal, maritime, and sheltered relative to the open Drake Passage — which is why the BAS chose it.

The Antarctic Peninsula is the mildest and most accessible part of Antarctica. It reaches toward South America, influenced by the relatively warmer maritime air of the Drake Passage and the Bellingshausen Sea. Rothera's climate is Antarctic in absolute terms but moderate by Tepenian standards — mean annual temperatures around −9°C, summer temperatures regularly above −5°C, significant precipitation including rain in the warmest months. No other major Tepenian city outside the Palmer subnet cities experiences weather this mild.

Adelaide Island's size gave Rothera more room to grow than stations on smaller islands. The island itself is mountainous — the Antarctic Peninsula's spine extends through it, with peaks rising to over 2,500m — and the city developed in the coastal lowlands around Rothera Point.

Rothera was one of the few Antarctic stations with a paved runway. The Bonner Laboratory airstrip — approximately 900 meters of paved surface — gave Rothera an aviation capability that most Tepenian cities lacked. In the exile era, this infrastructure made Rothera a secondary aviation hub for the Palmer subnet, distinct from the Byrd aviation network but relevant to the Peninsula's internal connectivity.

#### Notable Weather Phenomena

- **Maritime variability:** Rothera's weather is highly changeable — the Drake Passage drives rapid weather system movement through the Peninsula; conditions can shift from clear to whiteout within hours; this is the defining operational challenge for any aviation activity here
- **Rain:** one of the few Tepenian cities where actual liquid precipitation falls with any regularity; rain on city infrastructure has different implications than snow — drainage, surface ice formation, material weathering
- **Sea ice seasonality:** Marguerite Bay freezes in winter and breaks up in summer; maritime access is seasonal but more reliable than the more southerly Ross Sea cities due to the milder climate
- **Lemaire Channel conditions:** the channel to the north is dramatic and navigable for most of the year; currents, ice, and the narrow passage between high cliffs create specific maritime navigation conditions

#### Gameplay Notes

- **Mildest Peninsula climate:** in the context of the Palmer subnet DLC, Rothera's ruins are among the most accessible — the weather is the most forgiving of any southern Tepenian location; the hazards here are structural (ruins) rather than primarily environmental
- **The runway:** the paved airstrip in the ruins is a distinctive landscape feature; whether it's still usable, what aircraft (if any) are on it, and what the airfield infrastructure looks like in ruin are DLC design questions
- **Maritime approach:** the DLC 3 player may arrive at Rothera by sea; the Marguerite Bay approach, with Adelaide Island's mountains as a backdrop, is visually striking
- **Hardcore Mode influences:**
  - Rain and mixed precipitation: ice formation on surfaces after rain events; wet conditions affecting equipment differently than pure snow/cold environments
  - Maritime supply window: the relatively mild climate means longer maritime access than most southern cities; supply by sea is the most viable option for any Hardcore Mode resupply at Rothera

## Founding

**Settled:** Post-Falkland Treaty, on the Rothera Research Station infrastructure. The BAS had operated at Rothera since 1975 — nearly six centuries of continuous British Antarctic presence before the exile era. The exile settlement inherited one of the most developed and well-maintained station sites on the Peninsula.

**Founding population:** Primarily British exiles. Both Rothera and Halley have British Antarctic Survey origins, giving the British exile community a presence on two coasts — the Peninsula's west side and the Atlantic's Queen Maud Land coast. The cultural connection between Rothera and Halley, despite their geographic distance, would have been real.

The paved runway — inherited from the BAS era — gave Rothera a practical distinction from other Peninsula cities. Aviation infrastructure in Tepenia is rare; Rothera had it from the beginning.

## Character & Culture

Rothera had the character of a city that knew it was well-positioned. The maritime climate — mild, variable, demanding but not extreme — meant that daily life here was less relentlessly hostile than in the interior cities or even many coastal cities further south. The BAS institutional character, carried by the founding population, gave Rothera a specific pragmatic competence: decades upon decades of accumulated knowledge about how to operate in this environment, how to maintain infrastructure in changeable maritime conditions, how to keep a paved runway functional in Antarctic weather.

The aviation infrastructure gave Rothera a connectivity that most Peninsula cities lacked. People and goods could move through Rothera in ways that reinforced its role as a node in the Peninsula network.

The British cultural inheritance — distinct from the Norwegian-inflected Halley, the French Dumont d'Urville, the Japanese Dome Fuji — gave Rothera its social texture.

**Nationally known for:** The runway. The mild maritime climate. Being British. Adelaide Island's dramatic mountain backdrop. The Lemaire Channel approach from the north.

## Connection to Concordia

Rothera's connection to Concordia ran through the Palmer subnet — the Palmer ("American") Arcanet network, whose hub was Palmer City at the northern tip of the Peninsula. After the Planetary Split Brain, the Palmer subnet became isolated; Rothera's information, history, and perspective on the post-war world are part of the version of events that only DLC 3 can access.

No substantial Rothera diaspora community is established in Concordia — the Palmer subnet cities are geographically distant and the Long Night War hit the Peninsula hard and early. Whatever Concordia knows of Rothera, it knows mostly from before the war.

---

## Current Status / Destruction

Destroyed in the Long Night War. The Peninsula was the first major target — the most accessible part of Antarctica, closest to Upper Earth, struck before the war reached the more remote southern and eastern cities. Rothera's destruction was part of that first wave.

The ruins occupy Rothera Point on Adelaide Island. The paved runway survives in some form. The island's mountains remain.

---

## Legacy

Rothera's legacy is its infrastructure — the runway, the BAS institutional knowledge, the demonstration that the Peninsula could support an aviation-capable city in a climate this maritime. In the DLC 3 ruin landscape, Rothera is the site that most concretely represents what the Palmer subnet lost in practical operational terms: not just a community but a logistics capability.
