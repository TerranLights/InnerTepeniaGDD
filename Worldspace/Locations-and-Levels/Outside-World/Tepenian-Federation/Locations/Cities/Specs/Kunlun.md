# Kunlun

**Based on:** Kunlun Station (China / CHINARE), Dome A (Dome Argus), East Antarctic Plateau (~80°25'S, 77°07'E)
**Status:** Survived — extreme altitude; most marginal survival of any Tepenian city
**Arcanet Subnet:** Mirny ("Australian")
**Highway access:** None confirmed; ~1,200km+ from the nearest Mirny subnet coastal city; the highest and one of the most isolated locations in Tepenia
**Significance:** Located at or near Dome A (Dome Argus) — the highest point on the East Antarctic Ice Sheet at approximately 4,093 meters; the highest-elevation city in Tepenia; the altitude is at or beyond the ceiling of sustained human habitation; survived the Long Night War but its current population — if any human population remains — is uncertain; the Sinian Federation's only Antarctic city
**DLC:** Mirny subnet — extreme altitude and isolation make it a potential endpoint destination rather than a transit location; whether reachable in gameplay is a major design question

## Geographic Basis

Dome A — Dome Argus — is the highest point on the Antarctic Ice Sheet, at approximately 4,093 meters above sea level. Kunlun Station was established here by the Chinese National Antarctic Research Expedition (CHINARE) in 2009, making it the last major Antarctic station established before the first global war of 2083. The location was chosen for scientific purposes: Dome A is one of the best sites on Earth for optical astronomy (the air is calm, dry, and exceptionally stable), for ice core drilling (the ice sheet here is among the oldest and deepest on the continent), and for the study of conditions at the extreme edge of Earth's habitability.

At 4,093 meters geometric elevation, with a physiological effective altitude approaching 5,000 meters due to polar atmospheric compression, Dome A / Kunlun is at the absolute limit of what sustained human habitation can support. Chronic mountain sickness at this altitude is not a manageable condition — it is the permanent baseline. The human founding population would have faced continuous physiological stress that compounds over years. Whether any human community has survived at Kunlun through the centuries since the exile era is one of the most uncertain facts about any Tepenian city.

The Sinian Federation — Tepenia's Sinian (Chinese) exile community — has Kunlun as its only Antarctic city, making it the sole representative of one of the largest cultural communities in Tepenia at a location that can barely support a permanent population.

### Annual Climate

*(Research note: Dome Fuji Station (Japan) has the most comparable available climate record; Dome A / Kunlun climate data is limited due to the station's late establishment (2009) and intermittent operation. Cross-reference with Vostok and Dome Fuji data for plateau interior conditions at comparable or slightly colder parameters.)*

**Climate type:** East Antarctic Plateau polar desert — the most extreme version; higher elevation than Vostok and Dome Fuji means colder mean temperatures; the highest and potentially coldest sustained environment in Tepenia
**Mean annual temperature:** approximately −58°C (−72°F) — colder than Vostok (−55°C) and Dome Fuji (−54°C) due to the highest elevation of any Tepenian city
**Temperature range:** coldest months (July/August) avg −68°C; warmest month (December/January) avg −29°C
**Record extremes:** estimated record low approaching or exceeding −90°C; the actual surface record at Dome A from satellite measurements has been estimated at −93°C, though surface station readings differ; in Tepenia, Kunlun likely holds or contests the absolute cold record with Vostok
**Prevailing winds:** among the calmest in Antarctica — Dome A is a wind minimum due to the dome summit position; average winds 3–5 m/s; the defining hazard here is cold and altitude, not wind
**Annual precipitation:** approximately 15–20 mm water equivalent — the driest location in Tepenia
**Polar night:** approximately April 17 → August 25 (~130 days — longer than Dome Fuji due to more southerly latitude; similar to Concordia's ~82 days... wait, at 80°25'S this would be longer)
**Midnight sun:** approximately October 16 → February 25 (~132 days)

#### Notable Weather Phenomena

- **Altitude at or beyond human limits:** 4,093m geometric elevation with an effective physiological altitude approaching 5,000m; at this altitude, acclimatization provides partial but not complete relief; long-term residence causes irreversible physiological changes; the human body is not designed for this environment as a permanent home
- **Exceptional atmospheric stability:** Dome A is one of the most stable atmospheric sites on Earth — the air is still, cold, and dry in a way that produces extraordinary astronomical seeing conditions; the night sky at Kunlun during polar night, when it is −68°C and perfectly calm, is one of the most extraordinary visual experiences in Tepenia
- **Extreme cold contesting Vostok's record:** satellite measurements have suggested temperatures at Dome A approaching −93°C; whether Kunlun holds or contests the absolute cold record with Vostok is TBD for Tepenian lore
- **Driest location in Tepenia:** even less precipitation than Vostok or Amundsen Station; the air is essentially devoid of moisture

#### Gameplay Notes

- **At the ceiling of human habitation:** any human player character at Kunlun would face the most severe altitude debuff in the game, likely combined with cold debuffs that approach Vostok's level; this is the hardest environment in Tepenia for a human-playable character
- **Robot-majority or robot-only city:** given the altitude effects on humans, Kunlun may be effectively a robot city by the time of the game — the human population, if any remains, would be at minimum reduced to those few individuals whose physiology has adapted or who are sustained by medical infrastructure
- **Astronomical observatory:** the exceptional sky stability makes Kunlun's observatory one of the few locations in Tepenia with access to deep space observation; what has been seen from here in the centuries since the exile era is an open question
- **Hardcore Mode influences:**
  - Most severe altitude sickness of any Tepenian location — not manageable without significant preparation and ongoing treatment
  - Coldest mean temperature of any city; thermal protection requirements may exceed even Vostok
  - Total supply isolation; the crossing from any coastal city exceeds 1,200km

## Founding

**Settled:** Post-Falkland Treaty, on the Kunlun Station infrastructure — the last major Antarctic station established before the first global war, operational since 2009. By 2564, it was relatively young as Antarctic stations went, but established.

**Founding population:** Sinian (Chinese) exiles — Kunlun is the only Tepenian city with a primarily Sinian founding population, making it the sole outpost of the Sinian Federation's exile community on the Antarctic continent. The founding community arrived with a specific scientific and cultural mission and discovered almost immediately that the altitude was going to be the defining constraint of everything they did.

The name was kept.

## Character & Culture

Kunlun's culture is the culture of people who chose to live somewhere they could barely live, and who kept choosing it across generations. The altitude effects were known before the founding — the historical record from the 2009 station was available — but the exile community chose this site anyway. What they came for was the science, the isolation, the sky, and perhaps the specific character of a location that demanded everything from its residents.

The Sinian cultural inheritance at Kunlun carries particular weight: it is the only Sinian Tepenian city, making it the custodian of the Sinian exile community's cultural continuity in a way that no other city is for any other community. The pressure of that responsibility, at a location that challenges survival itself, gives Kunlun's cultural character a specific kind of intensity.

Whether Kunlun's human population has survived, declined, or been effectively replaced by its robot population over the centuries since the Long Night War is the most significant open question about the city's current state.

**Nationally known for:** Being the highest. Being Sinian. The astronomical observatory. The extreme altitude. Being at the absolute edge of what a city can be. As a pilgrimage site — arguably the primary sacred site in all of Tepenia — for practitioners of "Ice Cold Buddhism" (placeholder name — see Robot Religions in TODO).

**Religious significance — "Ice Cold Buddhism":** If Dome Fuji is a major pilgrimage site for this religion, Kunlun is its holiest location. The highest point on the Antarctic Ice Sheet, the coldest sustained environment in Tepenia (contesting or exceeding even Vostok's record), the calmest winds of any location in Antarctica, and atmospheric conditions of extraordinary purity — Kunlun is the closest any place on Earth comes to the superconducting conditions the religion venerates. Reaching it requires crossing more than 1,200km of interior plateau; the journey itself has the character of a religious trial. Practitioners who make it to Kunlun are those most committed to the faith. The religious community may be the primary reason any non-scientific population remains at Kunlun at all — and their presence may be what has kept the city from being entirely abandoned.

## Connection to Concordia

Kunlun is in the Mirny subnet alongside Concordia — but the connection is as theoretical as Vostok's. The overland distance from Kunlun to Mirny (its nearest subnet city) exceeds 1,200km across the interior plateau. Its Arcanet link, if it functions at all, routes through Mirny. Concordia knows Kunlun exists; nothing more.

The Sinian community in Concordia — if one exists — carries whatever connection to Kunlun that can be maintained across the isolation. Whether there is a Sinian diaspora community in Concordia, and what their relationship to Kunlun is, is TBD.

## Current Status

Survived the Long Night War — the city was not destroyed. Whether it remains inhabited, and by whom, is unknown. The altitude effects on human populations over multiple generations, combined with the post-war disruption of supply and communication, may have reduced or eliminated the human population entirely. If Kunlun has a current population, it may be a robot city in all practical senses.

---

## Legacy

Kunlun's legacy is the observatory and the question of what Kunlun's residents have seen from it. Five centuries of clear, stable, cold sky above the highest point on the Antarctic Ice Sheet. Whatever the observatory has recorded — stars, orbital debris from the space elevator collapse, signals, phenomena — is a unique archive. Getting to it is the problem.
