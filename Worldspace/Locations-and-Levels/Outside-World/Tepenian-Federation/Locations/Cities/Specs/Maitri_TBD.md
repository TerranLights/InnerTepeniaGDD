## Geographic Basis

The Maitri Station site sits in the Schirmacher Oasis — approximately 34km² of exposed bedrock and meltwater lakes in Queen Maud Land, surrounded by the larger ice sheet on all sides. The oasis was formed where bedrock topography interrupts the ice sheet, creating a sheltered depression where summer melt pools form and ice does not accumulate permanently. It is a genuinely different landscape from the surrounding Antarctic plateau: rocky, meltwater-channeled in summer, with liquid water visible in ways that most of Antarctica never offers.

The pre-exile Indian station was built here because of this terrain — the ice-free ground provides stable foundations, accessible rock surfaces, and meltwater lakes that offer liquid water resources unavailable elsewhere at this latitude. Whoever settled here inherited those advantages along with the infrastructure.

At ~70°46'S, the city has similar latitude to Neumayer, with similar polar night and midnight sun duration.

#### Notable Weather Phenomena
- **Summer meltwater:** the Schirmacher Oasis lakes are liquid in summer — one of the few places in the Halley subnet where standing liquid water is regularly visible
- **Oasis microclimate:** the exposed rock absorbs solar heat in summer, creating a slightly warmer microclimate than the surrounding ice sheet
