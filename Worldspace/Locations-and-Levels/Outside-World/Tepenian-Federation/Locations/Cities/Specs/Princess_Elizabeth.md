**Based on:** Princess Elisabeth Antarctica (Belgium / International Polar Foundation), Utsteinen nunatak, Sør Rondane Mountains, Queen Maud Land (~71°57'S, 23°21'E)
**Status:** Survived; operational
**Arcanet Subnet:** Halley ("Atlantic")
**Highway access:** Hwy 7 (Queen Maud Land coastal highway — eastern Halley subnet; Sør Rondane nunatak position); connection to Dome Fuji corridor TBD
**Significance:** Belgian Tepenian city; International Polar Foundation heritage; named after Princess Elisabeth of Belgium (born 2001); the most recently built of the Tepenian founding stations (opened 2009); designed from inception as a zero-emissions station — the first Antarctic station built to zero-waste, minimal-impact design principles; on Utsteinen nunatak in the Sør Rondane Mountains; the eastern position in Queen Maud Land puts it closer to Dome Fuji than to the western Halley subnet cities; the sustainability design principles of the founding station became a city-wide design philosophy
**DLC:** Halley subnet — DLC 5 (Atlantic Coastal Region); survived and operational

## Geographic Basis

Princess Elizabeth sits on Utsteinen nunatak, a rocky summit in the Sør Rondane Mountains of Queen Maud Land. The Sør Rondane is one of the more dramatic mountain ranges in Queen Maud Land — exposed rock peaks rising above the surrounding ice plateau. Utsteinen nunatak in particular has an elevated position with views across the ice plateau in multiple directions.

The eastern position in Queen Maud Land — longitude 23°21'E — puts Princess Elizabeth significantly east of the other Halley subnet cities clustered between 14°W and 12°E. It is geographically closer to Dome Fuji (39°42'E) than to Troll or Aboa, though still within the Halley subnet. This eastern position gives Princess Elizabeth a different relationship to the broader Halley-to-Dome-Fuji corridor.

The station was designed by the International Polar Foundation as the first zero-emissions Antarctic station: wind and solar power, no fossil fuels, zero waste discharge. This founding design philosophy — that the human presence in Antarctica should leave no mark — became the template for how Princess Elizabeth the city understood itself.

## Founding

**Settled:** Post-Falkland Treaty, on Princess Elisabeth Antarctica station infrastructure. The International Polar Foundation's station opened in 2009 — the most recently built of the founding-era stations. The exile community inherited both the zero-emissions infrastructure and the design philosophy behind it.

**Founding population:** Belgian exiles, with International Polar Foundation institutional character. Belgium's Antarctic presence was primarily through Princess Elisabeth; the Belgian exile community was concentrated here.

Named after Princess Elisabeth of Belgium (born 2001, daughter of King Philippe). The name carried across the transition from research station to exile city.

## Character & Culture

Princess Elizabeth had the character of a city that took its design principles seriously because they were built in. The zero-emissions founding philosophy wasn't just environmental policy — it was architecture. The renewable energy systems, the waste management design, the no-discharge infrastructure: all of it was physically present and had to be maintained. Living in Princess Elizabeth meant living in a city that had always been designed to leave no mark.

That design principle, extended across generations into a full community, produced a particular relationship to resource use and environmental accountability. Not necessarily a moralistic one — more practical: the systems that kept the city running were visible, understandable, and required active maintenance.

The Belgian cultural heritage — specific, distinct from the more numerically dominant British, Norwegian, and German communities in the Halley subnet — gave Princess Elizabeth its national identity alongside its design identity.

**Nationally known for:** Being Belgian. The zero-emissions design — the city that was always meant to leave no mark. The Sør Rondane mountain position. The eastern Halley subnet location closest to Dome Fuji.
