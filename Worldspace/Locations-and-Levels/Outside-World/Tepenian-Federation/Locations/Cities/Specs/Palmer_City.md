# Palmer City

**Based on:** Palmer Station (USA), Anvers Island, Antarctic Peninsula (64°46'S, 64°03'W)
**Status:** Destroyed (Long Night War) — off-map; referenced in lore throughout Concordia; DLC 3 setting (ruins)
**Arcanet Subnet:** Palmer ("American") — subnet hub
**Highway access:** Hwy 1 (western terminus / origin point)
**Significance:** First settled location in the Tepenian Federation; cultural and entertainment capital of pre-war Tepenia

---

## Geographic Basis

Palmer Station sits on Anvers Island off the Antarctic Peninsula — the northernmost finger of the continent, reaching toward South America. Of all the Antarctic stations, Palmer is the most temperate and the most accessible from the outside world. Temperatures rarely drop below −20°C in winter and occasionally rise above freezing in summer. The surrounding sea is navigable for longer periods than most Antarctic waters.

This accessibility is precisely why Palmer became the first point of arrival. When the Falkland Treaty exiles departed Upper Earth for Antarctica in 2564, ships put them ashore here first. They found an abandoned research station and made it home.

The same accessibility that made it Tepenia's beginning made it Tepenia's first target. Palmer City's position — coastal, reachable, the most accessible point in Antarctica from Upper Earth — meant it was among the first and most thoroughly destroyed cities in the Long Night War.

### Annual Climate

**Climate data source:** NSF / US Antarctic Program (USAP) — BAS READER (1991–2020 WMO standard normal, 30 years; full record 1976–2026)  _https://legacy.bas.ac.uk/met/READER/_

*(Research note: verify all figures against Palmer Station climate records — the US Antarctic Program (USAP) / NSF is the operating authority. NOAA and the Scientific Committee on Antarctic Research (SCAR) also publish Peninsula climate data.)*

**Climate type:** Maritime subpolar — the most temperate climate of any Tepenian city; the only one where above-freezing temperatures are a regular occurrence
**Mean annual temperature:** -1.7°C  _(READER station: Palmer; 1991–2020 WMO standard normal)_
**Temperature range:** coldest months (July/August) avg −8°C; warmest month (January) avg +2°C
**Record extremes:** record low approximately −20°C; record high approximately +8°C (rare summer peak; the Antarctic Peninsula has experienced record warming in recent decades)
**Prevailing winds:** strong westerlies off the Drake Passage; average 7–9 m/s; intense Southern Ocean low-pressure systems are frequent; gusts regularly exceeding 60–80 km/h during storm events; the Peninsula is one of the windiest Antarctic regions despite being the mildest in temperature
**Annual precipitation:** approximately 700–900 mm water equivalent annually — among the highest of any Tepenian city; snow predominates in winter, but rain is possible (and common) in summer; persistent high cloud cover year-round
**Polar night:** None — Palmer Station (64°46'S) is north of the Antarctic Circle (66°33'S); the sun rises every day of the year regardless of season
**Midnight sun:** None — the sun sets every night of the year at this latitude
**Winter solstice daylight minimum:** approximately 6–7 hours (June 21)
**Summer solstice daylight maximum:** approximately 17–18 hours (December 21)

#### Notable Weather Phenomena

- **Intense storm systems:** the Antarctic Peninsula sits in the direct path of intense Southern Ocean low-pressure systems funneled through the Drake Passage; major storm events are the norm; calm periods are notable
- **Fog and low cloud:** maritime conditions create persistent low cloud and fog banks; visibility can drop to near zero without warning regardless of wind conditions
- **Sea ice:** forms seasonally around Anvers Island in winter; extent varies dramatically year to year; in heavy ice years, maritime access from Upper Earth can be significantly limited
- **Rain and wet snow:** unlike drier inland Antarctic stations, Palmer receives frequent wet precipitation; heavy wet snow soaks through improper protection rapidly; rain is possible year-round and common in summer
- **Icing:** ship icing, equipment icing, and black ice on surfaces are persistent hazards; the combination of maritime moisture and subfreezing temperatures creates frequent surface and structural icing
- **Bergy bits and growlers:** small icebergs and submerged ice fragments in surrounding waters; a persistent maritime navigation hazard in the Peninsula region
- **Rapid weather changes:** conditions can shift from navigable to dangerous within hours; the Drake Passage system is fast-moving and difficult to forecast precisely

#### Gameplay Notes

- **Above-freezing temperatures:** Palmer City is the only Tepenian setting where this is a regular occurrence; major implications for what is physically possible — running water, different material degradation, potential surface plant life, different structural failure modes in ruins
- **No polar night:** the DLC 3 ruin setting never goes fully dark; very different atmospheric character from Concordia; eerie green or grey overcast light as the default rather than artificial lighting vs. total darkness
- **Storm windows:** high precipitation probability and frequent gale-force storms create natural travel-window mechanics — certain outdoor activities or movements are only feasible between storm events; the game would signal incoming weather
- **Rain in ruins:** the DLC 3 setting would experience wet conditions, not just snow; flooded lower levels, wet surfaces, rain sounds — distinct visual and audio environment from any inland city setting
- **Fog as visibility mechanic:** persistent fog in the DLC 3 ruins would create navigation challenge and atmosphere without relying on darkness
- **Hardcore Mode influences:**
  - Wet cold is more dangerous than dry cold — moisture penetration through inadequate protection accelerates hypothermia progression faster than equivalent dry temperatures suggest
  - Rain and wet snow create gear soaking: protection that holds up in dry cold may fail when wet; siligel/coolant consumption rates for robots may shift when external moisture interacts with thermal regulation systems
  - Storm event shelter requirement: extended outdoor exposure during a gale-force storm event (even at "mild" −6°C) may trigger escalating exposure debuffs; the player must track incoming storm windows and plan accordingly
  - Sea ice travel: if maritime movement mechanics exist in DLC 3, sea ice conditions would need to be tracked seasonally — passable in some winter configurations, impassable in others

## Founding

**Settled:** June 21, 2564 — the same day the Falkland Treaty was signed. The exiles did not wait.

**Founding population:** Three groups arrived together:
- Robots exiled under the treaty's blanket provision — by virtue of being robots, not for any individual act
- Humans in committed relationships with specific robots — partners who chose exile rather than remain in Upper Earth without the people they loved
- Human supporters who sided with robots on ideological grounds — people who believed the exile was unjust and refused to accept it as a condition of staying

These three groups built Palmer City together. The distinction between romantic exile and ideological exile was real but not socially significant in those early years. Everyone was there because they chose to be — which was different from most of the world.

The first residents took up the old pre-war station buildings immediately on arrival. What began as a camp became a city gradually, then all at once.

## Character & Culture

Palmer City became what its founding population made it: a city that celebrated the fact of its own existence. The jazz records found in the station buildings set the tone — a music born from a marginalized community creating something transcendent from difficult circumstances resonated immediately with an exile population building a country from abandoned buildings on the southernmost accessible point of the world.

At peak, Palmer City combined the spectacle and excess of Las Vegas, the boardwalk energy of Atlantic City, the cultural depth and musical soul of New Orleans, and the grand ambition of St. Petersburg. It was not the governmental capital of Tepenia — that distinction belonged elsewhere (TBD). It was something different: the place where Tepenia celebrated itself.

**Neutrality:** Palmer City's status as the first settlement — the arrival point, the place everyone passed through — gave it a character no later-founded city could replicate. No faction had claim to it because every faction's predecessors had walked its ice paths first. Neutrality was not a policy; it was an inheritance from being the beginning.

**Nationally known for:** Being the first. The jazz. The founding artifacts. The address 100 Miles Davis Boulevard. Every robot and human in Tepenia either arrived through Palmer City or descended from someone who did.

## Notable Locations

### Miles Davis Boulevard

The first main street of Palmer City was an ice path — the equivalent of a dirt road, packed and worn by foot traffic between the station buildings. It was named **Miles Davis Boulevard** after the discovery of the jazz records.

At one end stood a small building repurposed as Palmer City's first customs office — a makeshift reception point for incoming robots and their partners and supporters. The building was given the address **100 Miles Davis Boulevard, Palmer City** — the first official address in the Federation of Tepenia.

The customs office was not a formal bureaucratic institution. It had no official legal standing in those earliest days. It was simply a place where people arrived and were received — where robot-human pairs came through the door together and were acknowledged as having arrived. Its informality was part of what it was.

The address 100 Miles Davis Boulevard eventually became the most historically significant address in Tepenia — the first place the nation formally existed in physical space, the first threshold the exiles crossed into a country that was their own.

### The Antarctica Flag

A torn and tattered fabric flag of Antarctica — the real pre-war flag, a white silhouette of the continent on a light blue field — was found in the old station buildings. No one brought it; it was simply there.

The exiles kept it. The symbolic weight was not lost on anyone: they had been sent to Antarctica, and Antarctica had already named itself and put up a flag. The tattered condition — worn, imperfect, survived — became part of its meaning rather than a reason to replace it.

The Antarctica flag is distinct from the Federation of Tepenia's national flag (the circuit diamond design, developed later as a deliberate national identity). The found flag predates Tepenia's existence as a country. It is a historical artifact — displayed at 100 Miles Davis Blvd. and later in cultural institutions across Tepenia.

**Physical fate:** Unknown, and possibly resolved by the environment regardless of the war. Already torn and tattered when found, the Antarctic environment degrades fabric over decades regardless of care. Whether it survived to the destruction of Palmer City, was evacuated beforehand, or simply wore away over time is unresolved. What survives in Concordia, if anything, are reproductions, documentation, and the story.

### The Jazz Records

Also recovered from the station buildings: a collection of jazz records spanning the classic era — pre-War of Upper Earth, pre-2083. Artists from the full arc of jazz history, preserved in physical format.

These records became Palmer City's soundtrack. Jazz — a music born from a marginalized community creating something transcendent from difficult circumstances — resonated with an exile population building a country from abandoned buildings at the southernmost accessible point of the world. The music was old. The situation it fit was new. The connection was immediate.

## Connection to Concordia

Palmer City refugees in Concordia are relatively rare. The Antarctic Peninsula is as geographically distant from Concordia as any point in Antarctica — a Palmer City survivor who reached Concordia traveled an exceptionally long way and likely lost more than most.

What survived of Palmer City's culture arrived in Concordia through people, not infrastructure:
- Jazz as a musical tradition
- The story of 100 Miles Davis Boulevard and the founding moment
- Knowledge of the Antarctica flag and where it was found
- The founding image of robots and their human partners arriving together and being received at a door at the end of an ice path

**The Bonded Lattice faction's** philosophical roots trace to Palmer City's founding population. The first humans in Tepenia were largely there because of specific robots they loved. The Bonded Lattice is, in a sense, the institutional continuation of something that began at 100 Miles Davis Boulevard.

---

## Current Status / Destruction

Palmer City was destroyed in the Long Night War (early 2700s). Its coastal position and extreme accessibility from Upper Earth made it among the first and most thoroughly targeted of the Tepenian cities. The city that was built first was among the first to be destroyed.

The ruins exist on the Antarctic Peninsula. Whether they are accessible, and what remains of specific structures, is a DLC 3 design question — the DLC is explicitly set in Palmer City's ruins as an archaeology of what Tepenia was at its most vibrant.

---

## Legacy

Palmer City's legacy in Concordia is primarily cultural and philosophical:

- **Jazz** as the foundational music of Tepenian culture
- **The founding story** — the image of robots and humans arriving together at a door at the end of an ice path, the first moment Tepenia existed
- **The Bonded Lattice faction** as an institutional continuation of Palmer City's founding ethos
- **100 Miles Davis Boulevard** — the first address, the first threshold, the place Tepenia first existed in physical space; the story survives even if the street does not
