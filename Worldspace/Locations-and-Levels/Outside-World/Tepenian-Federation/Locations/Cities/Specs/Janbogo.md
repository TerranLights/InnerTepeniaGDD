# Janbogo

**Based on:** Jang Bogo Station (Unified Korea), Terra Nova Bay, Ross Sea (~74°37'S, 164°13'E)
**Status:** Damaged; partially operational — primary active connection between Concordia and the outside world
**Arcanet Subnet:** Janbogo — subnet hub
**Highway access:** Hwy 183 (hub — south to Fort McMurdo / Scott; north to Cape Adare / Zukelli; northern terminus is Concordia)
**Significance:** Unified Korea's primary Tepenian city; the Tepenian Federation's most critical surviving external link; the last coastal city still actively supplying Concordia
**DLC:** Janbogo subnet — DLC 6 (Janbogo Region); damaged but partially operational — DLC 6 centerpiece and subnet hub

## Geographic Basis

Janbogo sits on Terra Nova Bay on the Ross Sea coast — the Pacific-facing side of Antarctica. The Ross Sea has a longer navigable season than the Atlantic approaches, and Terra Nova Bay is among the more sheltered positions on that coast. Janbogo's location gave it advantages in both trade and resilience: close enough to the sea to function as a port, positioned in a bay that offered more protection than fully exposed coastal sites.

Hwy 183 (the Janbogo Highway) runs from Concordia's southern terminus south through Fort McMurdo and Scott on Ross Island, to Janbogo, and then north along the coast to Cape Adare and Zukelli. Of all the highways connecting Concordia to the outside world, Hwy 183 is the most actively maintained and the most strategically critical. Whatever enters or exits Concordia by ground moves through Janbogo.

Approximately 10km north on the same bay, the ruins of Zukelli — the Italian city based on Mario Zucchelli Station — are visible from Janbogo's waterfront. No other surviving city in Tepenia sits within sight of a destroyed one. Janbogo residents live with Zukelli's ruins on the horizon: what happened there is not history in the way it is everywhere else. It is a view.

### Annual Climate

**Climate type:** Katabatic coastal — continental polar with defining wind events; distinct from both the interior polar desert (Concordia) and the maritime subpolar (Palmer)
**Mean annual temperature:** approximately −14°C (+7°F)
**Temperature range:** coldest month (July) avg −27°C; warmest months (December/January) avg 0°C
**Record extremes:** record low approximately −45°C; record high approximately +5°C (during warm katabatic descent events)
**Prevailing winds:** strong katabatic winds from the SSW–SW (gravity-driven airflow off the East Antarctic interior ice sheet); average 8–12 m/s; storms regularly reaching 60–100+ km/h; Terra Nova Bay is one of the windiest locations on the entire Ross Sea coast; katabatic winds warm adiabatically as they descend, arriving drier and relatively warmer than ambient air — but wind chill at even moderate speeds creates lethal outdoor conditions
**Annual precipitation:** approximately 150–250 mm water equivalent; predominantly snow; katabatic winds actively suppress precipitation by bringing dry descending air from the interior; lower cloud cover than maritime sites
**Polar night:** approximately May 20 → July 23 (~64 days)
**Midnight sun:** approximately November 16 → January 25 (~70 days)
**Winter solstice daylight minimum:** N/A — polar night in effect; 0 hours
**Summer solstice daylight maximum:** N/A — midnight sun in effect; 24 hours

#### Notable Weather Phenomena

- **Katabatic winds:** the defining feature of Terra Nova Bay; gravity-driven winds that flow from the high interior ice sheet down to the coast; they warm as they descend (adiabatic warming) and arrive drier and relatively warmer than ambient air — but wind chill at even "moderate" katabatic speeds creates lethal outdoor conditions; extreme katabatic events can reach 150+ km/h; these are not storms in the conventional sense but a near-permanent feature of the local climate regime
- **Terra Nova Bay polynya:** a persistent open-water area maintained year-round by the katabatic winds, even when the surrounding Ross Sea is frozen solid; the katabatics blow sea ice away from the bay and prevent new ice from forming; a strategically unique maritime access point — the only location on the Ross Sea coast where ships can arrive regardless of season
- **Spindrift blizzards:** katabatic winds pick up loose surface snow (spindrift) and create near-zero-visibility conditions even when no precipitation is actively falling; the practical distinction between "blizzard" and "high-wind clear conditions" collapses — both are impassable
- **Rime ice:** heavy structural icing on anything facing the bay; sea spray combined with subfreezing temperatures coats surfaces rapidly; structures require specialized orientation and design to survive long-term
- **Sea ice:** forms around the bay in winter but the polynya itself remains open year-round; the ice edge varies and creates navigational complexity for ships approaching from open water

#### Gameplay Notes

- **Katabatic winds as the primary outdoor hazard:** unlike Concordia, where the cold is the constant threat, Janbogo's outdoor lethality comes from the combination of cold + wind force; an environment that is manageable in calm conditions at −5°C becomes impassable in a katabatic event at the same temperature; wind chill, not ambient temperature, is the design parameter
- **The polynya as strategic asset:** maritime access to Janbogo is year-round — supply ships and travelers can arrive by sea even in the depths of winter; this is a major strategic differentiator and should be mechanically reflected in DLC 6 (things that other coastal cities can only receive in summer, Janbogo can receive year-round)
- **Polar night (~64 days):** shorter than Concordia's 82-day night; present but less dominant; faction behaviors and outdoor event availability shift during the darkness period
- **The communal warmth design tension:** the violent outdoor environment versus Janbogo's renowned interior culture (teahouses, shared warmth) is the defining design contrast of the setting — sheltered interior spaces are survival, not luxury; transitioning between exterior and interior is a constant mechanical and atmospheric rhythm
- **Wind warning system:** katabatic events as a dynamic weather mechanic — the game signals incoming events; outdoor activity windows are time-limited; characters and NPCs may have routines that adjust to katabatic event patterns
- **Hardcore Mode influences:**
  - Wind chill tracking: ambient temperature alone is insufficient — wind speed must factor into exposure calculations; a katabatic event at "merely" −10°C ambient can generate effective wind-chill temperatures below −40°C
  - The polynya as a supply mechanic: in Hardcore Mode, the year-round maritime access to Janbogo represents a consistent external supply route that other cities cannot match; disruption of the polynya (unlikely — it's wind-maintained — but worth flagging as a sabotage scenario) or the route to it would be a critical supply-chain event
  - Siligel / coolant stockpiling: Janbogo as the primary supply node for Concordia means Hardcore Mode supply depletion is felt most acutely when the Janbogo connection is threatened; shortages trace back here
  - Shelter routing outdoors: during katabatic events, movement through Janbogo's exterior would require planning around shelter points; charging through an open katabatic without cover would escalate exposure debuffs rapidly regardless of protection rating

## Founding

**Settled:** Shortly after the Falkland Treaty (June 21, 2564). The real Jang Bogo Station was established as Unified Korea's Antarctic presence well before the exile era; the exile settlement built on that existing infrastructure rather than starting from nothing.

**Founding population:** Primarily Unified Korean exiles — robots from Korean society and their human partners and supporters — alongside the broader Tepenian exile population that reached the Ross Sea coast.

Janbogo developed as a distinctly Korean-influenced city from its earliest days, drawing on the cultural traditions its founders brought and adapting them to the Antarctic environment. The phonetic adaptation of the name — Jang Bogo to Janbogo — reflects the city's easy relationship with its own identity: it kept what it was without needing to perform it.

## Character & Culture

Janbogo developed a reputation for warmth and community that traveled well beyond its Unified Korean roots — a city known across Tepenia not just as the Korean city but as a place with a particular quality of life that set it apart from the industrial cities (Fort McMurdo) and the cultural capitals (Palmer City).

The communal culture was strong: shared spaces, hospitality traditions, an emphasis on the quality of everyday experience even in a difficult environment. The city produced people whose instinct, wherever they ended up, was to build something that made the people around them feel more at home.

The teahouse culture was its most nationally visible expression. Janbogo teahouses were not simply places to drink tea; they were social institutions, community anchors, places where the texture of daily life was deliberately made worth living. This tradition was strong enough that at least one Janbogo teahouse was famous throughout Tepenia — known to people who had never been to Janbogo and would never go.

**The Sejong connection:** Janbogo is not the only Korean city in Tepenia. Sejong, on King George Island in the Palmer subnet, was the other — a South Korean community on the opposite side of the continent, separated from Janbogo by the entire breadth of Antarctica. Both cities carry Korean cultural identity; both are named for Korean historical figures. Whether the two Korean communities had meaningful contact pre-Split Brain, and what the current relationship between surviving members of each diaspora might be, is a lore thread running through both cities.

**Nationally known for:** Its teahouse culture and the quality of warmth and communal hospitality its people carried with them; being the last active coastal link to Concordia.

## Economy & Industry

Janbogo's economy was built around its port position and its role as a supply hub on the Ross Sea coast. Before the Long Night War, it was a significant node in Tepenia's inter-city trade network — goods moving between the Ross Sea cities and the inland stations passed through or alongside Janbogo.

Since the war, its economic function has narrowed to what it can still perform in its damaged state: supplying Concordia via Hwy 183 is the dominant activity. What Janbogo still produces internally vs. what it receives and passes on from outside Tepenia is a DLC 6 design question.

## Connection to Concordia

Janbogo's connection to Concordia is the most direct and most operational of any external city:

- **Hwy 183** — the primary supply route; Concordia depends on Janbogo for goods it cannot produce internally; disruption of this route would significantly worsen Concordia's already-strained situation
- **The Janbogo diaspora in Gemini district** — the largest and most established non-native community in Concordia; culturally cohesive and organized around the Janbogo subnet nexus hardware physically located in Gemini
- **The Janbogo Arcanet subnet nexus** — the physical relay hardware for the Janbogo subnet sits inside Concordia's Gemini district; this is unusual (most subnet nexuses would be in their hub city) and reflects the depth and longevity of the Janbogo-Concordia relationship

**Characters from Janbogo currently in Concordia:**
- **Majyao Bisyugota** — Aquarius district; runs a teahouse; relocated from Janbogo

## Current Status / Destruction

Janbogo was hit during the Long Night War but not destroyed. It remains damaged and partially operational — the only coastal city in Tepenia still actively functioning as a supply source for Concordia. What "partially operational" means in terms of surviving population, infrastructure integrity, and governance is a DLC 6 design question.

The city's strategic importance as Concordia's supply lifeline means it has likely been a contested location in the post-war period. Whoever controls Janbogo controls Concordia's most critical external artery.

---

## Legacy

In Concordia, Janbogo's legacy is not historical — it is present-tense. The Gemini district enclave is a living piece of Janbogo culture, maintained by the diaspora community. The teahouse tradition survives through Majyao's establishment in Aquarius. The supply relationship is ongoing.

What Janbogo means to Concordia residents is not primarily "a city that was lost" but "the place our supplies come from" and "where the Gemini people are from." It is the most present of all the damaged cities.
