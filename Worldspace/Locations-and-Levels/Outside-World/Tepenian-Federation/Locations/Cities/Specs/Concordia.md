# Concordia

**Based on:** Concordia Station (France / Italy), Dome C, East Antarctic plateau (~75°06'S, 123°20'E)
**Status:** Survived — last major city; primary game setting
**Arcanet Subnet:** Mirny ("Australian")
**Highway access:** Hwy 110 (NE → Casey coast), Hwy 37 (NW → Kunlun → Vostok → Syowa), Hwy 183 (S → Fort McMurdo → Janbogo → Ross Sea cities)
**Significance:** The last functioning major city in the Tepenian Federation; the only place in Tepenia where survivors from all six subnets coexist

---

## Geographic Basis

Concordia sits on the East Antarctic plateau at Dome C — 3,233 meters above sea level, deep inland, far from any coast. Temperatures routinely reach −80°C in winter. Months of total darkness alternate with months of unbroken daylight. The altitude is thin enough to require acclimatization even for long-term residents. Of all the survivable inland stations, Concordia is the most isolated from any coastline while remaining below the altitude threshold where large-scale habitation becomes impossible.

Its survival was not luck. The Long Night War targeted coastal cities — the ones accessible from Upper Earth by sea or manageable overland routes. Concordia was too far inland, too high, too costly to assault. The city endured because destroying it would have required more logistical investment than Upper Earth was willing to commit.

Three highway exits connect Concordia to the rest of Tepenia: northeast to Casey and the Mirny subnet coast (Hwy 110), northwest across the plateau to Syowa and ultimately the Atlantic coast highway system (Hwy 37), and south to the Ross Sea cities (Hwy 183). The primary mode of surface travel between Concordia and outside points is the Kharkovchanka — a Soviet-designed tracked vehicle adapted for the Antarctic plateau.

At temperatures averaging −54°C annually — reaching −65°C in a typical winter and approaching −84°C at the coldest extremes — open-air habitation is not a viable baseline. It is an exception and a hardship. The vast majority of Concordia is enclosed: insulated dome structures connected by pressurized corridors, heated underground passages, and enclosed transit links that allow residents to live, work, and move between districts without sustained outdoor exposure. The dome-and-corridor architecture is not an amenity of Tepenian city design; it is the survival precondition that makes inhabiting Dome C possible at all.

Two districts break significantly from this pattern: **Capricorn** (the industrial yards) and **Sagittarius** (the Frostlands). Both operate predominantly outdoors. Both carry the full weight of Concordia's climate in their culture, their infrastructure, and their relationship with the rest of the city.

#### Notable Weather Phenomena

- **Diamond dust:** suspended ice crystal precipitation common during clear conditions; refracts light into halos and sun dogs, especially dramatic during the midnight sun period
- **Blizzards:** less frequent than coastal sites but occur; wind-driven snow at the surface creates near-zero visibility even when no snow is actively falling
- **Whiteout conditions:** during blizzards; the featureless white of plateau and sky merge; total spatial disorientation, even between buildings
- **Temperature inversions:** during polar night, the surface layer of air can be significantly colder than air hundreds of meters above it — the inverse of normal atmospheric behavior; a consequence of radiative cooling from the snow surface
- **Altitude effects (3,233m ASL):** newcomers experience acclimatization symptoms — headaches, cognitive impairment, physical fatigue; equivalent to altitude sickness in comparable mountain environments
- **Southern aurora (Aurora Australis):** spectacular displays during the ~82-day polar night; the longest reliable aurora window of any populated location in Tepenia
- **Corrosive cold:** at extreme temperatures, mechanical failure modes that don't occur in milder cold become common — lubricants freeze, metals become brittle, electronics behave unpredictably

#### Gameplay Notes

- The cold at Concordia is not weather — it is the constant baseline reality of the setting. Even at "summer" average (−27°C), every unprotected outdoor moment is dangerous.
- **Polar night (~82 days):** lighting conditions become a resource; certain questlines, faction behaviors, or outdoor events may only be available during specific light-condition phases; district atmospheres shift meaningfully between darkness and light periods
- **Midnight sun (~85 days):** persistent eerie brightness creates a distinct atmosphere; sleep disruption for human NPCs; the visual environment is fundamentally different from the polar night period
- **Altitude acclimatization:** a potential early-game mechanic — the player (and NPCs arriving from lower elevations) may have reduced performance until acclimatized, establishing Concordia's elevation as a physical fact, not just lore flavor
- **Blizzard events:** outdoor activity restricted or impossible; NPC movement behavior changes; travel between districts may require shelter routing
- **Diamond dust / aurora:** high-quality atmospheric events for environmental storytelling; the visual contrast between extreme beauty and lethal conditions is a design opportunity
- **Hardcore Mode influences:**
  - Cold exposure is the constant baseline mechanic, not an event — the player cannot simply "go outside without protection" even in summer; protection upkeep and repair become persistent resource concerns rather than emergency responses
  - Siligel and coolant consumption rates may be elevated by extreme cold — robot thermal regulation working overtime in −27°C "summer" conditions, let alone winter
  - Altitude at 3,233m: stamina depletion rates and recovery times for human NPCs (and potentially the player) may be higher than at sea level; relevant if the player ever travels to lower-altitude DLC regions and notices the contrast
  - Polar night phase: sleep and rest mechanics for human characters may be disrupted during extended periods of artificial lighting dependence; relevant to any companion or NPC systems that track rest states
  - Blizzard shelter events: during blizzard conditions, outdoor traversal between districts may require shelter waypoints; getting caught in the open could trigger rapid exposure accumulation regardless of protection quality

## Founding

**Settled:** Concordia Station predates the exile era as a joint French/Italian research station. Exile settlement followed the Falkland Treaty (June 21, 2564), though Concordia's inland position meant it was not among the first-wave settlements — those were the more accessible coastal cities, Palmer City foremost. Concordia received subsequent waves as the coastal cities filled, grew, and were eventually destroyed.

**Founding population:** A mix of robots and human partners directed or choosing inland, and successive waves of coastal refugees as the Long Night War progressed and city after city fell.

Concordia was not built in a moment. It grew as everything else shrank. It was not the glamorous choice, not a cultural statement, not what anyone would have planned. It was practical — a city that existed because high-altitude plateau conditions were survivable if you were willing to endure them, and enduring them became necessary.

## Character & Culture

Concordia is not the city Tepenia chose — it is the city Tepenia has left. Its character is defined by what it outlasted: every coastal city, every more accessible settlement, every more comfortable place to live. What remains is what the war could not reach.

The twelve districts, organized by zodiac name, each developed distinct identities as the city absorbed successive waves of refugees with different backgrounds, cultures, and needs. The result is the most culturally diverse place in Tepenia — not by design, but by attrition. Everyone who made it here from anywhere is here, alongside everyone who was always here.

Within the districts, subnet identities persist. Gemini is identifiably Janbogo. Refugees from the Atlantic coast congregate differently from refugees from the Indian Ocean coast. The city holds all of it simultaneously without having resolved it.

**The dome divide:** Within Concordia, "dome" versus "outdoor" is a quiet but real social distinction. Living inside the dome system means shelter, corridor access, and reliable ambient heat as a baseline. Living in the Frostlands or working the industrial yards means earning your warmth continuously. Dome residents often don't think about this. Frostlands residents rarely stop thinking about it.

**Nationally known for:** Being the last city. There is no longer any competition for that distinction.

## Economy & Industry

Concordia's economy runs on inter-district trade and external supply, with heavy dependence on Janbogo via Hwy 183 for goods the city cannot produce internally. The energy grid is failing — the full picture of why and what it would take to address it is documented in `Worldspace/Energy_Grid_Failure_Rationale.md`.

Food production is a particular vulnerability. The Frostlands (Sagittarius) is Concordia's primary internal food source, and its entire operation — including the heated livestock barns without which no animals survive — depends on continuous grid power. The grid failure crisis and the food supply crisis are therefore not two separate problems; they are the same problem expressed in two domains. A sustained grid outage in winter does not merely disrupt Concordia economically; it causes the Frostlands to begin losing its livestock, which degrades the city's internal food capacity in a way that cannot be quickly reversed even after power is restored. The animals take years to replace. This is why the grid situation carries existential weight rather than merely being a maintenance problem.

District economies vary by zodiac specialization and are documented in full in `Worldspace/Locations-and-Levels/Concordia-City/Districts/`.

## Notable Locations

- **The twelve districts** — Aries, Taurus, Gemini, Cancer, Leo, Virgo, Libra, Scorpio, Sagittarius, Capricorn, Aquarius, Pisces; each with distinct character and function; the majority are enclosed within the dome-and-corridor system — see district documentation
- **Janbogo subnet nexus (Gemini district)** — the physical Arcanet hardware of the Janbogo subnet; the Janbogo diaspora enclave is built around it
- **Red Spiral base/clinic** — the Red Spiral faction's operational headquarters and primary medical facility; district TBD
- **Calethina's lab** — site of the player's activation; district TBD

**The two outdoor districts — significant exceptions to the dome system:**

- **Sagittarius — the Frostlands:** Concordia's agricultural and pastoral district; located outside the main dome cluster.
  **Demonym:** Frostlander *(a person from the Frostlands; used in NPC dialogue, terminal entries, audio logs, and any in-world text referring to Sagittarius residents or the Sagittarius community as a group)* The Frostlands is not a contiguous enclosed zone — it is a scattered collection of insulated homesteads, heated barn complexes, work structures, and covered access paths spread across the plateau around Concordia's perimeter. Residents live in standalone insulated structures that are not connected to the main dome system, managing their own heating, their own power draws, and their own supply of the materials needed to stay alive.

  **Shelter:** Frostlands homes are purpose-built for extreme cold — thick insulated walls, minimal external surface area, airlocked entryways, and backup heating systems as a matter of survival rather than comfort. The aesthetics are functional. Decorative choices, where they exist, happen on the inside. A Frostlands home is designed to be a sealed warm box; everything else is secondary.

  **Outdoor work:** Most outdoor work is conducted in timed windows calibrated to temperature, wind conditions, and light. In polar night and during blizzard events, extended outdoor exposure is simply not survivable without a Kharkovchanka or equivalent mobile shelter nearby. Workers use the buddy system as standard practice — you do not work outdoors alone. If conditions deteriorate faster than forecast (and they do), the standing rule is that you shelter in the nearest structure and wait; heroics in a Concordia winter kill people. The culture of the Frostlands is built around taking that fact seriously.

  **Kharkovchanka ownership:** Kharkovchankas in the Frostlands are communally owned — one, possibly two, per tribe/clan (group name TBD). They are not personal vehicles; they belong to the group and are shared, maintained collectively, and treated as critical infrastructure rather than transportation convenience. A tribe without a working Kharkovchanka has no mobile shelter and no emergency evacuation capability. Keeping the vehicle operational is as important as keeping the heating systems running.

  **Design note — first Frostlander companion:** The buddy system is a natural and lore-motivated entry point for the player's first recruitable Frostlander companion (identity TBN). A player arriving in or passing through the Frostlands for any reason would be expected — by Frostlander custom — to be paired with a local before going outdoors. The companion doesn't need a contrived reason to attach to the player; the culture provides it. The buddy relationship that starts as a practical necessity is the same relationship that, over time, becomes something the companion chooses to continue.

  **Design note — "Where's your [buddy-word]?" (first Frostlands encounter):** Upon the player's first interaction with a local NPC in the Frostlands, that character's opening line is a variant of: *"Where the hell's your [buddy-word]?"* The Frostlander term for one's outdoor partner is TBD — they may not call it a "buddy" at all; whatever the word, it would be Frostlander-specific vocabulary. Player response options include, among others: *"...buddy?"* — stated with genuine incomprehension, not attitude.

  That single response tells the NPC everything. Dome Concordians generally know that Frostlanders operate cooperatively — it's common knowledge about a neighboring community — so a dome resident who stumbled into the Frostlands would at least recognize the concept even if they didn't know the specific term. A player who doesn't know the word, has no partner, and registers zero familiarity with the practice hasn't been in Concordia long enough to pick up even secondhand cultural awareness. The NPC immediately reads this as: *newly built*. The follow-up — how that NPC responds to realizing they're talking to someone who is, functionally, brand new to existence — is TBD for questline design, but the beat itself is established.

  This encounter also functions as undidactic world-building: the player learns the buddy system exists, learns they don't have one, learns this is a problem, and learns something about how they read to the people around them — all from one NPC line and one available response.

  **Livestock:** All animals kept in the Frostlands require heated, insulated barn structures with continuous powered heating. Nothing lives in a Concordia outdoor environment without a heated enclosure — at −65°C average winter temperatures, an unheated structure kills livestock within hours of power loss. The barn complexes are substantial: multiple insulated chambers with internal air circulation to prevent localized cold pockets, backup heating redundancy, and in some cases shared wall-heating systems between neighboring homesteads to pool energy costs. Exact species is TBD for game design purposes — cold-adapted livestock breeds, animals genetically modified for Tepenian conditions, and indoor aquaculture operations in heated tanks are all candidates. Whatever the species, the animals cannot go outside. The Frostlands does not have "pasture" in any conventional sense — all grazing and foraging occurs within the insulated barn system, with dried and preserved feed brought in from outside the district.

  **Energy grid dependency:** The Frostlands is the district most acutely and immediately exposed to Concordia's grid failure crisis. A grid outage that lasts more than a day in a Concordia winter does not merely inconvenience Frostlands residents — it kills their livestock and, without backup heating fuel, potentially them as well. No other district has the same survival-level stake in grid continuity. The enclosed dome districts lose lighting, transit, and comfort during a grid failure; the Frostlands loses the physical conditions necessary to stay alive. This makes Sagittarius politically invested in grid maintenance in a way that no dome resident can fully appreciate, and gives Frostlands people a relationship with the energy crisis that is visceral rather than abstract. They are not worried about the grid failing in principle. They are watching their backup fuel reserves and calculating how many days they have.

  **Culture:** Frostlands residents have the most physically demanding daily lives in Concordia. Their work routines are shaped by weather windows, protection checks, and contingency planning for sudden condition changes. Communal warmth is a defining cultural value — the shared meal, the heated gathering structure, the trust that your neighbor will check on you if you don't come back on time. The Frostlands has a reputation in the rest of Concordia for being rough, self-sufficient, and deeply skeptical of anyone who takes indoor comfort for granted. There is a quiet pride in knowing that the Frostlands feeds Concordia and does it in conditions that dome residents would not survive for a week.

- **Capricorn — the industrial yards:** Mining extraction, material processing, and heavy manufacturing operations requiring scale and infrastructure that cannot be enclosed within the dome system. Workers cycle between insulated work stations, pressurized equipment bays, and outdoor extraction or processing points. Machinery is specialized for extreme cold — lubricants rated for sub-−60°C performance, fuel systems with cold-start redundancy, components chosen for low-temperature durability rather than ambient-condition efficiency. Equipment failure in Concordia's cold is not a maintenance inconvenience; it is a production stoppage with safety implications if workers are midway through an outdoor operation when it happens.

  The extreme cold creates both persistent challenges and occasional working advantages. Metal fatigue and brittleness at extreme temperatures are constant engineering concerns. Cold as a natural refrigerant makes certain material storage and processing applications simpler than they would be in temperate conditions. The yards operate on the same weather-window logic as the Frostlands — outdoor crew rotations timed around conditions, with covered staging areas for work that can be paused mid-task. ## Current Status

Concordia is the last functioning major city in the Tepenian Federation. It is not thriving — the energy grid is failing, district tensions are high, and the city's long-term viability is the central question of the main game. The twelve districts are in various states of internal conflict and inter-district tension. No single faction controls the city. The player arrives into this instability.
