# Antarctic Station → Tepenian City Reference

Every Antarctic research station became a Tepenian city or settlement as the exile population expanded after the Falkland Treaty (June 21, 2564). This table documents the full mapping from real station to game city, organized by geographic region.

**Name conventions established so far:**
- Direct carry-over (most common): Neumayer, Belgrano, Mirny, Vostok
- Phonetic adaptation: Jang Bogo → Janbogo
- Modified with prefix/suffix: McMurdo → Fort McMurdo
- Unchanged: Concordia, Palmer City (Palmer Station → Palmer City)

**Status key:**
- **Destroyed** — Long Night War (coastal, targeted by Upper Earth militaries)
- **Survived** — Inland, not targeted or not reachable
- **Too high** — Inland but altitude too extreme for viable large settlement
- **Historical site** — Pre-exile expedition base; ruins/memorial rather than functioning city
- **TBD** — Status not yet determined

**Confirmed names are marked ✓. All others are suggested and subject to developer approval.**

## Summary Statistics

- **Total stations/sites on map:** ~40
- **Confirmed destroyed (coastal, Long Night War):** majority
- **Confirmed survived (inland):** Concordia (game setting), Vostok (isolated), Dome Fuji (too high), Kunlun (too high)
- **Status unclear:** Byrd Station, some historical sites
- **Confirmed Arcanet subnets** (already named in lore): Palmer, Halley, Mawson, Mirny, Janbogo, Byrd
- **Primary refugee source for Concordia:** Dumont d'Urville Sea coast and Ross Sea/Pacific coast (closest to Concordia's longitude)
- **Rarest refugees in Concordia:** Antarctic Peninsula (Palmer City area), Atlantic coast (Belgrano, Neumayer, Halley)

## Notes for Future Development

- **Fort McMurdo's "Fort" prefix** — the largest pre-war city in Tepenia gained a military designation at some point. Was this during the Long Night War (last major defensive hold before falling)? Or did it always have a military character given its size and logistics importance?
- **Byrd Station** — low altitude (1,530m), West Antarctica. Does it survive the Long Night War? Could it be a secondary surviving city alongside Concordia?
- **Halley** — built on a floating ice shelf in real life. The city would have needed to address ice shelf movement. Unique structural character.
- **Zhongshan and Sinian Federation identity** — Zhongshan Station was named after Sun Yat-sen. In the game world, the Sinian Federation rebuilt from China's collapse. Does Zhongshan keep its name, or was it renamed under the Iron Federation?
- **Unified Korea stations** — King Sejong and Jang Bogo both belong to Unified Korea in the game. Jang Bogo → Janbogo is confirmed. King Sejong → Sejong is the natural carry-over, but open to revision.
- **The Arcanet subnet cities** — Palmer, Halley, Mawson, Mirny, Janbogo, and Byrd are confirmed as Arcanet subnet hubs. These were the communication nodes of Tepenia, which tells us something about their relative importance and size.
