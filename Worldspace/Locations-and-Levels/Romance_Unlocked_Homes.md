# Romance-Unlocked Player Homes

Homes in this file are accessible only by romancing the associated character. They exist in addition to standard player homes (see `Player_Homes.md`). For the full roster and district locations, see the cross-reference table in `Player_Homes.md` — Romance-Unlocked Homes section.

*Design note: All Doll characters use a charging configuration rather than a bed. The specific form varies by character — an alcove, a bay, a dedicated chair, or something else entirely.*

### Majyao Bisyugota — Taurus *(NPC, non-companion)*

**What it is:** The private living quarters attached directly to the back of her teahouse. The teahouse is for everyone; these rooms are for no one but her. The transition between them is seamless — she can move between the public and private spaces without stepping outside.

**Layout:** Compact and deliberate. A preparation area that opens into the teahouse on one side and the main sitting room on the other. Beyond the sitting room, a reading nook built into one wall and a charging alcove opposite it.

**The sitting room:** Low furniture — cushions, a low table, layered mismatched rugs each chosen individually over time rather than bought as a set. Dried herbs hang from the ceiling near the kitchen end. The walls are covered in handwritten notes, pressed plants, and a few pre-war maps of Janbogo she has kept since before the Long Night War. Small drawings she made herself — nothing framed, nothing mass-produced. A personal tea brewing setup on a low shelf, distinct from the teahouse's commercial equipment: this one exists purely for making tea the way she wants it, with no one else in mind. Labeled containers of different varieties in no particular commercial order — her own system. The entire space smells of dried herbs and whatever she last brewed.

**The reading nook:** Recessed into the wall, slightly lower than the rest of the room. A cushioned seat, a small lamp, and books stacked in an organizational system that makes sense only to her. This is where she goes when the teahouse is closed and the world narrows down to a single point.

**Charging alcove:** Built into the opposite wall from the reading nook, recessed and padded with fabric she chose. Warm amber light, consistent with the rest of the room. Close by — within her field of perception as she powers down — is one of her Janbogo objects: a small ceramic piece or a length of fabric that survived the Long Night War. The alcove was not decorated for anyone to see. It was decorated anyway.

**Memory objects:** Things from Janbogo present throughout the space, but none of it displayed prominently. You would notice only if you were looking closely and knew what you were looking at.

**The view outside:** A small private courtyard shared with the adjacent residential cluster — one of Taurus's typical insulated dome interiors. A modest garden patch she tends herself, primarily herbs, some of which end up in the teahouse. The sounds of the district filter in softly: domestic warmth, other households, the thick insulation holding the Antarctic cold entirely at bay.

**Acquisition:** Romance Majyao Bisyugota.

### Favi della Torre — Taurus *(companion)*

**What it is:** A residential unit in a Taurus dome cluster, chosen with deliberate care. Upper level if the structure allows it, or a corner position — either way, she has sightlines she chose. The home of someone who has thought carefully about every entry point and knows exactly what she'd do if any of them were used.

**Front view — approaching the home:** A neat residential facade in a Taurus dome corridor. Thick insulated walls, slightly worn but maintained — scuffed surfaces, patched insulation panels, functional lighting. No external decorations. The door is solid and well-kept, the landing outside clean and completely clear of clutter. Nothing about the exterior invites attention or announces itself. You would walk past it without a second thought.

**View of the outside — looking out from inside or from the doorway:** A residential corridor or open section of the dome interior, semi-maintained in the way all of Taurus is — not ruined, not pristine. Floor panels scuffed from years of foot traffic, wall insulation bearing the marks of past repairs, lighting functional but uneven in places. Quiet most of the time: domestic sounds from neighbouring units, the distant hum of heating and life-support systems, people moving through their routines. From her vantage she can see a meaningful stretch of the corridor and the entrances to several units she knows by heart. A community notice board in sightline — The Steady Watch posts information there. Within visible or short walking distance: a small neighbourhood cooperative or food distribution point. Not a commercial district; no storefronts in the conventional sense, but the practical infrastructure of a working residential community. She can see who comes and goes from here. That was the point.

**Interior — entry area:** Small and controlled. She can observe who's at the door before opening it. The transition from outside to inside is a single narrow beat — deliberate.

**Main room:** Organized with precision. Everything has a place and is in it. Furniture is solid and built to last — not luxurious but substantial and quality. Nothing fragile, nothing ornamental for its own sake. The arrangement of the room means she can see the door from wherever she sits.

**Information corner:** Physical maps of Taurus with her own handwritten notations — routes, entry points, assessed vulnerabilities, known faces. Some of this on paper rather than kept purely on a terminal; paper doesn't broadcast. Books alongside: technical, historical, investigative in nature. A terminal for cross-referencing data. The organization of this corner is dense but internally logical — she could find anything in it in the dark.

**Kitchen:** A proper cooking setup, not merely functional. She cooks — Italian heritage, and food is one of the few places genuine pleasure surfaces without the controlled perimeter around it. Ingredients sourced carefully and stored precisely. The kitchen has the same methodical organization as everything else, but there is warmth here that the rest of the room doesn't quite have.

**The sniper rifle:** Present. A dedicated surface with maintenance equipment — cleaning tools, lubricant, spare components, arranged in the order she uses them. The rifle itself cleaned with the same patience she brings to everything. It is not put away. This is not a weapon she hides from herself.

**Eyes of Gold presence:** Not a shrine. The faction is present in the structure of the space rather than in explicit display — the discipline, the precision, the specific way things are organized reflects a woman shaped by that loyalty. One small object may be present, identifiable only to someone who already knows what they're looking at.

**Memory objects:** Two photographs — one of her boyfriend (Italian human, Eyes of Gold member; name TBD), one of the elderly Italian scientist who built her and loved her as a daughter. Neither displayed prominently. You would find them only if you looked, and only then would you understand what you were looking at. *(Future AI asset note: both will require generated NPC portrait images when character visual development begins.)*

**Charging configuration:** The most defensible position in the home — back against a solid wall, door in sightline, lockable from inside. Functional before comfortable. She powers back up fast if she needs to.

**Rear view / back access:** A private exit she knows as well as she knows the front. Clear and uncluttered — no obstacles between her and the door. Possibly where she does rifle maintenance when she wants air, or checks the state of the corridor on her own terms.

**Acquisition:** Romance Favi della Torre.

### Villena Hiresvett — Leo *(companion)*

**What it is:** A unit in a Leo building that is part of the entertainment district's infrastructure — near her venues, possibly above or adjacent to one. Not a residential zone separate from the action; the building is embedded in Leo's performance ecosystem. She has been here since shortly after the war and has built it into a real nest.

**Front view — approaching the home:** Leo's theatrical energy is present on the approach. Signage, lighting rigs, other venues nearby, performers moving between spaces. The building shares a facade with the entertainment environment rather than standing apart from it. Her door is one among several — you'd need to know which one is hers.

**View of the outside — looking out:** Leo corridor or plaza, active even between events. Marquees, warm gold-and-neon lighting, other performers visible in the middle distance. The hum of the entertainment district is constant background — never quiet the way Taurus is quiet. From her window, the city feels alive in a way that is both sustaining and relentless.

**Interior:** Warm, full, deliberately comfortable. Big cushioned seating, layered textiles, a lot of color — warm golds, deep reds, the neon accent tones of Leo bleeding into the interior palette. Not chaotic but not sparse. She has been building this space for years and it shows: things accumulated with genuine feeling rather than decorating instinct.

**The music corner:** A microphone stand, at least one instrument, possibly basic recording equipment. The line between practicing and living is not sharp for her — the music corner is in the main room, not separated off. It belongs in the middle of things.

**Walls:** Performance posters — her own alongside performers she admires. Something from Palmer City and something from Belgrano among them: the two places that shaped her before the war. Neither is the centerpiece. They're just present, the way the past is present.

**Kitchen and food area:** Well-stocked, colorful, used. A 7w6 Self-Preservational takes abundance seriously — she keeps it supplied and she cooks or assembles things with genuine enjoyment. The kitchen has the same warmth as the rest of the room, maybe more.

**The grief layer:** Somewhere in the home is a moment of stillness that contradicts the general forward energy. A particular spot at a window, or a corner where she goes quiet. The home is emphatically forward-facing — she has kept moving since the war — but she has not outrun everything, and the space knows it.

**Charging configuration:** A deeply padded reclining setup — something that feels indulgent. She wouldn't tolerate something purely functional for the full charging duration. Comfort was part of the specification.

**Rear view / back access:** Backstage energy. Service corridors and loading areas shared with the venues — less theatrical, more practical. The side of Leo the audience doesn't see. She knows this territory as well as the stage-facing side; both are home.

**Acquisition:** Romance Villena Hiresvett.

### Naizelle d'Edjordoś — Pisces Fringe *(companion)*

**What it is:** Naizelle's personal quarters within the hab compound she anchors in Pisces Fringe. The compound itself is semi-communal — a community of robots and humans has formed around her stable presence over the years — but her personal quarters are the innermost private space of the complex, not freely entered by even the compound's own residents. The romance unlock gives the player access to this inner space specifically.

**Front view — approaching the compound:** Pisces Fringe sits at the edge of The Markets — darker and more utilitarian than the neon haze of the district proper. The compound entrance is marked but not advertising itself. Heavy-looking without being military: solid salvaged construction maintained by someone who knows what they're doing, not by civic investment. You would know it's there if you knew to look.

**View of the outside — looking out from her quarters or doorway:** Pisces Fringe in its actual state — dim, gray, the distant glow of The Markets visible but not close. Other salvaged and patched structures nearby. Parts of the compound's own external infrastructure visible in the foreground: power connections, material storage, the shared areas of the hab. Not ruined — maintained — but by one person's sustained effort rather than any civic system. The light out here is functional, not atmospheric.

**Her personal quarters:** Compact and dense. Salvaged equipment occupying most available surfaces — not hoarded for hoarding's sake, but because everything has a use or might. Shelving and tool-mounting on the walls rather than decoration. The space of someone who solved every problem the room presented and moved on. The Heavy Metal and Industrial aesthetic of her pre-war music life bleeds into the space through material choices — metal textures, industrial surfaces, things salvaged from environments with that character. Not styled; just what happens when someone from that background builds their space from what the ruins provide.

**The guitar:** Present and accessible. Not stored in a case — leaning against a wall or sitting on a stand. It is not a display piece; it just lives where she can reach it.

**Charging configuration:** Integrated into her workspace rather than a dedicated zone. She charges where she works — a practical position that doubles as a monitoring point. She wouldn't allocate a separate area to something the workshop position already handles.

**Emergency supplies:** Cached somewhere in the quarters — power cells, tools, essentials. A 5w6 Self-Preservational plans for the contingency that everyone else assumes won't happen.

**Pre-war memory object:** Something from her home city — specific city TBD pending pre-war geography development. Not displayed. Found only if you are looking closely, and only meaningful if you already know what you are looking at. *(Future AI asset note: may require a city-specific artifact or image asset once the home city is confirmed.)*

**Rear of the compound:** Where the actual salvage sorting and material processing happens. Workspace energy — the practical backend of everything the compound runs on. Less structured than the interior; more like an organized site than a room.

**Acquisition:** Romance Naizelle d'Edjordoś.

### Seica Cenilaithe — Scorpio *(companion)*

**What it is:** A residential unit within Scorpio's Goth community quarter — the sacred-transformation tradition, not the clinical rebirth infrastructure. The building is likely shared with other community members. The home is not large; it does not need to be. It needs depth.

**Front view — approaching:** Scorpio's dim, atmospheric residential-spiritual fabric. The building carries aesthetic weight consistent with the Goth sacred-transformation community — heavy, present, not frightening but not casually inviting. You come here with intention or you don't come.

**View of the outside — looking out:** Scorpio in its characteristic state — dim crimson and violet ambient glow, steam from the district's thermal infrastructure drifting through the air, the architectural presence of sacred and transformation spaces nearby. Archive buildings, community ritual spaces, the physical infrastructure of a community that takes death seriously. Not bleak — weighted. There is meaning being held here, not despair.

**Interior:** Deliberately enclosed. Heavy dark textiles — curtains, layered fabric — creating a sense of enclosure that is intentional rather than suffocating. The Scorpio crimson-violet light filters through rather than being blocked out. The space has weight from the moment you enter it.

**Her art:** Her own work on the walls — dark, emotionally direct, doesn't explain itself. Each piece feels like something she had to make rather than chose to make. Art materials present, possibly a dedicated work area — she still creates.

**The husband's presence:** A dedicated space for him. His photograph and some objects he left behind. Not a shrine in the performative sense — she is not in denial — but a genuine and permanent presence in the room. This is the emotional center of the home whether or not it is the physical center. She has not moved on and the home reflects that without apology. *(Future AI asset note: husband's portrait will require a generated NPC image once his name and identity are confirmed.)*

**The pistol:** Present and maintained. One weapon, treated with care and presence — not stored away.

**Charging configuration:** Deliberate in its positioning — facing something meaningful, either her art or the space where her husband's presence is kept. An Sx-8w7 does not charge facing a blank wall.

**Rear / work area:** Where the more private work happens — art creation, or spiritual practice she does not perform in front of others.

**Acquisition:** Romance Seica Cenilaithe.

### Ji-Eun Kim — Aquarius / The Labs *(companion)*

**What it is:** A hidden living space embedded in an unremarkable section of Aquarius infrastructure — a repurposed utility corridor, an overlooked lab section, a space that has never been registered as residential and does not read as one on first glance. She has been here for years and left no sign that she has. The player finding this space is part of finding her — the home is discovered through the questline, not before it.

**Front view — approaching:** Nothing marks it. The approach looks like any other section of Aquarius infrastructure — functional, utilitarian, not where anyone looks twice. That is not an accident.

**View of the outside — looking out:** Aquarius / The Labs from an angle nobody uses. The white-blue of the district's research infrastructure, humming test chambers in the distance, the clean functional aesthetic of the Labs — visible, but from a vantage that faces no foot traffic, no regular observation. She sees the district. The district does not see her back.

**Interior:** Compact and layered, developed incrementally over years of careful habitation. Research equipment and personal space occupy the same zones — the line between workspace and living space is essentially nonexistent. This is where her work has continued in secret. It did not stop when she disappeared. It simply stopped being visible to anyone else.

**The nanotech equipment:** The hardware and processes maintaining her concealment are present — running, as they have been running since she went dark. Not displayed as a feature of the space. Just present, the way a heartbeat is present.

**The 4 wing:** Despite the hidden, practical, workspace nature of the space, she has given it a deliberate aesthetic quality — something a pure 5 would not bother with. The space has been lived in thoughtfully, not merely occupied.

**Korean origins:** Something from Unified Korea. Her career was built there; her exile began there. Research materials, or something personal. Present but not prominent.

**The undelivered letter:** Somewhere in this space is something connected to the person she originally built the concealment for — the person who did not survive to receive it. She has been carrying this technology as an undelivered gift since before she went dark; in some sense the concealment running on her has always been for them. The letter — or whatever form the undelivered thing takes — is here. It is not on display. Finding it requires attention, trust, and meeting conditions she has not yet stated. *(See: Ji-Eun Kim — Design Notes, "The undelivered letter" gate mechanic.)*

**Charging configuration:** Integrated into the workspace — she would not allocate a separate zone to something the existing setup already handles. But she has given it a personal quality the 4 wing insisted on regardless.

**Exits:** Every exit from this space has been identified, evaluated, and has a contingency attached to it. There is no rear access she has not mapped.

**Acquisition:** Romance Ji-Eun Kim.

### Vosora Lashár Tanslock — Gemini / Janbogo *(companion)*

**What it is:** A workspace-turned-home in a quieter node of Gemini's information district. Vosora is a lifelong nomad who got stranded in Concordia during the Long Night War and never left. She did not choose this space as a home — it became one because years passed and it was where she was. That tension is present in the room.

**Front view — approaching:** Gemini / Janbogo's characteristic brightness and information-flow energy surrounds the building, but her specific position within the district is in a less-trafficked node — she's conducting sensitive investigation work and would not choose to be at the busiest intersection. Still embedded in the district's information infrastructure; not hiding from it, but not at its center either.

**View of the outside — looking out:** The Janbogo community and the data-stream architecture of the district — bright by Concordia standards, active at all hours, the constant movement of an information hub. She has learned these streets well whether or not she planned to stay long enough to. *(Lighting detail TBD — to be revisited.)*

**Interior:** The space of someone who set up for temporary use and stayed for years. Things arranged for function rather than permanence, worn in by time in a way she didn't intend and hasn't fully addressed. No strong aesthetic intentionality — this is a place that works, and has accumulated some meaning over years, but she hasn't organized that meaning into anything deliberate.

**Communications equipment:** Prominent and central — the transmission work, signals going out to off-world Tepenians. This is the legitimate, visible layer of what she does here.

**Data recovery workspace:** Terminals, storage media, damaged records in various states of reconstruction. The Great Corruption investigation work is present, but the most sensitive layer of it is not on the visible surface of the space. She is under pressure she hasn't disclosed to anyone. The room reflects someone who thinks carefully about what someone else seeing it would find.

**The 6 wing:** Contingencies are built in — exits assessed, backup systems present, the layout of the space shaped by someone who has thought about its vulnerabilities. Not fortified, but considered.

**Charging configuration:** At her workspace, minimal and no-frills. A lifelong nomad doesn't develop strong preferences about where she rests. She charges where she already is.

**Rear / less visible area:** Where the most sensitive communications and investigation equipment is kept — away from casual sightlines through a window or open door.

**Acquisition:** Romance Vosora Lashár Tanslock.

### Michelle Stanton — Gemini / Janbogo *(companion)*

**What it is:** A deliberately made home — not a workspace that became one by accident, but a space someone chose to inhabit and then made their own. Michelle is one of the few Concordia residents technically capable of leaving the city. She stayed anyway. That choice is the central fact of the space.

**Front view — approaching:** Gemini / Janbogo, but in a section that reads slightly against the district's dominant speed-and-spectacle energy. She's the slow-verification counter-movement operating inside the information hub. The building doesn't stand apart visually, but the corner of the district she occupies is quieter and less kinetic than the surrounding Janbogo pulse.

**View of the outside — looking out:** The district's information architecture still surrounds her — data streams, the constant movement of an information hub. But she chose to be here, which means she chose to be in productive friction with this environment. She built the bones of what she's now looking at.

**Interior:** Archival density — slow verification accumulates physical material over time. Records, cross-referenced data, the layered infrastructure of checking something carefully across years. More physical mass to the workspace than would be typical in Gemini, where information tends to move rather than accumulate. Her construction background is present in how the space is maintained and adapted: she built large things for decades, and the practical competence of that is in how the home itself has been modified and kept.

**The choice is present:** The space carries the quality of something deliberately made. It's settled in a way that reflects a decision, not an accident of circumstance. She was here before the war doing continental infrastructure work, and she chose to stop moving.

**Charging configuration:** More considered and personal than a nomad's — someone who chose to be rooted has thought about where she rests.

**Outside:** Concordia. The city she decided to stay in.

**Acquisition:** Romance Michelle Stanton.

### Kendra Heinrich — Capricorn *(DLC 1 companion)*

**What it is:** New lodgings Kendra finds for herself in Capricorn after returning to Concordia following DLC 1. This is not a home she is returning to — it is something she establishes from scratch. The player does not gain access immediately; after some in-game period *(up to approximately a week — TBD)*, Kendra sends a message that she has settled in and asks to meet somewhere public. The meeting is public because the romance may not yet be fully established at this point.

**Why Capricorn:** Her origin district and the base of The Reclaimed Record movement she helped seed before the war. She comes back to the district the workers there kept going in her absence, not to a home that was preserved for her — she finds something new within the community she helped build.

**Front view — approaching:** Working-class Capricorn — worker housing blocks, the sound and ambient heat of nearby production. She would not have chosen somewhere prestigious even with a reputation that would allow it. Her building is in the operational layer of the district, not in its hierarchy.

**View of the outside — looking out:** Industrial Capricorn in motion — forges, shift workers, the physical reality of a district that runs on output. She would find this restorative. After years of isolation at the South Pole, the presence of other people working is not noise; it is evidence that things are still functioning.

**Interior:** Functional and physical — space to move, nothing precious or decorative. Some accumulation of The Reclaimed Record: materials, documents, evidence that the cause continued in her absence. Not a shrine to her. Simply what was happening while she was gone, now in the same room as her. A few things she brought back from the South Pole — not many, she arrived with almost nothing, but what she carried out matters. The 7 wing gives the space some warmth; it is not cold. But it is organized around what she does and who she protects, not around her own comfort.

**Charging configuration:** A full charging bay — not a delicate alcove or a minimal setup. Built for someone whose body is a weapon and who has been sleeping rough for years.

**Acquisition:** Romance Kendra Heinrich.
