# World & Quest-Building Design Principles

This document records guiding principles that must be respected when building out main questlines, side questlines, faction content, world events, and character interactions. It is a living document — new principles are added as they are established. Existing principles are binding.

---

## I. Romance Questlines — Opportunity Distribution

### The Rule

For every romanceable character, and for every individual step (beat) of their romance sequence, the player must have **at minimum five distinct, plausible opportunities** to complete that beat during normal play.

**Why this rule exists:**

1. **Non-interruption of flow.** The player should never have to stop what they are currently doing, possibly travel across the map, and perform one specific action just to advance a romance beat. If a beat can only be triggered in one specific context, it becomes an interruption. If it can be triggered in five different contexts across the player's normal activity, it becomes something the player encounters naturally.

2. **Missed-but-not-failed beats.** A beat is "failed" only if the player actively does the wrong thing (e.g., pushing past what a character has offered, lying when the beat requires truth, backing down when the beat requires holding ground). A beat that the player simply hasn't encountered yet is not failed — it is waiting. The five-opportunity minimum ensures that a player who missed the beat in one context will encounter it again in another without having to track it deliberately.

**What counts as a distinct opportunity:** A different context, location, initiating condition, or in-game moment that could naturally produce the beat. Opportunities do not need to be identical in surface content — they only need to be internally consistent with the character and the beat type.

**What does not count:** Minor variations on the same single scenario. Five dialogue trees in the same conversation during the same quest moment are not five opportunities; they are one opportunity with branches.

### Application to Each Romanceable Character

The following arrays document five or more opportunity contexts per beat for every character whose romance beat sequence has been designed. These are stated at the level of context-type, not specific quest or scene names — quest and scene design is future work and may not yet exist. Opportunity arrays must be revisited when actual questlines are built to ensure the opportunities are still internally consistent.

**Important:** These arrays document *possible* contexts. Not all of them will necessarily exist in the final game — they are a planning resource to ensure that when quest content is built, at least five of the types described here are instantiated per beat.

### Vosora Lashár Tanslock (5w6 Social)

**Beat 1 — Engage with the investigation, not just with her**
1. During a main-quest beat that intersects with her investigation, the player asks substantive follow-up questions about the findings rather than proceeding
2. During a side investigation within her companion quest, the player notices and flags something relevant that she hadn't mentioned — demonstrating independent thought about the work
3. A downtime/rest moment where the player initiates conversation specifically about the investigation's implications, not about her personally
4. During transit between investigation sites, the player asks a question that reveals they have been processing the data rather than merely witnessing it
5. During a faction briefing or third-party meeting that touches on the subject of the investigation, the player's engagement or questions are visible to her

**Beat 2 — Handle a difficult revelation without flinching**
1. The investigation implicates a faction the player is allied with — they acknowledge it clearly rather than rationalizing
2. A piece of evidence contradicts something the player believed about pre-war history or the Long Night War — they hold that without minimizing it
3. A companion-quest scene delivers a revelation requiring an immediate player response — several options are available, most of which flinch; the beat passes with a specific subset
4. A spontaneous world encounter (witness, recovered data, survivor account) produces something disturbing — the player's reaction is observed by her
5. During a rest moment after a difficult discovery, the player does not attempt to soften the implications — they sit with it alongside her

**Beat 3 — Respect the compartmentalization**
1. The player has access to one section of her notes or files and does not touch another section that is visibly present but not offered
2. A downtime conversation arrives near something she has clearly decided not to discuss — the player does not press it
3. During companion-quest preparation, the player follows her lead on what is shared with other parties rather than going further
4. In a faction meeting where she is present, the player does not reference something she shared in private
5. The player is offered early access to a part of her investigation that would be available later if they wait — they wait

**Beat 4 — Intellectual reciprocity**
1. The player provides information from an unrelated quest or encounter that turns out to be relevant to her investigation — she did not know, they connected it
2. During a downtime conversation, the player offers an interpretation of data she has been analyzing from an angle she hadn't considered
3. In a location the player visited without her, they bring back a specific observation that advances her work
4. During a faction encounter, the player makes a connection between two pieces of evidence and states it in her presence
5. The player cross-references something from their own background or a player-selected trait that unexpectedly sheds light on the investigation

**Beat 5 — She starts consulting, not just informing**
1. She sends the player a message outside of operational necessity, asking what they think about a specific finding
2. She mentions the player to a third party as someone whose judgment she trusts on a specific aspect of the work
3. She defers a decision she would normally make alone until the player is available to discuss it
4. During a companion-quest moment, she explicitly asks for the player's read before stating her own
5. She shares an early working theory rather than a concluded result — trusting the player with the process, not just the findings

**Beat 6 — The culmination, within the work**
1. While working side by side on a piece of evidence, she states it — briefly, in the same voice she uses for the investigation
2. During a late-night investigation session, looking at the same data, it comes out as an extension of what they were already discussing
3. A moment during the investigation where they are both looking at the same problem and she says what she has decided
4. After a breakthrough in the investigation, in the immediate quiet that follows, it is said once and clearly
5. While preparing the next phase of the investigation, it is addressed as a fact that will affect their working relationship going forward

### Michelle Stanton (5w6 Social)

**Beat 1 — Engage with the Arcanet as more than infrastructure**
1. The player asks specifically about a design decision she made when building the Arcanet — why that choice rather than a different one
2. During a location visit involving Arcanet relay infrastructure, the player initiates conversation about what the technology was built to enable for people
3. A downtime moment where the player brings up something they noticed about how people in a specific district use the Arcanet — an observation she didn't expect
4. After a faction meeting where the Arcanet is mentioned instrumentally, the player asks her what she was actually trying to make possible
5. During an Arcanet maintenance task the player assists with, they ask genuine questions about the design philosophy rather than just the mechanics

**Beat 2 — Ask the real question (why she stays)**
1. A downtime conversation where the player asks directly and without preamble why she stays when she could leave — then listens to the full answer
2. After a companion-quest beat that makes her reasons visible, the player asks explicitly rather than assuming they understood
3. During an Arcanet maintenance run, the player asks what she would do if the Arcanet were gone — essentially asking who she is beyond what she built
4. A quiet rest moment after a difficult main-quest beat, the player asks what keeps her here, prompted by what just happened
5. A player-initiated conversation at her workshop with no operational urgency, beginning with this question

**Beat 3 — The Kharkovchanka moment**
1. A companion-quest beat where she teaches the player vehicle maintenance — the player receives it with appropriate weight rather than as a tutorial
2. A moment where the player applies what she taught them competently — she observes the result
3. A downtime scene where the player asks a genuine follow-up question about the vehicle that shows they understood the significance of what was shared
4. A situation during the companion quest where the Kharkovchanka is relevant to a choice — the player treats the knowledge as trust, not a tool
5. An optional player-initiated follow-up after the teaching moment, returning to ask something she did not cover the first time

**Beat 4 — Choose the city when it would be easier not to**
1. A main-quest decision point where Concordia's needs and the player's personal interest conflict — the player chooses the city
2. A companion-quest beat where abandoning a Concordia commitment would be the fast path — the player takes the slow path
3. A side quest requiring the player to pass up a personal reward in favor of a community outcome she cares about
4. An unexpected complication during another quest requiring the player to give up planned resources for a Concordia benefit — they do so without being prompted
5. A faction-related decision where the city's needs are deprioritized in the proposed solution — the player pushes back and reorients the outcome

**Beat 5 — The view from outside**
1. A companion-quest culminating scene outside the city perimeter in the Kharkovchanka, looking back — she invites the player to see it from where she always stands
2. A Sagittarius/Frostlands location outside the city's formal boundaries during a companion-quest beat where the vantage point is mutual
3. An endgame-adjacent moment where leaving is literally possible and they discuss it at that threshold
4. A rest stop during an extended mission outside the city walls where the view happens incidentally
5. A player-initiated request to take the Kharkovchanka outside the city together — she agrees, and the conversation happens during the drive

### Favi della Torre (6w5 Self-Pres)

**Beat 1 — Protect someone she cares about, unprompted**
1. A random encounter in Taurus where a member of her neighborhood network is threatened — the player covers them without being directed to
2. A companion-quest scene where someone she has been watching over comes under pressure — the player acts first
3. A main-quest beat in Taurus where protection of civilians is available but not required — the player makes that choice
4. An ambient encounter in any district where a vulnerable NPC she monitors is present — the player prioritizes that person's safety
5. A non-combat situation where the player accepts a reputational or resource cost to protect someone connected to her, with no tactical benefit

**Beat 2 — Don't push her pace**
1. A downtime conversation where the player answers her questions without reciprocally pressing for more than she has offered
2. A companion-quest information exchange where she provides partial context — the player works with what they have
3. An opportunity to visit or contact her outside of established patterns — the player waits for her to reach out instead
4. A quiet scene where the natural next move would be to advance the relationship further — the player holds the current level
5. A series of interactions across in-game time where the player initiates at a measured frequency, not pushing beyond what she has shown she is comfortable with

**Beat 3 — Tell an inconvenient truth**
1. A companion-quest beat where a small lie would be undetected — the player tells the truth anyway
2. A main-quest moment where the tactically smart thing to tell her differs from what is actually true — the player chooses truth
3. A downtime scene where an easy omission would cover something she would rather know — the player mentions it
4. A faction situation where the true state of affairs reflects poorly on the player's position — they tell her regardless
5. A moment where a prior statement could be smoothed over with a comfortable non-answer — the player corrects the record instead

**Beat 4 — The shared watch**
1. A companion-quest scene that involves literally covering a position together — the quiet settles naturally
2. A rest moment during an extended mission where they are both alert but there is no immediate threat — the silence is comfortable
3. A downtime moment at a Taurus vantage point she uses regularly — both present, neither needing to fill it
4. An overnight mission wait where they take turns watching — a shift where both are briefly awake together
5. A post-mission wind-down at her home where she does not make the player leave and the quiet holds

**Beat 5 — She names it, almost as an aside**
1. During a protective action in her companion quest, it comes out while her hands are still doing something else
2. After covering someone successfully together, in the immediate quiet, she says it while still watching the horizon
3. A downtime moment at her station while she is maintaining equipment — it is said once, almost casually
4. During a Taurus patrol while she is still in operational mode, the statement arrives as if it were tactical information
5. At the end of a shared watch, as the other person relieves them, she says it before they part

### Naizelle d'Edjordoś (5w6 Self-Pres)

**Beat 1 — Don't push past what she has offered**
1. A downtime conversation at the compound where an obvious follow-up question exists — the player does not ask it
2. A companion-quest information exchange where she gives partial context — the player works with what they have and does not request more
3. An invitation to the compound that does not include a specific area — the player does not go there
4. A salvage run where she explains the objective without explaining why it matters to her — the player helps without asking
5. A series of visits where the player consistently does not press, which is what eventually opens the next stage

**Beat 2 — Intellectual contribution**
1. A main-quest puzzle or investigation where the player's Calculation-based approach produces a result she had not worked out — she is present when it happens
2. During a compound maintenance problem, the player applies knowledge from another quest context to find a solution she hadn't considered
3. A downtime conversation about a subject she has been thinking through — the player introduces a framework she has not used
4. A salvage run where the player identifies a material application she was not thinking of — adds to the exchange rather than following her lead
5. A third-party negotiation or information exchange she is observing — the player's contribution is substantive enough that she notes it afterward

**Beat 3 — The question she wasn't prepared for**
1. A downtime conversation where the player asks about something specific she mentioned once in passing — she did not expect anyone to retain it
2. During a salvage run, the player asks about the logic of a specific choice she made — specifically curious in a way no one usually is
3. A rest moment at the compound where the player's question about her pre-war life is clearly prompted by close attention to detail she shared
4. A companion-quest beat that reveals something about her background — the player's question afterward is about a particular nuance, not the broad shape of it
5. A player-initiated conversation that opens with an observational statement about her — she did not know they had been noticing

**Beat 4 — The test of patience**
1. She goes quiet after a companion-quest beat — the player's one check-in is genuine and non-pressuring, then they wait
2. A downtime absence where she does not reach out — the player does not pursue, and when contact resumes it is on her schedule
3. A companion-quest moment where progress stalls because she needs time — the player does not attempt to restart it
4. An in-world pause where the player occupies themselves elsewhere rather than checking on her
5. A post-revelation period where the player continues normal world activity and allows her to return when she is ready

**Beat 5 — Her first voluntary disclosure**
1. A downtime conversation where she begins talking about something personal without being asked — the player receives it without reaction
2. During a salvage run, she mentions something about her pre-war life that is unnecessary to the task — the player notes it and continues working
3. A rest moment at the compound where she says something she did not have to say — the player treats it as given information, not an event
4. A companion-quest beat where she adds personal context beyond what the situation requires — the player does not comment on the fact that she shared it
5. A quiet moment after completing something together where she volunteers something she has been carrying

**Beat 6 — The culmination**
1. A compound moment, probably at the end of an evening visit, stated precisely and without excess
2. A quiet beat during or after her companion quest, in the space where there is nothing else left to address
3. A downtime moment in Pisces during off-hours, stated the same way she states everything — clearly, once
4. After a shared salvage run, at the end of the route, stated as a fact she has worked out
5. A moment at the compound in the dark, with the aurora visible, stated with the same exactness as everything else she says

### Villena Hiresvett (7w6 Self-Pres)

**Beat 1 — Don't be an audience**
1. A performance moment in Leo where the player pushes back on something she is projecting rather than appreciating it
2. A downtime conversation where the player asks what she actually thinks about something she just performed an opinion on
3. A companion-quest beat where the player declines the audience role and engages her directly — she adjusts
4. A social situation where she is working a room and the player steps outside the dynamic to speak to her as herself
5. A moment after a performance where everyone else is offering compliments and the player says something true instead

**Beat 2 — Bring something new**
1. A main-quest location or experience the player encountered without her — they share it in a way that is genuinely new to her
2. A cultural artifact or piece of information from another district that she has not encountered — the player brings it to her
3. A downtime conversation where the player introduces a concept or frame from their background that she has not considered
4. A companion-quest activity she is guiding the player through — the player introduces a variation she had not tried
5. A skill the player demonstrates that is entirely outside her domain — she is genuinely surprised by it

**Beat 3 — The moment she stops performing**
1. During her companion quest, after something difficult, the performance lapses — the player's silence gives her the space to be unperformed
2. A rest moment after a high-energy sequence where she does not have the energy to maintain the register — the player is present for the ordinary version of her
3. A companion-quest beat where the outcome is unexpectedly costly — she drops the presentation, and the player does not rush to restore it
4. An unexpected moment of quiet in a normally loud Leo context — she drops briefly, and the player simply stays
5. A late-night downtime scene at her home when she is between performances and genuinely off-duty

**Beat 4 — The loyalty test (6 wing)**
1. A situation during her companion quest where she would genuinely understand if the player chose something else — they do not
2. A main-quest beat where her needs and a pressing objective conflict — the player stays with her
3. An unexpected complication where diverting to address something that matters to her costs the player's current activity — they divert
4. A social situation where prioritizing her costs the player something in a third-party relationship — they make the call anyway
5. A resource or time commitment the player could reasonably decline — they do not decline

**Beat 5 — The future question**
1. A companion-quest culminating scene where she describes something she imagines ahead — the player responds as if they are in that picture
2. A downtime conversation about what comes after — the player's yes is genuine, not flattering
3. A Leo moment (performance, community event) where she asks the player if they can picture it — the answer matters more than the question suggests
4. An endgame-adjacent conversation where the future is genuinely uncertain — she asks about it anyway, and the player's answer is real
5. A rest moment at her home, under no urgency, where she asks what the player wants the next part to look like

### Kendra Heinrich (8w7 Social) — Special Case

Kendra's romance does not use the standard beat sequence. Her gate system is unique: Gate 1 is defeating the combat threat that defeated her; Gate 2 is breaking through her emotional exterior across the DLC. The five-opportunity principle applies to Gate 2 specifically — the DLC must contain at minimum five distinct moments where the player can demonstrate the behaviors that break through for a Type 8.

**Gate 2 opportunities (5 minimum across the DLC):**
1. A moment where she is frustrated and difficult — the player does not treat her as a burden or make her feel like a problem to be managed
2. A moment where she will not accept direct help — the player finds a sideways approach that preserves her agency rather than insisting
3. A moment where she is confronting her own helplessness — the player does not rush to fill it with reassurance or solutions
4. A moment where she challenges or challenges the player's judgment — the player pushes back without condescension
5. A moment where she is guarding something personal — the player asks about it from genuine curiosity, not to fix or resolve it
6. A moment where she has been wrong and knows it — the player names it without ceremony and does not press the point

2. A companion-quest beat where the emotional cost of what she does becomes visible — the player is present when it shows
3. A late-evening downtime scene at her base where the performance has run out for the day and the player is still there
4. A rest moment during travel between two community obligations where she does not have energy to maintain the front
5. A companion-quest moment where the question of whether it's enough comes up — the player is there when she asks it

**Beat 3 — The player stays**
1. The player is present during or after Beat 2 and does not try to encourage her or solve the problem — they are simply there
2. A companion-quest beat where the instinct would be to reassure her — the player does not, and stays anyway
3. A downtime scene where silence is the right response and the player gives it
4. A rest moment where she is in a difficult emotional space and the player's continued presence is the entire beat
5. A companion-quest wind-down scene where the player's only action is not leaving

**Beat 4 — The follow-through**
1. The player shows up at the next Warm Circuit event without being asked again
2. A companion-quest moment where she needed the player and they came through — this is the second time, not the first
3. A series of visits to her Leo base that demonstrate consistent presence across in-game time
4. A Warm Circuit initiative where the player volunteers follow-up work after the initial activity
5. A main-quest beat that takes the player through Leo — they stop to check in rather than passing through

**Beat 5 — The opening**
1. A Leo downtime moment after she has reached her conclusion — stated directly, a little nervously, in her own register
2. A companion-quest culminating beat where the follow-through has been established — she names what she has decided
3. A Warm Circuit event aftermath where it comes out naturally rather than staged
4. A rest moment at her base, nothing pending, where she says what she has decided clearly
5. A player-initiated visit during a quiet in-game period where she has been thinking and says so

3. A companion-quest follow-up beat where debrief happens at her home rather than a neutral location
4. A post-quest rest moment where she needs the player's input on something — they come to her
5. A player-initiated visit that she agrees to at a natural juncture in the companion-quest resolution

**Beat 2 — The kept garment**
1. A visit to the atelier where the player asks about the garment on the hook — she answers once and moves on
2. A companion-quest scene in the atelier where the garment is visible — the player can choose to ask
3. A downtime conversation about her fashion work that naturally arrives at what she keeps and why
4. A rest moment during an atelier visit where the garment is visible and the player's observation of it opens a response

2. A companion-quest beat that surfaces her backstory — the player's question afterward is about him specifically

### Majyao Bisyugota (4w5 Self-Pres — Non-Recruitable NPC)

*Note: Majyao's romance arc runs through repeated teahouse visits and her NPC questline rather than a companion quest. The five-opportunity minimum applies in the same way — each beat must be triggerable across at least five distinct in-game contexts.*

**Beat 1 — The first real conversation**
1. A player visit to the teahouse during which the player asks something specific about a tea, a detail, or something on the menu — the conversation opens unexpectedly
2. A main-quest or faction visit that passes through Taurus — the player stops at the teahouse and asks a particular question
3. An early companion-quest beat that brings the player to Taurus — they enter the teahouse and initiate genuinely curious conversation
4. A downtime visit with no operational urgency where the player asks something specific rather than transactional
5. A player-chosen rest moment at the teahouse between missions where the question is real

**Beat 2 — The return**
1. A series of main-quest or faction beats that pass through Taurus — the player consistently stops at the teahouse
2. A companion-quest period requiring multiple visits to Taurus — the player visits the teahouse on each
3. A player-chosen pattern of regular teahouse visits that establishes them as a known presence across in-game time
4. A post-mission rest pattern the player establishes at the teahouse
5. A sequence in which the player prioritizes Taurus stops even when efficient routing would skip them

**Beat 3 — The depth moment**
1. A teahouse visit during her questline where the conversation goes somewhere she has not gone with anyone — the player listens
2. A companion-quest beat in Taurus that arrives at her questline content — the player's response is to stay and listen rather than respond
3. A downtime visit during a quiet in-game period where the conversation moves to Janbogo or the war — she says more than she planned
4. A late-evening teahouse visit after closing when she is more open — the player is present and listens without directing the conversation
5. A Taurus questline beat that intersects with her history — the player's question afterward is about a particular nuance, not the broad shape of it

**Beat 4 — The Blood River Tea moment (optional)**
1. The player has followed the Taylor Valley/Frostlands thread and brings her news of Blood River source material
2. A Sagittarius/Frostlands quest that passes near the Taylor Valley area — the player returns with something connected to her supply
3. A faction quest involving Concordia's sourcing or agricultural networks — the player learns something relevant and brings it to her
4. A main-quest beat that takes the player to a location near Blood Falls — they return with what they found
5. A player-initiated investigation of the Blood River Tea gap — they pursue it independently and report back to her

**Beat 5 — The silence**
1. After closing, the player is in the teahouse — the aurora is visible through the windows — and neither of them needs to fill it
2. A teahouse visit at an unusual hour (very late or very early) where the normal patronage is absent and the quiet is different
3. A companion-quest resolution beat that ends at her teahouse — the player stays after the conclusion and the silence settles
4. A rest moment the player has taken at the teahouse, with no urgency, where she stays with them after business is done
5. An endgame-adjacent moment where the player returns to the teahouse and the silence holds the full weight of the arc
