# Concordia — City Logistics Reference

## Basis: Concordia Station, Antarctica

The fictional Concordia is a "city-fication" of the real Concordia Station — a joint French-Italian research station located at Dome C on the Antarctic Plateau.

**Real Concordia Station key facts:**
- Coordinates: 75°06'S, 123°21'E
- Altitude: ~3,233 meters above sea level
- Distance from coast: ~1,100 km | Distance from South Pole: ~1,660 km
- Temperature range: −25°C in summer to below −80°C in winter (average winter around −50°C)
- Annual snowfall: minimal (Concordia is a polar desert — very dry)
- Polar night: approximately 3 months of complete darkness in winter
- Midnight sun: approximately 3 months of 24-hour daylight in summer

**What "city-fication" means for Inner Tepenia:** The fictional Concordia is a full city with 13 districts and a mixed human-robot population numbering in the tens of thousands (exact figure TBD). It grew from the research station through planned expansion, refugee influx, and political circumstance related to the Falkland Treaty and the War of Upper Earth. The dome-and-tunnel structure reflects what was physically required to make permanent habitation viable at this location.

## Physical Structure

Concordia is an enclosed city. Almost nothing happens outdoors that doesn't have to. The Antarctic environment — cold, altitude, radiation exposure during polar summer, complete darkness during polar winter — makes outdoor exposure a managed risk rather than a normal condition of life.

**General layout:**
- Radial structure centered on Janbogo Central Hub (Axis Mundi / District 13)
- Districts connected by three major surface/tunnel highways: Hwy 110, Hwy 37, and Hwy 183
- Undergrid: the underground transit and maintenance network connecting all districts below ground
- Inter-district travel: transit tubes, Kharkovchanka shuttles (surface), or on-foot through the Undergrid

**Dome structure:** Most districts are contained under domes — pressurized, temperature-controlled enclosures. Domes vary in size and condition by district. Outer districts (particularly Sagittarius / Frostlands) have partially completed or damaged domes requiring ongoing maintenance.

**Verticality:** Many districts have multiple levels: upper dome levels, ground level, and Undergrid levels below. The Undergrid is not just transit — it is maintenance infrastructure, storage, and in some areas, habitation.

## Food Production

Concordia is self-sufficient in food production by necessity. Everything consumed in the city must be produced in the city or brought in at significant cost and risk.

### Hydroponics (Primary System)

The backbone of Concordia's human food supply. The Cancer (TBN) district contains vast hydroponic arcologies — multi-story growing towers using LED lighting, controlled temperature, and nutrient solutions.

**Primary crops:** Leafy greens (lettuce, spinach, kale), root vegetables (potatoes, carrots, beets), legumes (soy, lentils, chickpeas), grains (wheat, rice), mushrooms (grown on organic waste throughout the city, no light required), tomatoes, peppers, herbs.

**System vulnerabilities:** Hydroponics is entirely dependent on controlled light and controlled temperature. The failing energy grid directly threatens food production. If power to the hydroponic arcologies fails for extended periods, Concordia faces starvation. This is what makes the Aries energy crisis existential rather than merely inconvenient.

### Algae and Microalgae

Grown in bioreactor tanks throughout the city. Spirulina and chlorella are the primary varieties — extremely nutrient-dense, requiring minimal space and energy relative to output. The blue-green glow of algae bioreactors is a distinctive visual element of several districts. In Cancer (TBN), "glowing algae lights" provide both illumination and food production in the same system.

### Livestock

Primary protein source for Concordia's human population. Standard cattle are absent at any meaningful scale — feed demands, space requirements, and altitude-related physiological stress make them impractical. Four species dominate:

**Yaks** — The best overall fit for Concordia's specific conditions. Yaks evolved for the Tibetan Plateau (~comparable altitude). Cold-tolerant, high-altitude adapted, low-maintenance. Provide meat, milk (richer in fat than cow's milk), and yak wool. Cancer's (TBN) primary large animal. *Yak milk is what most people drink — cow's milk is expensive and notable.*

**Alpacas** — High-altitude adapted (Andes). Lower-maintenance than yaks; fleece is lightweight, exceptionally warm, and water-resistant. Provide meat and fiber. Cancer's (TBN) secondary large herd animal, with secondary population in the Frostlands.

**Sheep** — Hardy enough for enclosed heated environments. Multi-resource: wool, milk, meat. Wool production complements alpaca fleece supply. Largest population in Cancer (TBN).

**Reindeer** — Cold-adapted rather than altitude-adapted; the only large animal with significant Frostlands presence. Tolerates temperatures far below what requires full climate control. The most practical large animal for the Frostlands' partially open dome sections. Provide meat, milk, and hide. *Reindeer hide is among the warmest natural insulating materials known.*

**Supporting species:**
- **Pigs** — Eat organic waste, convert to protein; the city's biological waste processor as much as a food source
- **Chickens** — Compact, egg-productive, consistent; small operations distributed across many districts
- **Rabbits** — Efficient feed-to-protein conversion; secondary protein source in small facilities
- **Cattle (limited, premium)** — Present but rare. Altitude creates genuine physiological stress at 3,233m (reduced feed efficiency, brisket disease). Only small, hardy breeds: Highland, Dexter, Galloway. Kept under premium conditions in Cancer's (TBN) most controlled paddock sections. *Beef as a regular food item essentially does not exist — it is a rare-occasion product or status marker.*
- **Insects** — Mealworms, crickets, black soldier flies. Almost no space or energy required; fed on organic waste; extremely dense protein per gram. Cultural acceptance varies by district.

**Geographic placement:**
- *Cancer (TBN) (primary):* Enclosed paddocks integrated with hydroponic arcologies. Inedible hydroponic biomass becomes animal feed; animal waste becomes nutrient solution for hydroponics. Nothing is discarded. Yaks, alpacas, and sheep primarily here.
- *Frostlands / Sagittarius (secondary):* Semi-exposed paddocks that would be a liability for human habitation become an asset for reindeer. Large cold-adapted animals moving through the outer edges of the city. The Frostlands' pastoral character is significant to its visual identity.

### Aquaculture

Indoor fish farming despite being ~1,100 km from the coast. Fish farming does not require ocean proximity — it requires water, controlled temperature, and feed.

**Species farmed:** Tilapia (extremely hardy, fast-growing), catfish (hardy, efficient), trout (higher quality, more resource-intensive; likely a premium product).

**Antarctic fish:** Limited imports of Antarctic seafood (toothfish/Antarctic sea bass, krill, icefish) via highway trade with coastal settlements. Relatively expensive and intermittent.

### External Food Supply

The Port of Concordia (small trucking docks, Cancer (TBN)) and the highway network provide limited external supply lines. Janbogo is a referenced source of supplies. External supply is supplemental, not essential — the city cannot survive on imports alone.

### The Siligel System (Robot Food)

Siligel is a synthesized food source for the robot population — not lubricant, not blood, not a structural fluid. Produced from mined precursor materials. Siligel is **lethal to humans**.

Production is centered in the Capricorn (TBN) industrial zone (siligel synthesis facilities and precursor quarries). The Siligel Purists faction is concerned with quality control. Illegal siligel refineries operate in the Black Market district.

The human and robot food systems are entirely separate — the city must maintain two parallel food supply chains, both dependent on energy, both with distinct vulnerabilities.

## Water Supply

Antarctica is the world's largest freshwater reservoir — almost all locked in ice. For Concordia, water supply is not a scarcity problem under normal conditions. It is an energy problem.

**Sources:**
- **Ice melting** — Primary source. Surface ice and snow collected and melted using waste heat from power systems (particularly geothermal). The failing grid makes this increasingly expensive in energy terms.
- **Subglacial water** — Dome C is near the Antarctic Plateau's subglacial lake system. Concordia may have access to subglacial freshwater through deep drilling (TBD in lore).
- **Water recycling** — All wastewater is recycled. The city's water system is closed-loop by design.

## Energy

The energy system is the central crisis of the game. See main story files for the full crisis design.

**Primary sources:**
- **Geothermal** — The Aries district (Power Core) contains massive geothermal pipes and reactor cores. The Antarctic Plateau has geothermal heat access through deep drilling. Primary heating and power source.
- **Nuclear** — Present in the power core system. Glowing reactor cores referenced in district description.
- **Wind** — The Antarctic Plateau experiences extreme katabatic winds. Wind power generation likely present on the city's exterior.

**The crisis:** The central grid is failing. Every city system that depends on power is affected — hydroponics, water processing, climate control, Undergrid transit. The energy crisis is not an inconvenience; it is a slow civilizational failure.

**The Lattice alternative:** A decentralized parallel power network exists (the Lattice / Bonded Lattice faction). Cross-district coverage is growing but not yet sufficient to replace the central grid.

## Internal Transportation

**Transit tubes:** Fast, enclosed transit between major points. High energy cost; frequency and reliability reduced by the energy crisis.

**Kharkovchanka shuttles:** Real Soviet-era Antarctic tracked vehicles — massive, enclosed, heated cabins on tracked chassis designed for extreme Antarctic conditions. In Concordia, these serve as inter-district heavy transport and surface travel between highway junctions. Slow, reliable, able to operate in conditions that disable lighter vehicles. A distinctly Antarctic form of transport.

**On-foot / Undergrid:** The underground network allows pedestrian movement between districts without surface exposure. Travel time is significant; the environment is hazardous in sections. Always available regardless of power availability (though lighting depends on power).

**Within-district movement:** Largely on-foot or by smaller electric vehicles. Cycling plausible in larger dome spaces.

## External Connections

Concordia is isolated but not completely cut off.

**Highway network:** Three major highways (Hwy 110, Hwy 37, Hwy 183) connect Concordia to other Antarctic settlements and the broader Tepenian Federation. Surface travel on the Antarctic Plateau is dangerous and slow — primarily via Kharkovchanka convoy. Highway maintenance is critical; the Neon Nomads (highway clans who live on the roads) perform a significant share of it.

**Port of Concordia:** Small trucking docks in the Cancer (TBN) district. Primary terminus for highway trade — a highway freight terminal, not a maritime port.

**Janbogo:** Another city referenced throughout the game but never visited. Regular supply convoys from Janbogo referenced in lore. Refugees from Janbogo have also arrived at Concordia. The supply relationship is significant but unreliable.

**Air transport:** Likely extremely limited. High altitude + extreme weather + energy costs make air transport impractical for routine supply.

**Off-world:** The Space Elevator that once connected Antarctica to Upper Earth was destroyed. There is no off-world supply or communication. The Vigil [NAME TBD] maintain signal towers attempting to contact the off-world evacuees.

## Manufacturing and Industry

**Capricorn (TBN) industrial zone** — primary industrial district:
- Siligel precursor material quarries
- Siligel synthesis facilities
- Robot manufacturing foundries
- Highway maintenance depots
- Eyes of Gold training grounds

**Key industrial outputs:** Siligel, robot chassis and components, metal fabrication, highway maintenance equipment, construction materials.

**Research and experimental manufacturing** (Aquarius / The Labs):
- Advanced Robotics Institute
- Experimental siligel and hybrid tech labs
- Cold-resistance testing chambers
- University of Concordia (Arcanet studies)

**The Reclaimers** provide a secondary manufacturing input — reclaimed and recycled materials from ruins and retired infrastructure.

## Healthcare and Social Services

**Medical challenges specific to Concordia's environment:**
- **Cold injury** — Frostbite, hypothermia; primarily affects people in damaged or exterior areas
- **Altitude effects** — At 3,233m, altitude sickness affects newcomers; long-term residents adapt but remain at elevated risk for cardiovascular issues
- **UV deprivation** — During polar night (3 months of darkness), vitamin D deficiency is a significant health concern; UV lamp therapy and supplementation are standard
- **Polar night depression** — 3 months of darkness has documented psychological effects on the human population; Scorpio's therapeutic community addresses this among many other issues
- **Grid failure effects** — As heating reliability decreases, cold-related injury and illness increase throughout the city

**Psychological/Therapeutic:** Scorpio's therapeutic community is a major city institution. The psychological cost of living in extreme isolation, under resource pressure, in an ongoing slow-motion crisis is substantial. Scorpio's services are load-bearing to the city's social cohesion.

**Education:** Schools and creches in the Taurus (TBN) residential rings. University of Concordia in Aquarius / The Labs.

## Climate Control

Everything inside the city's domes is climate-controlled. Temperature, air quality, humidity, and lighting are all managed systems.

**Heating:** Primarily geothermal, supplemented by Cancer (TBN) heating plants. As the grid fails, heating reliability decreases — outer districts with damaged domes most vulnerable.

**Lighting:** LED arrays simulate day/night cycles. During polar night, artificial lighting is the only source of light for the entire city. Algae bioreactors provide supplemental ambient light in some districts.

**Air quality:** CO2 scrubbing and oxygen production from hydroponic systems and algae bioreactors. The plants that feed the city also breathe for it.

**The failure cascade:** Grid failure → heating failure + lighting failure → hydroponic failure + water processing failure → food shortage + water shortage. The energy crisis is an everything crisis, and the reason it is existential is that every system depends on every other system.

## Commercial Activity

**Food and consumption:** Hydroponic product stalls and markets (Cancer (TBN)), meat and dairy markets (Cancer (TBN) primary; Frostlands for reindeer products), restaurants (all districts), siligel distributors (robot-facing, all districts), black market food operations.

**Fiber and textiles:** Yak wool, alpaca fleece, and sheep wool processed into insulating clothing — a survival necessity where a dome breach is always possible. Textile production centered in Cancer (TBN). Reindeer hide processing in the Frostlands for extreme-exposure garments.

**Technology and equipment:** Robot component shops (Capricorn (TBN) primarily), repair services (widespread — maintenance is survival), weapons dealers, Arcanet equipment (Gemini area), nanotech implants (rare; associated with Ji-Eun Kim's work and Eyes of Gold).

**Services:** Transit, information brokerage, therapeutic and medical services (Scorpio), arts and cultural services (Leo), memory archiving, legal and diplomatic services (Libra).

**Trade and logistics:** Highway freight (Neon Nomads, independent operators), black market (extensive, centered in The Markets / Pisces), Arcanet data trade (Gemini).

**Currency/economy:** TBD in lore. Likely some combination of resource credit (managed by Libra's resource allocation system) and informal exchange. The black market suggests a parallel informal economy alongside whatever official system exists.

## The Dual Economy (Human and Robot)

**What is shared:** Most services, entertainment, cultural products, and information are used by both humans and robots. Districts are mixed. Transit, governance, and most commercial activity serve both populations.

**What is separate:** Food systems are entirely separate (human food vs. Siligel). Robot maintenance and repair services are specialist industries. Robot religious practice and human religious practice exist in parallel.

**The Bonded Lattice dynamic:** The faction advocates for robot-human pair bonding and structural support for bonded pairs in the city's resource allocation. The Taurus (TBN) residential rings are a physical expression of this philosophy. The economics of the paired household — resource allocation, heating credits, Siligel vs. food budgeting — constitute a distinct area of city logistics.

## The Polar Calendar

**Polar night (~3 months, approximately April–July):**
- Complete darkness; no natural light reaches the surface
- All lighting artificial, consuming significant power
- Psychological effects on human population are substantial and documented
- Outdoor activity essentially ceases for non-essential purposes
- Eternal Winter Collective / Frost Ascetics treat this period as spiritually significant
- Maximum stress on the energy grid (heating + full artificial lighting simultaneously)

**Polar day / midnight sun (~3 months, approximately November–January):**
- 24-hour daylight; sleep cycles require active management (blackout systems in habitation areas)
- Solar exposure on dome exteriors; some energy recapture possible
- Psychological lift for human population is real and notable
- Outdoor activity theoretically possible but still limited by cold

**Transition periods:** The in-between months are the most variably active for surface travel and highway operations. Neon Nomads and highway trade peak during these windows.

## Key Vulnerabilities

1. **Energy** — the central crisis; everything else cascades from here
2. **Food supply** — entirely dependent on controlled environments and therefore on energy
3. **Water** — energy-dependent ice melting; recycling mitigates but does not eliminate
4. **Climate control** — dome failure = exposure to −50°C+ environments
5. **External supply chain** — the Janbogo highway is unreliable; if it closes, specific imports become unavailable
6. **Mental health** — polar isolation + resource crisis + polar night creates compounding psychological pressure on the human population
7. **Siligel supply integrity** — separate vulnerability specific to the robot population; grid failure affects Siligel production as it affects everything else
8. **Dome structural integrity** — particularly in outer districts; storm damage and deferred maintenance create ongoing vulnerability
