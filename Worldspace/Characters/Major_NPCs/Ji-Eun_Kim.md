# Ji-Eun Kim

**Status:** Major NPC — main game vs. DLC inclusion TBD (developer preference is main game)
**Species:** Robot
**Origin:** Unified Korea
**Current status:** Unknown — location unknown, believed alive by Calethina
**Primary connection:** Eyes of Gold (friend and technical partner, not a member)
**Secondary connection:** Calethina (Calethina knows her facility and tracks her)

---

## Background

Ji-Eun Kim is a master nanotech engineer — one of the most technically accomplished practitioners of nanotechnology in Concordia and possibly in the broader Tepenian Federation. She is a robot.

### Unified Korea

Ji-Eun was born and trained in Unified Korea, a geopolitical entity that emerged from the reunification of the Korean peninsula at some point before the War of Upper Earth. The specifics of Unified Korea's formation and its relationship to the broader world geopolitical order are TBD in the game's lore, but its existence establishes that significant political reorganization occurred in East Asia during the period leading up to the War.

In Unified Korea during the period immediately preceding the War of Upper Earth, **anti-robot sentiment reached an all-time high**. The specific triggers for this sentiment are TBD, but the cultural and political environment was one in which robots faced systematic discrimination, professional exclusion, and social hostility.

Ji-Eun fought her way to the top of her field in this environment. Not easily. The specific nature of what she overcame and how she overcame it is part of her character's backstory that deserves full development — it shaped who she is, how she works, and how she relates to institutions and authority.

### The Falkland Treaty and Exile to Antarctica

Following the War of Upper Earth, the Falkland Treaty (June 21, 2564) mandated the exile of robots as a class from Upper Earth to the newly established Federation of Tepenia. Ji-Eun was not named in the treaty. She was exiled because she is a robot — the provision was blanket, applying to every robot regardless of individual circumstance, profession, or conduct. She was not singled out as dangerous or notable. She was simply a robot, and that was sufficient.

She did not choose to come to Antarctica. She was sent here. That distinction — between exile and emigration — is a fact she carries.

### Work in Antarctica

Rather than abandoning her work, Ji-Eun continued her nanotech research after arriving in Antarctica. She established a testing facility — now in ruins — and continued her work on nanotech implants. The specifics of what she was testing, for whom, and toward what ends are part of her character questline.

Her most publicly known contribution to Concordia's world was her engineering of the **Eyes of Gold's gold ocular prosthetics** — the surgical eye implants that serve as the faction's rite of passage. She was a friend to the Eyes of Gold without ever being a member: she provided technical capability they couldn't build in-house, and the relationship was one of mutual benefit and genuine respect.

## Relationship to Calethina

Calethina knows Ji-Eun's testing facility and knows what it was used for. During the "Echoes of the Bridge" questline, Calethina narrates the ruins of Ji-Eun's facility to the player:

> *"These ruins were once the facility of Ji-Eun, used for testing nanotech implants. Ji-Eun may still be alive."*

Calethina is not simply reciting archival information. She is tracking Ji-Eun specifically. This implies either a prior relationship between Calethina and Ji-Eun, a specific reason Calethina considers Ji-Eun's survival significant, or both. The nature of this connection is a questline detail that should be developed during the "Echoes of the Bridge" design process.

The fact that Calethina believes Ji-Eun "may still be alive" — rather than stating Ji-Eun's confirmed survival or confirmed death — indicates that Ji-Eun's current status is unknown even to Calethina, and that her disappearance (presumably during or after the Long Night War) was not accompanied by a confirmed death.

## The Three-Way Connection

Ji-Eun Kim sits at the intersection of three significant game elements:

1. **Calethina's questline ("Echoes of the Bridge")** — Ji-Eun is the first named thread. Finding her (or finding what happened to her) is the questline's first major active objective beyond exploration and narration.

2. **The Eyes of Gold** — Ji-Eun is the engineer behind their defining physical rite of passage. Her history with the faction connects the player's Eyes of Gold storyline to her physical presence in the world.

3. **Nanotech implant technology** — Ji-Eun's work represents the game's highest-tier implant technology. The gold ocular prosthetic the Eyes of Gold offer at maximum standing is her design. Other applications of her nanotech work may exist and may be discoverable through her questline.

This three-way connection was not deliberately engineered — it emerged from the lore as it developed and should be preserved.

## Character Voice and Design Notes

Ji-Eun's background — a robot who fought her way to the top of a hostile environment, was exiled from everything she built, and continued her work anyway in the harshest environment on Earth — suggests a character who is:

- Deeply competent and aware of it, without arrogance
- Pragmatic about institutions: she has learned that they will not protect her, so she works around them rather than through them
- Not without warmth, but precise about where she extends it
- Someone who built a relationship with the Eyes of Gold not because they shared her values entirely, but because they were reliable, professional, and did what they said they would do — which, in her experience, is rare enough to matter

Her exile and continued work despite it suggest she values the work itself more than recognition, institutional standing, or safety. She is in Antarctica. She is still working. That tells you something.

## Status and Future Development

- **Main game vs. DLC:** Two options under consideration:
  - **DLC:** Starring role; the DLC is largely centered around her personal questline; she is located in a different region of Antarctica — not Concordia. This would explain why Calethina says "may still be alive" rather than confirming her survival: Ji-Eun left the Concordia area after the Long Night War and Calethina lost her signal. She would still be introduced in the main game through Calethina's narration and Eyes of Gold lore.
  - **Main game:** Ji-Eun is in Concordia and gets a full main-game questline alongside the Calethina and Eyes of Gold threads.
  Decision pending.
- **Current location:** Unknown. Her testing facility near Concordia is in ruins. If DLC, she relocated to another part of Antarctica after the Long Night War. If main game, she is somewhere in or around Concordia.
- **Questline:** Designed to emerge from the "Echoes of the Bridge" questline as its first major active thread. The player follows Calethina's knowledge to Ji-Eun's ruins, then follows Ji-Eun herself from there.
- **Eyes of Gold connection:** The gold ocular prosthetic surgery scene — the player receiving the Eyes of Gold's rite of passage — ideally involves Ji-Eun Kim directly, either performing the surgery herself or her designs being used by a surgeon working from her specifications. This would tie the Calethina questline, the Eyes of Gold questline, and the player's faction reward into a single moment.
