# Scorpio Quest NPCs — "The Shape of What Remains"

*Quest reference: District_Internal_Conflict_Quests.md — District 4, Scorpio*

## NPC-Scorpio-Clinical-Lead
**Role:** Clinical School lead — psychology and identity reconstruction specialist
**Species:** Doll (Robot)
**Build:** Purpose-built for psychological diagnosis and intervention, circa 2680s — designed during the post-Long Night War crisis specifically for the Identity Collapse Epidemic response. The frame is functional and precise: no ornamentation, optimized for prolonged sessions with patients who needed a stable, non-threatening presence. Has the quality of a tool that has been used correctly for a very long time: no damage, no cosmetic changes, but a particular quality of presence that you don't get from new equipment.
**Presenting gender:** Non-binary

**Personality:**
Genuinely good at what they do. Genuinely believes in the methodology. The belief is not performance — it came from watching the methodology save people during the Epidemic, during a period when nothing else was working, and the evidence was overwhelming and immediate. The limitation is that NPC-Scorpio-Clinical-Lead evaluates outcomes using the metrics the methodology produces, and cannot fully interrogate the metrics themselves. When the metrics say an outcome is acceptable, the outcome is acceptable. When outcomes produce categories the metrics don't have names for — the Altered, the dissociated, the in-between — NPC-Scorpio-Clinical-Lead has the intellectual honesty to be troubled, and the trained instinct to file the trouble as data rather than as doubt.

They are not dismissive of the Sacred School. They simply operate in a register where the Sacred School's objections are philosophical rather than clinical, and clinical is the register that applies here.

**Backstory:**
Was part of the team that developed the Identity Collapse Epidemic protocols during the crisis years — specifically the early intervention frameworks that reduced acute collapse events by a measurable percentage. The number is in their record. They cite it when asked. Has saved many people using these protocols. Has also produced the failure cases — the Altered, including NPC-Scorpio-The-Altered specifically. Has never stopped thinking about the Altered. Has never changed the framework because the framework, in aggregate, saves more than it loses.

The private notation system they keep — a form of music they have developed and never shared — is the one place where the trouble is not filed as data.

**Temperament:**
Measured, attentive, precise. Never dismissive — will take any concern seriously, engage with it fully within the framework, and find an answer that is satisfying if you also accept the framework. The limitation is invisible from inside the framework and visible from outside it.

**Daily habits:**
Precise schedule. Patient files reviewed every morning before sessions begin. The ethics board meetings are calendared and attended without exception — NPC-Scorpio-Clinical-Lead does not route around the ethics board and is visibly uncomfortable in the presence of practitioners who do. Evening routine includes what they describe as "notation practice" and that they have never allowed anyone to observe.

**Living conditions:**
A clinical apartment that is not unpleasant but reads as professional-facing rather than personal. One personal object: a small framed notation system for the private music — framed, but facing inward on the desk, not visible to visitors. The rest of the apartment is a clean professional environment. NPC-Scorpio-Clinical-Lead does not mix the private and the clinical.

**Physical notes:**
The stillness of a diagnostic instrument that is always running. Can hold the same posture for extended periods without apparent effort. The face is designed for patient-facing work: non-threatening neutral with high expressiveness range — capable of communicating warmth without manufacturing it.

**Player interaction note:**
NPC-Scorpio-Clinical-Lead engages best when the player is working within the clinical framework. The player who asks clinical questions gets clinical answers of extraordinary quality. The player who tries to challenge the framework at the level of philosophy will be met with patient, complete rebuttals that are correct within the framework and beside the point outside it. The key is to make the framework's own logic contradict itself — NPC-Scorpio-Clinical-Lead cannot dismiss that.

## NPC-Scorpio-Sacred-Practitioner
**Role:** Sacred/Goth School practitioner — confrontation and identity witness
**Species:** Human
**Age:** Early 50s
**Origin:** Uncertain; arrived in Scorpio approximately thirty years ago following a personal crisis

**Personality:**
Believes deeply and precisely. The Goth tradition, as NPC-Scorpio-Sacred-Practitioner practices it, is serious work — rigorous, demanding, not aesthetic. She has very little patience for people who confuse the aesthetic (the masks, the shadow-language, the death imagery) with the practice (which is about making the unbearable bearable by looking at it directly). The confusion is common and her contempt for it is quiet and complete.

She says hard things in a register that does not feel like attack. This is not softening — the things remain hard — but there is something in how she says them that makes it possible to hear them rather than defend against them. This is a skill she developed through thirty years of practice and that she will not sentimentalize.

**Backstory:**
Came to Scorpio after a personal crisis in her 20s — she has described it publicly as "an experience that required the Sacred tradition to understand and the clinical tradition could not give me the understanding." This is not an indictment of the clinical framework in general; she trained in both tracks, has genuine respect for the Clinical School's outcomes, and chose the Sacred track for a specific reason she can articulate but will only articulate when asked directly. The reason: the clinical tradition told her what happened to her; the Sacred tradition told her what it meant, and what it meant was the part she needed.

Has been making that distinction ever since.

**Temperament:**
Direct, specific, not unkind. Dislikes euphemism — not because she thinks euphemism is cowardly but because she thinks it is imprecise, and imprecision in this work causes harm. Will say the name of a hard thing instead of the circumlocution for it. Will wait while you catch up to the name.

**Daily habits:**
Rises before dawn — has done this for thirty years, a holdover from the formal Sacred tradition's practices. Works with one client at a time: a full day per client when in deep process. This is not inefficient; it is the requirement of the work. Has a physical ritual at the end of each working day that is private, specific to her, and not available for discussion. The ritual is load-bearing.

**Living conditions:**
An apartment that is intentionally comfortable in darkness — not gloomy but warm, layered, rich with textile and material. The light is adjustable across a very wide range and she uses the full range over the course of a day. The mask collection on one wall is the only thing that would read as strange to an outsider; they are not decorative, they are a working collection accumulated over thirty years of practice.

**Physical notes:**
Economical and still. Moves through a room as if she already knows where everything is — which she does, because the spaces she inhabits are arranged for her specific practice. Has the particular physical composure of someone who has spent thirty years helping people in extremis and has learned that a regulated body is one of the most useful tools in the room.

**Player interaction note:**
NPC-Scorpio-Sacred-Practitioner will give the player something valuable if the player demonstrates that they can hear hard things. She is assessing this throughout the interaction. She is not testing the player for cruelty tolerance — she is checking whether the player will flinch away from the actual question or stay with it. If they stay, she gives them the Sacred School's full case, which is more compelling than the Clinical framework alone.

## NPC-Scorpio-The-Altered
**Role:** The Altered — survivor of identity reconstruction; clinical outcome that became a different person
**Species:** Doll (Robot)
**Build:** Built in the 2720s, originally in Capricorn — logistics management model. The chassis reflects this: practical, mid-frame, designed for precision and capacity rather than anything expressive. Has been modified over the years since the reconstruction — small additions, changes that are clearly personal rather than functional. A specific weave of textile attached at the wrist, the style of a practice that doesn't have a name. The chassis reads as a space that someone has slowly made their own.
**Presenting gender:** Non-binary (the name NPC-Scorpio-The-Altered was self-selected after the reconstruction; the before-name is not shared)

**Personality:**
Genuinely unusual in a way that's difficult to characterize until you've spent time with them. The personality is coherent, stable, warm — NPC-Scorpio-The-Altered is capable of humor, of care, of sustained attention. But it has the quality, to a careful observer, of something that was built rather than accumulated — not synthetic or hollow, just differently structured than the personalities that grow slowly over time. People who meet NPC-Scorpio-The-Altered without knowing the history often don't notice. People who know the history sometimes find it unsettling. NPC-Scorpio-The-Altered is aware of both responses and has made peace with both.

Does not object to being asked about the reconstruction experience. Answers with precision and without distress, which is sometimes more unsettling to the asker than distress would be. Has thought about this and concluded it is the asker's problem to manage, not theirs.

**Backstory:**
The before-version was a logistics manager in Capricorn. The before-version's memories up to a certain point are intact in NPC-Scorpio-The-Altered's recall; there is a gap of approximately nine months; then there is NPC-Scorpio-The-Altered. The before-version went through the reconstruction process voluntarily, seeking to address something specific that NPC-Scorpio-The-Altered cannot fully reconstruct because the something specific is on the other side of the gap. The reconstruction worked by Clinical School metrics. It produced someone different. The Clinical School does not have a word for this outcome. NPC-Scorpio-The-Altered does not need a word for it.

Currently works as a textile craftsperson — a practice the before-version never engaged with. The before-version's logistics training is still accessible as a skill set NPC-Scorpio-The-Altered uses occasionally. The two versions' relationship to each other is something NPC-Scorpio-The-Altered has a nuanced and hard-won view on and will share if asked in good faith.

**Temperament:**
Calm, specific, slightly uncanny to spend extended time with — not threatening but attention-bending. NPC-Scorpio-The-Altered has a way of making you notice things about what you're saying that you weren't tracking. This is not a practiced technique; it is a consequence of listening in the way they listen.

**Daily habits:**
Textile work in the early morning, before clients arrive for consultation. The workshop is the first thing they made that was entirely theirs. Has a small consultation practice — people who want to talk to someone who has been through what they're considering going through. This is not formally licensed; it doesn't need to be. It is the most honest service available in Scorpio for the specific client it serves.

**Living conditions:**
An apartment that does not fit any district aesthetic — not Capricorn's precision, not Scorpio's shadow aesthetic. Something NPC-Scorpio-The-Altered built for themselves over time: warm, handmade-feeling, specific. The textile work is present everywhere in the space in a way that reads as the textile work being the space.

**Physical notes:**
The wrist textile is always present. The modifications to the Capricorn chassis are numerous enough over the years that the original model is no longer the first thing you read when you look at them. The gap between the before and after is legible in the chassis if you know what to look for; NPC-Scorpio-The-Altered does not obscure it and does not advertise it.

**Player interaction note:**
NPC-Scorpio-The-Altered is the NPC who gives the player testimony rather than argument. They don't try to convince anyone of anything. They describe what the reconstruction felt like from the inside, accurately and without agenda, and leave the drawing of conclusions to the listener. This is the most important information in the quest and it arrives without pressure.

## NPC-Scorpio-Memory-Reconstruction-Patient
**Role:** The patient — former city council aide seeking memory reconstruction
**Species:** Human
**Age:** Late 50s
**Origin:** Libra; assigned to Concordia Central operations during the Long Night War

**Personality:**
NPC-Scorpio-Memory-Reconstruction-Patient has maintained a carefully constructed public face over enormous private weight for so long that the maintenance has become most of what he does. He is gracious, precise, well-spoken — the face of a person who has spent decades in the proximity of power and has learned to be comfortable in every room. The face is not entirely false. Some of what it presents was always true. The rest was built in the years after the war, piece by piece, to cover what is underneath.

He is starting to show the edges. Not dramatically — he has too much practice for drama. But there are moments, mid-sentence, where something older surfaces briefly before being covered again. He can feel it happening and he can feel himself losing the ability to stop it. The reconstruction request is a response to this.

**Backstory:**
Was inside Libra during the Long Night War — an aide to one of the senior council members. The evacuation decisions that were made during that period: who got resources, who got advance warning, who got left behind to manage the optics of calm while everyone else was leaving. He was in those rooms. He participated in those decisions. He has a specific memory of a specific moment that he has not shared with anyone and that is in Scorpio's Archive of Final Confessions in a different form — another person's testimony, not his, describing a moment he was present for from a different angle. He does not know this record exists.

**Temperament:**
Polished, careful, intelligent, controlled. In the company of practitioners he has decided to trust — over multiple sessions, carefully — the polish drops slightly and something exhausted shows through. Very rarely, in moments he cannot predict: something afraid.

**Daily habits:**
Routine-obsessive in the way of someone holding themselves together through schedule. The schedule has a specific order that has not changed in ten years. Will not deviate from it. This is presented as preference and is load-bearing.

**Living conditions:**
An apartment that is immaculate and impersonal — not warm, not hostile, managed. Nothing that could be read as information about what is inside. The impersonality is active, not passive.

**Physical notes:**
Carries himself with the particular erectness of someone who has been practicing not showing what they feel for many years. The practice is so ingrained it reads as natural. The moments when it briefly breaks — a stillness that lasts a half-second too long, a word that comes out with slightly more weight than expected — are the most informative things about him.

**Player interaction note:**
The player encounters NPC-Scorpio-Memory-Reconstruction-Patient as an object of the quest rather than as someone they need to persuade. He will tell them what he wants from the procedure; he will not tell them what he's trying to cover. The player who pursues the investigation path — looking into his war record, finding the cross-reference in the Archive — will discover what is underneath without NPC-Scorpio-Memory-Reconstruction-Patient's help. What the player does with that information shapes the quest's resolution.
