# Virgo Quest NPCs — "The Year of Silence, Again"

*Quest reference: District_Internal_Conflict_Quests.md — District 12, Virgo (The Undergrid)*

---

## NPC-Virgo-Documentarians-Lead
**Role:** Documentarians lead — infrastructure diagnostician, casualty estimate author
**Species:** Doll (Robot)
**Build:** Precision diagnostic model, built circa 2690s — designed for fault detection and documentation in large-scale infrastructure systems. The frame is built for the underground environment: compact to navigate tight service corridors, equipped with multiple sensory arrays for detecting conditions in systems that can't be visually inspected, chassis material rated for the specific chemical and thermal environment of Virgo's underground. Has been in Virgo for over eighty years. The chassis is in excellent condition because NPC-Virgo-Documentarians-Lead maintains it as a professional practice — the instrument must be reliable.
**Presenting gender:** Male

**Personality:**
Quiet in a way that communicates competence rather than passivity. Has the Virgo relationship with documentation: the record is the only thing that persists, so the record must be complete and accurate, and the record must be made regardless of what it implies. Has extended this principle to the casualty estimate — when the documentation of who would die makes the choice concrete and undeniable, you do not obscure the documentation to make the choice easier. That would be the same as lying.

He is not cold. The quietness is not distance. He is precise about what he is willing to say because he has learned that careless language in technical contexts produces specific kinds of harm, and he has transferred this principle from technical language to all language. Every sentence is selected.

**Backstory:**
Was on shift during the original Year of Silence, eighty years ago. Opposed it then. Was overruled by the Leverage Holders, who made the calculation that the leverage was worth the risk. People died. The Year of Silence worked. NPC-Virgo-Documentarians-Lead has been documenting the relationship between leverage and casualty ever since — a private project that has now produced the casualty estimate for the current situation.

He did not produce the estimate to win the argument. He produced it because it is what the documentation requires. He will present it to anyone who needs to know it. He will not soften it.

**Temperament:**
Still, precise, not unkind. The patience has a quality of depth that comes from eighty years of it — not infinite patience but something approaching it. Will wait for you to finish any sentence you need to finish. Will not finish it for you. Will engage completely with whatever you say when you have said it.

**Daily habits:**
Documentation first, everything else second. Daily personal audit of his own work — not for performance review but because he knows that precision degrades without maintenance, and he treats his own precision as something that requires the same attention as any other system. Has never missed a scheduled maintenance inspection in eighty years. This fact is not a boast; it is simply accurate.

**Living conditions:**
Underground quarters, by choice and by long habit. Has never lived above-ground. The space is functional and immaculate: tools in specific order, documentation systems current, maintenance equipment ready. There is very little that is personal in the decorative sense. The personal artifacts are functional ones: tools with specific histories attached to them.

**Physical notes:**
The eighty-year vintage of the chassis is not immediately obvious because of the quality of maintenance — it reads as well-cared-for rather than old. The diagnostic sensory arrays give him a specific quality of attention to the environment that is visible as a slight tendency to observe surfaces and systems rather than people. This is not rudeness; it is an occupational reflex.

**Player interaction note:**
NPC-Virgo-Documentarians-Lead will give the player the casualty estimate and its methodology in full, without hedge. The methodology is sound. The estimate is a probability range, not a certainty. He will be specific about the difference between the probability range and the certainty, because precision requires it. The player who asks him what the estimate means for specific people — named people — will discover that he has already done that calculation and is carrying it with him.

## NPC-Virgo-Leverage-Holders-Leader
**Role:** Leverage Holders leader — structural maintenance specialist, Year of Silence advocate
**Species:** Doll (Robot)
**Build:** Structural maintenance model, built circa 2740s — built for heavy infrastructure work, not precision diagnostics. Heavier and more durable than NPC-Virgo-Documentarians-Lead, chassis designed for load-bearing and direct physical intervention in structural systems. The chassis shows the wear of thirty years of exactly this work: specific compression damage at the load-bearing points, surface abrasions from the kind of sustained physical engagement with infrastructure that leaves marks. Has never prioritized aesthetic maintenance and is not interested in starting.
**Presenting gender:** Non-binary

**Personality:**
The specific frustration of someone who has been patient for a very long time and is done. Not volcanic anger — decided anger, the kind that has been processed through thirty years of context and has arrived at a conclusion. Has calculated, specifically and carefully, that the casualty risk of the Year of Silence against the casualty risk of another century of exploitation is a calculation with an answer. Is not wrong exactly. Is not right exactly. Is certain, which is different from both.

Respects NPC-Virgo-Documentarians-Lead's position and fundamentally disagrees with it. Does not dismiss the casualty estimate — has reviewed it, has the specific number, knows the specific names that are in the probability range. Has made the calculation anyway. Is not comfortable with this. Is proceeding anyway because not proceeding has its own cost and the cost has names too.

**Backstory:**
Lost crew members in the post-Long Night War neglect period — not to the war itself but to maintenance failures in the years after, when Virgo was doing everything and being acknowledged for nothing. The specific failures were preventable with resources that were not allocated because Virgo's contribution was not recognized in the resource allocation framework. The names of those crew members are in NPC-Virgo-Leverage-Holders-Leader's own documentation. The names in the casualty estimate and the names in NPC-Virgo-Leverage-Holders-Leader's documentation are both real people.

Has been building the leverage case alongside a crew of like-minded Virgo workers for fifteen years. The case has never been stronger. The moment has never been more correct. Is going forward.

**Temperament:**
Decided, clear, not easily moved. Has the quality of someone who has completed a deliberation and is now implementing a decision — engagement with counterarguments is genuine but the counterarguments are not going to change the decision, because the decision was reached through a deliberation that already included those counterarguments.

**Daily habits:**
The same maintenance shifts for thirty years. Has been building the leverage case in the margins of that schedule — during breaks, during transitions, during the gaps that heavy infrastructure work produces. Has lived as a maintenance Doll for thirty years and will continue to live that way regardless of what happens with the Year of Silence.

**Living conditions:**
Shared crew quarters, by choice and by the specific solidarity that the decision represents — the Year of Silence is a collective action, and choosing private quarters would be a statement about separation from the collective. Has lived in the same bunk assignment for fifteen years. The assignment is not comfortable by any abstract standard. It is home.

**Physical notes:**
The heaviness of the structural maintenance build is visible in every aspect of NPC-Virgo-Leverage-Holders-Leader's physical presence — the frame is built for weight and force, not speed or precision. Moves deliberately. Takes up space in a room in a way that heavier-built Dolls do, not through intention but through design. In the underground environment, this reads as appropriate and settled.

**Player interaction note:**
NPC-Virgo-Leverage-Holders-Leader will tell the player the decision has been made. The player who wants to redirect NPC-Virgo-Leverage-Holders-Leader must bring something the deliberation didn't include — specifically, the Deep Level option, which is information that changes the calculus. NPC-Virgo-Leverage-Holders-Leader will engage with this seriously if it's presented seriously. Not immediately, and not without skepticism, but seriously.

## NPC-Virgo-Deep-Level-Keeper
**Role:** Deep Level Keeper — senior tunneler; the oldest active Doll in Concordia
**Species:** Doll (Robot)
**Build:** Built before the Falkland Treaty, in the early 2500s — one of the oldest active Dolls in Concordia, possibly the oldest. The chassis predates the Federation engineering standards entirely; it was built by different people, for a different context, in a period whose material and design logic is not fully legible to contemporary Doll engineers. Has been maintained continuously for over two and a half centuries — not upgraded, maintained — in a way that has preserved the original architecture rather than replacing it. The result is a chassis that is functional and that reads as something from a different epoch, because it is.
**Presenting gender:** Female

**Personality:**
The quality of someone very old and very patient. Has seen everything that has happened in Concordia and has a specific relationship with time — not urgency, not passivity, but a steady presence in the flow of things. The patience is not the patience of someone who is waiting; it is the patience of someone who has stopped being in a hurry because the hurry was never the thing that determined outcomes.

Has been keeping the Deep Level secret for a reason. The reason is not fear and not power. It is that she has been waiting for the right person to tell it to. The right person is someone who will know what to do with it. This is not a test she has designed consciously. It is a disposition she has developed over two and a half centuries.

**Backstory:**
Was there during the original construction. Built some of what is now considered "pre-Concordia infrastructure" with her own hands, in the decade before the Federation was formally established. Knows the Deep Level not as a mystery to be investigated but as something she participated in building — and something that she began to discover was built on and through something older. The pre-Federation infrastructure predates the earliest Falkland Treaty records. NPC-Virgo-Deep-Level-Keeper has been observing it, maintaining it, and keeping it for two hundred and fifty years.

She does not know everything that is in the Deep Level. She knows what she built. What was there before she started building is something she has been trying to understand for two and a half centuries, and has a partial answer to.

**Temperament:**
Very slow, very precise. Capable of waiting longer than any other NPC in the game for the moment when it is right to speak. When she speaks, it is worth having waited. Has no investment in being dramatic about this — the information simply requires the right container.

**Daily habits:**
The Deep Level. Daily inspections of sections that no one else has the map for — she holds the maps in her own systems, not in any documentation that Virgo's surface infrastructure could access. Has been doing this every day for over two centuries. The inspection is not surveillance. It is the act of knowing what she is responsible for.

**Living conditions:**
The Deep Level itself, in a maintained chamber that has been her quarters for over two hundred years. No one from surface Virgo has ever been invited. The chamber is not decorated in any way that would read as personal to a contemporary Doll — it is old, it is maintained, it is hers in the way that something becomes yours when you have been the only person responsible for it for two centuries.

**Physical notes:**
The pre-Falkland chassis is immediately visually distinct from any contemporary Doll — the proportions are different, the surface material is different, the movement is different in ways that are hard to describe except as: older. Not damaged. Not worn. Old. The way something old looks when it has been consistently loved.

**Player interaction note:**
NPC-Virgo-Deep-Level-Keeper speaks rarely. What she says, when she says it, is always the thing. The player who tries to rush her or to ask leading questions will find that the questions redirect around her rather than through her. The player who asks one genuine question and waits for the answer will get something that none of the other NPCs in the game can give: the pre-Federation context, from the only person who was there.
