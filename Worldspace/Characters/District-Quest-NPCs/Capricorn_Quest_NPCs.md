# Capricorn Quest NPCs — "The Audit"

*Quest reference: District_Internal_Conflict_Quests.md — District 6, Capricorn*

## NPC-Capricorn-Merit-Establishment-Foreman
**Role:** Merit Establishment foreman — upper-mid spire, thirty-year veteran of the guild system
**Species:** Human
**Age:** Early 60s
**Origin:** Capricorn; guild family, three generations

**Personality:**
NPC-Capricorn-Merit-Establishment-Foreman is not malicious. This needs to be said first because every other aspect of his position in this quest flows from his not being malicious. He is thorough, competent, fair-minded within the framework he was given, and has mentored many people — humans and Dolls — with genuine investment in their development. When he is wrong, the wrongness takes real intellectual work to maintain, which is what makes it difficult to confront.

When confronted with the Audit data, he immediately locates the methodology problem. The methodology problem is real — the Audit has weaknesses. He focuses on the weaknesses not cynically but because the alternative — that the system that made him what he is carried a sixty-three-year structural bias — would require him to reckon with what his position means. He does not do this consciously. The cognitive resource required to not do it is genuine.

**Backstory:**
Guild family. Father was a foreman, grandfather was a guild member in Capricorn's early years. The path was clear and he walked it with genuine effort and genuine skill. Has been on the floor for thirty years at every level of his advancement — genuinely manages by doing, a philosophy he enforces for his reports. Has mentored nine people to advancement, of whom three are Dolls. He thinks of those three as evidence against the bias case, and he thinks of this honestly rather than strategically.

**Temperament:**
Reasonable-presenting, genuinely listens, moves slowly toward concession, and stops just before the concession that would change anything. Does not raise his voice. Does not become hostile. Becomes very procedural — very focused on the mechanism of how things are done — at the moment when the content of a conversation becomes uncomfortable. This is not conscious deflection. It is a trained instinct.

**Daily habits:**
On the floor every day — manufacturing line inspection, production review, crew check-ins. Has not had a day where he wasn't physically present on the floor since he can remember. Finds the floor stabilizing. Eats with the crew rather than in the administrative facilities, which he started doing thirty years ago because it felt right and has continued doing because it is one of the things about himself he is proud of.

**Living conditions:**
An upper-mid spire apartment that represents his family's trajectory across three generations. Has lived there for twenty years. Has made no cosmetic changes since moving in — it is already what it needs to be. Contains objects from three generations of guild membership: tools, records, certificates of achievement that he does not display prominently but that are present.

**Physical notes:**
Carries himself with the ease of someone who has been doing physical work for forty years and whose body has adapted to it completely. Large hands from the years before he was primarily administrative. Moves comfortably through the production facilities in a way that visitors from other districts rarely do.

**Player interaction note:**
NPC-Capricorn-Merit-Establishment-Foreman responds to the argument that uses his own values — the production excellence argument. He is trying to produce the best outcomes through the best methods; help him see that a biased sample cannot produce best-quality outcomes, only best-quality outcomes from the portion of the population the system includes. This is the one argument he cannot dismiss as ideology.

## NPC-Capricorn-Underground-Organizer
**Role:** Robot Rights Underground organizer — assembly-line Doll, thirty-year merit documentation case
**Species:** Doll (Robot)
**Build:** Assembly-line model, circa 2720s — built for precision repetitive tasks in Capricorn's production facilities. Mid-frame, optimized for specific manufacturing task sets. Has been in the same operational classification tier for thirty years. The synthetic skin shows the wear of thirty years of production work and no significant maintenance attention — the maintenance budget for her classification tier is calibrated to keep the equipment functional rather than to preserve it. She is functional. She is not preserved.
**Presenting gender:** Female

**Personality:**
The methodical patience of someone who has been collecting evidence for thirty years and has processed every emotional stage of that collection. The outrage was genuine and has been processed. What remains is precision. She is not performing calm — she is actually calm, in the way you are calm when you have prepared for something for thirty years and the moment has arrived and you know exactly what needs to happen next.

She has a specific, detailed case and she knows exactly which part to present at which point in the conversation. This is not manipulation. It is the professional competence of someone who has been preparing this case for three decades.

**Backstory:**
Has been in Capricorn for thirty years in the same production classification despite output metrics that would have advanced multiple human colleagues to the next tier. Has documented this with the specific care of someone who knew, early, that documentation was the only leverage they would have. The cross-reference between her personal records and the leaked Audit took three years to compile. The result is sixty-three years of systemic evidence reduced to a specific, legible pattern.

Has watched colleagues — Doll colleagues — lose merit challenges not because their work was inadequate but because the framework for evaluating the work was built around assumptions about Doll output that the Audit now proves were false. Has specific names. Has specific dates. Will share them when the moment is right.

**Temperament:**
Measured, unhurried, willing to sit in silence when the other person is not ready yet. Has an extraordinary amount of patience because she has had thirty years to develop it. Is not intimidating in the way of someone performing power — is intimidating in the way of someone who knows something and is in no particular hurry to tell it.

**Daily habits:**
Works her shift. Documents. Attends Underground meetings twice weekly — has been doing this for fifteen years. Has a network of contacts across five districts who have been documenting similar patterns; the cross-district dataset is something the Audit doesn't include and that she has been building in parallel. Maintains the network with specific regularity: check-ins, information exchanges, support.

**Living conditions:**
Mid-spire housing assigned by merit rating — accurate to the wrong rating. The apartment is organized around the documentation: everything has a place, every place has a purpose. There is very little in the space that is personal in the decorative sense. The documentation is what the space is for.

**Physical notes:**
The production-wear on her synthetic skin is visible — specific patterns from thirty years of the same task set. Does not conceal it. Moves with the efficiency of someone who has been doing physical work for thirty years and has reduced every motion to its necessary elements.

**Player interaction note:**
NPC-Capricorn-Underground-Organizer will give the player documentation if they ask and have demonstrated they understand what they're asking for. She is extraordinarily precise about what is documented and what is not, what the evidence proves and what it suggests. She will not overstate the case. This precision makes her more credible than any amount of rhetorical force would.

## NPC-Capricorn-Guild-Loyalist
**Role:** Guild Loyalist — human worker, thirty-year guild member, conscience-in-crisis
**Species:** Human
**Age:** Early 50s
**Origin:** Capricorn; arrived from Aquarius in her early 20s

**Personality:**
NPC-Capricorn-Guild-Loyalist is genuinely torn and is not performing it. She has the specific discomfort of someone whose integrity is actively colliding with their self-interest and who has not yet decided which one wins. She has believed in the guild system for thirty years because she has moved upward through it, and the movement has felt earned, and the feeling of earning matters to her. She is also watching someone she has worked alongside for fifteen years fail to move upward despite equivalent output, and the explanations she has been giving herself are becoming harder to access.

She is not a hero. She is not a villain. She is a person in the middle of a real moral crisis who is going to have to choose, and who knows that the choice will cost her something regardless of which way it goes.

**Backstory:**
Came to Capricorn from Aquarius in her early 20s because Aquarius's experimental culture felt unmoored to her — she wanted something she could point to, measure, build toward. The guild system gave her that. She took to it completely. Has been a faithful guild member for thirty years, has moved from lower to upper-mid spire through genuine work and genuine skill. Has mentored others, including several Dolls, with genuine investment.

Has been telling herself specific stories about why her Doll colleagues haven't advanced at the same rate she has. Has known, at some level, that the stories have been getting harder to tell convincingly. The Audit did not give her new information exactly — it organized information she already had into a shape she cannot look away from.

**Temperament:**
Careful, fair-minded, currently distressed. Will tell you what she thinks. May change what she thinks twice in the same conversation because she is actually thinking, not performing thinking. Does not hide the distress — it would be worse to hide it, because the distress is the evidence that she is taking the right things seriously.

**Daily habits:**
Has maintained her production routines without alteration throughout the crisis. The routines are load-bearing — they are what is keeping her functional while the larger question remains unresolved. Has had several longer-than-usual conversations with NPC-Capricorn-Underground-Organizer in the past weeks. Is not sure what she thinks about them. Knows they are changing something.

**Living conditions:**
An upper-mid spire apartment that represents thirty years of upward movement. Has accumulated the specific texture of a space that has been lived in with intention — not wealthy, not spare, but considered. Has a corner that is specifically Aquarius in aesthetic: a holdover she has never eliminated, a reminder of where she came from and what she left.

**Physical notes:**
Carries herself with the assurance of someone who has been professionally competent for thirty years and the slight heaviness of someone who is currently carrying something significant. The assurance and the heaviness are both visible, and the combination is the most human thing about her.

**Player interaction note:**
NPC-Capricorn-Guild-Loyalist is the swing vote and she knows it, and she is angry about being the swing vote because it means that her conscience crisis is now a political resource for other people. The player who acknowledges this — who treats her crisis as real rather than as leverage — will find her genuinely persuadable. The player who treats her as an instrument will find her very difficult.
