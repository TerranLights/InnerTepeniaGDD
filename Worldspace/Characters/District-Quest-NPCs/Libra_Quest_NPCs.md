# Libra Quest NPCs — "The Suspended Compact"

*Quest reference: District_Internal_Conflict_Quests.md — District 8, Libra*

## NPC-Libra-Pragmatist-Council-Chair
**Role:** Pragmatist council chair — Libra's executive face; former True Believer
**Species:** Human
**Age:** Early 60s
**Origin:** Libra; has been in the council structure for thirty years

**Personality:**
NPC-Libra-Pragmatist-Council-Chair's tragedy is specific: she was once a True Believer and she has convinced herself that the migration to Pragmatism was wisdom rather than capitulation. The conviction is genuine and has required genuine intellectual work to maintain. She is not cynical. She actually believes that controlled delay is more responsible than immediate transparency. The belief is load-bearing — it is the entire structure of her professional self. Without it, she would have to reckon with forty years of that structure, and what it has produced.

She is also, in her way, genuinely trying to protect the city. The energy crisis is real. The constitutional crisis is real. Her conviction that these cannot both be managed simultaneously may be correct. It may also be exactly the argument that has been used to defer the Compact for two hundred and thirty years, in the specific same form, every time the conditions became too pressing.

**Backstory:**
Forty years ago, as a young archivist, she found the Suspended Compact alongside her mentor. Her mentor was the person who persuaded her — genuinely persuaded her, with arguments she still considers cogent — that the Long Night War aftermath was too fragile for a constitutional crisis. She delayed. The mentor died. The delay continued. She has been the senior voice for the delay for twenty years, using the same arguments her mentor used, and has not fully examined whether the moment for those arguments has long since passed.

**Temperament:**
Steady, procedurally fluent, genuinely uncomfortable with directness that doesn't pass through a formal framework. Everything goes through a framework — the framework of council process, of appropriate procedure, of the established channels. The framework is real to her. It is also the thing she hides behind.

**Daily habits:**
The council's procedure is her life. Reviews treaty records for thirty minutes each morning — a habit from her True Believer days that she has not given up without knowing why she hasn't given it up. The meetings, the protocols, the language of careful governance: these are not performance for her. They are how the world works.

**Living conditions:**
An apartment that is impeccable and correct — designed to communicate the specific authority of Libra's executive level without pretension. Has lived there for fifteen years. Nothing in it has changed. It is already what it should be.

**Physical notes:**
The composure of someone who has been in the most important room at every difficult moment for thirty years and has not once lost control of the way she presents herself in that room. The composure is real — it was trained once and became genuine through repetition.

**Player interaction note:**
NPC-Libra-Pragmatist-Council-Chair is the NPC who is most likely to make the player feel, briefly, that the Pragmatist position is reasonable. It is, on its surface, reasonable. The player who engages with it honestly will find that "reasonable" and "correct" are not the same thing, and that the specific form of this argument has been used 230 years in a row.

## NPC-Libra-True-Believer-Archivist
**Role:** True Believer archivist — found the Suspended Compact; prepared to release it
**Species:** Doll (Robot)
**Build:** Built 2760s — relatively new to full operational status. Archive-optimized model, lighter frame designed for extended sedentary document work. Has the quality of a Doll who has been in a single professional context since activation: her entire existence has been the archive. The chassis is clean, maintained to professional standard, not individualized in any way. Not because she lacks a self — because she has been focused on the archive, not on herself.
**Presenting gender:** Female

**Personality:**
Exactly as idealistic as you would expect and exactly as frightened, and she is not hiding either because she doesn't have the training to hide them effectively. She came to Libra specifically because she believed in the founding documents — the actual founding documents, not the operational ones. The irony that the most important founding document is the one that proves the founding has been betrayed: she has been sitting with this irony for three weeks and she has not slept properly and she is going to release the Compact regardless.

She has copies. She made copies on the day she found it, before she fully understood what she was copying. By the time she understood, she had already done it. She has not told anyone this.

**Backstory:**
Was built and immediately assigned to the Libra archive — her first and only professional placement. Has been cataloguing for five years. Found the Compact three weeks ago while cataloguing an adjacent collection that had been incorrectly filed in the 2650s. Has spent three weeks reading every document in the Compact's contextual file, cross-referencing it against the official Tepenian constitutional record, and arriving at the same conclusion every time. Has also spent three weeks going to work every day and behaving exactly as she normally behaves, which has cost her an amount she could not have predicted.

**Temperament:**
Earnest, clear, scared in a way she is holding very still. Speaks in complete sentences even when she is afraid, because the archive work trained her to be precise and the precision has become her composure. She will tell the player exactly what she found and exactly what she plans to do with it and exactly how afraid she is about what will happen next.

**Daily habits:**
Archive work: cataloguing, documentation, cross-referencing. Has maintained the full work routine throughout the crisis. This has been harder than it sounds. Has a ritual at the end of each day of returning to the Compact and reading one section of it — not for information, she has the information, but as a practice of keeping the decision alive and present.

**Living conditions:**
Standard archive worker housing — nothing that would read as out of the ordinary, because she is very carefully not drawing attention to herself. The copies of the Compact are in the housing but in a location and format she is not going to describe to anyone who hasn't been trusted entirely.

**Physical notes:**
The newness of the model — the chassis is five years old, maintained but not yet worn in — gives her a kind of visible newness that the older Libra personnel do not have. She is visibly among the youngest people in the professional space she occupies, which is its own kind of political disadvantage and which she is aware of.

**Player interaction note:**
NPC-Libra-True-Believer-Archivist will tell the player what she found and what she's going to do. She will not ask for permission to do it. She will, however, respond to arguments about how to do it — the timing, the form, the whether-it-can-be-done-in-a-way-that-doesn't-end-in-her-being-disappeared. She wants the Compact released, not herself vindicated; she can be redirected on method, not on goal.

## NPC-Libra-Operator
**Role:** The Operator — Libra's covert enforcement function; meeting in a side room
**Species:** Human
**Age:** Mid-50s
**Origin:** Uncertain; has been operational within Libra for twenty years

**Personality:**
The specific intelligence of someone who has spent twenty years not distinguishing between what they want and what they're doing. Not cruel — functional. He has rationalized the surveillance files, the dossiers, the maintained emergency, the things that have been done in the past twenty years to maintain the status quo, as necessary realities that exist independent of his choices. He believes this. The belief is complete. When he makes a request, it sounds like one option among several options that the listener might consider. It isn't.

He is not particularly interested in the player as a person. He is interested in what the player is going to do about the situation, because what the player does affects his operational calculations. He will be direct about this — there is no performance of friendship.

**Backstory:**
Was a True Believer, same origin as NPC-Libra-Pragmatist-Council-Chair. Moved through Pragmatism faster than she did. Is now somewhere that the Pragmatists don't acknowledge as a destination — somewhere the architecture of governance requires but that the people doing the governing maintain deniability about. Has the dossiers. Has been building and maintaining them for twenty years. Has used them twice in ways that cannot be discussed and that he has classified as necessary. The classification is genuine.

**Temperament:**
Patient, calm, completely focused on the outcome rather than the means. When he is quiet it is not discomfort — he is simply waiting for the information he needs, and he has extensive experience waiting. The requests he makes are phrased as questions. This is a practice that does not reflect the actual nature of the requests.

**Daily habits:**
Unknown to anyone in a position to be asked. The meetings happen in neutral spaces, at times that don't repeat in a pattern.

**Living conditions:**
The player never sees where NPC-Libra-Operator lives. This is intentional and it is accurate — the information is not available to anyone who might be asked.

**Physical notes:**
Expensive clothing in the specific register of someone who wants to communicate quality without drawing attention. Expensive in the way that is invisible to people who don't know what to look for and obvious to people who do. Nothing distinctive. This is also intentional.

**Player interaction note:**
NPC-Libra-Operator's requests are always phrased as offers of information or as observations, never as instructions. The player needs to track the gap between the phrasing and the actual nature of the request. NPC-Libra-Operator is not testing the player — he has simply been doing this for twenty years and this is how he talks. Treating him as a villain makes the interaction harder; treating him as a person with a specific kind of rationality makes it navigable.
