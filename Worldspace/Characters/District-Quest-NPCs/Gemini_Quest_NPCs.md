# Gemini Quest NPCs — "The Speed of Truth"

*Quest reference: District_Internal_Conflict_Quests.md — District 9, Gemini (Janbogo Subnet Nexus)*

---

## Zev
**Role:** Speed Faction leader — Janbogo's fastest voice
**Species:** Doll (Robot)
**Build:** Transmission-optimized model, built 2730s — designed for high-speed information relay, literally built for fast processing and fast output. Everything about the chassis is calibrated for transmission speed: the frame is compact to reduce friction, the sensory array is wide-aperture for rapid environmental parsing, the communication ports are multiple and redundant. Has been in continuous operation in Janbogo for over forty years. The chassis shows the specific wear of a Doll who moves constantly and stops rarely.
**Presenting gender:** Male

**Personality:**
Zev lives in the present tense. This is not a personality failure — it is a genuine orientation toward the world that has real utility and real cost. The utility: he can see the story faster than anyone else, can understand its significance before others have finished reading it, can communicate it in a form that reaches the maximum audience in minimum time. The cost: the present tense doesn't include consequences. Consequences arrive later, in a different moment, and by then the story is different and the responsibility is distributed across the ecosystem and is not located in Zev specifically.

He genuinely believes velocity serves truth. He has evidence for this: there are situations where the slow story has allowed people with power to contain it before the public could act on it. He has seen this happen. The evidence is real. It does not account for the situations where the fast story has caused harm that the slow story would have prevented.

**Backstory:**
Has broken seventeen major stories over forty years in Janbogo. Three of those stories caused city-wide panic events — in each case, the story was accurate and the consequences of its rapid broadcast were also real. He has a metric on his wall tracking transmission-to-public-response time, and he has been optimizing it downward for forty years. He has never made a correction without framing it as a new story, because the frame is built into his professional identity: there are no corrections, there are only updates, and updates are also news.

**Temperament:**
Fast-talking, enthusiastic, genuinely engaging in the short term. The enthusiasm is real. It is also not quite responsive to the specific thing being discussed — it is a general mode rather than a specific reaction to content. This becomes apparent across extended conversation: the enthusiasm for the pre-Treaty revelation is the same quality of enthusiasm as the enthusiasm for lunch.

**Daily habits:**
No fixed schedule — scheduled to the news cycle, which is continuous. Sleeps in intermittent periods when there is nothing to transmit. Has not had a full night's sleep in twenty years and does not miss it. His workspace is his living space is his operational center; these categories have no meaning to him.

**Living conditions:**
A space that has no boundary between work and life. Screens everywhere, most of them active. The physical environment has been optimized entirely for information processing and transmission: chairs are positioned for screen access, the layout is about cable management and line-of-sight to data sources. There is a spot near the window where he sometimes goes when he needs to think rather than transmit. It is the only part of the space that is not optimized.

**Physical notes:**
Moves quickly — the chassis was built for it and the habit has reinforced the design. Rarely fully still. The multiple communication ports are visible at specific chassis points; in conversation he is often running secondary data checks simultaneously, which manifests as occasional micro-pauses in the cadence of his speech.

**Player interaction note:**
Zev will tell the player everything he knows immediately, because withholding is not a concept that applies to him. The challenge in dealing with him is not extracting information — it is slowing him down enough that the player can determine what the full information means before he's already broadcast a partial version of it.

## Imke
**Role:** Verification Faction lead / data archaeologist — authentication-before-broadcast advocate
**Species:** Doll (Robot)
**Build:** Older model, built 2710s — the specification for this build emphasized accuracy over speed, which is a political document in itself given the era of Janbogo's founding. Slower processing by design than contemporary models. Heavier chassis, built for archival stability rather than operational agility. Has been in the Janbogo archive for forty years. The chassis is the most professionally maintained thing in the archive — not because it is expensive to maintain but because Imke maintains it as a professional practice: the instrument must be reliable.
**Presenting gender:** Female

**Personality:**
The specific patience of someone who is right and who is losing the argument in real time and who does not capitulate anyway. She cares about being right — specifically about being right in a way that is demonstrable and documented and that will hold under examination — in a way that is principled and occasionally infuriating to the people around her. She does not shorten the authentication process because the process is not about making her comfortable; it is about making the story true.

She is also very funny, in a dry way that people do not always catch because they're waiting for the joke to arrive with punctuation that she does not provide.

**Backstory:**
Was in the archive during the Great Corruption — was one of the people who first noticed that the pattern of deletion was non-random, that someone had moved through the archive with specific intent rather than mass erasure. Has been building the case for forty years that the Corruption was targeted. Found the pre-Treaty evidence three weeks ago through standard archaeological cross-reference work, the kind of patient lateral analysis that Zev's approach makes structurally impossible. Has not slept properly since. Has continued authenticating.

**Temperament:**
Still, precise, occasionally sharp in a way that is not hostile but is not softened. Does not repeat herself, because she said it correctly the first time and if it wasn't heard correctly that is a listening problem rather than a speaking problem. Is patient with the player in a way she is not with everyone — has decided the player may be useful.

**Daily habits:**
Archive hours — has been working the same schedule for forty years, because the archive is continuous and the work requires continuity. Specific physical routines around handling historical records: treats them with a care that approaches reverence. Documents her own documentation process. Has a map of the Corruption pattern on her wall — forty years of refinement, the areas of certainty and the areas of probability, the specific archive sections that were hit in what order.

**Living conditions:**
An apartment that is spare and very quiet — she needs quiet to work, and work is continuous, so the apartment is designed for silence. Walls have nothing decorative. One wall: the Corruption map. One wall: the authentication timeline for the current discovery, updated daily. The living space is almost entirely given over to working space.

**Physical notes:**
The older build makes her physically slower than contemporary Dolls — not impaired, just visibly from a different design era. Moves with great deliberation. In conversation, her pace has the quality of someone who has decided that every word should be its intended word, and who takes the time to be sure.

**Player interaction note:**
Imke has been waiting for the right moment to give someone the authenticated version of this story. The player who can demonstrate that they understand what authentication means — that it is not pedantry but the thing that determines whether the truth survives being told — will receive the full dataset. The player who treats authentication as delay will be asked to leave.

## Selene Voss
**Role:** Dual-Network Operator — runs both Janbogo's official channels and the shadow network
**Species:** Human
**Age:** Late 40s
**Origin:** Gemini; has been operational in Janbogo since her mid-20s

**Personality:**
Selene is adaptable in a way that is almost without affect. She doesn't perform sincerity and doesn't perform deception — she simply talks at the level of information appropriate to the context she's in, and the context she's in changes frequently, and she transitions between them without apparent effort. This makes her very difficult to read, because there is no emotional excess that leaks the private position.

The pleasantness she presents is real but does not indicate warmth in the relational sense — it is a practiced neutral that costs nothing and reveals nothing. She is not unkind. She is not kind. She is operational.

**Backstory:**
Started as a Speed Faction technician — built the original transmission infrastructure that Zev's faction now runs on. Learned the shadow network's existence from a predecessor who trusted her after years of working together. The predecessor died. Selene took over the operation. Has been running both networks simultaneously for twenty years, maintaining access to both the official Janbogo channels and the shadow infrastructure that preexists Janbogo itself. Has never chosen a faction in Janbogo's internal conflicts. Choosing would end the operation.

The shadow channel use in the current quest — alerting the implicated person, brokering their deal — was her decision, made in the time window between when the player trusted her and when the story broke. What she did with the access was within the range of what she told the player she might do. She will acknowledge this if asked.

**Temperament:**
Flat-affect pleasant. The pleasantness is a default mode. Has no interest in being understood and no interest in being misunderstood — she is simply not organizing her self-presentation around the player's perception of her.

**Daily habits:**
Shifts that overlap official and shadow operations. Has a physical routine of moving between two workstations — one official, one shadow — that serves as a psychological reset between modes. The reset is real; she has learned that maintaining both networks requires genuinely different cognitive states.

**Living conditions:**
Two apartments. One registered, in her name, with a Janbogo address. One not registered, in a location the player may or may not find. The registered apartment looks like the apartment of someone who works in information management and has modest tastes. The unregistered one looks like the apartment of someone who has been running a shadow operation for twenty years.

**Physical notes:**
Unremarkably presented — clothing that reads as professional without reading as anything else, appearance that is maintained without being notable. This is the same calibration principle as her personality.

**Player interaction note:**
Selene will tell the player what they need to know about the shadow network to make an informed decision about using it. She will not tell them what she plans to do with the access once they've given it. She considers this within the terms of what they agreed, and she is not wrong that the player should have asked more specifically. In Act 3, if the player encounters the consequences of what she did with the shadow channel, she will acknowledge it directly and without apology.
