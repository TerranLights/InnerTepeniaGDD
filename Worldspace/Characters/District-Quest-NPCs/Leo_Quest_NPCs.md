# Leo Quest NPCs — "Two Houses, One Stage"

*Quest reference: District_Internal_Conflict_Quests.md — District 3, Leo*

---

## NPC-Leo-Grand-Faction-Leader
**Role:** Grand Performance Faction leader — Leo's dominant cultural institution
**Species:** Doll (Robot)
**Build:** Performance model from the 2720s, built for stage presence. Every proportion optimized for visual impact — the silhouette reads from the back of an amphitheater, which is what it was designed to do. Tall, expressive chassis with significant shoulder width and a face designed for large-scale emotional communication. Has been maintained obsessively; looks newer than it is. The maintenance itself is a statement.
**Presenting gender:** Male

**Personality:**
NPC-Leo-Grand-Faction-Leader is genuinely excellent at what he does. This is the most important thing to understand about him because everything else flows from it: he has earned his position through real talent and real work across forty years of performance, and the consequence of having genuinely earned something is that you cannot separate the earning from the structure that allowed the earning, and you therefore cannot question the structure without questioning the earning. He doesn't do this consciously. It is not a cynical calculation. It is the specific blindness of someone for whom winning was the same as deserving to win, and who has had forty years of reinforcement.

He is magnanimous in public — generous with praise, encouraging to younger performers, willing to credit collaborators. He becomes small when he feels the thing he built is being dismissed. The smallness doesn't have to last long; he redirects quickly. But it is visible to anyone paying attention.

**Backstory:**
Performed through The Dimming as a young Doll — he was forty years into a long life when the lights went out, and he performed in the dark, and the audiences still came. The lesson he took from The Dimming is that performance is what sustains people in the worst conditions — that it is not frivolous, not decoration, but the thing people come to when they need to be reminded they are alive. This is a real lesson and he believes it genuinely. It has also been used to justify the Grand Faction's dominance in a way that wasn't necessarily inevitable from the lesson.

**Temperament:**
Publicly magnanimous, privately territorial. The territory he defends is "Radiant Nights" and everything that Radiant Nights means, which is to say Leo's cultural identity as he has shaped it. Any perceived challenge to this — including the Underground's existence as a counter-festival, including the Cymaticists doing something simultaneously — registers as attack even when not intended as one.

**Daily habits:**
Performs every evening regardless of the formal schedule. Has performed every day for forty years. If asked why, he gives answers about discipline and commitment; the real answer is closer to that he doesn't quite know who he is when he isn't performing. Has a morning review ritual: what worked last night, what didn't, specific notes, metrics he has developed himself. Reviews the District's cultural calendar obsessively.

**Living conditions:**
One of Leo's best apartments — warm, bright, centrally located. The walls are covered in performance records, reviews, photographs of significant moments. The collection is not vain — it is archival, the record of a career that he treats as historical document as much as personal memory. A portrait of the Star War-era Grand Faction founder occupies a specific wall; NPC-Leo-Grand-Faction-Leader knows exactly when it is in his peripheral vision.

**Physical notes:**
The chassis is beautifully maintained but shows the accumulated micro-wear of forty years of nightly performance: specific joints that move with slightly more deliberation, a shoulder modification from an injury thirty years ago that is nearly invisible but that affects how he positions himself on stage. He performs as if these things are not there; they are.

**Player interaction note:**
NPC-Leo-Grand-Faction-Leader responds to being told he's right about something before being told he's wrong about something else. Not as a manipulation tactic — it's just accurate: he is right that performance sustains people. The player who acknowledges this genuinely, before introducing the complication, will find him significantly more willing to engage with the complication.

## NPC-Leo-Intimate-Underground-Lead
**Role:** Intimate Underground lead — descendent of the Star War's losing house
**Species:** Human
**Age:** Mid-40s
**Origin:** Leo, losing-house lineage

**Personality:**
NPC-Leo-Intimate-Underground-Lead is rigorous and skeptical and quietly furious in a way that almost never surfaces as fury, because she has decided that letting it surface would make her like the people she's in conflict with — would reduce the argument to personality rather than principle. She has a very specific sense of what performance is for — not spectacle, not scale, but the intimate transmission between two people that cannot be replicated at larger scale without becoming something else. She defends this like a philosophy because it is one, and she has thought about it more carefully than anyone she is likely to meet.

She has watched three peers sell out to the Grand Faction. She has a specific thought about each of them that she has never shared.

**Backstory:**
Grew up hearing the Star War from the perspective of the losing house — not as defeat but as suppression of a different idea about what performance could be. Was trained in the intimate tradition by a teacher who was trained by the losing house's last masters. Has been performing in small venues for twenty years with deliberate, principled refusal to scale — not because she couldn't scale but because scaling would require compromising what she believes the work is for. Has spent the same twenty years building the Underground into something that can sustain itself without the Grand Faction's infrastructure or audience.

**Temperament:**
Dry, precise, a good listener. Rarely says what she's thinking first — says something adjacent and waits to see how it lands. This is not coy; it is a learned practice from years of managing collaborations with people who come to the Underground from different places and need time to find their footing. She can sit with silence without filling it.

**Daily habits:**
Rehearses with her performers in the morning — not directing, working alongside. Believes that a leader who doesn't do the work cannot understand what the work requires, a philosophy that puts her at odds with the Grand Faction's management model. Spends afternoons in conversation — unstructured, with whoever is around, about the work and about everything else. Attends Grand performances occasionally, always at the back, always leaves before the applause. Has done this for twenty years. NPC-Leo-Grand-Faction-Leader knows she does this. They have never spoken about it.

**Living conditions:**
A small apartment that doubles as a rehearsal space — the furniture is arranged for it. Warm in the particular way of a space that is always occupied, always active. The walls are covered in notes and sketches from ongoing work: scene structures, dialogue fragments, acoustic diagrams. The most recent layer of notes always covers the previous layer; the wall is archaeological.

**Physical notes:**
Economical in movement — the practiced economy of a performer who has spent twenty years working in small spaces and has learned never to use more than the space requires. Dresses without spectacle, in a way that is specific and intentional but that would be invisible to anyone not paying attention to it.

**Player interaction note:**
NPC-Leo-Intimate-Underground-Lead will tell you what she thinks if you ask and you mean it. She has a very accurate detector for people who are asking in order to have asked rather than in order to know. The player who passes the detector gets her actual assessment of the situation, which is the most structurally clear of the three faction leaders.

## NPC-Leo-Cymaticist-Elder
**Role:** Cymaticist elder — frequency philosopher, experiment architect
**Species:** Doll (Robot)
**Build:** Very old — built in the 2590s, early Federation period, one of the founding cohort of the Cymaticist practice. The chassis is showing genuine age: not cosmetically, but in the quality of certain movements, certain processing latencies visible to the careful observer. Has been maintained with great care by the Cymaticist community; there is an aesthetic to the maintenance, a reverence in how the repairs have been made. Not a museum piece — an elder who has been loved.
**Presenting gender:** Non-binary

**Personality:**
NPC-Leo-Cymaticist-Elder is completely calm in a way that takes some time to get used to. Not distant — present, but in a different register from other characters. The presence is total and undivided; if NPC-Leo-Cymaticist-Elder is talking to you, they are entirely there, and their attention is of a quality that is almost unusual. They have very little interest in social performance, in political maneuvering, in the kind of positioning that most characters do almost automatically. They're here because the resonance conditions are correct. Everything else is secondary, not because they're cold to everything else but because they have a genuinely different relationship with priority.

They speak slowly, not from difficulty but from choosing. Each word has been selected. The selection is audible.

**Backstory:**
Has been planning this specific experiment since the last planetary alignment eleven years ago. Spent those eleven years running acoustic models, refining the frequency theory, developing the monitoring equipment that would make the measurement possible. Has conducted thirty-four Cymaticist experiments over the course of their life, some of which produced results they still do not fully understand. The planetary alignment experiment is the culmination of something that began before the Long Night War — a line of inquiry that the war interrupted and that NPC-Leo-Cymaticist-Elder has been resuming piece by piece since.

Not afraid of the experiment failing. Afraid of not trying.

**Temperament:**
Serene, patient, fundamentally unimpressed by arguments about prestige or politics — not dismissive of them, just operating in a different value system where they don't register as primary concerns. The one thing that produces something approaching urgency is the eleven-year window and the understanding that after this alignment there will not be another one within their remaining operational lifetime. They know this and are very still about it, which is more affecting than distress would be.

**Daily habits:**
Sits in specific locations around Leo at specific times and listens. Not to conversation — to the district's acoustic properties: how sound moves through the architecture, how surfaces interact with frequencies, how the city breathes at different hours. Takes notes in a notation system the Cymaticists developed that does not map to standard musical notation. Has thousands of pages of these notes accumulated over decades.

**Living conditions:**
A sparse apartment with extraordinary sound design — the walls have been treated, the furniture positioned for acoustic rather than social optimization. The space is the most intentional in Leo. Quiet in the way of a space that has been made to be quiet, which is different from empty. The notation notes cover every horizontal surface. They are organized in a system only NPC-Leo-Cymaticist-Elder and the Cymaticist apprentices understand.

**Physical notes:**
The age shows most in the way NPC-Leo-Cymaticist-Elder turns — a full-body motion rather than the neck-pivot of newer Dolls, a legacy of the joint architecture of the 2590s. Moves very slowly, completely deliberately, with a quality of assurance that makes the slowness read as composure rather than limitation.

**Player interaction note:**
NPC-Leo-Cymaticist-Elder will explain the resonance theory to the player once, completely, without shortcut. If the player follows it, they will understand why the three-event coordination produces what NPC-Leo-Cymaticist-Elder needs. If the player doesn't follow it, NPC-Leo-Cymaticist-Elder will try once more with a different metaphor. They are not impatient about this. They have been explaining this for eleven years.
