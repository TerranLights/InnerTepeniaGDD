# Character Concept Bank
## Reusable Archetypes, Factions & Questlines

These concepts were originally generated during character development sessions but were found to not accurately match the specific characters they were generated for. They are preserved here in full — any of them may fit a future character, NPC, minor named figure, or background faction that has not been designed yet.

Each entry notes which character originally prompted the concept and why it was reassigned.

---

## From: Naizelle d'Edjordoś

*Reassigned because: Naizelle actually lives in Pisces (The Markets) and is a scavenger/hab compound homesteader — not a Virgo undergrid specialist.*

### Archetype: The Undergrid Cartographer

A robot (or human) who has spent years — possibly decades — mapping the pre-Concordia chambers below the standard Undergrid, the so-called **Deep Level**. They work alone. They pass their knowledge through Virgo's oral tradition rather than formal record, because putting it in documents felt dangerous. They have been furthest in of anyone currently alive in the district.

They are not a Virgo maintenance worker in the standard sense. They are the person the maintenance workers come to when something doesn't match the official maps — because the official maps don't go deep enough.

**Personality fit:** Self-Preservational 5-type (Investigator). Withdrawn, meticulous, resource-conserving. Has built their security through specific, irreplaceable knowledge. Does not share freely. Can be recruited only by earning trust or by presenting a problem too large to solve alone.

**District:** Virgo (The Undergrid)

**Potential affiliation:** The Oldest Maps (see faction below)

---

### Faction: The Oldest Maps

A tiny, entirely informal knowledge custodianship — less a faction and more a secret chain of succession. A handful of people (robots and possibly some humans) who have been to the Deep Level and come back, who pass that knowledge person-to-person because committing it to any networked record felt like an invitation for the wrong kind of attention.

**Philosophy:** Some knowledge is safer in people than in systems. The Deep Level exists beneath Concordia's founding — what it is, what it was, and what it means should be decided by the people who actually know it, not by Libra or Capricorn or Janbogo.

**What they know:** The pre-Concordia chambers below the standard Undergrid. Origin unclear per Virgo district lore: "pre-Falkland war infrastructure, something older, geological formations that happen to be navigable." The Oldest Maps have not yet determined which. They may never.

**Size:** Extremely small. Four to eight people at any given time, across decades of succession.

**Tension point:** Whether to keep the knowledge oral indefinitely, or whether something has changed that requires wider disclosure.

**District anchor:** Virgo (The Undergrid)

---

### Questline: "The Oldest Maps"

**Core:** The Deep Level below Concordia predates the city. What was it? What is it now? And what does it mean that everything built above it — the utility tunnels, the power grid, the residential districts — was constructed on top of something nobody fully investigated?

**Structure:** Goes deeper. Literally. Each stage of the questline takes the player further into the pre-Concordia chambers, finding things that progressively recontextualize Concordia's founding history.

**Themes:**
- The burden of knowledge nobody else wants or is ready for
- What a city's identity costs when you find out what it is built on — literally and metaphorically
- The relationship between what is preserved and what is hidden

**Possible Endings:**
- *Disclosure:* The discovery is brought to light. Concordia's founding history is publicly complicated in ways the city has to reckon with.
- *Protection:* The chambers are sealed — documented but not released. The questline character becomes the permanent custodian of knowledge waiting for a better moment.
- *Foundation:* The discovery becomes the foundation of a new faction or research program — The Oldest Maps formalized into something that can do the work without secrecy.
- *Too Late:* Something has been actively using those chambers the whole time. The questline becomes about what that is and whether it can be addressed.

## From: Villena Hiresvett

*Reassigned because: Villena is actually a Leo entertainer — not a Sagittarius frontier explorer.*

### Archetype: The Frontier Route-Keeper

A robot (or human) in Sagittarius (The Frostlands) who has mapped more routes than she will ever need, who operates on the principle that unknown terrain is the same as having no exit, and who is addressing Sagittarius's most serious structural problem: when experienced scouts die, their knowledge dies with them.

**Personality fit:** Self-Preservational 7-type (Enthusiast) or Assertive Instinctive builds. Adventure as a response to the fear of being trapped. Always has a next destination. Will follow anything more interesting than what she is currently doing. Has a destination she has been circling without approaching, which is the emotional center of her personal arc.

**District:** Sagittarius (The Frostlands)

**Potential affiliation:** The Open Routes (see faction below)

---

### Faction: The Open Routes / The Next Ridge

A loose, genuinely non-hierarchical coalition of Sagittarius explorers who believe route knowledge should be shared rather than kept proprietary. Operates against the dominant culture of the Sagittarius explorer guild, where individual prestige accrues to the person who holds a route exclusively.

**Philosophy:** A route only one person knows is a route that dies with that person. Every closed route is a potential future death. Every open route is a potential future life. The knowledge belongs to everyone who might need it.

**What they do:** Maintain a shared, continuously updated map archive — partly oral, partly encoded in formats Libra cannot easily surveil. Recruit new scouts. Pressure the explorer guild to treat route-keeping as a communal responsibility rather than a source of individual status.

**District anchor:** Sagittarius (The Frostlands)

**Tension point:** The explorer guild views them as a threat to the prestige economy that makes dangerous frontier work worth doing. The Open Routes argues that prestige should accrue to the quality of exploration, not the hoarding of results.

---

### Questline: "What She's Been Mapping Around"

**Core:** An explorer who has been circling a specific destination for years without approaching it. The movement is real — the routes she maps are real — but there is something she has been mapping *around* rather than toward. The companion quest is about what that destination is and what reaching it actually means.

**Themes:**
- The difference between adventure and escape
- What rest means to someone who has only ever moved
- Whether "always having a next destination" is freedom or a more complicated relationship with stopping

**Possible Endings:**
- *Discovery:* She reaches the destination. It is something real — a ruins, an answer, a place — with implications she has to decide what to do with.
- *Revelation:* There is no destination. The movement was always the point. She has to decide if that is okay.
- *The Past Route:* She has been mapping around something she found on a previous expedition and never told anyone about. The companion quest is about what to do with what she found.
- *Rest:* She actually stops — for the first time, in a specific place, for a specific reason. The quest ends quietly.

## From: Trisha Miller

*Reassigned because: Trisha is actually a radio DJ (district TBD) — not an Aries shift crew veteran.*

### Archetype: The Aries Shift Crew Veteran

A senior-ranking robot (or human) in Aries (The Power Core) who has been on the floor long enough that she de facto governs her section of the district, independent of her official title. She does not hold authority — she *is* the authority, in the sense that her section runs on her judgment and her crew's loyalty to her supersedes their loyalty to the district itself.

**Personality fit:** Social 8-type (Challenger) with 7 wing. Community-protective rather than personally ambitious. Her "community" is her crew — specific people on specific shifts. She will do anything to keep them safe, including things that cross lines, and she will be honest about that. She does not perform virtue. She performs competence.

**District:** Aries (The Power Core)

**What she knows:** Exactly what happened during the Black Silence — and what Libra's classified timeline gets wrong. She was there. She was one of the people who kept the grid from failing. She does not talk about it in polished terms. She talks about it in the exact terms of someone who was in the room.

**Story function:** Can be an obstacle (if the player's goals harm her crew), an unlikely ally (if the player demonstrates real competence in Aries terms), or an information source (specifically about Aries history that official records have softened or revised). Not a companion. Too embedded to leave.

## From: Majyao Bisyugota

*Reassigned because: Majyao actually runs a teahouse (district TBD) — not a Scorpio archive artist.*

### Archetype: The Scorpio Archive Artist / Goth Witness

A robot (or human) whose primary creative and spiritual practice involves the Archive of Final Confessions in Scorpio — treating the testimonies of the dying as raw material, not for documentation, but for art. They are not clinical. They are not therapeutic. They are on the Goth subculture side of Scorpio's internal tension between institutionalized transformation and sacred-transformation practice.

**Personality fit:** Self-Preservational 4-type (Individualist) with 5 wing. Creates a very specific, carefully maintained interior world and requires isolation to sustain it. Art is the vehicle through which the interior world becomes externally legible — without losing its essential privacy. Does not explain the work. The work speaks or it does not.

**District:** Scorpio (Psychological/Transformative district)

**What they do:** Archive testimonies — not as records, but as presences. They find forms that keep the voices active: sound installations, material compositions, visual archives that cannot be read linearly. They believe a person who is documented is preserved; a person who is *continued* is something else.

**Story function:** Guards access to the Archive — not officially, but in practice. Is the person who knows whether a specific testimony exists, and whether you have earned the right to encounter it. Can explain Scorpio's mask culture to an outsider in terms that are honest rather than clinical.

---

### Faction: The Living Archive Community

A Scorpio-based informal community (overlapping with the Goth subculture) that holds the dead through art rather than documentation. In direct philosophical tension with Scorpio's clinical rebirth infrastructure.

**Philosophy:** The Archive of Final Confessions was created to preserve dying testimonies before they were lost. But preservation is not the same as continuation. Documentation freezes. Art continues. A voice that becomes part of an ongoing work is still speaking. A voice in a record is a voice that has stopped.

**Position within Scorpio:** Treats institutionalized rebirth therapy as a corruption of what should be sacred — methodology imposed on mystery. They are not against transformation; they are against the lab conditions around it.

**Tension point:** Scorpio's clinical side views them as romanticizing what should be rigorous. The Living Archive community views the clinical side as having bureaucratized the most important experience a person can have.

**District anchor:** Scorpio (Psychological/Transformative district)

## Miscellaneous Faction Seeds

Generated during character development sessions as concepts seeded by specific characters. Those characters may or may not anchor these factions in their final form — the factions themselves are worth preserving as potential world-building elements regardless.

### The Crossroads Claim

A political identity movement among Hub-born residents who argue that "loyalty to nowhere" is a legitimate political stance deserving formal recognition in city governance — not an absence of identity but an identity of its own. Assertive, anti-tribal, and quietly ironic about not noticing that they are their own tribe.

*Original character seed: TBN [XT-21 "Angelina"] — 7w8, Hub-born*

---

### The Fringe Curriculum

An unofficial study circle in Aquarius organized around questions the University of Concordia's ethics boards will not sanction. Not a resistance movement — a group of students and researchers who believe the most important questions are the ones official institutions have decided are dangerous to ask. Run by or around an unorthodox teacher whose methods are too idiosyncratic for the university's official track.

*Original character seed: TBN [XT-17 "Charlene"] — 5w4, Aquarius*

---

### The Found / The Assembled

A cross-district belonging network for robots (and humans) who do not fit their district's dominant culture. Not crisis intervention like Cancer; not entertainment like Leo. Focused specifically on creating chosen community for people who need to build belonging rather than find it pre-made.

*Original character seed: TBN [XT-03 "Mei-Li"] — 6w7:Sc*

---

### The Warm Circuit

A cross-district entertainment cooperative that brings Leo's morale infrastructure capacity to districts that cannot access it: Virgo, Cancer, Sagittarius outposts. Operates against Leo's elitist entertainment hierarchy. Small-scale, continuously underfunded, beloved by everyone who benefits, resented by Leo's mainstream.

*Original character seed: TBN [FW-25 "Pink Lucy"] — 7w6:Sc, Leo*

---

### The Steady Watch

A quiet preparedness mutual-aid collective rooted in Taurus — not a formal faction, more like an informal community security network that catalogues threats and prepares responses without ever announcing itself. Distinct from the Eyes of Gold (intelligence as power) and from The Veilkeepers (information as control) in being purely defensive: knowing things so nothing bad happens, not so someone can gain leverage.

*Original character seed: Favi della Torre — 6w5:Pr, Taurus*

---

### The House Network

A distributed community care-and-awareness network — working robots in Taurus who collectively track community welfare outside official channels. Related to Cancer's Mother's Circuit but rooted in Taurus stability rather than Cancer caregiving. Not a political faction. More like the informal infrastructure that formal factions have to navigate around.

*Original character seed: TBN [IT-021 "Fenny"] — 6w5:Pr, Taurus*

---

### The No-One-Left-Behind Registry

A Cancer advocacy organization that formally documents everyone the district has taken in and monitors compliance with the "no one left behind" compact. Operates through documentation, shame, and organized community voice rather than political ambition. In direct tension with Libra's "productive grief timelines" legacy and Taurus's historical hoarding during the Long Night crisis.

*Original character seed: Salagéa Aparast — 1w2:Sc, Cancer*

---

### The Long Frequency (dual-wing faction)

Two complementary operations within Gemini / Janbogo:

**Verification wing:** A counter-movement to Janbogo's speed-over-accuracy culture. A small group of analysts who insist on source-checking before broadcast and have accumulated enemies across the district's mainstream because of it.

**Recovery wing:** An ongoing data archaeology investigation into the Great Corruption — the targeted (possibly) destruction of Janbogo's pre-Falkland Treaty archives. Tracing the shape of what is missing toward the answer of who benefited and why.

Together, these two wings form a complete inquiry into Janbogo's most significant unsolved information problem.

*Original character seeds: Michelle Stanton (verification wing) — 5w6:Sc; Vosora Lashár Tanslock (recovery wing) — 5w6:Sc. Both still plausibly connected to this faction in their actual character versions.*
