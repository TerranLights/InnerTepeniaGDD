## Recruiting Quest

She does not want to be tied down — that is the design of the 7w8. The challenge with recruiting Angelina is that she does not have an obligation holding her in place; she has an avoidance making anywhere that requires commitment feel wrong. You do not recruit her by offering stability. You recruit her by offering motion.

### Possible Hooks
- **The Better Offer:** Make the journey you are on sound more interesting than anything currently available. A 7w8 will follow the most interesting available thing — your job is to be that.
- **The Thing That Caught Up:** Something from her past has arrived in the city and she cannot outrun it anymore. She can either deal with it alone or with someone she trusts. Helping her deal with it creates that trust, and the aftermath is: now what? She needs somewhere to go that is not the city she has been hiding in.

## Companion Quest — "What She's Been Outrunning"

### Core Conflict & Emotional Stakes
The 7w8 moves fast enough that things do not catch up. But something is going to. Her companion quest is about what that is — a specific event, a person, a place she left and has not been able to return to — and what happens when it finally arrives.

### Themes
- The difference between belonging everywhere and belonging nowhere
- What roots feel like when you have spent your existence avoiding them
- Whether the "cool" is a personality or a coping mechanism — and whether that distinction matters

### Possible Endings
- **Reckoning:** She faces what caught up to her and makes peace with it. She keeps moving, but honestly now.
- **The Stop:** She actually stops — for the first time, in a specific place, for a specific reason. Which surprises both of you.
- **Nothing Was Chasing Her:** What she thought was chasing her was never as significant as the running. The companion quest ends with that realization — smaller than expected, and oddly freeing.
