# Vosora Lashár Tanslock — Questline Notes

## Recruiting Quest

Vosora works the data recovery side of the Great Corruption investigation — reconstructing what was destroyed rather than verifying what remains. Her work has gotten dangerous: whoever targeted those records is aware someone is getting close. She has been receiving pressure she has not disclosed to anyone.

### Possible Hooks
- **Cover and Purpose:** You find out about the pressure she is under. You provide cover — and a reason the recovery matters enough to risk. She joins because you represent an exit from the exposed position she has been locked in alone.
- **The Critical Recovery:** She is one step from a specific recovery that would confirm the Corruption was targeted rather than accidental. She needs protection for the final step. You provide it.
- **The Threat Made Visible:** Whatever has been pressuring her steps out of the shadows. Dealing with it together changes the dynamic from hiding to motion.

## Companion Quest — "What the Silence Says"

### Core Conflict & Emotional Stakes
The shape of what is missing in the Great Corruption is itself evidence. The size of the gaps, their location in the timeline, what topics they cluster around — all of this says something about who was erased and why. Vosora has been tracing that shape toward an answer.

### Themes
- What a civilization that controls its own history does when someone starts recovering what it edited
- The gap between "lost" and "hidden" — and what it means when the distinction becomes provable
- The personal cost of having proof that powerful people would prefer not to exist

### Possible Endings
- **Publication:** The truth of the Great Corruption is recovered and published. Political earthquake: implications for Libra, Janbogo's political structure, possibly the Falkland Treaty legacy.
- **Leverage:** The recovered records are held as private leverage. Quieter, more personal consequences — more certain.
- **The Shape of the Gap:** The records cannot be fully recovered, but she maps the shape of what is missing with enough precision that the shape itself is a document. Published, it tells a story even without the original content.
- **The Wrong Answer:** What she recovers is something she wishes she had not found. The truth is real and worse than the theory — and now she has to decide what to do with it.

### Cross-Questline Connection
- Michelle Stanton works the slow-verification side of the same investigation. Together they form the two halves of a complete inquiry into the Great Corruption.
- Probable political connections: Libra's Suspended Compact; the Janbogo subnet nexus inside Concordia; the Planetary Split Brain questline.
