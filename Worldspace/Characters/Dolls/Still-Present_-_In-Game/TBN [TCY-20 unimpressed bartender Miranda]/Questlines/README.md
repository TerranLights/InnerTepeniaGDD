# TCY-20 "Miranda" — Story & Faction Notes

*Miranda is not recruitable. These are narrative/faction design notes.*

## District Role — Hub (Axis Mundi)

A 1w9:Pr bartender at the city's crossroads. She has been watching every district's representatives pass through for long enough that nothing moves her anymore. She has standards so internalized that she applies them without announcement — if you meet them, she serves you; if you do not, she serves you anyway, but she notices.

She is the still point around which the Hub's tourist culture rotates. She is not cynical. She simply has a standard and has been watching people miss it for a very long time.

## Faction / Collective Affinity

**The Quiet Shift** (existing preliminary faction candidate): "Night workers and maintenance crews who believe important decisions should only be made during the long Antarctic darkness." Miranda is the bartender's expression of this ethic — she hears everything at closing time, when people are done performing, and has formed very specific private opinions about all of it that she has never shared with anyone who asked.

## Narrative Hooks for Player Interaction
- She is the Hub's unofficial institutional memory — she has been serving everyone for long enough to know things that are nowhere in the official record
- She will tell the player the truth about something, once — flatly, without drama — if the player demonstrates they can actually hear it
- She never recruits anyone into The Quiet Shift directly. But people who have worked the closing shift long enough start to notice she has opinions, and some of them ask her what she thinks, and some of those people end up believing the same things she does.
