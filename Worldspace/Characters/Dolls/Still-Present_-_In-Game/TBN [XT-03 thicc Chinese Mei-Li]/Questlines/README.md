## Recruiting Quest

A Social 6w7 is the anchor of the community she helped build. She will not leave while the community depends on her as its center of gravity — but the dependence is also her anxiety management mechanism. The path to recruiting her is showing her that the community is stronger than that dependence.

### Possible Hooks
- **The Stable Point:** Help bring the community she built to a point of stability that does not require her constant presence. Not by replacing her — by strengthening the people around her so the center is distributed rather than single.
- **The Other Anchor:** Identify someone who can share the weight with her. Not a replacement — a partner. Once she has someone to share with, she can also move.
- **The Reveal:** Show her that what has been holding the community together was never solely her — it was something she participated in building, and it can continue without her at the center. This may be the most destabilizing thing she has ever heard, and also the most freeing.

## Companion Quest — "What It Actually Looks Like"

### Core Conflict & Emotional Stakes
A Social 6w7's core fear is being without support — but her arc is about discovering that she has become the support itself, and that this has been her way of managing the anxiety rather than resolving it. The community she built is real. But she built it the way she did because she needed it to need her.

### Themes
- The difference between being loved and being needed
- What community looks like when it is healthy — not organized around a single anchor
- What the 6 discovers when the security she was seeking was never going to come from being indispensable

### Possible Endings
- **The Realization:** The community does not need her as much as she thought. Terrifying, then freeing.
- **The Partner:** She finds someone to share the weight with — a real co-anchor, not a successor. The community becomes something distributed and genuinely stable.
- **The Discovery:** What was actually holding the community together was something she does not have full credit for — and understanding that changes what belonging means to her.
