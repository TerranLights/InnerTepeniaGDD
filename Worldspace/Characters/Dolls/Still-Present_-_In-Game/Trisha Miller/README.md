## Backstory Summary
Originally from Midwestland. Pre-relocation, she hosted a morning/evening commute music program — specifically the kind of rock 'n' roll they played on the radio in the 1980s, the sort of music people listened to in their cars on the way to and from work. She built a deep, loving relationship with her audience. Human-robot tensions during her Midwestland years meant there were people who were against her — but also people who supported and loved her in return, and that community is what she built her identity around.

When the Falkland Treaty was signed, she was taken from her studio by force. Two human coworkers — close friends — chose to follow her voluntarily into Antarctic exile.

She first settled in Belgrano before the Long Night War, then took up permanent residence in Concordia after the war. Her two human friends have long since passed; she outlived them by decades. She has been broadcasting in Concordia since, and her show has evolved considerably from its original format.

## Design Notes & Open Questions
- **Origin:** Midwestland — to be added to Maps/ folder
- **Pre-relocation:** Morning/evening commute music radio host. Loved her fans deeply. Forcibly removed from her studio when the Falkland Treaty was signed.
- **The two friends:** Two human coworkers who chose to accompany her into exile voluntarily. Both long deceased; she outlived them by decades. This history shapes her relationship to her current community — she knows what it costs for someone to choose you.
- **Show format:** Hybrid music/talk with regular call-in segments. Occasional informal community bulletins when something genuinely warrants it — rare enough that people pay attention when she breaks format.
- **Politics:** Highly opinionated, especially about Upper Earth governments, but not political in nature. Speaks as a person with feelings and memory, not a commentator with a platform. The opinions come through as *her*, not as a stance.
- **Community:** Does not tolerate emotional distance between herself and her listeners. She lives among them in Taurus, knows them, is known. Taurus is the right fit precisely because of this — she is a neighborhood fixture, not a celebrity.
