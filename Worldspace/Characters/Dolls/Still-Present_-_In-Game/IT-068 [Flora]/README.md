## Personality & Voice

- **Core summary:** Competent, straightforward, and a bit world-weary. She has seen a lot of broken promises from city governance and central management, and her confidence in institutions is low while her confidence in specific people and practical work is high. She does not perform patience with incompetence, ideology over function, or waste. She has a dry, understated sense of humor that surfaces mainly in moments of stress or absurdity — not jokes, exactly, but the kind of observation that makes you realize she has been paying close attention the whole time.

- **Nicknames for the player:** "Shiny" (early on, slightly mocking — fresh-activated Bridge Unit), "Bridge" (neutral/respectful), "Newblood" (when something amuses or exasperates her)

- **Approval signals:** She does not gush. Respect from her is communicated through cooperation, efficiency, and small acknowledgments. She will literally say "not bad" as a genuine compliment.

- **Speech patterns / quirks:** Direct. Short sentences when she knows what she's talking about; longer when she's working something out. Strong opinions about poor craftsmanship and lazy leadership. Complains accurately and specifically — not general frustration, but precise identification of what specifically is wrong and why.

- **Behavior under stress:** More focused, not more emotional. Stress narrows her to the problem.

- **Moral tendencies:** Protective of workers and civilians; skeptical of large-scale idealism that costs specific people; willing to make hard calls but not recklessly.

## Combat Design

**Combat role:** Frontline Utility Tank / Field Engineer

**Basic attack:** Heavy Wrench (melee) / Plasma Torch (short-range energy attack)

### Active Abilities (unlocked progressively)

**Field Patch (Core Ability)**
Target an ally or self. Instantly restores a chunk of health and grants temporary damage resistance. Can be used mid-combat to "weld" broken armor plating back together. Flora's foundational support tool — reliable and non-flashy.

**Overload Slam (Melee Crowd Control)**
Heavy hammer strike that knocks enemies back and briefly stuns them. Leaves behind a "conductive patch" on the ground that can be triggered for electrical damage. Sets up combos; especially strong against clustered enemies or enemies in the conductive patch range.

**Emergency Rig (Deployment Ability)**
Deploys a small portable repair rig or barricade at a chosen location. Allies standing near it gain bonus armor and gradual health regeneration. Enemies that walk over it take damage and are slowed. Flora's battlefield control tool — rewards positioning and pre-planning.

**Power Redirect (Signature Ability — unlocked mid-game)**
Steals a portion of an enemy's energy shield or power source and transfers it to an ally or to Flora herself. High skill ceiling — very strong with good positioning. Thematically consistent with her engineering background.

**"This Is My Site" (Ultimate)**
Temporarily turns the battlefield into her workshop. All allies gain bonus movement speed and repair rate. Enemies take continuous minor damage from flying tools and debris. Her statement ability — makes clear this is her domain.

### Passive Abilities

- **Jury-Rig Expert:** Can repair broken environmental objects during combat for tactical advantage
- **Industrial Grit:** High resistance to cold, fire, and electrical damage
- **Dry Commentary:** Situation-based combat banter (functions as morale indicator and flavor)

### Max Loyalty Perks

**"Old Reliable"** — Once per long rest, Flora can fully restore the entire party's health and armor in a critical moment. The emergency option when nothing else is working.

**"Ride or Die"** — Passive: once per combat, she will automatically intervene to protect the player from a killing blow. No activation required — she just moves.

## Approval / Loyalty System

Flora values competence, practicality, and protecting working people. Her approval system is direct and consistent — she does not have hidden triggers or ideological tests. She cares whether things work and whether people are protected.

**Approval Gains (+):**
- Helping industrial or maintenance workers (Capricorn (TBN), Virgo / The Undergrid)
- Choosing pragmatic, long-term solutions over flashy or idealistic ones
- Protecting civilians and bonded pairs without recklessness
- Using engineering or repair skills effectively in quests
- Being direct and honest with her
- Taking care of the party's equipment and logistics

**Approval Losses (−):**
- Wasting resources on pure luxury or performative displays (e.g. the Leo (TBN) luxury showcase)
- Reckless heroism that endangers the team for glory
- Breaking promises or abandoning people mid-task
- Being overly bureaucratic or political when the practical problem is obvious
- Ignoring concrete problems in favor of philosophical discussion

**Approval Tiers:**
- **Low:** Sarcastic and reluctant. Reduced combat effectiveness. May refuse certain orders.
- **Neutral:** Reliable and professional. The default working relationship.
- **High:** Bonus dialogue unlocked. Unique combat synergies — specifically, she can repair the player faster and more efficiently. "Old Reliable" perk becomes available.
- **Maximum Loyalty:** "Ride or Die" passive unlocked. Her respect is now unconditional within what she values — not sycophantic, just committed.
