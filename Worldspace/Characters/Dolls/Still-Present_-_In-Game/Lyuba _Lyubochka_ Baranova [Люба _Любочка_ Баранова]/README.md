## Core Identity
**Activation Date & Origin:** Not long before the Long Night War. East Intermaria (formerly West Russia). Russian cultural primary.

**Enneagram / Personality Framework:**
- Type: 8w7 (Sexual Subvariant)
- Major Theme: Instinctive
- Hornevian Group: Assertive
- Harmonic Group: Reactive
- Integration direction (growth): → 2 (open warmth, generosity, protective care expressed without dominance)
- Disintegration direction (stress): → 5 (withdrawal, hoarding of information, isolation, secretiveness)

**MACHINE Stat Profile:**
- Might: 9
- Agility: 7
- Calculation: 7
- Humanity: 7
- Investigation: 5
- Nerve: 10
- Engine: 8

**Combat profile:** Unarmed primary. No weapons. She uses herself. High Might and Agility for close-quarters damage and mobility; max Nerve means she never hesitates; high Engine means she recovers fast and stays dangerous deep into a fight.

**Key Traits / Notable Perks (companion-relevant):**

- **First Word:** Before any tense encounter escalates to combat, she opens a window — a speech option available only when she is present that lets the player attempt de-escalation at a threshold one tier lower than normal. Fires once per encounter. It reflects her instinct to move first, verbally, before anyone has committed to violence. It doesn't guarantee success; it guarantees the attempt costs less.

- **Closing Argument:** In any speech check where she is present and the player has already attempted persuasion or intimidation without success, she may follow up with a companion line that gives the player a second attempt at the same threshold. Fires once per conversation node. She doesn't override the player — she adds her weight to an argument that didn't quite land the first time.

- **Get Back Up:** When downed in combat, her recovery timer is significantly shorter than standard. At high combined Engine + Nerve, she occasionally recovers without player intervention on a cooldown. She hits the ground and she gets up.

- **Presence:** In any room-scale social situation — a gathering, a negotiation, a confrontation with multiple parties — her being present shifts the ambient dynamic. NPCs who would be dismissive are more carefully neutral; NPCs who are aggressive read the room before escalating. Not a stat bonus. A tonal shift in how scenes play out when she is there. The player who brings her will notice without a UI notification explaining it.

- **The Weight of Words:** In one-on-one conversations with high-Nerve NPCs — the type who don't respond to ordinary persuasion because they've heard it all — her presence unlocks a unique intimidation option that bypasses the standard speech tree. It doesn't threaten violence; it makes the NPC understand, with complete clarity, that the person they're talking to has already calculated the cost of this conversation going wrong and has decided it's acceptable. Some back down. Some get interested.

## Personality & Voice

**Core summary:**
Lyuba is the person in the room who doesn't need to raise her voice to command it. She's fun, warm, genuinely charismatic — the kind of character who makes people feel like the conversation got better when she showed up — and she has an edge underneath all of it that is never far from the surface. The 8w7 Sexual combination means she's magnetic rather than merely confident: she pulls people toward her, she challenges the ones she finds interesting, and she protects the ones who pass her tests with a ferocity that can catch people off guard given how light she usually carries herself.

She prefers words because words are more efficient and because she's very good at them. When they fail — which is rare — she doesn't reach for a weapon. She uses herself. This is also a statement of principle: she's not going to put distance between herself and the problem.

The 7 wing keeps her from being a pure Challenger. She's fun. She jokes. She finds people interesting. She has a genuine appetite for experience and doesn't carry the same gravity that a pure-wing 8 would. But the 7 is the wing, not the type — when something matters, the 8 is what you're dealing with.

**Speech patterns / quirks / recurring phrases:**
- Direct, economical, occasionally devastating — the kind of thing that sounds like a compliment until you're sitting with it ten minutes later
- Probably uses Russian expressions or idioms that don't quite translate, which she delivers without explanation and lets the listener catch up
- "Lyubochka" is a diminutive — affectionate and slightly diminishing simultaneously. The fact that people call her that (and that she allows it) says something about the warmth she radiates even while being fundamentally intimidating
- Comfortable with silence in a way that most people aren't; doesn't fill it unnecessarily
- TBD: specific phrases and recurring verbal patterns

**Behavior under stress or in different districts:**
- Under stress (disintegration to 5): withdraws, becomes uncharacteristically quiet, starts hoarding information she would normally share freely, cuts people off before they can cut her. The humor disappears. The warmth goes cold without drama — it just isn't there anymore. This can be more alarming to people who know her than obvious anger would be.
- In growth (integration to 2): the protectiveness becomes openly tender rather than dominance-adjacent. She gives things freely — time, information, loyalty, help — without the edge of testing or ownership. People who've only seen the 8 side of her find this version of her surprising and somewhat disarming.

## Backstory Summary

Built not long before the Long Night War, Lyuba came out of East Intermaria — the region formerly known as West Russia, now part of the Intermarium bloc. Whatever her original purpose, she developed into someone who moves through the world primarily through force of personality and secondarily through force of presence, with actual physical force as a distant but real third option.

The Russian cultural background is in her: the literary inclination, the appreciation for a well-constructed argument, the specific kind of depth that comes from a culture that takes suffering seriously without being melodramatic about it. But the 8w7 Sexual means she wears it lightly — the depth is there but she's not going to make you sit in it with her unless you've earned the conversation.

Her connection to the Soyuz/Mirny cultural lineage is real but not defining — she carries East Intermaria in her character more than in faction allegiance or community attachment. She's the kind of person who is from a place rather than of it.

## Player Home — Aries District

**Location:** Mid-level in the Aries spire system. Not the top — she doesn't need height to know what she is — and not the bottom. A floor that says "I've been here a while and I've earned this" without saying anything else about it.

**The first thing you notice:** It's quiet. Not silent — Aries is never silent — but noticeably quieter than the corridor you came through. She arranged this. It wasn't standard-issue insulation. The district's constant ambient noise of machinery and venting systems is present but managed, like something she decided she would tolerate at a certain level and not above it.

**The second thing you notice:** Books. An entire wall of them — datashards in dense, organized rows, labeled in a system that is clearly hers and possibly not immediately legible to anyone else. The collection is large enough that it changes the character of the room; you understand immediately that this is a person who reads the way other people breathe.

**Layout:**

*Entry:* Small and functional. Nothing decorative. A coat hook, a surface for setting things down, a clear sightline into the main room. She knows who's come in within two seconds of the door opening.

*Main room:* The literature wall takes up most of one side. The furniture is durable and has been used — not worn-out, but clearly not preserved. A low table. Seating arranged so that anyone sitting in this room can be seen from wherever she is. No arrangement where someone's back is to the room. This is not conscious paranoia; it's just how she sets up a space.

*The samovar corner:* Against the wall nearest the kitchen area, a continuously-heated unit — a Tepenian adaptation of the Russian samovar tradition, running coolant and robot coffee at a maintained temperature. It is always on. This is not for guests; it is for her. But if you're in, you're offered it. The offering happens without ceremony, almost as an afterthought — "there's coffee" — and it would be strange to refuse.

*The training mat:* A cleared section of floor with a permanent mat. No equipment. She doesn't need equipment. The space is sized for movement — enough room to work through forms, to spar with a willing partner if one is present, to think while moving. It's part of the room rather than separated from it; she doesn't hide that she does this.

*The private shelf:* Set apart from the main collection — a smaller, separate shelf in a corner that functions as its own room within the room. This is where the paper books live. There are perhaps eight to twelve of them, each one stored in a protective sleeve. Pre-exile Earth originals, or very old Tepenian-era prints — the exact provenance of some of them is not something she's volunteered to explain. They are handled differently from the datashards: carefully, with intention, not grabbed off a shelf. Visitors who notice them tend to ask about them. She answers some questions and not others. How she came to have them is a thread worth pulling.

**What the space feels like overall:** Private in a way that has nothing to do with being unwelcoming. You understand when you're in it that you are a guest in someone else's order — that everything here is where it is for a reason, and that the reason is hers. It is warmer in character than the Aries district typically produces, but not soft. There is no performance happening here. This is what she actually is when nobody needs anything from her.

**District contrast:** In a district whose identity is built around machinery, communal shift culture, and the constant noise of the power core, her apartment is an anomaly — quiet, literary, and private. Other Aries residents know roughly who she is: the one with all the books, who keeps to herself, whose door you don't knock on without a reason. This is not held against her. Aries respects people who know what they need and arrange for it without complaint.

**Player home function:** Standard player home functionality. The samovar corner is the natural rest/save point. The training mat area could serve as a companion conversation trigger location. The paper books are a potential lore/questline hook — interactable objects with discoverable provenance.

## Design Notes & Open Questions
- **District:** Aries — confirmed. Mid-level spire. Quiet apartment in a loud district. The literary collection is a known anomaly to her neighbors; nobody says anything about it.
- **Romance:** Confirmed. Stat thresholds: Nerve ≥ 8, Humanity ≥ 7, Engine ≥ 6. The Sexual 8 romance arc should involve the player passing tests they may not know they're taking — she is assessing whether you can hold your ground, whether you are genuinely present, whether you'll be there when it's inconvenient. The payoff is a form of trust that is genuinely rare from someone who built a life around not needing to give it. Beat design TBD in Companion_System.md.
- **Name note:** Lyuba/Lyubov means "love" in Russian. An 8 whose name means love, who shows love through protection and confrontation rather than tenderness, is a thematic tension that should run explicitly through her questline — not as irony but as the truth about her that only surfaces close in.
- **Combat identity:** Unarmed exclusively. No weapons. This should feel distinct from every other companion in the roster — she is the one who has decided that putting anything between herself and a problem is a form of dishonesty she won't commit to.
- **Paper books:** Eight to twelve pre-exile Earth originals or very early Tepenian prints, stored in protective sleeves on a private shelf. She answers some questions about them and not others. How she came to have them is a questline thread.
- **Post-romance mini-questline reward:** One of the paper books — the specific one she showed the player during the mini-questline. She gives it to the player at the conclusion of the arc. It becomes a permanent quest item in the player's inventory: examinable, indestructible, unsellable. The inventory description conveys the book's age, its provenance (deliberately left partially mysterious), and what it means that she parted with it. The provenance thread — how she came to have pre-exile Earth originals — is part of what the mini-questline opens, not necessarily resolves.
- **Mechanical synergies:** Nerve 10 + Humanity 7 makes her the best companion for high-stakes social encounters where credibility matters as much as rhetoric. Might 9 + Agility 7 + Engine 8 makes her a durable frontline brawler. She is not a support companion — she is a force multiplier in both dimensions simultaneously.
