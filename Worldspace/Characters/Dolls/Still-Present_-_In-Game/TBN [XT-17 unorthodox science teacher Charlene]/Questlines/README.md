## Recruiting Quest

She is mid-investigation on something she cannot leave before it reaches a conclusion. She teaches to fund and sustain the research. Her teaching is genuine — she cares about her students — but the research is what she is actually doing.

### Possible Hooks
- **The Stable Stopping Point:** Help her reach a conclusion — or a stable enough pause — in the research that she can step away without it dying. She will not leave mid-experiment.
- **The Next Step Is You:** Show her that what you are doing is the next step in the research. What she has been investigating in theory, you are about to pursue in practice. Joining you is not abandoning the work — it IS the work.
- **The Permission:** Her students need her. She feels that weight. Help arrange for her students' continued education in her absence — someone else who can carry the fringe curriculum forward — and she can go.

## Companion Quest

### Core Conflict & Emotional Stakes
A 5w4 has a specific obsession — the thing she has been investigating alone — that is simultaneously her greatest intellectual work and her primary means of avoiding full presence in the world. Her companion quest follows that obsession all the way to its end: what does she find, and what does finding it cost?

"Unorthodox" means the methods and questions are ones the University of Concordia's ethics boards would not sanction. The quest takes her (and the player) to people and places outside official channels.

### Themes
- The intellectual obsession as both genuine pursuit and escape mechanism
- What unorthodox methods actually reach that orthodox methods cannot
- The cost of knowing something that required unauthorized means to discover

### Possible Endings
- **The Finding:** She follows the obsession to its conclusion. What she finds is real — and has implications outside the research context.
- **The Method Was the Point:** The investigation reaches an end, but the most important thing was what the process revealed about how questions get suppressed in Aquarius. The finding and the suppression are equally significant.
- **The Price:** The unorthodox methods required going to people or places that now have a claim on what she found. She has to decide how to handle that.
