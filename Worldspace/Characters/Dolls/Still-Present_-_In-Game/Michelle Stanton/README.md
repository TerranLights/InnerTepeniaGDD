## Backstory Summary
Originally built as a construction robot in Midwestland, exiled to Tepenia following the Falkland Treaty. She became a leading figure in the laying and construction of the infrastructure that became the Arcanet — the Antarctican internet. Before the Long Night War she divided her time between resting in various nearby cities and travelling the frozen wastes in a technologically advanced Kharkovchanka, building out network infrastructure across the continent.

Her role in Concordia now — data archaeology, Great Corruption investigation, slow-verification counter-culture — is a second career that grew from the same underlying drive: she built the Arcanet's bones, and now she is trying to make sure what it holds is actually true.

Following the Long Night War she chose to take up permanent residence in Concordia and help maintain the city's infrastructure.

See `Personal_Background/` for details as they develop.

## Design Notes & Open Questions

- **The Kharkovchanka and the choice to stay:** Michelle is one of the very few residents of Concordia who is technically capable of leaving the city — her Kharkovchanka gives her a means of continental travel that most people do not have. She stays anyway. This is both a source of personal character revelation (what does it mean to choose confinement when you could leave?) and a viable DLC trigger — she could serve as the player's means of departure for at least one DLC, with the question of why she's never used that capability herself as a narrative thread.
