# Salagéa Aparast — Story & Faction Notes

*Salagéa is not recruitable. These are narrative/faction design notes.*

## District Role — Cancer

A Social 1w2 who reforms the world through relationships and community organizing — not through rules, but through building community moral authority. In Cancer, she is the voice that says "this is enough, and here is why" in terms the community recognizes as true.

She functions as:
- The organizing force behind the **No-One-Left-Behind Registry** — the formal body that holds Cancer's leadership accountable to the motto when resource pressure makes it tempting to quietly stop meaning it
- The person Cancer calls when it cannot agree on what is right — not to make the decision, but to articulate the principle the decision has to measure against
- The living memory of what Cancer agreed to be, and the person who notices first when it is being quietly abandoned

## Faction / Collective Affinity

**The No-One-Left-Behind Registry** (potential faction seed): A Cancer advocacy organization that documents everyone the district has taken in and formally monitors compliance with the founding compact. Not a political faction with ambitions — a moral authority that operates through documentation, shame, and organized community voice.

Direct tensions:
- **Libra:** The "productive grief timelines" directive legacy. The Registry is the formal institutional expression of Cancer's refusal.
- **Taurus:** The quiet hoarding during the Long Night crisis that Taurus has never acknowledged and Cancer has never forgotten.

## Narrative Hooks for Player Interaction
- She has information about Cancer's Overcrowding Decision of 2721 — the event whose records were quietly destroyed. She does not have the documents, but she knows what was decided and who was in the room.
- She can mobilize Cancer's community voice on the player's behalf — or against the player, if the player's actions violate the district's moral compact.
- She will tell the player, clearly and without judgment, what a decision will cost the vulnerable people of Concordia — making her a moral compass that is neither preachy nor naive.
