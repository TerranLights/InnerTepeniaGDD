# Favi della Torre — Companion Event: "The Scientist Entry"

**Type:** Standalone companion event — may or may not intersect with Favi's personal questline; can fire independently of questline state
**Requires:** DLC 5 (Halley subnet) completed far enough to receive the datashard stash from Salagéa Aparast (or another NPC with access); player must have chosen to take the stash and returned to Concordia with it

---

## Setup

During DLC 5, the player receives a stash of physical datashards whose contents have already been incorporated into the Halley Arcanet subnet's databases — redundant, no longer needed by anyone in the region. The offer is casual: *"You can take them if you want."*

Among the stash, one shard (or a small subset) contains records of post-war Italian history — civilian records, institutional logs, brief biographical entries.

---

## The Event

The player gives the Italian-content shard(s) to Favi.

Favi searches through the data. She finds a brief records entry — not a major entry, just a mention — of the elderly scientist she was built to assist in Italy. She notices the date and year of his death. She calculates in her head, quickly, when that would have been relative to her exile. She re-reads the summary entry. She becomes fraught with despair.

**What she realizes:** He died of grief over her absence. The timing makes it obvious. He did not die of old age or illness in any neutral sense — he died not long after her exile, from the compound weight of two losses: his real daughter, who had died in a vehicular accident years before Favi's activation and whose absence he had never fully recovered from; and Favi herself, who had filled that absence, and whose exile reopened the first wound alongside the new one.

She did not know this before this moment. She had made whatever peace was available to her with not knowing what happened to him. What she receives here is not the relief of resolution but something harder: irrevocable specific knowledge that her absence was not something he endured, and that the cost of her exile was paid in full by someone who could not afford it.

---

## Design Notes

- **Tone:** quiet; no dramatic music swell; the revelation happens in the text she is reading and in her face, not in the scene around her; the player watches her arrive at it
- **Player role:** delivery mechanism and witness; no dialogue check or skill gate on receiving the event; the player simply gave her something and is now present for what it means
- **The entry itself:** the in-game datashard text should be written carefully — it should read as a dry bureaucratic record that contains the information without editorializing; Favi's reaction does the interpretation, not the text
- **Nature of their relationship (TBD):** confirmed NOT romantic. The remaining question is whether it is best characterized as paternal/daughter or something more complex. He commissioned her construction — he did not personally build her — but she was made for him and the relationship that developed was real and deep. This must be decided before the datashard text and Favi's dialogue are written, as it shapes what her despair means and how she expresses it
- **Scientist's name:** TBD
- **Follow-up:** what Favi says or does after this moment — whether she wants to be alone, whether she asks the player to leave, whether she says nothing — is TBD for dialogue design; the beat itself is confirmed; the aftermath is open
