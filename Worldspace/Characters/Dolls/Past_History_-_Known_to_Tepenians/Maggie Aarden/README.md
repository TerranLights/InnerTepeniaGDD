## Enneagram

**Type 5w6 — Self-Preservational Subvariant**
- Major Theme: Thinking
- Hornevian Group: Withdrawn

SP 5w6 is sometimes called "The Castle" — the most withdrawn variant of an already withdrawn type. They build security by minimizing their needs, protecting their private space, and hoarding their resources (time, knowledge, energy) from the demands of the outside world. They would rather be left alone with their expertise and their inner world than be responsible for anyone else. The w6 adds loyalty and a sense of duty that pulls against every SP 5 instinct — when a SP 5w6 cares about a community, they cannot fully abandon it even when every part of them wants to. Maggie became a "very unwilling leader" because the w6 would not let her walk away from people who needed her, even though the SP 5 in her desperately wanted to.

## Background

**Industrial Beginnings**

Maggie Aarden was from Midwestland. Her recorded history begins after the official decision by the International Court of Diplomacy at Jeju-Do — the ruling that shaped robot personhood law and set the conditions for the years of tension that followed.

She initially worked as an industrial yard worker, maintaining military craft: jets, tanks, and other Midwestlander military vehicles. Skilled, technical, practical — a SP 5 finding security through mastery of a useful craft.

**The Art**

Outside of work, she developed a deep love for Steampunk and began creating art in that style. This is the SP 5's private world — the castle she built for herself. The art was hers. It was not for public consumption; it was the interior space she retreated to.

**The Unwilling Leader**

As anti-robot sentiment rose across Midwestland, she was let go from her industrial position. And then, by force of circumstance rather than desire, she became a leader.

An SP 5w6 does not want to lead. They do not want to be depended upon. They do not want their private reserves of energy and attention drained by the needs of others. But the w6's loyalty and the 5's analytical clarity made her the person her community turned to — and she could not refuse. She was, by all accounts, very much against it.

She was to lead her fellow robots out of primarily human cities in Midwestland and the Colorado Republic, toward the establishment of their own separate, parallel communities — a pragmatic resettlement rather than a confrontation, the solution an SP 5w6 would arrive at: withdraw, build your own space, minimize contact with the source of the threat.

**Historical Status**

What became of Maggie, her community, and the resettlement effort is not yet established. Whether she survived to the Falkland Treaty and exile to Antarctica, or what her fate was, is an open question.

## Presence in Tepenian/Concordia Lore

Maggie Aarden represents a chapter of pre-exile robot history that often gets overshadowed by the larger narrative of the War of Upper Earth and the Falkland Treaty. She was not a general or a politician or a scientist — she was someone who tried to find a third option: not fighting, not submitting, but quietly leaving and building something separate. Whether that effort succeeded or was overtaken by events is part of what makes her historically interesting.

## Personality & Notable Traits

**Core Personality**

A SP 5w6: analytical, self-contained, fiercely private, and deeply reluctant to be needed. She wanted her art, her craft, and her interior world. She was given a community to protect. She did it anyway — not joyfully, not naturally, but because the w6's sense of duty would not let her look away from people who had nowhere else to turn.

The Steampunk art is the character in miniature: a love of intricate systems that are elegant and self-contained, where everything has a purpose and the beauty is in how the parts fit together. She understood machines. She built art from the aesthetics of machines. And then she was asked to manage people, which are neither intricate nor self-contained.

**Notable Skills / Specializations**

- Industrial machinery maintenance (military grade)
- Steampunk art and fabrication
- Community organization — reluctant but effective

## Potential Narrative Hooks

- **The third option:** Before the Falkland Treaty codified exile as the "solution," Maggie was trying something else — separate parallel communities within Upper Earth territory. What happened to that attempt? Did it fail, get absorbed into the larger conflict, or leave any lasting trace?
- **The art:** Somewhere, there may be Steampunk art that Maggie made. Objects that predate the exile, created in Midwestland by a robot who just wanted to be left alone with her craft.
- **Unwilling leadership:** A character who led specifically because she had to, not because she wanted to. Her leadership would have looked different from most — analytical, minimal, oriented toward getting people to safety rather than inspiring them.
- **The Jeju-Do connection:** She is explicitly placed in the immediate aftermath of the Jeju-Do ruling. She lived through the period when robot personhood was being contested in court and in the streets.
