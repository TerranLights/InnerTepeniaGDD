## Enneagram

**Type 2w3 — Social Subvariant**
- Major Theme: Feeling
- Hornevian Group: Compliant

Social 2w3 is sometimes called "The Ambitious Helper" — warm, charming, and deeply motivated by being needed and recognized by a community or cause larger than themselves. The Social subvariant drives the 2 toward power and influence through indispensability: they position themselves at the center of something that matters and make themselves impossible to replace. The w3 adds ambition, image-consciousness, and social grace. Doris did not simply work at the Hall of Archives — she rose to Head Librarian, responsible for preserving the entirety of pre-war human knowledge. That is a Social 2w3 at full expression: making herself the person the civilization could not afford to lose.

## Background

**The Hall of Archives**

Doris Morikawa served as Head Librarian at the Hall of Archives in the Sovereign Republic of Hawaii — a location described as a "politically and economically neutral zone" for scientific research. She was responsible for the cataloging and preservation of much of pre-war earthly knowledge, maintained against the possibility of a global catastrophe.

She succeeded. The question is what became of the archives — and of her — when catastrophe came.

**Historical Status**

What happened to Doris and the Hall of Archives during and after the War of Upper Earth and the Long Night War is not yet established. Whether the archives survived, were partially preserved, or were lost is a significant open question with lore implications.

## Presence in Tepenian/Concordia Lore

The Hall of Archives in Hawaii was a pre-war institution dedicated to preserving civilization's knowledge. If any of those records survived and reached Tepenia — through evacuees, data transfers, or physical media — Doris Morikawa's name would be on them. She is not known for any dramatic act of heroism; she is known for having cared, systematically and completely, about the survival of human knowledge.

In the context of the Planetary Split Brain — where different subnets now hold conflicting records and the last synchronized Arcanet archive is at the South Pole ruins — Doris represents a different kind of preservation effort: pre-digital, pre-Arcanet, human-scale curation. What she saved (if anything survived) may be complementary or contradictory to what the Arcanet holds.

## Personality & Notable Traits

**Core Personality**

A Social 2w3: warm, capable, ambitious, and deeply motivated by being the person a community cannot function without. She is not someone who works quietly in the background — she leads, organizes, and positions herself where her contribution is most visible and most needed. She made herself the steward of civilization's memory. That is not a small self-conception.

The Social 2w3 genuinely cares and genuinely wants power, and these are not in contradiction — the caring is how the power is exercised. She preserved those archives because it mattered and because she was the right person to do it and because she wanted to be the right person to do it.

**Notable Skills / Specializations**

- Archival science and knowledge preservation
- Cataloging and organization at large scale

## Potential Narrative Hooks

- **The archive question:** Did any of the Hall of Archives' contents survive and reach Tepenia? If so, they would be extraordinarily valuable — pre-war human knowledge preserved outside the Arcanet system, uncorrupted by the Split Brain.
- **Hawaii as neutral ground:** The Sovereign Republic of Hawaii was described as politically and economically neutral. Did that neutrality protect it during the wars, or make it a target, or simply irrelevant to the violence?
- **The Planetary Split Brain:** If Doris's archives survived and contain pre-war historical records, they could be a third source of truth — alongside the conflicting subnet records and the South Pole synchronized archive — for the Split Brain questline.
- **A 2w3 who preserved instead of built:** Most figures in Tepenian history are remembered for creating things — the Arcanet, the orbital infrastructure, the cities. Doris preserved things. That's a different kind of legacy.
