# Enneagram Dynamics — Integration & Disintegration

**Source:** *Personality Types* by Don Richard Riso & Russ Hudson (photographed charts)
**Status:** Global character design ruleset — binding for all character behavior descriptions

This document records the two directional movement charts that govern how each Enneagram type behaves under different emotional conditions. These charts apply universally to all characters in Inner Tepenia whose Enneagram type is confirmed. When writing "behavior under stress" and "behavior in good times" sections for any character, use this document as the baseline.

---

## How to Use This

**Integration (good times):** When a character is thriving — feeling secure, loved, purposeful, or at peace — they move *toward* the healthy expression of another type. They do not become that type; they acquire its best qualities while remaining themselves.

**Disintegration (bad times):** When a character is under prolonged stress, feeling threatened, overwhelmed, or cornered — they move *toward* the average or unhealthy expression of another type. This shift tends to feel sudden and out-of-character from the outside; internally it is an escalation of the type's core fear or core wound.

**The Reversal Note:** The disintegration chart is subtitled "with reversal." This means the directional arrows are not absolute one-way flows. Under *extreme* disintegration, a character may paradoxically draw on the *positive* qualities of their stress point as a coping mechanism — a last-resort reaching for any available resource. Under *extreme* integration (deep, long-term security), a character may temporarily slide into the *unhealthy* qualities of their integration point — they relax so thoroughly they lose productive tension. Both reversals are edge cases; the primary directions below are the default.

## The Direction of Integration

*When times are good. When the character is healthy, secure, and thriving.*

| Type | Primary movement | What this looks like |
|------|-----------------|----------------------|
| **1** | → **7** | Angry, critical Ones become more spontaneous and joyful, like healthy Sevens |
| **2** | → **4** | Prideful, self-deceptive Twos become more self-nurturing and emotionally aware, like healthy Fours |
| **3** | → **6** | Vain, deceitful Threes become more cooperative and committed to others, like healthy Sixes |
| **4** | → **1** | Envious, emotionally turbulent Fours become more objective and principled, like healthy Ones |
| **5** | → **8** | Avaricious, detached Fives become more self-confident and decisive, like healthy Eights |
| **6** | → **9** | Fearful, pessimistic Sixes become more relaxed and optimistic, like healthy Nines |
| **7** | → **5** | Gluttonous, scattered Sevens become more focused and profound, like healthy Fives |
| **8** | → **2** | Lustful, controlling Eights become more open-hearted and caring, like healthy Twos |
| **9** | → **3** | Slothful, self-neglecting Nines become more self-developing and energetic, like healthy Threes |

## The Direction of Disintegration

*When times are bad. When the character is under stress, threatened, overwhelmed, or cornered.*

| Type | Primary movement | What this looks like |
|------|-----------------|----------------------|
| **1** | → **4** | Methodical Ones suddenly become moody and irrational |
| **2** | → **8** | Needy Twos suddenly become aggressive and dominating |
| **3** | → **9** | Driven Threes suddenly become disengaged and apathetic |
| **4** | → **2** | Aloof Fours suddenly become overinvolved and clinging |
| **5** | → **7** | Detached Fives suddenly become hyperactive and scattered |
| **6** | → **3** | Dutiful Sixes suddenly become competitive and arrogant |
| **7** | → **1** | Scattered Sevens suddenly become perfectionistic and critical |
| **8** | → **5** | Self-confident Eights suddenly become secretive and fearful |
| **9** | → **6** | Complacent Nines suddenly become anxious and worried |

## Quick Reference — Per Type

### Type 1 (The Reformer / Perfectionist)
- **Integration →** Spontaneous, joyful (like healthy 7)
- **Disintegration →** Moody, irrational (like average/unhealthy 4)

### Type 2 (The Helper)
- **Integration →** Self-nurturing, emotionally aware (like healthy 4)
- **Disintegration →** Aggressive, dominating (like average/unhealthy 8)

### Type 3 (The Achiever)
- **Integration →** Cooperative, committed to others (like healthy 6)
- **Disintegration →** Disengaged, apathetic (like average/unhealthy 9)

### Type 4 (The Individualist)
- **Integration →** Objective, principled (like healthy 1)
- **Disintegration →** Overinvolved, clinging (like average/unhealthy 2)

### Type 5 (The Investigator)
- **Integration →** Self-confident, decisive (like healthy 8)
- **Disintegration →** Hyperactive, scattered (like average/unhealthy 7)

### Type 6 (The Loyalist)
- **Integration →** Relaxed, optimistic (like healthy 9)
- **Disintegration →** Competitive, arrogant (like average/unhealthy 3)

### Type 7 (The Enthusiast)
- **Integration →** Focused, profound (like healthy 5)
- **Disintegration →** Perfectionistic, critical (like average/unhealthy 1)

### Type 8 (The Challenger)
- **Integration →** Open-hearted, caring (like healthy 2)
- **Disintegration →** Secretive, fearful (like average/unhealthy 5)

### Type 9 (The Peacemaker)
- **Integration →** Self-developing, energetic (like healthy 3)
- **Disintegration →** Anxious, worried (like average/unhealthy 6)

## Design Notes

**For companion approval/disapproval:** A character's disintegration direction is particularly useful for writing high-disapproval moments — when the player has done something the character finds deeply threatening or wrong, and the companion responds in a way that is recognizably *them* but pushed to a less flattering version.

**For romance beats:** Integration movement tends to surface in late-arc beats, when the character feels genuinely safe with the player. Disintegration movement can inform what the "signal line" (failed Gate 2 moment) sounds like — a brief flicker of the character at their least secure.

**For quest design:** Companion quest climaxes can deliberately push a character toward disintegration and then — through the player's choices — pull them back toward integration. The direction of that movement is now mechanically defined by this chart.
