# Blocking Decisions

Three decisions must be resolved before substantive GDD work can proceed in the areas they affect. None of these are design questions with an obvious right answer — they are choices that have been left open, and leaving them open is causing downstream documents to be built on undefined ground.

Make these before moving to Phase 1.

---

## Decision 1: Echoes of Amundsen — Keep, Redesign, or Cut?

**What it is:** A faction concept (FD-7 slot in Faction Devotion endings) built around romantic longing for the off-world evacuees and the destroyed Space Elevator. Members maintain memorials, run signal towers trying to contact orbit, and archive evacuation stories. Their fame/infamy is tied to the player either helping preserve those memories or declaring the off-worlders "gone forever."

**Why it's blocking:** FD-7 is one of 12 Faction Devotion endings. The ending can't be written until the faction is finalized or the slot is reassigned. The faction also affects what lore is accessible to the player — if the Echoes exist, they're a primary vector for off-world lore delivery.

**The tension:**
- **Keep as-is:** Works thematically. Nostalgia and displaced hope are genuinely interesting emotional territories in the Inner Tepenia world context. The signal tower mechanic (actually trying to contact orbit) is distinctive. The risk is that the faction skews sentimental rather than complex — the Unity of Opposites test asks: *what do they need that only their opposition can provide?* Their opposition (people who have moved on, or who want the signal towers scrapped for parts) would need to provide them with something essential that they can't get otherwise.

- **Redesign:** Keep the memorial / grief-archiving function but add a second layer that creates a genuine faction tension, not just "hope vs. pragmatism." One option: the Echoes possess the most complete archive of pre-evacuation lore in Concordia, making them an information power. The grief is real; the archive is also leverage. This creates meaningful infamy options (players who leak or destroy archival data) that have real cost rather than symbolic cost.

- **Cut:** FD-7 slot gets reassigned to another faction. Lose the off-world nostalgia vector but simplify the faction count. The concern here is that the off-world lore delivery vacuum it leaves — someone needs to hold that information.

**Recommendation (not binding):** Redesign. The emotional territory is the right territory; the faction needs a second layer that creates the Unity of Opposites tension. The archive-as-leverage angle makes them play like the Veilkeepers with grief instead of secrecy — which also raises a question about whether they overlap too much with the Veilkeepers and should be differentiated.

**Decision needed from:** Creator.

## Decision 2: Salagéa Aparast — Main Game or DLC 5?

**What it is:** A scaffold character — backstory exists, personality not yet written — currently held in a placement limbo. She is listed as a potential DLC 5 companion (Atlantic Coastal Region) but there has been no final call on whether she belongs in the main game or the DLC.

**Why it's blocking:** 
- If she's a main game character, she needs to be on the character completion schedule in Phase 3 alongside the other scaffold characters, and her district/home base in Concordia needs to be established.
- If she's a DLC character, she moves to Phase 7 (long-term planning) and does not block main game work.
- Writing her personality and questline before knowing her placement context is wasted work — her personality should be shaped by her narrative context.

**The tension:**
- **Main game:** The main game already has 6 companion Dolls (all complete) plus Ji-Eun Kim and Calethina (in progress). Adding a ninth would be a significant scope expansion. The question is whether she brings something to the main game that none of the eight companions already cover.
- **DLC 5:** DLC 5 (Atlantic Coastal Region) is an independent storyline anchored to a different geography. A DLC companion can carry the full weight of the DLC narrative without competing with the main game roster. The risk is that the DLC may not get made, or may be made much later, and the character stays undeveloped indefinitely.

**Decision needed from:** Creator.

## Decision 3: Calethina's Passive Link Mechanic

**What it is:** The "Echoes of the Bridge" questline (Calethina's personal quest) has two unresolved design threads:
1. How does the "passive link" work — what does the player actually experience during the background Calethina-connection? Is it ambient world-state changes? Intrusive memory fragments? Dialogue options that appear? Something else?
2. How does the five-step personality-download design relate to the wrist-device download mechanic? Are these the same system at different points in the questline, or two competing designs that need to be rationalized into one?

**Why it's blocking:** The Calethina questline is deliberately deferred, but it cannot be deferred forever — it's the most critical emotional arc in the game and everything else is eventually measured against it. More immediately, the passive link mechanic affects:
- What text/documentation goes in the main story files (any main quest moment where Calethina is present)
- Ji-Eun Kim's questline (which is the first thread of the Calethina arc and is currently incomplete)
- The design of the wrist-device mechanic (which appears in other contexts)

The questline doesn't need to be written now. The passive link mechanic does need to be defined, because other things depend on knowing what it is.

**What's currently understood:**
- The archive-narrator direction is established (Calethina speaks to the player in a specific register)
- The Ji-Eun Kim first thread is established
- The wrist-device download concept exists
- The five-step personality structure exists
- How these connect is undefined

**Decision needed from:** Creator. Specifically: what does the player physically experience during a passive link moment? Even a rough directional answer ("it's like hearing a voice from another room" or "it's seeing through her eyes briefly" or "it's the player character having a thought that isn't quite theirs") is enough to unblock the downstream work.

## After These Are Resolved

Once these three decisions are made, Phase 1 work can begin. The decisions do not need to be resolved simultaneously — Decisions 1 and 2 are independent of Decision 3. Decision 3 is the most time-sensitive because the Ji-Eun Kim questline work (Phase 3) cannot be completed without it.
