# Blocking Decisions

Three decisions must be resolved before substantive GDD work can proceed in the areas they affect. None of these are design questions with an obvious right answer — they are choices that have been left open, and leaving them open is causing downstream documents to be built on undefined ground.

Make these before moving to Phase 1.

## Decision 1: The Vigil [NAME TBD] — Keep, Redesign, or Cut?

**~~RESOLVED — June 2026~~**

**Decision:** Downgraded from full faction to significant minor group. The The Vigil [NAME TBD] are not a Faction Devotion ending faction. FD-7 slot is now open for reassignment.

**Rationale:** The concept is strong enough to anchor off-world lore delivery and a dedicated quest arc, but not strong enough to carry an end-game Devotion ending. As a significant minor group — more developed than the Quiet Shift or Treaty Scholars, with a proper quest line and named NPCs — they serve the game better than they would as a mandatory faction slot the concept can't fully support.

**Downstream effects:**
- FD-7 slot is open — needs a new faction assigned (can be deferred until Phase 2 faction design surfaces a candidate that fits)
- Echoes move to Phase 5 side content (quest arc) rather than Phase 2 faction design
- Off-world lore delivery responsibility stays with the Echoes; they remain the primary player access point for evacuation history and the confirmed-alive-but-unreachable context

## Decision 3: Calethina's Passive Link Mechanic

**~~RESOLVED — June 2026~~**

**Decision:** Calethina is a holographic projection from the wrist device — technically not a companion in the code sense (no companion slot, no companion system triggers), but present, interactive, and visible in the world as a sprite/avatar the player can see and speak to.

**The mechanic has two phases, distinguished visually:**

**Before the download (midpoint of main quest):**
Calethina projects from her server through the city grid. Her presence is signal-dependent.
- Good signal area: clean, stable holographic image
- Poor signal / interference: flickering, glitching sprite
- No signal at all: she disappears entirely and is absent until the player returns to signal range
- Her visual quality is a direct readout of world state — grid health, story events, and interference all affect her appearance

**After the download:**
Calethina is local to the wrist device. No grid dependency. Clean, stable holographic image everywhere. The visual transformation — the sprite that used to flicker is now steady — is itself the payoff of the download decision.

**Implementation note:** She is technically a projection system, not a companion. This creates a recognizable mechanical and code distinction that the before/after download also honors visually.

**What remains to be designed (Phase 5 questline work, not blocking):**
- Specific triggers for flickering vs. no-signal states (grid events, story beats, specific districts, quest states)
- Whether signal quality affects the quality or completeness of information she can provide
- The wrist device's visual design as her anchor point in the world
