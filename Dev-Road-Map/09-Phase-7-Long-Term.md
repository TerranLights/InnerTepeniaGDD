# Phase 7 — Long-Term and DLC

**Prerequisite:** Phases 5 and 6 complete. Main game GDD should be near-complete before this phase begins.

**What this phase does:** Plans and designs the DLC content, completes deferred world-building, and addresses the lower-priority items that couldn't justify attention earlier. This is not polish in the "bug fixes" sense — it is design depth that the main game doesn't require but the full vision does.

---

## 7.1 — DLC 1: South Pole (Kendra Heinrich)

**Priority: First among DLC work.**

Kendra Heinrich's backstory is scaffolded; her personality is Phase 3 work (or deferred here, depending on creator decision). DLC 1 is the most developed DLC concept because it has a protagonist character, a geographic location, and an implicit thematic focus (the South Pole as the most extreme environment in an already extreme world).

**What needs to be designed:**
1. The central conflict — what is the situation at the South Pole that justifies a DLC? What is Kendra's role in it?
2. The DLC's thematic contribution — how does DLC 1 extend the main game's guiding principles rather than just adding content? The best DLC (cf. Fallout: New Vegas DLCs) is thematically distinct from the main game while being of a piece with it. Dead Money is about letting go. Old World Blues is about the inability to leave the past. What is DLC 1 about?
3. Key NPCs and their design standard
4. The questline structure (apply the same quest anatomy as Phase 5)
5. The DLC's ending and how it connects to the main game's epilogue

---

## 7.2 — DLC 2-4: Geographic Expansion

**Priority: Low. Design begins only after DLC 1 is substantially designed.**

Three DLC concepts with no assigned characters and no storyline:
- DLC 2: West Antarctica / Byrd
- DLC 3: Antarctic Peninsula  
- DLC 4: Mawson Region

Each needs the same starting process as DLC 1: identify the central conflict, identify the thematic contribution, assign a companion character (or determine it's a solo DLC), and then begin questline design.

These can be designed in parallel or sequentially — there's no dependency between them.

---

## 7.3 — DLC 5: Atlantic Coastal Region (Salagéa Aparast)

**Priority: Depends on Blocking Decision 2.** If Salagéa is moved to the main game, this DLC slot needs a new protagonist. If she stays in DLC 5, this DLC gets her personality work (from the blocking decision resolution) as its starting point.

The Atlantic Coastal Region is geographically the furthest from Concordia and therefore potentially the thematically most distinct. What does the Atlantic coast look like in 2786? What survived the Long Night War in that region? What relationship does it have with the remnants of the Old World (Europe, Africa, South America, which are all within Atlantic reach)?

This DLC has the most potential for exploring the world beyond Antarctica — which makes it thematically important for the Outer Tepenia series continuity.

---

## 7.4 — DLC 6: Janbogo Region

**Priority: Low. Janbogo's development in Phase 6 is a prerequisite.**

Janbogo is already significant within the main game (Veilkeepers base, Arcanet archives, the Subnet Nexus). A Janbogo DLC would need to add a dimension that the main game doesn't explore — either going further into the Veilkeepers' operations, or exploring what's beyond the Janbogo region, or excavating the city's history in ways the main game only references.

Design begins only after Phase 6 completes Janbogo's full cultural sketch.

---

## 7.5 — Sinian Federation

**Priority: Deliberately deferred. Design when relevant.**

The Sinian Federation has been explicitly deferred. It should stay deferred until a specific GDD need (a questline, a DLC, a character backstory) requires it to be defined. Designing it in Phase 7 is appropriate only if one of the DLC concepts creates a dependency on it.

---

## 7.6 — Remaining 35 Tepenian Cities

**Priority: Low. Station-mapping is complete; cultural depth is not required for the main game.**

The ~35 remaining Tepenian cities beyond the five targeted in Phase 6 have station-to-city mappings but no lore. Full cultural sketches for all 35 would be an enormous undertaking and are not required for the GDD to be complete. 

**Approach:** Design as-needed. When a character backstory, questline, or DLC references a specific city, design a one-page sketch for that city at that time. Keep a running list of which cities have been sketched.

---

## 7.7 — Outer Tepenia Series: GDD Awareness Pass

**Priority: Medium for a single, targeted task.**

Inner Tepenia is a prequel to three planned Outer Tepenia RPGs. The design notes flagged as "future-proofing" in the GDD are intentional, not premature. This task is a single awareness pass — not designing the Outer Tepenia games, but ensuring that Inner Tepenia's design doesn't accidentally close off options the Outer Tepenia series will need open.

**What to check:**
- The Amundsen Tower is destroyed; can it be rebuilt or replaced in the Outer Tepenia timeframe?
- The off-world evacuees are alive; are there design choices in Inner Tepenia that would make the Outer Tepenia return-of-evacuees storyline harder to execute?
- The robot personhood political landscape at the end of Inner Tepenia's possible endings — which endings create usable starting conditions for Outer Tepenia, and which create complications?
- The Calethina questline's resolution — if Calethina's ending state varies by player choice, which ending states does the Outer Tepenia series need to be able to assume?

This is not a design task; it's an audit task. The output is a short document flagging any compatibility concerns.

---

## 7.8 — Player Re-Spec: Specific Costs and Trade-Offs

**Priority: Low. Framework complete; specifics TBD.**

The player re-spec system has a complete framework: the mechanism for re-speccing exists, the costs are generally described, the Identity Fragmentation mechanic is designed. What doesn't exist: the specific cost values (how many Identity Fragmentation points for a full stat re-spec? For a partial skill re-spec?). These numbers need playtesting to finalize but should at least have initial design values in the GDD.

---

## 7.9 — Speedrun Integration

**Priority: Low. Speedrun path structure is complete; integration with main story is partial.**

The speedrun path is designed. Its specific integration with the main story — where the speedrun path diverges from the standard path, what the speedrun player misses, what they experience uniquely — is only partially documented. Complete this documentation as part of Phase 7, since it's lower priority than content work.

---

## 7.10 — GDD Completeness Audit

**The final task of Phase 7 is a full GDD audit against the Completion Matrix.**

Before the GDD can be called complete (or complete enough to hand to a team), run one final pass:
- Update the Completion Matrix with all Phase 7 changes
- Check every ⚠️ flag in the GDD for whether it has been resolved
- Verify that every referenced document exists (no broken internal links)
- Verify that the guiding principles (robot experience, bittersweet-only, Unity of Opposites) are honored in all Phase 5-7 content
- Write a one-page "GDD Status Summary" that can accompany the GDD when shared externally
