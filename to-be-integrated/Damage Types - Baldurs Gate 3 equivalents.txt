BG3 damage types page (and cross-referenced for the full confirmed list of 13). BG3 organizes them into Physical (Bludgeoning, Piercing, Slashing) and Elemental (Acid, Cold, Fire, Force, Lightning, Necrotic, Poison, Psychic, Radiant, Thunder). They drive resistances/vulnerabilities, build variety, and thematic flavor in a high-fantasy setting.For Inner Tepenia ("Firm Sci-Fi" — grounded in real physics/chemistry/engineering where feasible, with sentient robots/"Bridge Units," cyberware/augmentations, siligel energy systems, Arcanet data networks, geothermal/power infrastructure, post-nuclear remnants, and Antarctic harshness), we can translate most of them effectively. The goal is scientific plausibility (energy transfer, material science, electromagnetism, chemistry, biology) while preserving fun gameplay: distinct builds, counters, environmental interactions, and district flavor. Some fantasy abstractions get reinterpreted or sacrificed for enjoyment (e.g., no true "holy radiant" magic; plasma bolts don't behave like perfect sci-fi projectiles in atmosphere).Damage types should integrate with existing systems:
    • MACHINE stats modifiers (e.g., Engine for energy/thermal resistance or endurance against DoT; Might for kinetic soak; Calculation for system optimization vs. overloads; Nerve/Humanity for mental/neural effects).
    • AP economy and combat (costs vary by type or special effects).
    • DT/DR layered armor (type-specific values; district augments like industrial plating in Capricorn or experimental dispersion in Aquarius).
    • Districts & world (color-coded map + prominent features): Hazards, weapons, and enemies tie into themes (e.g., industrial spills in Capricorn = corrosive; power core in Aries = thermal/electrical surges; Scorpio "Death Ward"/transformative = necrotic-like or neural; undergrid Virgo = maintenance/chemical risks; Frostlands Sagittarius = cold exposure).
    • Robots vs. Humans: Robots often resistant to biological/poison but vulnerable to EM/disrupt; humans more varied, with cyberware creating hybrid weaknesses.
    • Power grid story and reactivity: Choices could alter environmental damage prevalence (e.g., unstable power = more electrical/radiant hazards).
Translations of BG3 Damage TypesPhysical (Kinetic/Impact — Core of most conventional weapons; dominant in grounded ballistics/projectiles/melee):
    • Bludgeoning: Blunt force trauma, concussive impact, shockwaves. Physics: Momentum transfer, material deformation/crushing (Newton's laws, Young's modulus). Gameplay: Good vs. armored/plated targets or structures; DoT from internal bleeding/bruising. In-world: Hammers, mauls, explosive shockwaves, falls, industrial presses (Capricorn), or gravitic impacts. District augments could add blunt resistance.
    • Piercing: Penetrating trauma from points/projectiles. Physics: Focused force overcoming material strength (stress concentration). Gameplay: High vs. unarmored/soft targets; AP ammo synergy. In-world: Needles, spikes, AP rounds, monofilament or railgun darts, undergrid maintenance tools (Virgo).
    • Slashing: Shearing/cutting trauma. Physics: Edge geometry and material tensile strength. Gameplay: Good vs. unarmored or for dismemberment effects. In-world: Blades, monowire, industrial saws, or high-velocity fragments. Less dominant than in fantasy due to advanced armor prevalence.
Elemental (Energy/Chemical/Biological — More "exotic" or environmental; strong thematic ties to tech, industry, and Antarctica):
    • Acid (Corrosive/Chemical): Material dissolution or chemical burns. Physics: pH-driven reactions, oxidation, solvent action on polymers/metals. Gameplay: Strong DoT, armor degradation over time (ignores some DT). In-world: Industrial chemicals/solvents (Capricorn factories), nanite-assisted cleaners, battery leaks, or bioweapon residues. Excellent for environmental hazards and black market (Pisces) weapons.
    • Cold (Cryogenic/Thermal - Low): Freezing, embrittlement, hypothermia. Physics: Reduced molecular kinetic energy, thermal contraction, ice crystal formation in tissues. Gameplay: Slows movement/AP, brittle armor (increased vulnerability to kinetic follow-up). In-world: Cryo weapons (liquid N2 or advanced coolants), Antarctic exposure (Frostlands Sagittarius), or power grid cooling system failures. Strong environmental synergy.
    • Fire (Thermal/Heat/Combustion or Plasma-adjacent): Burning/heat transfer. Physics: Molecular excitation, oxidation/combustion, or infrared radiation. Gameplay: DoT, panic/forced movement. In-world: Plasma torches or contained plasma (Aries power core), laser thermal effects, geothermal vents, or incendiary munitions. Reinterpret pure "fire" as high-energy thermal discharge.
    • Force (Kinetic/Concussive or Inertial Transfer): Raw energy dump, often shockwave-like. Physics: High-velocity momentum or pressure waves (explosive or accelerated mass). Gameplay: Reliable baseline damage; good for knockback or structure destruction. In-world: Explosives, mass drivers, railgun impacts, or gravitic/acceleration weapons. Serves as "pure kinetic" or concussive fallback.
    • Lightning (Electrical/Electromagnetic Current): Current flow, arcing, overload. Physics: Electron flow, Joule heating, dielectric breakdown. Gameplay: Chain effects on conductive targets, stun/DoT. In-world: Tasers, high-voltage arcs, railgun rails, cyberware feedback, or power surges (Aries grid). Excellent anti-robot/electronics tool.
    • Necrotic (Biological Decay or Entropy Acceleration): Tissue/system degradation leading to "death" of cells or function. Physics: Cellular apoptosis, radiation-like or toxic breakdown, or advanced nanotech disassembly (reinterpret "life drain" as molecular disassembly or accelerated entropy). Gameplay: Strong DoT or max HP reduction; thematic for "death ward" areas. In-world: Engineered pathogens/prions, radiation-induced necrosis, or targeted nanites (Scorpio district flavor, or industrial contaminants).
    • Poison (Toxic/Chemical/Biological Agents): Systemic toxicity. Physics: Enzyme inhibition, membrane disruption, neuro/hemotoxicity. Gameplay: DoT with varied effects (paralysis, damage over time). In-world: Industrial toxins, bioweapons, siligel contaminants, or black market chems (Pisces). Less effective vs. sealed robots unless delivered via nanites.
    • Psychic (Neural/Cognitive Disruption): Mental or interface overload. Physics: Neural overstimulation, synaptic disruption, or direct brain-machine interface feedback (since cyberware/robots exist). Reinterpret fantasy "mind magic" as electromagnetic or chemical neural attack, or memetic/psychological but grounded in sensory overload. Gameplay: Affects Nerve/Humanity/Calculation; confusion or control loss. In-world: Neural disruptors, sonic infrasound (Cymaticists tie-in), interface hacks, or hallucinogenic agents. Strong vs. augmented humans/companions.
    • Radiant (Electromagnetic Radiation or Coherent Energy): High-energy photons or particle effects. Physics: Ionization or thermal excitation from UV/X-ray/gamma/lasers (coherent light). Gameplay: Good vs. certain materials or as precision damage. In-world: Laser weapons (thermal + ionization), reactor leaks, or high-energy particle beams from experimental tech (Aquarius district). Avoid pure "holy light."
    • Thunder (Sonic/Acoustic Pressure): Pressure waves from sound or explosions. Physics: Rapid compression/rarefaction of air/media causing mechanical stress. Gameplay: AoE, disorientation, or structural damage. In-world: Sonic weapons, explosive shockwaves, infrasound (disruption without lethality), or industrial noise/vibration (Cymaticists faction synergy; Virgo maintenance hums).
Additional Suggested Damage Types (Beyond BG3 Equivalents)These fill gaps for sci-fi flavor while staying grounded or "firm" plausible. They add build variety, counters (especially vs. robots/tech), and world integration:
    • Radiation (Ionizing): Alpha/beta/gamma/neutron exposure causing cellular/DNA damage. Physics: Highly realistic in post-nuclear setting. Gameplay: Delayed DoT + long-term debuffs (not just instant); environmental in certain areas. Distinct from Radiant (more penetrating/biological). Ties to history and Aries power infrastructure.
    • EMP / Disrupt (Electromagnetic Pulse/Interference): High-intensity EM fields disrupting electronics without primary thermal/kinetic damage. Physics: Induced currents frying circuits. Gameplay: Stuns/disables robots/cyberware (temporary or permanent on unshielded targets); area denial. Strong anti-tech tool; pairs with Lightning but more systemic. Excellent for hacking-adjacent or grid-related play.
    • Plasma (Ionized Matter): Superheated ionized gas. Physics: Contained via magnetic fields; combines thermal + electrical effects. Gameplay: High damage + secondary effects (burn + arc). Distinct from Fire (more exotic containment issues) or Lightning. In-world: Advanced weapons or containment breaches in power/experimental districts.
    • Nanotech / Molecular Disassembly: Targeted or self-replicating nanites breaking bonds at molecular level. Physics: Advanced materials science (plausible near-future). Gameplay: Armor-ignoring DoT or precision destruction; bypasses some DT. Fun "gray goo" lite or repair-denial effect. Ties to Aquarius experimental labs or industrial nanites.
    • Gravitic / Inertial (Acceleration or Tidal Forces): Extreme G-forces or manipulated gravity/acceleration. Physics: Inertia, tidal stresses (real in astrophysics/engineering). Gameplay: Knockback, crushing, or disorientation; good for heavy weapons or environmental (e.g., damaged gravity plating if any). Adds variety to Force.
    • Neural / Interface (Refined Psychic variant): Direct attack on neural implants or robot control systems (feedback loops, OS corruption). Physics: Electromagnetic or signal injection into wetware/hardware. Gameplay: Control loss, error states, or stat drain on augmented targets. Synergizes with Investigation (hacking) and Calculation.
These keep the system varied (13+ types for counters/builds) without overcomplicating. Physical remains king for conventional fights; energy/chemical/EM shine in tech-heavy or environmental scenarios. Robots gain natural resistances to Poison/Necrotic but vulnerabilities to EMP/Disrupt/Lightning; district augments provide player customization.This translation maintains firm sci-fi grounding (real energy transfer, chemistry, EM, materials) while enabling fun (distinct playstyles, district hazards reacting to power choices, anti-robot specialists). It slots neatly into your DT/DR framework and AP combat.
