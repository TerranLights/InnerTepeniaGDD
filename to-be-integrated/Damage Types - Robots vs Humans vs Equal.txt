We can create two leaning lists drawn from the translated BG3 damage types + the extra firm-sci-fi suggestions. The goal is fairness:
    • Each side gets strong, thematic counters.
    • Lists are close in length (not identical).
    • Plenty of overlap/versatile types that work against both (with different flavor/effects).
    • Robots generally resist traditional biological damage but are vulnerable to system/electronic disruption and material corrosion.
    • Humans are vulnerable to biological/cellular/neural trauma but more resistant to pure EMP unless heavily augmented.
    • This encourages build variety (anti-robot specialist, anti-human specialist, or generalist) while supporting mixed encounters, district hazards, and the power-grid story (e.g., unstable power increases electrical/EMP risks city-wide).
Anti-Robot Lean (Strong vs. Machines, Electronics, Chassis & Systems)These exploit electronics, conductive materials, logic/control systems, and physical structure. Excellent for targeting robots, augmented humans with heavy cyberware, or industrial/power infrastructure.
    • EMP / Disrupt (Electromagnetic Pulse/Interference) — Primary hard counter. Fries circuits, stuns/disables robots, causes system errors or temporary shutdown. Minimal effect on unaugmented humans.
    • Lightning (Electrical) — Overloads systems, causes arcing through conductive chassis/frames, feedback into power sources. Strong DoT or stun on robots.
    • Acid (Corrosive/Chemical) — Dissolves or weakens metal, plastics, and robot chassis over time. Degrades armor and structural integrity.
    • Radiation (Ionizing) — Damages sensitive electronics, sensors, and memory/storage components. Can cause long-term degradation or malfunctions.
    • Plasma (Ionized Matter) — Combines intense localized heat with electrical effects; excellent at melting or disrupting contained systems and materials.
    • Neural / Interface (Direct system attack) — Hacks or overloads robot OS/control interfaces, causing control loss, error states, or forced shutdowns. Synergizes with Investigation/Calculations for hacking-focused builds.
    • Cold (Cryogenic) — Embrittles metal and materials, making structures more prone to follow-up kinetic damage or fractures.
    • Slashing (Cutting/Shearing) — Effective at targeting joints, wiring, cabling, or exposed components if it can bypass outer plating.
Why this list works: It leans into the mechanical nature of robots. These types often have secondary effects like stuns, armor degradation, or disablement that feel "anti-machine."Anti-Human Lean (Strong vs. Organic Tissue, Biology & Psychology)These exploit biology, cells, nerves, psychology, and soft tissue. Excellent against humans (including lightly augmented ones) and biological hazards.
    • Poison (Toxic/Chemical/Biological Agents) — Classic systemic toxicity affecting organs, nerves, or blood. Very effective on organic physiology.
    • Necrotic (Biological Decay / Molecular Breakdown) — Accelerates cell death, tissue necrosis, or targeted molecular disassembly. Strong DoT and "wounding" feel.
    • Radiation (Ionizing) — Causes cellular/DNA damage, leading to acute sickness or long-term effects. Highly realistic threat in the post-war world.
    • Psychic / Neural (Mental/Neural Disruption) — Overloads nerves, causes psychological effects, disorientation, or pain. Affects both organic brains and (to a lesser extent) augmented interfaces.
    • Fire / Thermal (Heat) — Causes severe burns to flesh and organic material. Strong DoT and panic/forced movement.
    • Acid (Corrosive) — Chemical burns on skin and tissue. Overlaps with robot list but with more immediate organic pain/DoT.
    • Bludgeoning (Blunt/Kinetic Impact) — Crushes bones, organs, and soft tissue. Reliable trauma against unarmored or lightly protected humans.
    • Thunder (Sonic/Acoustic Pressure) — Pressure waves cause internal trauma, disorientation, or ruptured organs/tissues.
Why this list works: It plays into organic vulnerabilities. These often carry debilitating secondary effects (pain, debuffs, DoT) that feel punishing to humans.Balance Notes & Overlaps
    • Shared / Versatile Types (work well against both, with flavor differences):
        ◦ Force (Kinetic/Concussive/Inertial) — Universal blunt trauma or shockwave damage.
        ◦ Piercing — Good penetration against both armor and flesh.
        ◦ Radiant (EM Radiation / Lasers) — Precision thermal/ionization damage; effective on both but different secondary effects.
        ◦ Gravitic / Inertial (if used) — Extreme acceleration or crushing forces affect structures and bodies alike.
        ◦ Cold and Fire/Thermal also have crossover potential in extreme environments (Antarctic exposure or power surges).
    • Fairness Mechanisms:
        ◦ Robots get baseline resistance or immunity to classic Poison and traditional biological Necrotic, but become vulnerable when nanites or chemical delivery systems are involved.
        ◦ Humans get some resistance to pure EMP/Disrupt unless they have significant cyberware (creating interesting hybrid targets).
        ◦ Many types have type-specific DT/DR adjustments and district armor augments (e.g., Capricorn industrial plating strong vs. Acid/Corrosive; Aquarius experimental gear with dispersion vs. Radiation/EMP).
        ◦ Environmental hazards in districts naturally favor one side or the other (industrial spills = Acid strong vs. robots; Scorpio transformative areas = Necrotic/Neural strong vs. humans).
        ◦ Player choice and the power-grid story can shift city-wide prevalence (e.g., damaged grid increases Lightning/EMP/Radiation risks).
    • Gameplay Feel: This creates meaningful specialization without hard counters that trivialize encounters. A player can build an "EM Specialist" (strong vs. robots and tech) or a "Toxin/Decay Specialist" (strong vs. humans and biology), or mix for general use. Mixed groups of enemies reward adaptability.



SUMMARY



Damage Types: Anti-Robot Specialization(Particularly effective against machines, electronics, chassis, and robotic systems)EMP / Disrupt — Electromagnetic interference that fries circuits, causes system failures, and temporarily disables or stuns robots.
Lightning — Electrical overloads and arcing that damage power systems and conductive components.
Acid (Corrosive) — Chemically degrades metal, polymers, and structural chassis over time.
Radiation (Ionizing) — Damages sensitive electronics, sensors, and data storage.
Plasma — Superheated ionized gas that melts materials and disrupts contained systems.
Neural / Interface — Direct attacks on robot OS, control systems, and logic cores (hacking-style disruption).
Cold (Cryogenic) — Embrittles metals and materials, making them prone to fracturing.
Slashing — Targets joints, wiring, cabling, and exposed mechanical components.

Damage Types: Anti-Human Specialization(Particularly effective against organic tissue, biology, and psychology)Poison (Toxic/Biological) — Systemic toxins that damage organs, blood, and nervous systems.
Necrotic (Biological Decay) — Accelerates cell death, tissue breakdown, and molecular degradation.
Radiation (Ionizing) — Causes cellular and DNA damage leading to acute or long-term biological failure.
Psychic / Neural — Overloads nerves and cognitive functions, causing disorientation, pain, or mental disruption.
Fire / Thermal — Severe burning and heat damage to flesh and organic material.
Acid (Corrosive) — Chemical burns and tissue dissolution on skin and organs.
Bludgeoning — Crushing force that damages bones, internal organs, and soft tissue.
Thunder (Sonic) — Acoustic pressure waves causing internal trauma and disorientation.

Quick Balance NotesRobots typically resist or ignore Poison and classic biological Necrotic unless delivered via nanites or specialized carriers.
Humans are largely resistant to pure EMP/Disrupt unless heavily cyber-augmented.
Many types include secondary effects (stun, armor degradation, DoT, movement penalties) that enhance their thematic strength.
District environments and power-grid choices can naturally amplify one side or the other (e.g., Aries power surges = more Lightning/EMP; Capricorn industrial zones = more Acid).
