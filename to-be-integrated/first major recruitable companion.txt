Here's a detailed design for IT-068 "Flora" as your first recruitable companion.Combat Role: Frontline Utility Tank / Field EngineerFlora is a durable, practical support tank who excels at controlling the battlefield, repairing allies mid-fight, and turning the environment into a weapon. She’s not the flashiest damage dealer, but she’s incredibly reliable and makes the whole party stronger — perfect for an early-game companion.Core Stats Focus (when leveling her):
    • High Structural Integrity (health + resistance)
    • High Precision Engineering
    • Good Thermal Engineering
    • Moderate Social Resonance (dry wit in combat)

Combat Style & AbilitiesBasic Attack: Heavy Wrench / Plasma Torch (melee + short-range energy attack)Active Abilities (unlocked progressively):
    1. Field Patch (Core Ability) 
        ◦ Target an ally (or self). Instantly restores a chunk of health + grants temporary damage resistance. 
        ◦ Can be used in combat to “weld” broken armor plating back together.
    2. Overload Slam (Melee Crowd Control) 
        ◦ Heavy hammer strike that knocks enemies back and briefly stuns them. 
        ◦ Leaves behind a “conductive patch” on the ground that can be triggered later for electric damage.
    3. Emergency Rig (Deployment Ability) 
        ◦ Deploys a small portable repair rig / barricade at a location. 
        ◦ Allies standing near it gain bonus armor and gradual health regen. 
        ◦ Enemies that walk over it take damage + slow.
    4. Power Redirect (Signature Ability – unlocked mid-game) 
        ◦ Steals a portion of an enemy’s energy shield or power source and transfers it to an ally (or herself). 
        ◦ High skill ceiling — very strong if you position well.
    5. “This Is My Site” (Ultimate / Cooldown) 
        ◦ Temporarily turns the battlefield into “her workshop.” All allies gain bonus movement speed and repair rate. Enemies take constant minor damage from flying tools/debris.
Passive Bonuses:
    • Jury-Rig Expert: Flora can repair broken environmental objects (doors, turrets, generators) in combat for tactical advantage.
    • Industrial Grit: High resistance to environmental hazards (cold, fire, electrical).
    • Dry Commentary: Unique combat banter based on situation (e.g. mocking enemies, encouraging the player, complaining about poor maintenance).

Approval / Loyalty SystemFlora has a straightforward but flavorful approval system. She values competence, practicality, and protecting working people.Approval Gains (+):
    • Helping industrial / maintenance workers (Forgeward, Undergrid)
    • Choosing pragmatic, long-term solutions over flashy or idealistic ones
    • Protecting civilians and bonded pairs without being reckless
    • Using engineering / repair skills effectively in quests
    • Being honest and direct with her
Approval Losses (-):
    • Wasting resources on pure luxury or performative displays (e.g. Resonance Crown)
    • Reckless heroism that endangers the team
    • Breaking promises or abandoning people mid-task
    • Being overly bureaucratic or political
    • Ignoring practical problems in favor of philosophy
Approval Tiers & Effects:
    • Low Approval: She becomes sarcastic and reluctant. Reduced combat effectiveness. May refuse certain orders.
    • Neutral: Reliable but professional.
    • High Approval: Unlocks bonus dialogue, unique combat synergies (e.g. she can repair you faster), and a powerful personal perk (“Old Reliable” — once per long rest, she can fully restore the party’s health/armor in a pinch).
    • Maximum Loyalty: She gains a unique “Ride or Die” passive where she will automatically intervene to protect you from a killing blow once per combat.

Flavor Notes for Flora:
    • She calls the player things like “Shiny”, “Bridge”, or “Newblood” affectionately.
    • She has strong opinions about poor craftsmanship and lazy leadership.
    • Her loyalty feels earned — she doesn’t gush over you, but once she respects you, she’s ride-or-die.
