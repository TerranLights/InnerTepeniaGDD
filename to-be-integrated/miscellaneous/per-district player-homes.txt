# Per-District Player-Homes
#### (available either by purchase or via completing questlines)

District 1: Cancer
Cancer’s player homes emphasize safety, emotional warmth, communal bonds, and quiet resilience amid resource strain and emotional labor. Homes feel like personal sanctuaries within the larger protective network of warm, humid domes, hydroponic gardens, and care facilities. They are rarely luxurious—more like hard-won safe havens patched together from district resources. Upgrades often involve helping with care work, gathering supplies, or strengthening bonds rather than pure currency.

1. Integrated Creche-Adjacent Shelter Pod (Starter / Entry-Level Home)

Feel & Vibe:
Cozy, protective, and slightly communal. A compact, softly lit pod or small suite built into or right next to a creche/nursery cluster. Thick insulation, gentle blue-green lighting, the constant sound of water features or soft airflow, and the distant sounds of children/young robots. It feels like a warm burrow or safe nest—nurturing but with an underlying sense of shared vulnerability and emotional labor.

Proximity:
Directly adjacent to creches, nurseries, and basic medical support areas. Short walk to hydroponic healing gardens and grief support circles. Easy access to caregivers, refugee integration staff, and vulnerable NPCs (orphans, new arrivals, elderly residents).

Difficulty to Acquire:
Low-to-moderate effort. Requires basic reputation with Cancer caregivers (helping with triage, supply runs, or simple integration tasks) plus modest resource contribution (scavenged materials, siligel donations, or volunteer shifts). Feels like “earning your place in the shelter network.”

Quest-Locked Options:
None for the base version. However, a small upgrade path (extra storage or a bonded companion slot) can tie into minor side quests helping specific refugees or caregivers.

2. Hydroponic Garden Loft / Greenhouse Apartment

Feel & Vibe:
Serene, life-affirming, and hands-on. A modest loft or converted greenhouse space above or woven into the hydroponic healing gardens and therapy parks. Lush (but resource-strained) plants, soft natural-ish lighting filtered through insulation, the smell of soil and growing things, and gentle water sounds. It has a hopeful, nurturing “life goes on” atmosphere mixed with the practical strain of maintaining the gardens during shortages.

Proximity:
Embedded in or overlooking the hydroponic healing gardens and therapy parks. Close to medical/psychological care centers and bonding ceremony halls. Good access to gardeners, therapists, and residents who use the gardens for healing.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Moderate effort. Requires noticeable contributions to the district’s food/medicine production (regular gardening shifts, supply runs from other districts, or fixing garden systems during blackouts). Reputation with the garden crews and care staff is key. Feels earned through tangible help sustaining the district’s nurturing core.

Quest-Locked Options:
A premium version or major upgrade (better climate control, private meditation nook, or expanded growing plots) can be unlocked by completing a mid-tier questline involving protecting or expanding a specific healing garden plot, often tied to helping traumatized refugees or bonded pairs find peace there.

3. Private Bonded Family / Grief Support Suite

Feel & Vibe:
Intimate, emotionally heavy but deeply comforting. A self-contained suite designed around the district’s bonding ceremony halls and grief support circles. Soft, curving architecture, memorial elements (small personal shrines or memory walls), warm lighting, and space for quiet reflection or small gatherings. It feels like a personal sanctuary for processing trauma while still being part of the larger protective network—warm and supportive but carrying the weight of loss and emotional labor.

Proximity:
Very close to bonding ceremony halls, grief support circles, and advanced psychological care centers. Short distance to orphanages/integration centers and family habitation clusters. Ideal for interacting with counselors, bonded pairs, and long-term residents dealing with grief or integration.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Moderate-to-high effort. Requires building significant trust with the district’s emotional care network (consistent help with grief support, refugee integration, or emotional labor tasks). Often involves donating resources during shortages or aiding in triage decisions. Feels like proving you can be a steady, nurturing presence.

Quest-Locked Options:
Strongly recommended as a quest-reward home. Unlocked after completing a notable questline demonstrating loyalty—e.g., helping a specific group of refugees/orphans integrate, protecting a bonded human-robot pair through a crisis, or aiding in a major grief support initiative. This version can include unique upgrades like a private memorial alcove or bonded NPC companion options that reflect story choices.

4. Emergency Shelter / Cold-Shock Recovery Annex (Quest-Heavy / High-End Option)

Feel & Vibe:
Rugged yet deeply protective and resilient. A reinforced annex or converted emergency shelter optimized for cold-shock recovery, with thick insulation, backup systems, and a no-nonsense but caring atmosphere. It has a “we survive together” Fallout-like practicality mixed with Cancer’s emotional warmth—functional bunks or pods alongside spaces for quiet support and healing.

Proximity:
Located within or right beside major emergency shelters and care facilities. Excellent access to medical staff, refugee intake areas, and the broader network of shelters. Close to integration centers for new arrivals.

Difficulty to Acquire:
High effort. Base access requires substantial demonstrated value to the district’s protective infrastructure (major supply contributions during crises, helping during blackouts, or significant volunteer work). Pure reputation grinding is involved.

Quest-Locked Options:
Yes—this is the prime candidate for a complex questline home. Unlocked only after completing a substantial, multi-stage quest arc showing deep loyalty to Cancer’s ideals (e.g., organizing or protecting a large refugee/integration effort, uncovering and resolving internal resource-hoarding conflicts, or aiding the district during a major external threat like power diversion from Aries). Rewards can include advanced defensive upgrades, private medical storage, or story-significant NPC ties (e.g., a grateful bonded group or caregiver companion).




District 2: Taurus
Taurus player homes emphasize stability, material comfort, self-sufficiency, and long-term security. They feel like hard-earned, well-insulated personal fortresses within family rings and residential clusters. Upgrades focus on better heating, storage, and defensive insulation rather than flashy luxuries. Acquiring them rewards consistent reliability, resource contribution, and respect for established families — very much in the old-school Fallout spirit of proving you can “hold the line.”

1. Modest Family-Ring Habitation Pod (Starter / Entry-Level Home)

Feel & Vibe:
Solid, grounded, and quietly comfortable. A compact, heavily insulated pod or small apartment within one of the established family habitation rings. Thick walls, warm ambient heating, practical storage, and a small private courtyard or window overlooking communal green spaces. It has a “this is mine and it will last” reliability — cozy but never wasteful.

Proximity:
Inside a stable residential ring, near communal heating hubs, small hydroponic micro-farms, and family gathering areas. Short walk to local markets and maintenance workshops. Easy access to long-term residents and domestic NPCs.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Low-to-moderate effort. Requires basic reputation with Taurus family councils (completing supply runs, helping reinforce insulation, or assisting during minor blackouts) plus a modest contribution of scavenged materials or siligel. Feels like being accepted as a reliable neighbor.

Quest-Locked Options:
None for the base version, but minor upgrades (extra storage or better climate control) tie into small side tasks helping local families.

2. Reinforced Insulation Loft / Workshop Apartment

Feel & Vibe:
Practical, self-reliant, and enduring. A converted loft or reinforced workshop space with heavy insulation layers, tool benches, and visible repair projects. Warm, lived-in atmosphere with stockpiled goods, personal reinforcement plating on walls, and a “I built this to last” pride. It feels like a personal bunker that also functions as a comfortable home.

Proximity:
Overlooks or sits near private maintenance yards and communal reinforcement projects. Close to material depots, family workshops, and residential security patrols. Good access to engineers, homemakers, and long-term residents focused on self-sufficiency.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Moderate effort. Requires regular contributions to district infrastructure (insulation repairs, material scavenging runs from outside, or helping families fortify their own homes). Reputation with workshop crews and family elders is essential.

Quest-Locked Options:
A premium upgraded version (advanced private workshop, superior heating loops, or hidden safe room) can be earned through a mid-tier questline involving protecting a residential ring from a major threat or resolving a resource-hoarding dispute.

3. Multi-Generational Bonded Family Suite

Feel & Vibe:
Warm, rooted, and legacy-oriented. A larger, self-contained suite designed for bonded pairs or small chosen families. Multiple connected rooms, reinforced family memorial wall, private hydroponic nook, and spaces for quiet domestic routines. It carries a deep sense of continuity and stubborn protection of “what matters most.”

Proximity:
Within established family clusters, near bonding ceremony areas (shared with Cancer) and long-term residential archives. Close to schools/creches for younger residents and elder gathering spots.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Moderate-to-high effort. Demands consistent trust-building with multi-generational families (long-term volunteer work, protecting family assets during shortages, or mediating internal disputes). Significant material investment required.

Quest-Locked Options:
Yes — this home is strongly tied to loyalty. Unlocked after completing a notable questline demonstrating commitment to Taurus values — e.g., helping a bonded human-robot family survive a crisis, recovering lost family heirlooms from outside the district, or successfully defending the ring against external pressure (Aries power cuts or Pisces smuggling influence). Upgrades can include private family NPC ties or enhanced security features.

4. Old-Timer’s Reinforced Corner Annex (High-End / Prestige Home)

Feel & Vibe:
Ruggedly luxurious in a practical way. A heavily fortified corner annex or converted old-timer’s residence with the best insulation in the district, redundant heating systems, expansive storage, and a commanding view of the residential rings. It feels like the ultimate “I earned this and no one is taking it” safe haven — solid, warm, and quietly proud.

Proximity:
In a prestigious, well-defended section of the district. Excellent access to family council meetings, elite workshops, and central heating distribution nodes. Close to influential long-term residents and security leadership.

Difficulty to Acquire:
High effort. Requires major reputation grinding, substantial resource donations during crises, and proven long-term reliability. Pure “old blood” respect is hard to earn as an outsider.

Quest-Locked Options:
Strongly quest-gated. This home is only accessible after completing a complex, multi-stage questline that proves deep loyalty to Taurus stability — such as exposing and stopping a large-scale resource sabotage plot (Aries or Pisces linked), helping establish a major new family ring, or resolving a generational conflict that threatened the entire district’s self-sufficiency. Rewards can include unique defensive upgrades, private siligel cache, or influential NPC allies within the family networks.





District 3: Leo
Leo player homes emphasize radiance, spectacle, self-expression, and public prestige. They feel like stages, dressing rooms, or personal spotlights — dramatic, warm, and designed to be seen. Acquiring them rewards charisma, public performance, festival contributions, and building a personal legend. Upgrades often involve showy customizations, lighting rigs, or reputation-boosting décor rather than pure practicality. They perfectly suit a player who wants to live like a star in Concordia’s cultural furnace.

1. Backstage Bolthole / Green Room Pod (Starter / Entry-Level Home)

Feel & Vibe:
Vibrant, energetic, and slightly chaotic. A compact, colorful pod tucked behind one of the smaller performance venues or rehearsal halls. Bright accent lighting, costume racks, half-finished props, and a large vanity mirror with glowing bulbs. It has that “just stepped off stage” buzz — warm, creative, and always ready for the next show.

Proximity:
Directly behind or beneath mid-tier theaters, rehearsal domes, and minor festival stages. Short walk to performer hangouts, talent agencies, and street-level busking spots. Easy access to aspiring artists, technicians, and festival staff.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Low-to-moderate effort. Requires basic reputation with Leo’s performance circles (running errands for troupes, performing small gigs, helping with stage setup, or donating glow-tech/scavenged lighting parts). Feels like earning your first dressing room.

Quest-Locked Options:
None for the base version, but cosmetic/performance upgrades (better lighting rig, small practice space) are available through minor side gigs and talent contests.

2. Spotlight Balcony Loft

Feel & Vibe:
Flashy, elevated, and performative. A stylish loft with a large balcony overlooking a popular promenade or secondary performance square. Dramatic red-gold lighting, holographic poster walls that can display player achievements or custom art, and a small private stage area. It radiates “rising star” energy — warm, proud, and intentionally visible to the public.

Proximity:
Overlooking major promenades, open-air performance plazas, and mid-level festival grounds. Close to talent scouts, street performers, and popular nightlife venues. Good access to Leo’s social scene and gossip networks.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Moderate effort. Requires consistent public performances, helping organize or promote festivals, and building favor with venue owners or influential artists. Some resource investment in flashy materials (glow panels, decorative insulation) is expected.

Quest-Locked Options:
A premium version (larger balcony, interactive holo-wall, or private green room extension) can be unlocked via a mid-tier questline centered on helping a rising troupe succeed or stealing the spotlight during a major event.

3. Private Performer’s Lounge Suite

Feel & Vibe:
Luxurious yet theatrical. A spacious, multi-room suite with high ceilings, custom lighting rigs, a small private performance space, and trophy/memorial walls for accolades. Rich fabrics, warm radiant heating tuned for comfort during long rehearsals, and subtle sensory elements (scent emitters, sound dampening). It feels like the residence of a respected artist or local celebrity — generous, proud, and a little larger than life.

Proximity:
In the more prestigious performer residential rings, near grand theaters, arena back entrances, and high-end after-party venues. Close to established stars, patrons, and cultural influencers.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Moderate-to-high effort. Demands significant reputation with Leo’s elite circles (headlining multiple shows, sponsoring events, or resolving rivalries). Notable contributions of rare performance tech or siligel for lighting are usually required.

Quest-Locked Options:
Yes — this home has a strong loyalty path. Unlocked or significantly upgraded after completing a notable questline demonstrating commitment to Leo’s spirit, such as organizing a major district festival, helping an aging star reclaim their legacy, or winning a high-stakes performance rivalry that boosts the whole district’s prestige.

4. Radiant Apex Penthouse / Idol’s Atrium (High-End / Prestige Home)

Feel & Vibe:
Spectacular, commanding, and legendary. A top-tier penthouse or illuminated atrium with panoramic views of the district’s glowing domes, a full private stage with professional rigging, customizable public holo-displays, and luxurious radiant heating that makes the entire space feel like a permanent spotlight. It screams “I am the main character” — dramatic, warm, and intentionally iconic.

Proximity:
At the highest or most central prestige locations, overlooking the Central Amphitheater or major festival grounds. Excellent access to district leadership, top-tier talent, and major cultural events. Perfect for hosting influential NPCs or public appearances.

Difficulty to Acquire:
High effort. Requires exceptional reputation, major financial/resource donations to the arts, and visible long-term contributions to Leo’s identity as the cultural heart of Concordia.

Quest-Locked Options:
Heavily quest-gated. This home is only available after completing a complex, multi-stage questline that cements the player as a Leo legend — for example, saving the district’s biggest festival from sabotage (involving Aries power politics or Pisces rivals), orchestrating a city-wide cultural renaissance event, or resolving a major inter-district scandal through a spectacular public performance. Rewards include unique showpiece upgrades, personal fan NPC followers, or story-significant celebrity status.





District 4: Scorpio
Scorpio player homes emphasize depth, transformation, intimacy with shadow, and rebirth. They feel intense, private, and psychologically charged — spaces for confronting truths, shedding old selves, and emerging changed. Acquisition rewards willingness to engage with taboo subjects, endure personal trials, and demonstrate loyalty to the district’s unflinching pursuit of truth and renewal. Homes often include ritual elements, memory archives, or symbolic rebirth features rather than conventional luxury.

1. Shadow Alcove Recovery Pod (Starter / Entry-Level Home)

Feel & Vibe:
Intimate, dimly lit, and introspective. A compact, reinforced alcove or small chamber with heavy sound-dampening, low crimson/amber lighting, and symbolic rebirth motifs (e.g., shedding chambers or mirrored confession walls). It feels like a cocoon or confession booth — safe for vulnerability but always carrying an undercurrent of psychological intensity.

Proximity:
Tucked within or near lower-level rebirth clinics and shadow-work centers. Short walk to Archive of Final Confessions, endurance ritual spaces, and discreet counseling chambers. Easy access to trauma therapists, rebirth guides, and residents in various stages of transformation.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Low-to-moderate effort. Requires basic trust with Scorpio practitioners (participating in introductory shadow sessions, helping with confidential errands, or donating psychological data/logs). Feels like proving you’re willing to face yourself.

Quest-Locked Options:
None for the base version, but small upgrades (better memory archive terminal or private ritual space) come from minor confession or support quests.

2. Sub-Level Transformation Loft

Feel & Vibe:
Raw, authentic, and metamorphic. A converted loft in the mid-levels with exposed structural elements, adjustable lighting for different ritual moods, and spaces for personal endurance or identity work. Sparse but powerful décor — perhaps a central “shedding circle,” private confession recorder, or symbolic art from past rebirths. It carries a heavy, focused atmosphere of ongoing change.

Proximity:
Near mid-tier rebirth facilities, underground endurance arenas, and taboo research archives. Close to shadow augmentation specialists and residents undergoing (or recovering from) major transformations.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Moderate effort. Demands active participation in district practices (assisting with rituals, protecting vulnerable patients during crises, or sourcing rare components for augmentation procedures). Reputation with both official clinics and underground practitioners is needed.

Quest-Locked Options:
A stronger version (enhanced privacy shielding, custom augmentation station, or hidden sub-chamber) can be earned through a mid-tier questline involving helping a patient through a dangerous rebirth or resolving an internal clinic scandal.

3. Private Shadow Sanctum Suite

Feel & Vibe:
Deeply personal, luxurious in darkness, and psychologically potent. A multi-room suite with thick privacy barriers, ritual chambers, a secure memory archive, and symbolic rebirth elements (e.g., a custom “death/rebirth” alcove or dual-identity wardrobe). It feels like a true sanctuary for confronting the self — intimate, powerful, and slightly dangerous.

Proximity:
In more exclusive transformation residential rings, near advanced psychological research labs and high-level confession archives. Good access to elite shadow workers, reformed (or still struggling) individuals, and those who have completed profound rebirths.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Moderate-to-high effort. Requires significant demonstrated commitment to Scorpio’s ethos (handling sensitive truths, protecting the district’s secrets, or aiding in ethically complex transformations).

Quest-Locked Options:
Yes — this home has a strong loyalty path. Unlocked or greatly enhanced after a notable questline showing dedication to transformation and truth, such as helping expose (or bury) a major hidden trauma affecting the district, guiding a powerful NPC through a full rebirth cycle, or resolving a crisis involving fractured identities.

4. The Abyss Watchtower / Rebirth Apex (High-End / Prestige Home)

Feel & Vibe:
Commanding, profound, and transformative. A high or deeply recessed sanctum with panoramic (or deeply introspective) views, multiple ritual spaces, advanced memory/past-life simulation tech, and symbolic architecture representing death and renewal. It feels like the residence of someone who has stared into the void and returned changed — intense, respected, and quietly feared.

Proximity:
In the most prestigious or symbolically charged locations, overlooking (or beneath) major rebirth temples and the Archive of Final Confessions. Excellent access to district leadership in psychological matters, influential survivors, and hidden power brokers.

Difficulty to Acquire:
High effort. Demands exceptional reputation, willingness to endure personal trials, and major contributions to the district’s work (rare psychological tech, protecting high-stakes rituals, or resolving existential threats to the community).

Quest-Locked Options:
Heavily quest-gated. This home is only accessible after completing a complex, multi-stage questline that proves profound loyalty to Scorpio’s principles — for example, descending into a dangerous collective shadow crisis affecting multiple patients, orchestrating a district-wide “great rebirth” event, or making a personally costly choice that reveals or buries a transformative truth with city-wide implications. Rewards include unique ritual upgrades, personal shadow NPC companion options, or story-significant identity-altering boons.






District 5: Aries
Aries player homes emphasize raw power, resilience, heroism, and functional toughness. They feel like fortified bunkers, overclocked workshops, or frontline outposts — loud, hot, and alive with the hum of machinery and the constant awareness that everything could overload at any moment. Acquiring them rewards grit, direct action, crisis response, and loyalty to the “keep the lights on” ethos. Upgrades focus on redundant systems, emergency power taps, and defensive reinforcements.

1. Shift Worker’s Overclocked Barracks Pod (Starter / Entry-Level Home)

Feel & Vibe:
Rugged, utilitarian, and buzzing with energy. A compact, reinforced pod in the worker barracks with exposed piping, emergency lighting, and a heavy-duty recharge/heating station. It hums with the sound of nearby generators and feels like a place where people crash between brutal shifts — honest, sweaty, and unapologetically industrial.

Proximity:
Inside or directly attached to worker barracks and lower-level power regulation stations. Short walk to geothermal taps, emergency response bays, and shift canteens. Easy access to overworked engineers, operators, and crisis crews.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Low-to-moderate effort. Requires basic reputation with Aries crews (completing emergency repair shifts, helping during minor blackouts, or donating scavenged parts). Feels like proving you can handle the heat.

Quest-Locked Options:
None for the base version, but practical upgrades (extra emergency battery bank or reinforced door) come from minor overtime or containment missions.

2. Pipe-Room Maintenance Loft

Feel & Vibe:
Industrial, high-adrenaline, and hands-on. A converted loft nestled among major heating/ power conduits with reinforced plating, tool rigs, and direct access to diagnostic terminals. Warm (sometimes uncomfortably hot), noisy, and alive with the glow of active pipes. It feels like living inside the beating heart of the district — raw and powerful.

Proximity:
Deep in the pipe networks and maintenance tunnels near critical junctions. Close to overclock monitoring stations, emergency response hubs, and veteran operator hangouts. Good access to heavy-duty repair teams and resource allocators.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Moderate effort. Demands consistent physical labor and reliability (multiple high-risk repair jobs, helping contain surges, or running dangerous scavenging runs for critical components).

Quest-Locked Options:
A stronger version (private power tap, better surge protection, or hidden armory) can be earned through a mid-tier questline involving stabilizing a failing section of the core or exposing minor sabotage.

3. Veteran Operator’s Reinforced Hab-Suite

Feel & Vibe:
Battle-worn, proud, and commanding. A solid, multi-room suite with reinforced blast doors, personal memorial wall for fallen shift mates, redundant backup generators, and a small tactical planning nook. It carries the weight of Long Night War stories and a “we held the line” atmosphere — warm, sturdy, and respected.

Proximity:
In the more secure veteran residential blocks, near command monitoring stations and Long Night memorials. Close to district leadership, elite operators, and high-priority response teams.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Moderate-to-high effort. Requires proven competence in crises and building trust with veteran crews (leading repair teams during major incidents or making tough calls under pressure).

Quest-Locked Options:
Yes — this home has a strong loyalty path. Unlocked or upgraded after a notable questline demonstrating commitment to Aries values, such as helping prevent a catastrophic overload, protecting a key operator during sabotage, or resolving an internal power struggle that threatened the grid.

4. Core Overwatch Citadel / Hero’s Apex (High-End / Prestige Home)

Feel & Vibe:
Dominant, high-stakes, and legendary. A fortified citadel-style residence overlooking or embedded near the main power core with panoramic monitoring windows, multiple redundant systems, a private tactical command room, and heavy defensive plating. It radiates “I keep the city alive” authority — intense, hot, and unmistakably powerful.

Proximity:
In the most prestigious and secure zones near the heart of the power core. Excellent access to top leadership, elite engineering teams, and critical decision-making hubs. Perfect for influencing city-wide power politics.

Difficulty to Acquire:
High effort. Requires exceptional reputation, heroic actions during major crises, and substantial contributions (rare components, personal risk in overclocks, or major political support for the district).

Quest-Locked Options:
Heavily quest-gated. This home is only accessible after completing a complex, multi-stage questline that cements the player as an Aries legend — for example, single-handedly averting a grid-wide collapse during a coordinated sabotage event (possibly involving Aquarius or Pisces), leading a daring expedition to secure a vital new siligel source, or making a sacrificial choice that saves the core at great personal cost. Rewards include private high-capacity power taps, elite defensive upgrades, or influential operator NPC allies.





District 6: Capricorn
Capricorn player homes emphasize discipline, ambition, merit, and long-term craftsmanship. They feel like fortified workshops, spires, or production-adjacent residences — structured, efficient, and built to last. Acquiring them rewards consistent output, technical skill, and loyalty to the district’s meritocratic ideals. Upgrades often focus on personal fabrication tools, quality testing bays, or reinforced private production spaces rather than pure luxury.

1. Apprentice Habitation Spire Pod (Starter / Entry-Level Home)

Feel & Vibe:
Practical, orderly, and ambitious. A compact, well-insulated pod in one of the merit-based worker housing spires. Clean lines, modular storage, a small personal workbench, and visible progress markers (e.g., a wall for tracking completed projects or certifications). It feels like the home of someone climbing the ladder through hard work.

Proximity:
Inside the worker housing spires, near engineering academies and apprenticeship towers. Short walk to material testing labs and lower-level forges. Easy access to mentors, apprentices, and merit-tracking administrators.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Low-to-moderate effort. Requires demonstrating basic competence (completing apprenticeship tasks, contributing to production quotas, or passing simple quality checks) plus modest resource donations.

Quest-Locked Options:
None for the base version, but small upgrades (better workbench or certification display) come from minor merit-based side tasks.

2. Quality-Control Workshop Loft

Feel & Vibe:
Focused, precise, and industrious. A mid-level loft with reinforced floors, integrated testing equipment, material analysis stations, and organized storage for components. It has a no-nonsense, high-standards atmosphere — warm from nearby forges but dominated by the hum of precision tools.

Proximity:
Near material testing labs, quality-control complexes, and mid-tier forges. Close to engineering teams, quality auditors, and production supervisors.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Moderate effort. Requires regular contributions to quality standards (assisting with testing, fixing production flaws, or supplying high-grade materials) and building a solid reputation with quality-control staff.

Quest-Locked Options:
A stronger version (advanced testing rigs or private cold-resistance simulation chamber) can be earned through a mid-tier questline involving resolving a major production defect or protecting a critical quality process.

3. Merit-Based Engineer’s Residence

Feel & Vibe:
Solid, prestigious within the system, and achievement-oriented. A well-appointed suite in a higher spire with reinforced construction, personal fabrication bay, display cases for successful projects, and efficient layout optimized for long work sessions. It carries a quiet pride in results and long-term contribution.

Proximity:
In the upper levels of the worker housing spires or near engineering academies. Good access to senior engineers, academy faculty, and decision-makers on production priorities.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Moderate-to-high effort. Demands consistent high output, leadership in projects, and strong reputation with the merit hierarchy (mentoring others, optimizing processes, or delivering major results under deadlines).

Quest-Locked Options:
Yes — this home has a strong loyalty path. Unlocked or upgraded after a notable questline demonstrating commitment to Capricorn values, such as leading a major infrastructure project, resolving a critical safety-vs-deadline conflict, or exposing corruption that threatened production integrity.

4. Industrial Command Spire / Master Builder’s Apex (High-End / Prestige Home)

Feel & Vibe:
Commanding, legacy-focused, and highly disciplined. A top-tier residence in a prestigious spire with panoramic views of the forges, private R&D lab, advanced quality-testing facilities, and reinforced command-style rooms. It radiates authority and long-term vision — the home of someone who shapes the district’s future through results.

Proximity:
In the highest and most secure spires, near central production oversight and long-term planning offices. Excellent access to district leadership, major project coordinators, and influential merit-based figures.

Difficulty to Acquire:
High effort. Requires exceptional reputation, major proven contributions (large-scale projects, process innovations, or resource breakthroughs), and significant investment in the district’s infrastructure.

Quest-Locked Options:
Heavily quest-gated. This home is only accessible after completing a complex, multi-stage questline that cements the player as a Capricorn leader — for example, successfully delivering a district-critical megaproject under extreme pressure (possibly involving Aries power constraints or Taurus resource disputes), resolving a major internal conflict between efficiency and worker safety, or making a high-stakes decision that secures long-term industrial dominance. Rewards include unique fabrication upgrades, private project oversight tools, or influential NPC allies in the merit hierarchy.






District 7: Aquarius
Aquarius player homes emphasize innovation, eccentricity, collective ideals, and radical experimentation. They feel unconventional, futuristic (sometimes unstable), and full of quirky, forward-thinking modifications. Acquiring them rewards curiosity, willingness to test unproven tech, support for visionary projects, and loyalty to the district’s idealistic (if chaotic) pursuit of progress. Upgrades often involve experimental prototypes, data interfaces, or reality-bending customizations.

1. Prototype Habitation Capsule (Starter / Entry-Level Home)

Feel & Vibe:
Futuristic, minimalist, and slightly glitchy. A compact, modular capsule with adjustable smart walls, experimental climate controls, and glowing holographic interfaces. It has an optimistic “beta version” energy — clean, innovative, and occasionally prone to quirky malfunctions that the resident learns to love (or patch).

Proximity:
In the lower experimental residential clusters near basic R&D labs and prototype testing yards. Short walk to communal innovation forums, young radical hangouts, and low-security experiment zones. Easy access to eccentric inventors and idealistic newcomers.

Difficulty to Acquire:
Low-to-moderate effort. Requires basic participation in district projects (testing early prototypes, contributing data logs, or helping with minor experiments) plus modest resource donations of rare components.

Quest-Locked Options:
None for the base version, but fun cosmetic/experimental upgrades (custom holo-skins or adaptive furniture) come from small volunteer testing gigs.

2. Idea Incubator Loft

Feel & Vibe:
Creative chaos meets visionary hope. A mid-level loft with open-plan workspaces, jury-rigged collaborative terminals, half-built inventions scattered around, and large windows overlooking experimental domes. It feels alive with possibility — inspiring but messy, with a constant hum of ongoing projects and debates.

Proximity:
Overlooking or embedded near collaborative innovation hubs, social experiment chambers, and mid-tier prototype workshops. Close to radical thinkers, data pattern analysts, and humanitarian project teams.

Difficulty to Acquire:
