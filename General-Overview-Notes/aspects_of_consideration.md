### Amundsen Tower
A space elevator (Orbital Elevator) with its base anchored at the South Pole (its name being a reference to old pre-war Amundsen Station). During the Long Night War, the base-anchor was destroyed, and the orbital elevator cable snapped off and upwards into outer space. All that's left at the South Pole is a gigantic mountain of scrap)

### International Data Syndicate
It's become a topic of understanding that there's great value, knowledge, and wisdom in preserving human history. Therefore, something like a sort of "international data syndicate" has arisen and taken shape. This essentially involves locating, securing, and retrieving datashards, old hard drives, and other records of the old pre-war global internet, but this is fraught with peril, as, post-Falkland Treaty (as of the year 2564), robot presence in Upper Earth is globally considered illegal, and homans who'd moved to Antarctica to be with the robots are considered defectors and are forbidden from returning to Upper Earth. Robots (and humans) who are willing to make the dangerous journey (by land, sea, or sky) dedicate their efforts to retrieving datadrives and bringing them to Tepenia to create an "Antarctican Internet", which comes to be known as the Arcanet.

### Arcanet --> Solarnet
As detailed above, the Arcanet is the Antarctican Internet. Following the Long Night War (or also known as The Midnight War) in the early-2700s, as Tepenians (both robot and human) escape into outer space, the data of the Arcanet expands into a "Solar-System-wide Internet", which comes to be known as the Solarnet. This information is held aboard Von Braun Wheels, O'Neill Cylinders, and just humble data server satellites at various locations throughout the Solar System, and is accessible as far as Saturn. There are plans to eventually extend this out past Neptune, but maintenance being what it is, this is a rough prospect to bring to fruition.

### The Eyes of Gold
A faction. These are people who believe that 007 James Bond was actually a real person and model their code of honor and discipline around him. They derive their name from "Goldeneye", as, in the 2400s/2500s, it had come to have been thought that this was a reference to having an actual golden ocular implant. Thus, when a member of the Eyes of Gold proves his or her competency and dedication, they get their dominant eye replaced with an ocular optic with a gold iris, which is capable of high-resolution Zoom. One of the most prominent engineers who crafts "gold eyes" is a robot from Korea by the name of Ji-Eun Kim. Following the Long Night War, not much is known about what happened to Ji-Eun.

### refurbishing reusable rockets
There have been a number of reusable rockets left over from the past. Tepenians make a point about refurbishing these reusable rockets in an effort to relocate more robots and humans into outer space, as Amundsen Tower has effectively been destroyed

### Concordia: industrial/economic/transit hub
A future "robot/human Sci-Fi update" of what we currently know today as Concordia Station (which is a real place). Due to its geographical setting, ended up being the most populous surviving city in post-war Antarctica.

### Kharkovchankas
Enormous post-Soviet-style ice vehicles used to traverse the wastelands of Antarctica in order to build the infrastructure for the Arcanet. The most prominent figure in the construction of the Arcanet is a robot named Michelle Stanton.

### Heating-systems (nuclear, volcanic, etc)
As Antarctica is a frost-locked wasteland, humans (as well as robots) need heating to survive, and to build their cities around. They take (or create) this heating from a variety of sources.

### mining resources for siligel
Siligel is essentially robot food, as, following in line with Newton's First Law of Energy, energy cannot be created nor destroyed. Therefore, robots need to get their energy from somewhere. They do have internal batteries, as well as "sleeping" in charging bays, but they still need an outside source of energy. This comes in the form of siligel, which is synthesized from compenents mined from quarries which mostly exist in cold places. Another prominent source of ingredients to create siligel is the Republic of Alaska.

### hydropons (i.e., to feed humans)
In addition to heating, humans also need to be able to grow plants and raise livestock for meat. These come in the form of hydropons.

### Goth spirituality (the ossuary, etc)
As I've come to gather, Goth (although not a "religion" in any real sense) is more of an "understanding" of Death as being the one true God. This is not something you "understand" with your brain, but rather, a reality that you "understand" with your existence. As such, there is an Ossuary for Goth robots (as well as humans) which is a cathedral, the interior of which is decorated with the metal bones of fallen and/or killed robots who did not survive the War of Upper Earth. Here, Goth robots and humans alike can come and pay their respects to the fallen.

### Palmer City
A future "robot/human Sci-Fi update" of what we currently know today as Palmer Station (which is a real place, at the tip of Antarctica near South America). This is a sort of robot/human version of Las Vegas, Atlantic City, and New Orleans in one (offering arts and culture, gambling, and any number of various activities), and exists as something like a sort of "Neutral Zone" municipality

### the Cymaticists
These are practitioners (mostly robots, although humans are allowed to join) who revere sound and vibration. They have a strong sense of reverence for the power of sound and vibration, and so by extension, the power of music.

### The Red Spiral
A faction. These are most akin to the "Followers of the Apocalypse" as seen at the time of the events of Fallout: New Vegas. Thus, they're essentially something like a sort of "doctors without borders", and live by codes of honor and duty oriented around the Hippocratic Oath, principles of First Aid, and Medical Attention (Emergency, Urgent, and non-Urgent)
